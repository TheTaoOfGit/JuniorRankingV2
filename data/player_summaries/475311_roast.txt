<p>⭐⭐ "Emily Feng's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for a satisfying dining experience. Walked in expecting a full-course meal but got served a lukewarm appetizer that left me wanting. The 45% success rate on their menu items is honestly generous - half the time you're leaving hungry.</p>

<p>The signature dish appears to be "Franklin Xu Cheng Special" (served 26 times and counting), which suggests either impressive loyalty to their suppliers or a concerning lack of menu diversity. The chef also occasionally pairs with Eesha Vikram and Joanna Zhang, but the kitchen seems to function best when Emily's handling the singles menu - currently ranked #16 in the U13 category, which is like being the 16th best taco truck in a small town. Respectable, but you're not exactly Michelin starred.</p>

<p>The most memorable moment was their runner-up performance at the 2025 Austin Leander South OLC - think of it as getting to the finals of a local chili cook-off only to lose to someone's grandmother's secret recipe. Close, but no trophy-shaped cigar. Their recent 0W-5L performance at Schafer SLG South OLC was particularly brutal - imagine a restaurant serving nothing but empty plates while confidently asking for payment.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due: showing up to the U.S. Junior National Championships and going 8W-8L is like a food truck holding its own at a fancy culinary convention. Not winning any awards, but hey, you didn't get completely roasted either. The improvement from 2024's disastrous 1W-6L nationals showing suggests the kitchen is learning, even if slowly.</p>

<p>Bottom line: Emily Feng's establishment has potential but needs more time to develop their recipes. Currently serving up mediocre badminton with a side of "maybe next tournament." The dining experience isn't terrible, just... forgettable. Two stars because the effort is there, but I'll wait for the Yelp reviews to improve before making another reservation.</p>
