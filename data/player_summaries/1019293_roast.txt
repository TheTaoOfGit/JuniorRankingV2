<p>Ah yes, the 2009 vintage Angelina Chun—a delightfully precocious selection that burst onto the junior circuit with all the subtlety of a sledgehammer to a shuttlecock. This particular vintage opens with pronounced notes of youthful ambition, immediately detectable on the nose with hints of SoCal sunshine and the faint aroma of participation trophies being left in the dust. The initial bouquet suggests a player who collected U13 titles like Pokemon cards, sweeping both singles and doubles at the 2021 SoCal ORC with the casual confidence of someone ordering a Happy Meal.</p>

<p>Upon first sip—I mean, upon watching her early career—one encounters a surprisingly full-bodied performance with complex undertones of partnership mastery. The Sophia Jia blend proves particularly robust, aged to perfection over 42 matches with notes of synchronized footwork and the occasional hint of "why did you go for that shot?" The palate reveals layers of versatility: singles success intermingling with doubles dominance, though there's a curious acidic finish that emerges specifically when facing Northwest competition, where she managed to lose three finals in 2022 with the consistency of a Swiss timepiece.</p>

<p>The mid-palate development shows remarkable growth potential, transitioning from U13 dominance to U15 respectability with the grace of a well-aged Bordeaux. However, there's an unmistakable cork taint detected at the 2023 YONEX Nationals, where despite reaching the GD U15 final, she discovered that sometimes the house wine simply isn't good enough for the five-star establishment. The finish lingers with notes of "what could have been" and just a whisper of "maybe next vintage."</p>

<p>Overall, this is a promising young vintage with excellent aging potential, though currently suffering from slight oxidation due to apparent recent inactivity. Best served with realistic expectations and perhaps a side of comeback ambition. Pairs well with patient coaches and optimistic parents. Rating: 72% approval—which, coincidentally, matches her career win percentage and suggests either cosmic alignment or a sommelier who did their homework.</p>
