<p><b>OCEAN'S U13: The Aayush Jaladurgam Job</b><br>
<i>[Camera pans across a dimly lit badminton hall. A shuttle drops. A scoreboard flips. They said a kid with a 2014 vintage couldn't knock over the vault in 23 tournaments flat. They were wrong. <b>Aayush Jaladurgam</b> assembled his crew, ran the numbers — <b>156-64, a 70% career win rate</b> — and executed one of the most audacious heists in junior badminton history. Eight titles. Three runner-ups. And one man standing between him and the #1 spot.</p>

<p>Every heist movie needs a nemesis, and Aayush's is <b>Vignan Chandrasekaran</b>. This rivalry is the film's central tension: three of Aayush's runner-up finishes came at Vignan's hands — the 2025 Austin Leander BS U13, the 2024 Northwest BD U11, and the 2024 South OLC BS U11. But here's the twist: Aayush <i>also</i> beat Vignan for the <b>BS U13 title at the 2026 DFW South ORC</b>. They're partners too (11 matches together), which makes this less "Heat" and more "The Departed" — you're never quite sure who's on which side. Vignan is simultaneously the mark and the inside man.</p>

<p>The crew is deep. <b>Siddhi Thorat</b> (13 matches) is the mixed doubles specialist — the safecracker, if you will — handling the XD U13 operations where Aayush currently ranks #7. <b>Leo Ye</b> (14 matches) was the early doubles wheelman, powering a <b>13-1</b> rampage at the 2024 NorCal ORC that netted both the BS U11 and XD U11 titles. <b>Jeremy Wang</b> took over as the BD partner for the 2026 season, contributing to an 11-5 outing at SoCal ORC and a 13-3 haul at DFW. <b>Kavya Balaji</b> (11 matches) and <b>Qingyao Liu</b> (10 matches) round out the ensemble — everybody has a role, everybody delivers.</p>

And the BD U13 #5 ranking, while strong, sits noticeably below the BS U13 #2 — as if Aayush occasionally forgets he has a partner on court and tries to solo the entire doubles match.</p>

<p><i>[Camera pulls back.
