Her breakthrough came early with the XD U15 crown at the 2022 South CRC, followed by a dominant GS U17 victory at the 2024 SOUTH OLC, and most recently the XD U17 title at the 2025 SOUTH CRC. This rare combination of singles and doubles excellence, spanning both girls' and mixed events, showcases her adaptability and tactical intelligence on court.</p>

<p>Currently ranked #30 in GD U19 (19,970 points) and #21 in XD U19 (23,985 points), Kam has demonstrated remarkable consistency at the highest levels, reaching four additional finals across her career. Her steady climb through the rankings tells the story of sustained improvement – from #33 in GD U15 in late 2023 to breaking into the top 30 at the U19 level. This upward trajectory reflects both her dedication and her ability to compete successfully against older, more experienced players.</p>

This versatility has been crucial to her 54% career win rate across 27 tournaments and 184 total matches.</p>

<p>Kam's mental toughness shines brightest on the national stage, where she posted an impressive 6W-4L record at the 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, improving significantly from her 6W-6L showing the previous year. Her standout tournament performance came at the 2023 South CRC, where she went 9W-2L while capturing a finals appearance, demonstrating her ability to maintain peak performance across multiple days of competition. With her combination of technical skill, strategic awareness, and championship experience, Kam continues to be a formidable presence across all junior divisions.</p>
