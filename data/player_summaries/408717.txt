Michael Sun is the number-one ranked player in both U13 boys' doubles and U13 mixed doubles, and his career record of 205-49 -- an 80-percent win rate across 20 tournaments -- marks him as one of the most dominant forces in U.S. junior badminton. Born in 2014, Sun has been a fixture near the top of multiple age-group rankings since late 2023, when he sat at number seven in BD U11, BS U11, and number four in XD U11. What distinguishes his 2025-26 campaign is the way he has weaponized his doubles game to an elite level while his singles ranking has taken a curious dip. He holds 7,087 points in both BD U13 and XD U13 -- tied for the highest totals in those divisions -- yet his BS U13 ranking has slipped to number 21 with just 2,519 points, a stark contrast to his BS U15 ranking of number 12 at 5,580 points. The disparity suggests that Sun has prioritized doubles competition in recent tournaments, and the results speak for themselves: back-to-back BD U13 and XD U13 title sweeps at the 2026 SoCal ORC and the 2026 DFW South ORC, both achieved with partner Vignan Chandrasekaran in doubles and Grace Cheng in mixed.

The Sun-Chandrasekaran partnership has become the most formidable boys' doubles pairing in the U13 division, and their repeated finals victories over Anirudh Rudharraju and Kevin Xie have established the defining rivalry of the age group. Sun beat Xie and Rudharraju in both the BD U13 and XD U13 finals at SoCal, then did it again at DFW, creating a four-title streak of dominance that has pushed Sun and Chandrasekaran to 26 shared matches and counting. Before Chandrasekaran, Sun's primary doubles partner was Siddhi Thorat in mixed doubles (34 matches), a collaboration that produced XD U11 titles and carried Sun through the 2023 Junior Nationals. Grace Cheng has now taken over the mixed doubles role with 24 matches alongside Sun, and the pair's back-to-back XD U13 titles suggest they are well synchronized. The one area where Sun has room to grow is singles, where his U13 ranking has dropped sharply from number three in mid-2025 to number 21 by March 2026. Whether that decline reflects a strategic choice to focus on doubles or a genuine struggle in singles form, the answer will likely shape whether Sun is remembered as a doubles specialist or a complete player. His 14-0 perfect run at the 2024 South ORC, where he swept BS U11, BD U11, and XD U11 titles in a single weekend, proves the all-around talent is there.
