<p>Archaeological Survey Report #2026-RK: Following extensive excavation of the Roger Kung competitive badminton site, our team has unearthed a fascinating collection of artifacts spanning the years 2023-2026. Initial carbon dating suggests this specimen emerged from the 2014 birth cohort, though advanced stratigraphy indicates a playing style that may predate the invention of the shuttlecock itself.</p>

<p>Layer Analysis reveals a curious pattern: while most junior players show improvement over time, the Kung site displays what our team has termed "horizontal excellence" - a remarkable ability to maintain identical point totals (1536, 2134, 1771) across multiple age divisions, as if fossilized in competitive amber. Of particular interest is the 2025 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS stratum, where we discovered evidence of a devastating 1W-6L extinction event, suggesting the subject may have mistaken the tournament for an archaeological dig site and spent most matches examining the court surface.</p>

<p>The Doubles Partnership Pottery Shards tell a compelling story of collaboration with notable artifacts including fragments of matches with Deepika Vaishnavi Venkatraman and Terrance Jiang. However, carbon analysis of the 48-62 career record suggests these partnerships may have been less "dynamic duo" and more "archaeological curiosity," with both parties likely spending considerable time excavating their own games from the depths of defeat.</p>

<p>Most intriguingly, we uncovered a singular golden artifact: one BD U13 championship trophy from the 2026 SEATTLE NW CRC dig site, buried beneath layers of runner-up medals and participation certificates. This rare find suggests that on one glorious weekend in February, young Kung briefly transcended his natural habitat of ranking mediocrity to achieve actual victory - a discovery so remarkable that our research team initially suspected contamination from a more successful player's burial site.</p>
