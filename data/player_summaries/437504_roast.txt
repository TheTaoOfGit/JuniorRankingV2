<p>EXPEDITION REPORT: Archaeological Survey of the Kim Dynasty Badminton Site (Est. 2022 CE). Our excavation team has successfully unearthed what appears to be the fossilized remains of a junior badminton career, buried under layers of shuttle feathers and participation ribbons. Carbon dating confirms this specimen belongs to one Kaitlyn Kim (b. 2011), whose career strata reveal a fascinating evolution from "adorable disaster" to "competent chaos."</p>

<p>The deepest archaeological layer, circa 2022-2024, contains numerous artifacts of youthful ambition meeting harsh reality. Of particular note: a 1W-6L specimen from the 2024 Junior Nationals, preserved in amber-like embarrassment, and the haunting 1W-3L fragment from her 2022 Midwest debut when she was barely 11. Our carbon analysis suggests this period was characterized by what ancient badminton scholars called "getting absolutely bodied by teenagers who hit puberty first."</p>

<p>The middle stratum (2024-2025) reveals the emergence of what we've termed "The Rudransh Dependency Period." Archaeological evidence shows 18 separate doubles matches with partner Rudransh Majumdar, suggesting Kim had discovered the ancient art of "finding someone better than you and clinging to them like a life raft." This era also yielded two finalist trophies from 2024 Midwest CRC, though sadly these were runner-up artifacts, indicating she was still perfecting the craft of "almost but not quite."</p>

<p>The uppermost layer (2025-2026) contains the most remarkable find: a pristine 9W-0L specimen from February 2026's Play N Thrive MW CRC, where Kim apparently achieved what archaeologists call "peak pubescent dominance." This recent stratum also contains multiple championship artifacts and rankings that no longer require a microscope to see. Our conclusion: after years of archaeological evidence suggesting she was mainly good at collecting participation medals, Kim has finally evolved into something resembling an actual threat on court.</p>

<p>Further excavation is recommended, as preliminary soil samples suggest this site may contain even more impressive artifacts in future seasons. Current hypothesis: Kim's career appears to be transitioning from "adorable archaeological curiosity" to "legitimate badminton civilization."</p>
