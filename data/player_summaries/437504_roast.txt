<p><b>Six Titles but Still Getting Destroyed by Charvi Srivastava in Every Final That Matters.</b></p>

<p>Kaitlyn Kim's 6-4 finals record and 69% three-game win rate look great on paper. She's clutch, she closes matches, she wins titles — all at the Midwest tournaments nobody outside Ohio has heard of. Her recent title run at the CBA Midwest OLC and Play N Thrive MW CRC sounds impressive until you realize she's farming points in a region where the competition is thinner than her mixed doubles ranking.</p>

<p>The real problem? She keeps losing to Charvi Srivastava in doubles finals. Charvi and Elena Zhang have beaten her twice, and Kaitlyn's runner-up finishes against them read like a recurring subscription to disappointment. She can beat everyone in the Midwest except the one person who actually matters.</p>

<p>Her 60% overall win rate is solid but unremarkable, and her progression from GD U13 #95 to GD U17 #34 shows steady improvement — the kind of slow, grinding climb that suggests talent is being outworked by sheer volume of tournaments. Eighteen partners in 19 tournaments means nobody wants to stick around for the long haul.</p>
