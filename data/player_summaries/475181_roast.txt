<p><b>4-6 in Finals: Venba Senkadir Wins More Semis Than Championships</b></p>

<p>Venba Senkadir has a 10-2 semifinals record, which is absolutely elite — and then a 4-6 finals record, which turns all that dominance into disappointment. She's the gatekeeper of the final round: she crushes everyone to get there and then rolls over when it actually counts. Ten semis wins and only four titles is the badminton equivalent of acing every exam but failing the final.</p>

<p>Her 75% three-game record (15-5) shows she has grit, but that grit mysteriously disappears when there's a trophy involved. She went from U11 #1 rankings territory down to U13 #19 in GD, suggesting the age-up transition has not been kind. Getting blown out by Xiner HU and Zelenia Shen confirms the U13 field has already taken her measure.</p>

<p>She and Devaansh Mahato have played 24 matches together — practically a common-law doubles partnership at this point. Four titles and six runner-ups mean she knows exactly what winning feels like, and even more intimately, what losing in the last round feels like.</p>
