<p>⭐⭐⭐ "Celine's Badminton Bistro" - Been serving up mediocre shuttlecock cuisine since 2021, and after 36 visits (tournaments) I can confidently say this place is the definition of "consistently average." With a 53% success rate, you're basically flipping a coin on whether your meal will be satisfying or leave you hungry for more. The menu promises championship-level dishes but usually delivers mid-tier rankings in the 50s-80s range.</p>

<p>The head chef Celine has been experimenting with different recipes since she was tiny, and while she's managed to perfect exactly TWO signature dishes (her 2024 NORTHWEST CRC and OBA NW OLC titles), she's also burned the soufflé in FOUR different finals. That's a lot of smoke alarms going off in what should be her specialty kitchen. The most tragic was probably that 2023 Dave Freeman Jr. OLC disaster - you could smell the disappointment from the parking lot.</p>

<p>What really grinds my gears is the revolving door of sous chefs - she's gone through partners like a restaurant burns through line cooks during rush hour. Poor Cynthia Wang has been her most loyal kitchen companion (19 services together), followed by Cayden Wang (17 shifts), but even they can't seem to elevate this establishment beyond "decent neighborhood spot." The coordination in doubles feels like watching two line cooks bump into each other during dinner rush.</p>

<p>I'll give credit where it's due though - that recent 8W-0L performance at the 2026 SEATTLE NW CRC was like watching Gordon Ramsay take over the kitchen for one perfect night. Absolutely flawless service, zero complaints, probably the best meal I've had here. But then you remember she's been operating since 2021 and has only managed two perfect dishes in all that time.</p>

<p>Look, if you're in the neighborhood and need a quick badminton fix, Celine's will fill you up without breaking the bank. Just don't expect Michelin star quality - you're getting solid B-tier dining that'll leave you neither raving nor requesting a refund. The young chef shows promise, but until she can consistently deliver those championship-caliber meals, I'll stick to the more established joints in town.</p>
