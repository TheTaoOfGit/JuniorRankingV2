The 2023 NW OLC bottling shows promise in its singular win, though the four losses provide a robust tannic finish that lingers uncomfortably. One detects notes of untapped potential aging alongside pronounced flavors of "still figuring out which end of the racket to hold."</p>

<p>The ranking profile offers an intriguing duality – BD U11 #127 paired with BS U11 #132, creating a harmonious blend of consistent mediocrity across both disciplines. <p>While critics might dismiss this as a modest offering with limited aging potential, I find Zhang's 2023 vintage charmingly unpretentious. Best consumed immediately before expectations have time to develop, though with proper cultivation, future vintages may surprise us all. Currently drinking at peak disappointment, but the terroir suggests room for improvement – assuming he remembers to bring his own racket next time.</p>
