<p>★☆☆☆☆ "The Olivia Zhang Story: A Descent into Badminton Darkness" - What starts as a promising sports biopic quickly devolves into an unintentional comedy of errors. Our protagonist, fresh off respectable U11 rankings (#31 GS, #36 GD), makes the bold narrative choice to age up into higher divisions, where she promptly embarks on a tour de force of strategic losses that would make even the most experimental filmmaker blush.</p>

<p>Director Zhang showcases remarkable consistency in her artistic vision, maintaining a flawless 0-15 record across five tournaments with the dedication of a method actor who never breaks character. Her performance at the 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC (0-4) was particularly haunting, followed by an encore at MIDWEST OLC that somehow managed to be even more devastating. The supporting cast rotates frequently - Sophie Che, Aiden Yu, Pradhyumn Surendran - like a badminton version of Spinal Tap's drummer situation, except instead of exploding, they just... lose.</p>

<p>The cinematography of Zhang's career arc is genuinely fascinating to watch unfold. Her current rankings read like a mathematical poem: #237 in GS U13, #432 in U15, #579 in U17 - each number growing larger as if counting down to some cosmic badminton reckoning. The XD rankings (#334, #665, #915, #1077) follow a similar trajectory, suggesting either a deliberate artistic statement about the futility of human ambition or just really, really bad luck at finding the net.</p>

<p>While critics have been harsh on this young auteur's work, there's something admirably fearless about Zhang's commitment to her craft. At barely 11 years old, she's already mastered the art of dramatic irony - every tournament entry suggesting hope while the audience knows exactly how this story ends. "The Olivia Zhang Story" may not win any trophies, but it's certainly memorable. Two stars for sheer persistence, minus three for execution. Coming soon to a local tournament near you.</p>
