<p>Abhinav Reddy Nimma (born 2010) is an American junior badminton player best known for his remarkable ability to maintain a sub-.500 win percentage across multiple age categories[citation needed]. Currently ranked #267 in Boys Singles U19, Nimma has demonstrated exceptional consistency in avoiding championship finals throughout his five-tournament career, a feat that statisticians describe as "mathematically improbable yet somehow inevitable"[citation needed].</p>

<p>Nimma first gained national attention at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, where he posted a flawless 0-6 record that tournament officials initially suspected was a clerical error[citation needed]. His performance was so memorably poor that the tournament director allegedly added an extra consolation round just to give players like Nimma "one more chance to win literally anything"[citation needed]. Despite this setback, Nimma has persevered with the determination of someone who clearly didn't get the memo about when to quit[citation needed].</p>

<p>Known for his rotating cast of doubles partners—including Lukas P Chu, Nathaniel Volfbeyn, and Anya Kuan—Nimma has mastered the art of spreading disappointment across multiple teammates[citation needed]. His partnership strategies have been compared to a badminton version of musical chairs, except the music never stops and everyone loses[citation needed]. Current partner Lukas P Chu was quoted as saying "At least with Abhinav, my expectations are properly calibrated"[citation needed].</p>

<p>In a surprising turn of events, Nimma's recent performance at the 2026 SEATTLE NW CRC showed marked improvement with a 6-3 record, leading experts to theorize that he may have finally discovered which end of the racket to hold[citation needed]. This breakthrough performance has elevated him to what coaches euphemistically call "aggressively mediocre" status in the Pacific Northwest junior circuit[citation needed].</p>
