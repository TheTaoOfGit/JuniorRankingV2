<p><b>Archaeological Dig Report: Meghana Nishant</b> — The excavation of this 32-tournament site reveals layers of steady persistence buried beneath a 53% win rate (119-103). The most significant artifacts — two GD U13 titles from 2021 — were unearthed in the earliest strata and nothing of comparable value has surfaced since.</p>

<p>The Mihir Thaha layer is by far the thickest deposit, spanning 59 matches and constituting the foundation of this entire dig. The XD U19 #26 ranking is built almost entirely on this partnership, which has proven more durable than most ancient ruins. GD U19 #14 represents the crown jewel currently on display, a significant climb from the GD U15 #38 found in the 2023 stratum.</p>

<p>Recent fieldwork has been mixed: the 2024 NorCal ORC yielded a barren 0-3 layer, while the 2023 Junior Nationals produced a respectable 9-6 haul. The 2026 SoCal ORC (3-4) suggests this site still has material to offer, but the absence of any titles or runner-up finishes since 2021 is the archaeological equivalent of a five-year drought between civilizations.</p>
