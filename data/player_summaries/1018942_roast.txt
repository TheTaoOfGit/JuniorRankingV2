<p>Archaeological Survey Report - Site Classification: "The Darron Su Excavation" - Following extensive carbon dating of tournament draw sheets, our team has successfully unearthed the fossilized remains of a junior badminton career spanning 2021-2024. Initial soil samples from the 2022 Junior National Championships reveal promising sediment (7-6 record), suggesting this was once a thriving civilization, before subsequent geological events buried it under layers of disappointment and ranking decay.</p>

<p>Artifact Analysis reveals a fascinating migration pattern across tournament sites, with the subject appearing to have wandered from NorCal to SoCal to Northwest regions like a nomadic tribe searching for favorable court conditions. Particularly intriguing is the discovery of multiple partnership pottery shards - eight matches with one "Kody Seow," suggesting either a successful hunting partnership or co-dependency issues. The abundance of different partner artifacts (Daphne, Tanvi, Rithika, Aidan) indicates either remarkable social skills or the badminton equivalent of musical chairs.</p>

<p>Stratigraphic evidence suggests a catastrophic event occurred during the 2024 ABC OLC period, where excavation yields only a single 0-1 loss artifact - possibly indicating the moment this ancient civilization realized it had better things to do. Carbon dating places the specimen's peak ranking achievements at BD U19 #99, BS U19 #118, and XD U19 #144, which in archaeological terms translates to "definitely existed but you'd need a really good telescope to see them from the top."</p>

<p>Conclusions: This dig site represents a classic example of "Mediocris Athleticus" - a species that survived in the harsh tournament ecosystem through sheer persistence rather than natural selection. The 23-37 overall record (38% win rate) suggests this civilization knew just enough about badminton to keep showing up, but not quite enough to avoid becoming an archaeological curiosity. Recommended for display in the Museum of "Well, At Least They Tried."</p>
