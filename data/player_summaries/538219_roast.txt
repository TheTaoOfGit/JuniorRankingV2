<p>Bhargav Vanaparthy (born 2011) is an American badminton player widely regarded as living proof that participation trophies may have gone too far.[citation needed] Currently ranked #276 in Boys Singles U17 and #415 in Boys Singles U19, Vanaparthy has distinguished himself as one of the few players to achieve a lower ranking in an older age group than a younger one, a feat described by experts as "mathematically improbable" and "concerning for the future of American badminton."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Vanaparthy's professional career spans an impressive three tournaments, during which he has compiled a stellar 1-5 win-loss record (16.7% win rate), placing him firmly in the bottom percentile of players who bothered to show up.[citation needed] His tournament debut at the 2025 FORTIUS SOUTH CRC resulted in a commanding 0-2 performance, immediately establishing his reputation as someone opposing players could schedule dinner plans around.[citation needed] He briefly flirted with mediocrity at the 2025 AUSTIN LEANDER SOUTH OLC, achieving a .500 record before returning to form with another 0-2 showing at the 2025 PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC.</p>

<p>In doubles competition, Vanaparthy has formed what can generously be called a "partnership" with Murari Danavulapati, though records indicate they have played exactly one match together.[citation needed] Sports analysts have speculated that Danavulapati may have been participating in a charity program or lost a bet.[citation needed] The duo's chemistry has been described as "nonexistent" and "a crime against the sport," though these assessments may be premature given their extensive one-match sample size.</p>

<p>Perhaps most remarkably, Vanaparthy has managed to achieve negative career momentum, sliding from respectable rankings of #119 in Boys Singles U17 and #134 in Boys Doubles U17 to his current positions in the statistical basement.[citation needed] This dramatic decline has led some observers to wonder if he has been playing badminton backwards or possibly with a tennis racquet.[citation needed] Despite his consistent underperformance, Vanaparthy remains optimistic about his future prospects, though this optimism is not shared by tournament directors, fellow competitors, or basic mathematics.[citation needed]</p>
