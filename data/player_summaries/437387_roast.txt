<p><b>Three-Game Record: 19-29. Charvi Srivastava Folds Faster Than a Lawn Chair in a Hurricane.</b></p>

<p>Charvi Srivastava boasts a 66% career win rate and a GS U15 #6 ranking, which sounds impressive until you learn she wins only 39% of her three-game matches. When a match goes the distance, Charvi's legs apparently don't. She's the player opponents WANT to push to a third game because they know she'll hand them the win like a consolation gift.</p>

<p>Her 9-9 finals record is the most perfectly mediocre stat in junior badminton. She's collected 8 titles and 6 runner-ups, proving she can get to the big stage — she just can't decide whether she wants to win or lose once she's there. And her partner Elena Zhang has carried her through 36 matches. Without Elena, Charvi's ranking would probably need a search party to find.</p>

<p>The blowout losses tell the story: Sophia Li, Sophia Sun, Kenneth Wu, and Carsten Lam all made her look like she picked up a racket for the first time. At least her progression from #53 to #7 in doubles shows improvement — or maybe just that she switched to a better partner.</p>
