<p>⭐⭐ "Kira Chu's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for the older crowd</p>

<p>Went to Kira Chu's establishment expecting a fine dining experience, but honestly, this place has serious consistency issues. The U11 menu was actually pretty solid - she served up two championship titles at the OBA NW OLC and YONEX SYNERGY NORCAL ORC that were genuinely impressive. But the moment you ask for anything from the U19 section, suddenly you're looking at #274 in singles and #475 in mixed doubles. It's like ordering the chef's special and getting gas station sushi instead.</p>

<p>The service staff seems confused about their roles too. Nina Kuan has been working overtime with 20 shifts together, while poor Kaishi Yang is pulling 16-hour weeks trying to keep this operation afloat. The new hire Brayden Liu only lasted 4 matches before apparently realizing this wasn't the fine dining establishment he thought he was signing up for. Even the most recent YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC visit was a complete disaster - 0 wins, 3 losses, like showing up to a restaurant and finding out they're out of literally everything on the menu.</p>

<p>Look, I get that this is a family business that's been growing since 2024, and the 59% success rate shows potential. The problem is the ambitious expansion into markets they're clearly not ready for. Stick to what you know, Kira's - that U11-U13 sweet spot where you actually know how to run a kitchen. Stop trying to compete with the big chains in the U17-U19 space until you've figured out your recipe. Two stars because that OBA NW OLC championship dinner was genuinely memorable, but I'm not coming back until they sort out their age-appropriate portion sizes.</p>
