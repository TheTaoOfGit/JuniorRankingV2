<p>Mia Hundley burst onto the national stage with a spectacular 2021 season, claiming not one but two U15 national titles at the USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships in both girls' doubles and mixed doubles. At just 14 years old, she demonstrated the rare versatility to excel across multiple disciplines, showcasing the court vision and tactical awareness that would become her trademark throughout her junior career.</p>

<p>The foundation of Hundley's success lies in her exceptional partnership chemistry, particularly her dynamic pairing with Emma Wei, with whom she has contested 26 matches across multiple tournaments. This partnership has been the cornerstone of her competitive achievements, contributing to her impressive 80-67 overall record spanning 19 tournaments. Her ability to adapt to different partners is equally notable, as evidenced by her successful collaborations with Michael Jiang in mixed doubles and strong showings with various teammates including Lukas Rujia Chang and Benjamin Dictado.</p>

<p>Hundley's competitive fire shines brightest in high-pressure situations, having reached seven major finals throughout her career. Beyond her national championship victories, she added another significant title at the 2024 YONEX South ORC in mixed doubles U19, proving her ability to maintain excellence as she progressed through age groups. Her four runner-up finishes, including appearances in U17 finals at the Junior National Championships and various ORC events, demonstrate her consistent ability to reach the business end of premier tournaments.</p>

<p>At her peak, Hundley achieved a career-high ranking of #18 in girls' doubles U19, while maintaining strong positions across multiple categories including #43 in girls' singles U17 and #28 in mixed doubles U19. Her 54% career win rate reflects not just success, but the courage to consistently challenge herself against top-level competition across the demanding ORC circuit, from Northwest to SoCal regional events.</p>

<p>What sets Hundley apart is her unwavering commitment to improvement and competition, having maintained active tournament participation across multiple seasons while building meaningful partnerships that have defined some of junior badminton's most exciting doubles combinations. Her journey from U15 national champion to seasoned U19 competitor represents the evolution of a player who understands that greatness comes through both individual skill and the collaborative spirit that makes doubles badminton so compelling.</p>
