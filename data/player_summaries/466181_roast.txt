<p><b>5-4 in Finals but 47% Three-Game Rate: Ayaan Kale Collects Titles and Close Losses in Equal Measure</b></p>

<p>Ayaan Kale has 5 titles and a 57% career win rate, which is solid for a U13 player. But his 8-9 three-game record (47%) means he loses more close matches than he wins -- a troubling stat for someone who plays 21 tournaments. His 5-4 finals record includes 4 runner-ups, meaning for every time he celebrates a title, he watches someone else celebrate almost as often.</p>

<p>All 5 titles came in BD U11, and his U13 results have been humbler: 5-3 here, 5-3 there, but no titles at the new age level. His blowout loss to Allen Wang in XD U11 and Amogh Lanka in doubles suggests he has a ceiling defined by the truly elite players. His primary partner Chris Lin (17 matches) has been loyal through the ups and downs.</p>

<p>His progression from unranked to BD U13 #22 is impressive, but the transition from U11 champion to U13 contender has been bumpy. Five titles is five more than most, but that 47% three-game rate says the close-match demons followed him to the bigger stage.</p>
