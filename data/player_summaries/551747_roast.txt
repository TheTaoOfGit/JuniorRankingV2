<p>Following extensive excavation at the FORTIUS SOUTH CRC dig site, archaeologists have uncovered the earliest known artifacts of the Omprakash civilization, dating back to the ancient year of 2025. Initial carbon dating of match results reveals a fascinating pattern: a pristine, untouched collection of zero victories, preserved in their original state across multiple tournament strata. Lead researcher Dr. Shuttlecock noted, "We've never seen such consistent preservation of defeat—it's almost like she actively avoided contamination by winning."</p>

<p>The Austin Leander South excavation proved particularly fruitful, yielding an unprecedented six consecutive losses in a single archaeological layer. Pottery shards found nearby suggest this may have been a ceremonial burial ground for her hopes and dreams. Radiocarbon analysis of her partnership artifacts shows brief alliances with local chieftains Adhvik Ramakrishnan and Adhya Santhoshkumar, though these diplomatic relations appear to have produced no territorial gains whatsoever.</p>

<p>Most intriguing is the discovery of her ranking tablets, which demonstrate remarkable consistency across age groups—maintaining her position in the lower archaeological strata from U11 through U19 categories. Scientists theorize this represents an early form of time travel, as she somehow managed to rank 539th in U17 despite being chronologically closer to U8. The mixed doubles findings are particularly puzzling, with XD rankings descending deeper into the earth with each age category, suggesting either advanced geological subsidence or a career trajectory following the laws of badminton gravity.</p>

<p>The PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC site yielded only a single loss artifact, leading researchers to conclude this was either a brief, unsuccessful hunting expedition or that she learned the ancient art of strategic retreat. Future digs are planned pending the discovery of actual victories, though current geological surveys suggest excavation may need to extend to previously unknown depths. Carbon dating indicates she still has several years before reaching junior fossil status, giving hope that future archaeologists may yet uncover evidence of her first winning civilization.</p>
