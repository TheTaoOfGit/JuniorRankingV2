<p><b>Six Titles, 6-5 Finals, But a 47% Three-Game Rate. Samyutha Aluri Wins Titles Despite Herself.</b></p>

<p>Samyutha Aluri has six titles and an 11-7 semis record, which is legitimately impressive for a 2015-born player. But her 47% three-game record (8-9) means she's below average in close matches, and her 6-5 finals record suggests those five finals losses haunt her just as much as the six wins comfort her. She went 1-6 at the 2023 South OLC in her early career — six losses in one tournament is generational bad luck.</p>

<p>Her blowout losses include getting destroyed by her own doubles partner's friends — Mandy Lu in GD U11 and Fiona Liu in GS U11 both blew her out, which is the competitive equivalent of your friend group collectively dunking on you. Her progression from GD U11 #79 to GD U13 #20 is a rocket ride, but the 47% three-game rate is a ticking time bomb.</p>

<p>Shubhan Sarikonda (22 matches) has been her ride-or-die partner. At GS U13 #19, Samyutha is good enough to be a consistent threat — just not consistent enough to be a reliable one.</p>
