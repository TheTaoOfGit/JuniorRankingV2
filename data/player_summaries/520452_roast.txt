<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Daniel's Badminton Bistro expecting a quick bite, ended up staying way longer than planned. The service here is... how do I put this delicately... extremely thorough. Like, they make sure you get your money's worth by extending every rally until you're questioning your life choices. At the 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals, I watched this kid serve up a 3W-4L special that had more plot twists than a soap opera.</p>

<p>The doubles menu is interesting - they rotate through partners faster than a food truck changes locations. Reyan Rastogi seems to be the house favorite (appeared 3 times), though I'm not sure if that's because he's good or because he's the only one who keeps coming back. The Xintong Darren Lu combo showed promise but only lasted 2 matches - probably realized the kitchen was a little too chaotic for fine dining.</p>

<p>Speaking of consistency, this place has some serious quality control issues. Started the year ranked #61 in the kids' menu but somehow dropped to #104 by 2026 - that's like going from "acceptable cafeteria food" to "gas station sushi." The 2024 YONEX NORCAL ORC experience was particularly memorable: a clean 0W-2L that was so efficient, I barely had time to finish my popcorn.</p>

<p>Look, I get it - this is clearly a family-owned operation run by someone who's still learning the business (kid was born in 2015, for crying out loud). The 6-14 career record suggests they're still working out the kinks in their recipe. But hey, at least when you visit Daniel's Badminton Bistro, you know exactly what you're getting: an authentic underdog experience with no pretense of fine dining. Sometimes you want a Michelin star, sometimes you just want heart. This place has plenty of the latter, even if the former is still a work in progress.</p>
