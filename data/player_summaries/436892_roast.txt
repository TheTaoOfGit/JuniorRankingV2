<p>Ah yes, the Paige Xu 2011 vintage—a promising young blend from the Midwest terroir that's still developing its character, though admittedly showing more sediment than clarity in its early pours. This 46% win-rate vintage presents with bold notes of inconsistency, opening with a harsh 0-4 debut at the 2022 Midwest CRC that would make even the most forgiving palate wince, followed by an absolutely brutal 0-6 showing at the 2024 Nationals that tasted like drinking vinegar through a tennis racket.</p>

<p>The bouquet reveals interesting partnerships—a dominant Eric Zhun Hu blend that's been decanted 15 times with mixed results, complemented by subtle Sahana Samarthy undertones across 11 tastings. There's a certain naive charm to this vintage's approach, like a sommelier who confidently pairs boxed wine with caviar, managing to reach two GD U15 finals only to finish second both times—a classic case of all nose, no finish.</p>

<p>What's particularly amusing about this vintage is its ambitious aging potential—currently ranked #62 in GS U17 yet somehow #112 in U19, suggesting this blend gets more confused the longer you let it breathe. The mixed doubles expression (#121 U17, #222 U19) shows similar identity crisis tendencies, like a Pinot Noir pretending to be a Cabernet while tasting suspiciously like grape juice.</p>

<p>To be fair, this vintage has shown marked improvement from its catastrophic early bottlings, evolving from complete undrinkability to merely mediocre with hints of promise. While it may never achieve grand cru status, it's developed enough complexity to avoid being poured down the drain—though I'd recommend waiting several more years before serving to guests, and perhaps keeping the receipt.</p>
