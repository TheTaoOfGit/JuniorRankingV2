<p>Xiaoyue Liu (born 2007) is a former junior badminton player best known for her remarkable ability to disappear from rankings faster than a shuttlecock hit by Carolina Marín.[citation needed] Liu achieved her career peak in 2023 when she won two Girls' Singles U17 titles, a feat that reportedly caused her to immediately develop an allergy to consistent performance.[citation needed] Her current unranked status represents what badminton historians call "the most spectacular ranking vanishing act since the invention of the drop shot."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Liu's partnership with Chloe Lim became the stuff of legend, spanning 20 matches and what insiders describe as "the most codependent doubles relationship since peanut butter met jelly."[citation needed] Their collaboration was so intense that Liu reportedly forgot how to play singles, leading to her 58% win rate which mathematicians have calculated as "aggressively mediocre."[citation needed] The duo's chemistry was so strong that Liu's other partners, including poor Tsun Wang Lam (11 matches), were allegedly treated like temporary roommates at a summer camp.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Her 2023 campaign included a crushing defeat in the XD U17 final at the Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, where she proved that winning two titles in the same tournament format was apparently her annual quota.[citation needed] Tournament directors noted that Liu's presence guaranteed exactly one thing: unpredictability, as evidenced by her 1W-3L showing at the 2025 YONEX SOUTH ORC, a performance so disappointing it made her previous 1W-0L record at South Frisco look like a masterclass.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Liu's ranking progression from GS U17 #3 to completely unranked has been described by sports analysts as "either the most dramatic fall from grace or the most successful witness protection program in badminton history."[citation needed] Her 82-60 career record stands as a monument to the concept of "good enough to win sometimes, not good enough to matter consistently," a philosophical approach that has reportedly influenced a generation of recreational players.[citation needed]</p>
