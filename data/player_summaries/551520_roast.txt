<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Wang's Badminton Bistro - "Still Working Out the Kinks"</p>

<p>Visited this establishment four times over the past year and honestly, the consistency is... challenging. The venue keeps changing locations (SoCal, DFW, Nationals, NorCal) but somehow the service remains reliably unpredictable. Out of 16 total interactions, only 3 resulted in satisfactory experiences - that's like a 19% success rate, which would get most restaurants shut down by the health department. The signature dish appears to be "Doubles Delight" where she teams up with various sous chefs like Jason Chang (her most frequent collaborator - they've worked together twice!) but even with different kitchen partners, the results are disappointingly uniform.</p>

<p>The ambition is admirable though - this place simultaneously tries to serve customers in the U13, U15, U17, AND U19 demographics, which is like trying to be a fine dining establishment and a Chuck E. Cheese at the same time. Currently ranked #102 in the junior division, which puts her solidly in the "we'll seat you eventually" category rather than "reservations required." Her mixed doubles venture is particularly adventurous, sitting at #204 in U13 - it's like watching someone try to make sushi when they're still learning to boil water.</p>

<p>That said, she's clearly new to the business (born 2015, so she's practically still in culinary school), and there's something endearing about her determination to keep the doors open despite the reviews. The recent performance at Nationals showed she can occasionally deliver when it counts, managing to satisfy one out of four customers. Will I be back? Probably not immediately, but I'll keep an eye on this place - sometimes the restaurants that struggle most in their early days end up becoming the most beloved neighborhood gems. Just maybe give her a few more years to perfect the recipe.</p>
