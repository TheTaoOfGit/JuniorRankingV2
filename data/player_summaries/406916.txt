<p>Emily Chan's meteoric rise through junior badminton began with a statement-making #3 ranking in Girls' Singles U11, and she hasn't looked back since. Now competing as a 13-year-old in U15 divisions, Chan has maintained her competitive edge with a current #36 GS U15 ranking backed by 3,744 points, proving her ability to excel against older, more experienced opponents across multiple age categories.</p>

<p>The defining moment of Chan's young career came at the 2024 ABC OLC, where she captured her first major title in Girls' Doubles U13, showcasing the tactical maturity and court awareness that sets her apart from her peers. Her 82-67 career record across 22 tournaments reflects not just talent, but remarkable consistency, with standout performances at prestigious events including back-to-back appearances at the YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships where she posted impressive 9-6 and 7-6 records in 2025 and 2024 respectively.</p>

<p>Chan's versatility shines through her multi-discipline success, currently ranked in the top 100 across Girls' Singles in three age groups while maintaining competitive standings in both Girls' and Mixed Doubles. Her exceptional chemistry with doubles partner Madison Bach, forged over 21 matches together, has become a cornerstone of her doubles strategy, complemented by successful partnerships with Madison H Bach and rising talents like Nihal Reddy Gajjala.</p>

<p>What makes Chan truly special is her fearless approach to competition, consistently entering premier events like the YONEX ORCs across multiple regions from NorCal to SoCal to Northwest. Her willingness to test herself against the nation's best junior talent, combined with her steady ranking progression from her early U11 dominance to her current multi-age group success, positions her as one of the most promising young talents in American junior badminton.</p>
