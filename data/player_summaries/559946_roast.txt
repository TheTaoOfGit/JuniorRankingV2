<p>⭐⭐ (2/5 stars) - Went to Elena Yang's Badminton Bistro expecting a feast, but left hungrier than when I arrived. The menu promised exciting singles and doubles options, but after 13 visits across two locations (SGVBC and YONEX ARENA), I only got one satisfying dish out of the entire experience. That's like an 8% success rate - even my local Taco Bell does better than that!</p>

<p>The service was inconsistent at best. My server Kylie Ou tried her hardest through 6 different visits, but even she couldn't salvage this dining disaster. When they switched to Jacob Zhang for 3 attempts, things didn't improve much. The kitchen seems overwhelmed - they're trying to serve everything from U11 appetizers to U19 entrees simultaneously, but they're clearly not ready for that kind of volume. Stick to the kids' menu for now!</p>

<p>The restaurant's ranking system is confusing too. They claim to be #46 in Girls Singles U11, but somehow they're also #543 in U19? Pick a lane! It's like a diner trying to be both a neighborhood joint and fine dining - you end up disappointing everyone. The Mixed Doubles section is particularly tragic at #1153 in U19. I've seen food trucks with better consistency.</p>

<p>Look, I get it - this place just opened in 2025 and the chef is still learning. The potential is there (that one win at SGVBC shows promise), but right now it's more educational experience than satisfying meal. Maybe come back in a few years when they've figured out their signature dishes. For now, I'd recommend the children's menu only - they're not ready for the adult palate. Will grudgingly return because my kid insists, but tempering expectations accordingly.</p>
