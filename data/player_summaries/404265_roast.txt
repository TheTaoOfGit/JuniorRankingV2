<p>Welcome to the Hamilton Le Career Trail, a challenging 8-tournament trek through the wilderness of junior badminton that's best attempted with low expectations and plenty of water. This particular path, established in 2021, is notorious among hikers for its steep difficulty curve and frequent dead ends. At 2 wins and 20 losses, you'll find this trail has roughly the same success rate as a tourist trying to summit Everest in flip-flops.</p>

<p>As we begin our descent, notice how the trail immediately plunges into treacherous terrain at the 2021 SoCal ORC, where our young explorer first learned that badminton courts, much like unforgiving mountain passes, show no mercy to the unprepared. The path continues its downward trajectory through 2022, featuring scenic stops at both the NorCal ORC and Synergy NorCal ORC—two particularly barren stretches where Hamilton managed to collect losses like a hiker gathering pine cones, except significantly less useful.</p>

<p>Seasoned trail guides will want to point out the fascinating phenomenon occurring around mile marker 2024, where our subject attempted to traverse the treacherous Yonex U.S. Junior National Championships. Here, visitors can observe Hamilton's natural habitat: the first-round elimination zone, where he went 0-4 with the determination of a GPS system that's completely given up. The trail markers here are somewhat confusing, as his partnership with "Kezhen Tao" appears to have evolved into "Kezhen TAO"—either a documentation error or Hamilton's doubles game is so disorienting it affects capitalization.</p>

<p>The trail concludes at elevation rankings of #144 in boys' doubles, #125 in boys' singles, and a breathtaking #251 in mixed doubles—altitudes so modest they're technically considered sea level in the badminton world. While other hikers have scaled championship peaks, Hamilton has mastered the art of consistent valley exploration, proving that sometimes the journey really is more important than the destination, especially when the destination keeps moving further away.</p>
