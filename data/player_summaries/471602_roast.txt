<p><b>2 Titles but 42% Three-Game Rate and 5-6 Semis: Jida Wu Grinds Hard for Diminishing Returns</b></p>

<p>Jida Wu has 2 titles and a 55% career win rate across 21 tournaments, which sounds decent until you see his three-game record of 8-11 (42%) and 5-6 semis record. He loses more close matches than he wins, and he loses more semis than he wins. His 2-3 finals record means even when he makes it, he usually loses. The math doesn't lie: Jida is the tortoise of junior badminton, except the tortoise doesn't win the race either.</p>

<p>Both titles came at the Schafer SLG South OLC, a single tournament where he went nuclear. Outside that event, his resume is a collection of 1-3s and 2-4s. He went 1-4 at the Midwest ORC and 1-3 at the NorCal ORC. His blowout losses include Jason Gu and Melvin Cui, and his partner Liam Xiao (18 matches) and Mrugakshi Kulkarni (13 matches) have been loyal through the mediocrity.</p>

<p>Ranked BD U15 #36 and BS U15 #26, he's good enough to be ranked but not good enough to win consistently. The Schafer SLG titles are carrying his entire career on their backs.</p>
