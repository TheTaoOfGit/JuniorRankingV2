<p>Ah, the 2012 Srineet Atluri—what a fascinating vintage we have here! Upon first inspection, this specimen presents with remarkable early complexity for such a young vintage, displaying notes of Junior National Championships gold from the very first sip. The initial bouquet is dominated by strong doubles undertones, with particularly pronounced mixed doubles characteristics that have aged beautifully from U11 to U15, though one detects a certain... how shall I put this... singles deficiency that becomes more apparent as the vintage matures.</p>

<p>The palate reveals an intriguing partnership with the 2011 Stephanie Jiayi Song—75 matches worth of symbiotic flavors that create a harmonious blend, complemented by secondary notes of Mikael Chang collaboration. However, I must note a peculiar phenomenon: this vintage has an almost supernatural ability to reach the final stages of fermentation, yet struggles with the finishing process. Twenty-one finals losses create a rather distinctive aftertaste—like a wine that promises greatness but leaves you wondering what might have been.</p>

<p>What's particularly amusing about this vintage is its geographic promiscuity—from NorCal to SoCal, Midwest to South, this little grape has rolled around more ORCs than a sommelier at a convention. The 2024 season shows remarkable improvement, with the vintage finally learning to close out tournaments rather than simply provide excellent entertainment in finals. The current rankings suggest BD U15 #2 and XD U15 #4 positioning—clearly this vintage has found its optimal serving temperature in the doubles department.</p>

<p>The finish is surprisingly long for such a young vintage, with hints of future potential that suggest this 2012 will age magnificently. Though I suspect when he reaches full maturity, we'll look back on these early finals losses and chuckle—much like how we now laugh at thinking Beaujolais Nouveau was sophisticated. A promising vintage that's still learning the difference between reaching the cellar and actually storing properly, but one I'd certainly recommend adding to your collection before the price goes up.</p>
