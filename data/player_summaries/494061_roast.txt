<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Matthew Lau's with high expectations after hearing about their U11 specials, but what a rollercoaster dining experience. The service started strong – they absolutely crushed it during the 2025 Nationals tasting menu, going 11W-2L which had me thinking this was a Michelin-starred establishment. But then I made the mistake of ordering from their ABC OLC section and got served a devastating 0W-3L platter that left me questioning everything I thought I knew about this place.</p>

<p>The doubles pairings here are inconsistent at best. The Aiden Yu combo meal (13 servings) was decent, and the Saanvi Jha fusion dishes (12 attempts) showed promise, but by the time they're mixing in Tyler Jiang and Meera Patel, you can tell the kitchen is just throwing ingredients together hoping something works. The mixed doubles section is particularly disappointing – one XD U11 final at Nationals was promising, but then they completely bottomed out, ranking #658 in U19 XD. That's like advertising fine dining and serving cafeteria food to teenagers.</p>

<p>What really gets me is the false advertising. This place bills itself as competitive across all age groups, but take one look at their BS U17 ranking (#342) or their XD U17 performance (#502) and you realize you're basically eating at a Chuck E. Cheese that thinks it's The French Laundry. Sure, they're killing it in the U11 scene with #16 BS and #13 BD rankings, but the moment they try to serve older customers, the quality drops faster than a net cord winner.</p>

<p>I'll give credit where it's due – those Dave Freeman Jr. SOCAL OLC championships weren't flukes, going 8W-3L with double titles is genuinely impressive. But then you see them struggle through events like the 2025 Dave Freeman Jr. tournament (3W-4L) and realize this place is more inconsistent than a restaurant that changes chefs every week. The 62% win rate sounds good on paper, but when you're bouncing between serving championship-level dishes and complete disasters, diners never know what they're getting.</p>

<p>Bottom line: Matthew Lau's is a solid neighborhood spot if you're looking for U11 comfort food, but don't expect the same quality if you're hoping for a more mature dining experience. They've got potential and the 2025 Nationals performance proves they can cook when it matters, but until they figure out their identity beyond the kids' menu, I'd recommend managing your expectations. Worth a visit if you're in the area, but maybe call ahead to see which version of the restaurant is open that day.</p>
