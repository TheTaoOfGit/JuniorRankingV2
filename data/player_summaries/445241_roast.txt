<p>Ah, the Daksha Ravindhar 2010 vintage—a delightfully precocious offering that arrives with all the confidence of a Beaujolais Nouveau but the win-loss ratio of a gas station Chardonnay. This spirited blend presents an immediate bouquet of youthful ambition, with subtle notes of "0W-4L at Midwest ORC" and a lingering finish of "maybe next tournament." The palate reveals layers of complexity: she's simultaneously ranked #84 in Girls' U17 Singles yet maintains a robust 10% career win percentage that speaks to either remarkable consistency or a profound commitment to the learning experience.</p>

<p>The 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals vintage shows remarkable promise—a rare 2W-1L showing that suggests this young wine may have finally found its terroir. Like a fine Burgundy that suddenly develops character after years in the cellar, Daksha demonstrated she could occasionally defeat opponents who weren't expecting much resistance. Her partnership with Surbhi Sakshi across four matches creates a doubles blend with all the predictability of a box wine, yet somehow manages to surprise with unexpected moments of competence scattered between the inevitable losses.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this vintage is its remarkable aging potential. The progression from #122 to #84 in singles suggests a wine that's actually improving with time, unlike her tournament results which remain stubbornly consistent in their disappointment. Each ORC tournament becomes another tasting note: "hints of first-round elimination with undertones of character-building defeat." The CAN-AM NORCAL showing—a respectable 1W-5L—demonstrates that while this vintage may not win any competitions, it's developing the kind of resilience that makes for excellent stories at dinner parties years later.</p>
