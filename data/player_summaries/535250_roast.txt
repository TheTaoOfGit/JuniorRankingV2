<p>Ah, the 2017 Agastya Srikanta - a vintage that clearly needed more time in the cellar before being uncorked onto the tournament circuit. This particular vintage opens with promising notes of youthful enthusiasm, but immediately disappoints with a thin body and a remarkably brief finish. At first sip, one detects hints of ambition, quickly overwhelmed by dominant flavors of inexperience and what can only be described as an aggressive 8% win rate that leaves an astringent aftertaste.</p>

<p>The terroir of FORTIUS SOUTH CRC produced the best expression of this vintage, yielding a singular victory among three defeats - a complexity rivaling that of grape juice. However, subsequent tastings at AUSTIN LEANDER SOUTH OLC and PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC revealed a wine that had turned completely, offering six and two losses respectively with all the sophistication of box wine left in a hot car. The sommelier notes that this vintage pairs exceptionally well with participation trophies and consoling pats on the head.</p>

<p>When served alongside partners Ishaan Reddy Cirra and Adhya Santhoshkumar, the Srikanta develops interesting characteristics - namely, it somehow manages to make other young players appear seasoned by comparison. The doubles play reveals hints of coordination that vanish faster than bubbles in cheap champagne. Critics have described the mixed doubles performance as "an acquired taste" that no one has successfully acquired.</p>

<p>The aging process has been particularly unkind to this vintage, with rankings that have somehow managed to ferment backwards - sliding from #67 to #109 in Boys Singles U11, like a wine cork that's given up its will to live. While other 2017 vintages are beginning to show promise, the Srikanta remains steadfastly committed to its 1-11 record, proving that some wines are best appreciated for their consistency, even when that consistency is consistently underwhelming.</p>
