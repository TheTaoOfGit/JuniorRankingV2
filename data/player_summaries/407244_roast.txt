<p>After months of careful excavation at the Felix Zhang dig site, our archaeological team has uncovered a fascinating civilization that once flourished between 2022-2025 CE. The sedimentary layers reveal a culture primarily sustained by the worship of one sacred artifact: a single BD U17 trophy from the 2024 MIDWEST OLC, discovered buried beneath seventeen other tournament programs like some sort of badminton Rosetta Stone. Carbon dating confirms this lone relic represents the apex of an entire civilization, making it roughly as rare as finding a decent singles game in Zhang's fossil record.</p>

<p>The stratigraphy tells a remarkable story of adaptation and survival. Our team has identified the "Vedaant Period" (16 matches deep), during which this ancient society appears to have developed a symbiotic relationship with one Vedaant Sreedharan, likely for protection against the harsh realities of playing actual competitive badminton alone. Pottery shards from various tournament sites suggest Zhang's people practiced a curious 42% win-rate ritual, possibly believing that losing more than half the time would appease the badminton gods and eventually grant them passage to the mystical land of "positive ranking points."</p>

<p>Perhaps most intriguing is evidence of what archaeologists are calling the "Great Singles Extinction Event" of Zhang's career. Layers of sediment from the BS U19 #297 period indicate a catastrophic collapse in solo performance, with artifact fragments suggesting the entire singles civilization may have migrated permanently to doubles territory for survival. Contemporary cave paintings (ranking sheets) show a dramatic population boom in the BD category, while mixed doubles settlements remained mysteriously sparse at #562, likely due to inadequate resources or the simple fact that playing with actual girls required social skills not yet developed by this ancient culture.</p>

<p>The most recent excavation layers reveal promising signs of evolution, with BD U19 rankings climbing from the archaeological depths to a more respectable #89. However, our final analysis suggests this civilization peaked early and hard with that 2024 MIDWEST OLC victory, then spent the remainder of its existence trying to convince itself that "building character through adversity" was actually the plan all along.</p>
