<p><b>Gender Listed as "Male." The Data Has Questions, and So Do We, Catherine.</b></p>

<p>Catherine Du's data file lists her gender as "Male," which is either a clerical error or the most aggressive form of sandbagging in junior badminton history. Regardless, she's racked up 7 titles against 3 runner-ups with a 7-3 finals record, which is genuinely impressive — until you notice most of those titles came at the CBA Midwest OLC and Midwest CRC, tournaments so small they might as well be held in someone's garage.</p>

<p>Her three-game record is a perfectly flat 10-10, the competitive equivalent of a shrug emoji. She climbed from GS U11 #57 to GD U15 #38 in three years, which is solid progress but still leaves her firmly in the "who?" tier of national rankings. Her blowout losses to Vindhya Chekuru in U11 suggest she peaked early and has been coasting on doubles partnerships ever since.</p>

<p>With 18 matches alongside Chelsie Cheng, Catherine has found her comfort zone: letting someone else do the heavy lifting while she collects trophies in the Midwest.</p>
