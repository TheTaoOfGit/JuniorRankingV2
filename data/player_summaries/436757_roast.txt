<p>After months of painstaking excavation at the Southern California badminton sites, our team has uncovered what appears to be the scattered remains of Andrew Zhang's junior career, dating back to the ancient period of 2022. Initial carbon dating of shuttle feathers found at the Dave Freeman Jr. OLC dig suggests this civilization suffered a catastrophic 0-3 extinction event, with similar evidence of societal collapse found across multiple strata including the 2023 SoCal ORC and YONEX SoCal CRC sites.</p>

<p>Most intriguing is the discovery of partnership pottery shards bearing the names "Vihaan Tungala" and "Luna Zhang," suggesting Zhang's people attempted to form strategic alliances to survive the harsh tournament environment. Archaeological evidence indicates these partnerships yielded mixed results, with the Tungala artifacts showing signs of repeated use across four separate excavations, while other alliance fragments with Kyle Zhan and Dylan Ji Daniels appear to have been hastily abandoned after single-use attempts.</p>

<p>Perhaps most remarkable is the recently unearthed 2025 SGVBC SOCAL CRC settlement, where Zhang's civilization briefly achieved a rare 3-3 equilibrium—the closest this ancient society ever came to sustainable living. However, subsequent geological surveys reveal a dramatic decline in regional influence, with ranking inscriptions showing a precipitous fall from the heights of #124 BS and #129 BD to the current archaeological depths of #318 and #236 respectively.</p>

<p>While our 15-year-old subject's empire currently lies in ruins across eight tournament sites with a haunting 4-19 battle record, ongoing carbon analysis suggests this Bronze Age civilization may yet evolve. After all, even the mightiest archaeological sites started as humble settlements, and Zhang still has several millennia of junior eligibility to perhaps construct something more permanent than his current collection of beautiful, perfectly preserved losses.</p>
