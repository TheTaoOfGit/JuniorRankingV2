<p>Ah yes, the 2015 Arjun Sharma vintage – a curious little number from the junior terroir that presents with an immediately perplexing bouquet. Upon first swirl, one detects notes of unfulfilled potential and the faint aroma of participation ribbons, though there's an unexpected hint of promise buried beneath layers of 18-21 sediment that suggests this bottle may have been opened far too early.</p>

<p>The palate reveals a surprisingly unbalanced structure, with the singles expression showing particularly weak body across all age classifications – from a modest #370 in U13 to an almost comically thin #1045 in U19. However, the doubles blend shows marginally more character, achieving a respectable #342 vintage in U13 before gradually losing its complexity as it aged into older categories. One must note the curious partnership with Daksh Thakur appears to bring out the best qualities, though six collaborative tastings hardly constitute a reliable assessment.</p>

<p>The most remarkable characteristic of this vintage is its extraordinary inconsistency – ranging from the disastrous 0-2 debut at the 2024 YONEX South ORC (a performance so forgettable it wouldn't qualify as cooking wine) to the absolutely stunning 7-1 masterpiece at the 2025 NE OLC, where somehow this humble bottle managed to claim a BD U11 title. It's as if someone accidentally mixed a Two Buck Chuck with a rare Burgundy.</p>

<p>The finish is perhaps most telling – what began as a promising February 2025 showing at #29 and #35 has since degraded into a bewildering spread across the rankings table that would make a sommelier question their palate entirely. With its 46% success rate and tendency toward dramatic inconsistency, this vintage is best enjoyed with lowered expectations and perhaps a stronger bottle nearby for backup. Not recommended for serious collectors, but may surprise at casual tournaments with sufficient aging and proper coaching guidance.</p>
