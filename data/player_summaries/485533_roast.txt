<p>⭐⭐ "Ethan Li's Badminton Bistro" - Went here expecting a feast, left hungrier than when I arrived. The service was inconsistent at best - out of 28 total orders placed, only 10 were delivered successfully (36% success rate, yikes). The management really needs to work on their delivery game.</p>

<p>The menu promised big things, especially after that decent showing at the 2025 Capital NE OLC where they actually went 6-3, but every other visit has been disappointing. The recent Valentine's weekend disaster in Dallas (0-3 at DFW South ORC) was particularly brutal - it's like they forgot how to cook entirely. And don't even get me started on their performance at the Junior Nationals championships - went 0-4 like they were serving raw chicken to Gordon Ramsay.</p>

<p>The doubles pairings with Xintong Darren Lu show some promise (they've collaborated 5 times), but the mixed doubles menu with partners like Kaydence Cheung needs serious work - currently ranked #218 in U13, which is restaurant code for "maybe try the drive-through instead." The kitchen seems overwhelmed trying to serve multiple age groups simultaneously, ranked anywhere from #89 to #579 depending on what you order.</p>

<p>Look, the kid's only been in business since 2024 and he's barely 11 years old, so there's room for growth. But right now, dining at Ethan Li's feels like ordering a 5-course meal and getting a happy meal instead. Two stars because the effort is there and honestly, the potential for improvement is huge. Just maybe lower your expectations and bring a backup dinner plan.</p>
