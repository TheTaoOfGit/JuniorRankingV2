<p>Ah yes, the Brian Hu 2023-2024 vintage – a particularly challenging collection that opens with bold notes of optimism, though sadly lacking in any discernible finish. This young vintage from the Class of 2006 presents an intriguing bouquet of ambition, with subtle undertones of "maybe doubles will work better" that unfortunately never quite develop on the palate. The initial nose suggests promise, but upon first sip at the 2023 Midwest ORC, one encounters a surprisingly harsh 0-4 tannin structure that leaves the mouth rather dry and questioning one's life choices.</p>

<p>The 2024 MIDWEST CRC bottling shows marginal improvement – a single victory that provides just enough sweetness to prevent complete oxidation, though the 1-4 body remains disappointingly thin. One detects faint hints of partnership experimentation, with the Laasya Aduri blend showing the most promise at three tastings, though even this pairing lacks the complexity one hopes for in a mature vintage. The Sri Raghuram Vasamsetti and Roger Hongbo Fang cuvées, while showing potential in their limited two-match runs, ultimately fail to elevate the overall composition beyond "beginner-friendly table wine."</p>

<p>The most recent 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC expression demonstrates concerning consistency – another 1-3 vintage that suggests this particular vineyard may be struggling with soil conditions. The Arthur Yan single-match collaboration reads more like a wine flight sampler than a serious pairing attempt. With an overall 15% success rate, this collection would pair well with... perhaps a career change seminar, or maybe some nice participation certificates.</p>

<p>In summary, the Brian Hu collection shows admirable persistence despite lacking any notable awards or final-round appearances. While currently unranked and unrefined, this vintage may benefit from significant aging – perhaps in a different sport entirely. For the adventurous palate seeking an authentic underdog experience with notes of determination and just a splash of delusion, this could be your house wine. Though I'd recommend keeping receipts.</p>
