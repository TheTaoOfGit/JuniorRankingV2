<p>Welcome to the Luna Han Career Trail, a moderately challenging 15-tournament trek that begins promisingly at base camp but quickly becomes a cautionary tale about peak-bagging too early. Our hike starts in 2021 at the scenic Egret OLC overlook, where our young explorer managed a rare triple-crown summit, collecting U15 titles in singles, doubles, and mixed like a overeager scout collecting merit badges. The views were spectacular, the air was thin, and Luna was clearly suffering from altitude sickness because she thought this was how the rest of the journey would go.</p>

<p>Following the ridge line into 2022, we encounter the treacherous MBC CRC switchbacks, where Luna managed one more mixed doubles summit before the trail took a decidedly downward turn. Notice how the path becomes littered with runner-up medals from various U17 finals – these are what we call "participation rocks," and they're surprisingly heavy to carry. The Midwest OLC descent in 2023 was particularly brutal, featuring not one but two silver medal boulders that our hiker still drags behind her like a reluctant pack mule.</p>

<p>The middle section of our trail is notable for its revolving door of hiking companions – Luna has cycled through partners like a indecisive backpacker who can't settle on trail mix flavors. Her longest partnership with Eli Cui lasted 18 matches, which in junior badminton years is practically a common-law marriage, before she moved on to sample the Aditi Gummadi experience for 16 matches, then the Justin Xia variety pack for 12.</p>

<p>As we near the current trailhead, observe how our hiker has somehow managed to wander from the main U17 path onto the more challenging U19 terrain, where she now finds herself ranked #135 in girls doubles – roughly the equivalent of being lost somewhere behind the visitor center gift shop. The mixed doubles ranking of #464 suggests she's not just off-trail but possibly in a completely different national park entirely, perhaps one where badminton is played with tennis rackets and good intentions.</p>
