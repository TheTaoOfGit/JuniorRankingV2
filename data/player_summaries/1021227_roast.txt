<p>David Purnomo (born 2006) is an American badminton player best known for his remarkable ability to reach tournament finals and then spectacularly fail to win them[citation needed]. With an impressive collection of five runner-up finishes and zero titles, Purnomo has established himself as the sport's most consistent second-place finisher, earning him the unofficial nickname "The Silver Collector" among junior circuit regulars[citation needed].</p>

<p>Purnomo's career peaked during the 2021-2022 season when he managed to lose three separate finals at the 2021 NW CRC alone, including both Boys' Singles U17 and Mixed Doubles U17 on the same weekend[citation needed]. This feat of consecutive disappointment was so remarkable that tournament organizers reportedly considered creating a special "Most Creative Ways to Snatch Defeat from the Jaws of Victory" award in his honor[citation needed]. His partnership rotation system, featuring notable collaborations with Thomas Wang (9 matches), Daniel Wu (8 matches), and others, suggests either an innovative approach to team chemistry or a revolving door of partners seeking escape routes[citation needed].</p>

<p>The 2023 season marked a turning point in Purnomo's career trajectory, though not necessarily in the intended direction. His 0-3 performance at the SoCal ORC represented what badminton analysts called "a masterclass in efficient elimination"[citation needed]. Currently unranked after previously holding positions of BD U19 #41, BS U19 #31, and XD U19 #82, Purnomo has achieved the rare distinction of disappearing from rankings entirely while still actively competing[citation needed].</p>

<p>Despite maintaining a respectable 52-44 overall record (54% win rate), Purnomo's legacy remains defined by his unique talent for peaking at precisely the wrong moments. His ability to consistently reach finals while never quite crossing the finish line first has led some to theorize that he suffers from an rare condition known as "Trophy Allergy Syndrome"[citation needed], though medical professionals have yet to confirm this diagnosis[citation needed].</p>
