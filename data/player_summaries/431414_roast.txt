<p>⭐⭐ Would not recommend Hanson Zhu's Badminton Bistro. Went in with high expectations after hearing about their "specialty doubles menu" featuring partners like Alexander Liu and Kavin Adhav Sivadhaya, but left thoroughly disappointed. The service was inconsistent at best - sometimes you'd get a decent 5W-4L experience like at the 2025 NE OLC, other times you'd walk out completely empty-handed like that disastrous 0W-1L showing at the recent Wayside NE CRC in January.</p>

<p>The real kicker was when I tried their "National Championships Special" back in 2024 - absolutely inedible! Zero wins, four losses, and I'm pretty sure they forgot to even show up to half the matches. For a place that's been operating since 2023 with 7 different locations, you'd think they'd have figured out the recipe by now. Instead, their win percentage is stuck at a measly 42%, which wouldn't even pass a health inspection if this were an actual restaurant.</p>

<p>The ambiance tries to be upscale with rankings like "BD U15 #103" plastered on the walls, but don't be fooled - they've clearly gone downhill since then. Current ranking of BS U19 #433 tells you everything you need about the quality decline. The staff keeps rotating between different partnerships, which explains why nothing ever feels cohesive. Even tried their mixed doubles option with Lili Yuan Li, but that didn't save the experience.</p>

<p>Look, I get that Hanson's Badminton Bistro is still a relatively new establishment (born 2009, so they're practically teenagers in restaurant years), but at some point you need to deliver on the basics. Until they figure out how to consistently serve up wins instead of constantly shuttlecocking between mediocrity and outright disasters, I'll be taking my business elsewhere. Maybe try again in a few years when they've had time to mature their menu.</p>
