Sophia Sun sits atop the U15 girls' singles rankings and is making a serious case as the most complete player in her age group. With a career record of 202-83 across 29 tournaments and a 70 percent win rate, the 2012-born Sun has been a fixture in national-level competition since she was nine years old, when she won both the 2021 NorCal CRC and 2022 Junior National Championships in GS U11. Now ranked No. 1 in GS U15 and No. 13 in GS U17, she has successfully navigated the leap from age-group prodigy to elite junior competitor. Her singles ranking trajectory tells the story -- from No. 5 in GS U13 in late 2023 to No. 3 in GS U15 by mid-2025, and finally ascending to the top spot by March 2026. The only blemish in her recent singles record is a runner-up finish at the 2026 SoCal ORC, where she fell to longtime rival Mihika Jaiswal, and a loss to Adriel Ammy Chandran in the 2025 NorCal ORC final.

Sun's doubles game, while not at the same level as her singles, still ranks her No. 12 in GD U15, and she has shown versatility with a recent XD U15 title at the 2026 Bintang NorCal OLC. Her longest-standing partnership is with Alice Meng, with whom she has shared 48 matches stretching back to their U11 days. More recently, she has teamed with Jacob Ma in mixed doubles (25 matches) and newer partners like Elena Zhang and Phillip You. Sun's ability to post strong results at both ORC and OLC-level events -- going 11-2 at the 2026 SoCal ORC and consistently winning seven or more matches per tournament -- demonstrates the kind of sustained excellence that separates the top tier from the rest. With her GS U17 ranking already at No. 13 and climbing, Sun appears ready to challenge older competition and could be a serious contender at the next Junior National Championships.
