<p>Ah yes, the Mingyuan Zang 2024-2025 vintage – a peculiar little number from the SoCal region that promises much on the nose but delivers a surprisingly tepid finish. Upon first swirl, one detects hints of promise with that early U11 triple crown at the 2024 SOCAL CRC, where young Mingyuan swept through boys singles, mixed doubles, and boys doubles like a precocious Pinot Noir bursting with youthful exuberance. The bouquet suggested we might be tasting a future champion.</p>

<p>However, further examination reveals a wine of decidedly middling character. The palate presents notes of inconsistency – observe how this vintage managed an impressive 11W-9L showing at Junior Nationals, only to follow with the utterly forgettable 0W-1L disaster at the SoCal ORC, a performance so brief it barely registered on the tongue. The 76-68 overall record (53% win rate) leaves one with the distinct impression of a table wine pretending to be a premier cru – adequate for casual consumption, but hardly the stuff of legend.</p>

<p>What's particularly amusing about this vintage is its ambitious aging process across multiple age brackets simultaneously. Currently ranked #10 in U13 boys singles while sitting at a rather pedestrian #199 in U19, it's as if our young vintner is desperately trying to convince us his Beaujolais Nouveau belongs in the same cellar as aged Bordeaux. The partnership with Eden Wong (19 matches) suggests a reliable house blend, though one suspects Eden might be doing most of the heavy lifting in that particular vintage.</p>

<p>The finish is decidedly short and somewhat anticlimactic – three titles sounds impressive until you realize they're all from the U11 division, making this less a fine wine and more like grape juice with delusions of grandeur. Still, there's potential in this young vintage, assuming the vintner learns to stop serving it at tournaments where it's clearly outmatched. Perhaps in a few years, when properly aged, we'll revisit this label with more generous appreciation.</p>
