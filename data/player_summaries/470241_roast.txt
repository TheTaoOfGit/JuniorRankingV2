<p><b>5-8 in Semis Despite Being BS U13 #10: Mingyuan Zang Is Too Good to Lose Early, Too Weak to Win Late</b></p>

<p>Mingyuan Zang is ranked BS U13 #10, which is legitimately impressive. But his 5-8 semis record and 3-2 finals record tell the story of a player who underperforms in elimination rounds. His three-game record of 8-9 (47%) means he's below .500 in close matches, which is not ideal for someone in the top 10.</p>

<p>Three titles (BS U11 and XD U11 at SoCal CRC, BD U11 at Dave Freeman OLC) and two runner-up finishes (including losing to Naksh Shetty at Nationals) show he can peak but not consistently. He went 1-3 at both the NorCal ORC and SoCal ORC, and 2-3 at the SoCal CRC. His blowout losses include getting destroyed by Adrian Zhang in doubles, a player he should be competitive with.</p>

<p>His partner Eden Wong (17 matches) has been his ride-or-die through the ups and downs. He's a top-10 player who plays like a top-20 player when the stakes rise. The talent is there; the killer instinct is on backorder.</p>
