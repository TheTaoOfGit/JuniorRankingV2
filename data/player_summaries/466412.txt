The 17-year-old from California has surged up the national rankings, climbing from #82 to #41 in BD U19 over just 13 months while accumulating an impressive 18,000 ranking points. His doubles prowess significantly outshines his singles game, where he holds a respectable #82 ranking in BS U19, clearly indicating where his future lies on the court.</p>

<p>The breakthrough moment came at the 2024 DAVE FREEMAN JR. OLC, where Kandalam reached his first career final in BD U17, showcasing the tactical awareness and court chemistry that has become his trademark. This performance highlighted his ability to elevate his game when it matters most, going 3-2 at the tournament despite facing top-tier competition. His partnership with Lenny Zhang has been particularly formidable, with the duo teaming up for eight matches and developing the kind of synergy that separates good doubles teams from great ones.</p>

<p>What sets Kandalam apart is his relentless pursuit of improvement at the sport's highest levels. He's competed twice at the prestigious YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, gaining invaluable experience against the nation's elite junior players. While his overall 11-29 record might seem modest, it reflects his willingness to consistently challenge himself against superior competition rather than padding stats at lower-level events. This approach has clearly paid dividends, as evidenced by his dramatic ranking improvements across both disciplines.</p>

<p>Kandalam's versatility in partnerships demonstrates exceptional court intelligence and adaptability. Beyond his primary alliance with Zhang, he's found success with Oliver Lien Strayer, Sanchay Devnath, and Rajat Shriranga Naik, proving he can quickly build chemistry with different playing styles. As he continues his ascent through the junior ranks with another year of U19 eligibility remaining, Kandalam represents the future of American badminton doubles—a player whose best performances lie ahead of him.</p>
