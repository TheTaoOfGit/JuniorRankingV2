<p>⭐⭐ "Emily Ou's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for special occasions. Went here 12 times over the past few years and the experience is wildly inconsistent. Sometimes you get a decent meal (like that surprising 7-3 performance at Midwest ORC in 2023), but more often you're left hungry and confused about what just happened. The 1-6 disaster at Junior Nationals was particularly indigestible.</p>

<p>The staff turnover is concerning - she's been through more doubles partners than a speed dating event. Allison Hsu and Benjamin Zhuge seem to be the only servers willing to come back for multiple shifts, bless their hearts. The coordination between kitchen and wait staff is questionable at best, especially in mixed doubles where the service ranking plummeted from #87 to #166 faster than a soufflé collapsing.</p>

<p>What's really frustrating is the false advertising - the 2023 menu promised she was ranked #18 in girls' singles, but by 2025 she'd completely fallen off the rankings board. It's like ordering the chef's special only to be told they don't actually serve food anymore. The inconsistency is staggering: one day you're getting a respectable 5-4 at NorCal ORC, the next you're suffering through a 1-4 nightmare at the same venue a year later.</p>

<p>Look, I get that she's still learning the business (born in 2009, so she's basically running a restaurant with a learner's permit), but at some point you need to deliver on the hype. The 36-48 overall record suggests this establishment is more "participation trophy" than "Michelin star." Maybe try the drive-through instead - less commitment, lower expectations, and you can escape quickly if things go sideways.</p>
