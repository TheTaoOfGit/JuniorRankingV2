<p>⭐⭐⭐☆☆ Went to Arudh Sundar's for dinner and honestly, the experience was more inconsistent than a South OLC shuttle service. The singles menu (BS U11) is actually pretty solid - dude's ranked #8 and even took home the championship special at the 2025 South OLC. But don't even think about ordering from the boys doubles section unless you enjoy the taste of bitter disappointment with a side of runner-up ribbons.</p>

<p>I've watched this establishment lose SIX BD U11 finals since 2024, including back-to-back heartbreaks at the South OLC and PEAK SPORTS events. It's like they keep promising you the perfect doubles entrée but then serve you a participation trophy instead. The kitchen clearly knows how to cook - that 8W-6L performance at Junior Nationals was chef's kiss - but something about the doubles preparation just falls flat when it matters most.</p>

<p>The mixed doubles experience with partner Prarthana Sanghani is actually their signature dish - they've perfected that recipe over 20+ matches and even won the XD U11 championship. It's comfort food at its finest. But when Arudh teams up with the Devansh duo, it's like ordering the same disappointing dish 16 times hoping it'll taste different. Spoiler alert: it usually doesn't end in victory.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due - this place has improved dramatically since 2023 when they were ranked #129 in doubles. Now sitting pretty at #6, it's clear the chef has been studying the recipe book. The 60-42 overall record shows potential, but until they figure out how to close the deal in those big doubles finals, I'll keep coming back for the singles and mixed doubles while side-eyeing that BD menu section.</p>
