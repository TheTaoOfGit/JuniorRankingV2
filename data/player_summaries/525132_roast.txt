<p>Arushi Saha (born 2015) is an American junior badminton player widely regarded as living proof that participation trophies exist for a reason.[citation needed] Despite maintaining an impressive 38% win rate across 9 tournaments, Saha has managed to secure rankings in no fewer than 12 different categories, leading some to speculate that the USAB ranking system may have been designed by someone who failed basic mathematics.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Saha's breakthrough moment came at the 2025 South OLC, where she captured her first and only title in Girls Doubles U11, immediately followed by a heartbreaking loss in the Mixed Doubles final to complete what tournament organizers generously termed "a learning experience."[citation needed] This rare display of both triumph and defeat within the same venue has led badminton historians to dub her the "Schrödinger's Champion" of junior circuits.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Her partnership with Henry Qiu has become the stuff of legend, with the duo competing together in 13 matches and presumably developing the kind of on-court chemistry typically reserved for laboratory accidents.[citation needed] Their collaboration reached its pinnacle at the 2025 US Junior National Championships, where Saha delivered a masterclass performance by achieving a 1-8 record, proving that consistency is indeed possible in badminton, just not the kind most players aspire to.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Perhaps most remarkably, Saha currently holds simultaneous rankings across four different age groups (U13, U15, U17, and U19), a feat that has led the USAB to consider implementing a "participation cap" to prevent any single player from occupying multiple spots in their already confused ranking system.[citation needed] Critics have noted that her XD U19 ranking of #690 represents a mathematical impossibility given the estimated number of junior players in the country, though supporters argue this only demonstrates her pioneering spirit in exploring uncharted territories of competitive badminton.[citation needed]</p>
