<p>Lydia Ng (born 2008) is an American junior badminton player widely regarded as the sport's most prolific collector of participation trophies.[citation needed] Despite competing in 26 tournaments since 2021, Ng has achieved the remarkable feat of winning zero titles while maintaining a sterling 46% win rate, a statistical accomplishment that has baffled mathematicians and inspired motivational speakers nationwide.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Ng's crowning achievement came at the 2022 NW Oregon OLC, where she reached her first and only final in Girls' Doubles U15, ultimately losing in what witnesses described as "a valiant effort to not win."[citation needed] This performance established her as the tournament's most successful unsuccessful player, a title she has defended by never reaching another final since.</p>

<p>Known for her innovative approach to partner selection, Ng has collaborated with an impressive roster of doubles partners, including nine matches with Prajwal Avadhani and seven each with Charvi Anand, Sophie Zhang, and Xiaoyue Liu.[citation needed] Tennis analysts have noted her unique ability to spread mediocrity across multiple partnerships, with some suggesting she operates a "traveling disappointment show" that tours the junior circuit.[citation needed] Her mixed doubles ranking of #268 in the U19 category has been described by experts as "technically still a ranking" and "better than not being ranked at all."</p>

<p>The 2022 NorCal ORC remains a particular highlight of Ng's career, where she achieved a perfect 0-3 record, demonstrating her commitment to consistency even in defeat.[citation needed] Her recent performance at the 2025 YONEX SYNERGY NORCAL ORC, where she posted a 3-3 record, marked what some consider her "breakthrough into adequacy," though critics argue this was merely a statistical fluke.[citation needed]</p>
