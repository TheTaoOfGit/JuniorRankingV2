<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Pranav Vijay's Badminton Bistro expecting a fine dining experience, but left feeling like I'd been served reheated leftovers from 2021. The establishment has been open for 10 tournaments now, and while the 55% success rate sounds decent on paper, the quality has been sliding faster than his BD U13 ranking (which plummeted from #63 to #130 - yikes!).</p>

<p>The service is wildly inconsistent. Sometimes you get paired with the reliable Ellie Chen for 10 matches, other times you're stuck with Shravan Chockalingam (who apparently changed his name capitalization mid-service?). The mixed doubles department is particularly understaffed - ranked #917 in U19 XD with only 464 points. I've seen vending machines with better selection.</p>

<p>The signature dish appears to be "Almost Champion Surprise" - they served it fresh at the 2026 BINTANG NorCal OLC where our young chef made it all the way to the BD U13 finals before completely burning the soufflé. The kitchen clearly has potential (that 7W-3L record shows promise), but zero titles on the menu after all these years? Even my local Denny's has won something.</p>

<p>Most concerning is the declining quality over time. What started as a promising #32 BS U13 ranking has degraded to #86, like watching a restaurant's health rating slowly slip from A to C+. The 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals visit was particularly brutal - a 6W-8L performance that left everyone wondering if they'd accidentally wandered into a food truck instead of a proper establishment.</p>

<p>Look, the kid's got heart and that 2024 YONEX NORCAL ORC performance (6W-3L) showed real potential, but until this place starts delivering championships instead of just participation trophies, I'll be taking my badminton dining dollars elsewhere. Two stars because at least he's consistent at being inconsistent.</p>
