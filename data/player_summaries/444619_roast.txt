<p>Ah yes, the 2015 Adrian Zhang vintage - a precocious little number that's been aging quite rapidly through the junior circuit. Upon first taste, one detects notes of unbridled enthusiasm with subtle hints of "wait, how old is this kid again?" The palate opens with a surprisingly robust 108-64 record, though one can't help but notice the vintage performs considerably better when served at lower age classifications - much like how a decent Beaujolais should never be confused with a Burgundy.</p>

<p>The doubles blend is where this vintage truly shines, having cultivated a particularly harmonious partnership with Franklin Xu Cheng across 18 matches. The chemistry here is reminiscent of a well-balanced Champagne - effervescent, reliable, and occasionally capable of producing something spectacular, as evidenced by that delightful BD U13 finish at the 2026 CBA MIDWEST OLC. However, when mixed with older vintages in U17 and U19 categories, the complexity becomes somewhat... overwhelming, ranking a modest #164 and #287 respectively.</p>

<p>One must appreciate the ambitious terroir of this young vintage - simultaneously competing across four age groups like a sommelier who insists on pairing the same wine with sushi, steak, dessert, and breakfast cereal. The mixed doubles expression shows particular character in the U13 range (#65) but becomes increasingly diluted as it ages into U15 and beyond, much like trying to stretch a bottle of Two Buck Chuck across a dinner party.</p>

<p>The tournament portfolio reveals a vintage with excellent domestic appeal - eight titles from the comfort of familiar venues like MIDWEST CRC and various ORCs. Yet when exposed to the rarified air of the YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS, our 2015 Adrian produces respectable but not legendary performances. It's a promising vintage that knows its strengths: stick to the younger appellations, maintain those doubles partnerships, and perhaps consider cellaring those U19 ambitions for a few more years until the tannins have properly developed.</p>
