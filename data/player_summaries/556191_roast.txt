<p>Ah yes, the 2017 Vasappanavara vintage - a delightfully modest offering from the Ananth vineyard. Upon first inspection, this junior displays a wonderfully consistent amber hue across all age brackets, maintaining precisely 362 points in singles like a sommelier who refuses to deviate from his house blend. The nose reveals subtle hints of optimism with strong undertones of "maybe next tournament," complemented by faint traces of Aurik Krishna Ghosh Moulick partnership residue.</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage opens with a crisp 29% win rate that dances playfully across the tongue before quickly dissipating. The 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals tasting notes reveal a surprisingly complex 1-2 finish, though connoisseurs will appreciate the consistency - he achieved this exact same record at the Synergy NorCal, demonstrating remarkable terroir stability. The recent 2026 SoCal offering, however, presents as somewhat thin-bodied with a rather abrupt 0-1 finish that leaves one yearning for more.</p>

<p>The doubles expression shows marginally more character, climbing to a respectable #65 in U11 with 404 points - clearly benefiting from the blending process with partner varietals. The Riaan Prabhune single-match collaboration suggests potential for future vintages, though the brief encounter leaves much to the imagination.</p>

<p>Overall, this is an honest, unpretentious selection perfect for casual consumption. While it may lack the bold complexity of championship contenders, the Ananth 2017 shows promise with proper aging. I recommend decanting for several more seasons before expecting any significant awards, though its youthful enthusiasm and remarkable point consistency make it an endearing addition to any junior tournament cellar. Rating: 2.5 stars - "Shows potential, needs time."</p>
