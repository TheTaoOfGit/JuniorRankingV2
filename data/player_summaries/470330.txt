Prarthana Sanghani has put together one of the most impressive twelve-month stretches in U11 girls' badminton. Born in 2016, she has rocketed from 76th in GD U11 and 66th in GS U11 in December 2024 to second in GD U11, second in XD U11, and fourth in GS U11 by March 2026. Her career numbers, 65 wins against 36 losses for a 64 percent win rate across 13 tournaments, are strong, but what truly stands out is her recent dominance: a flawless 10-0 at the 2025 Peak Sports South OLC, an 8-1 mark at the 2025 Capital NE OLC, and a 12-1 record at the 2026 DFW South ORC. Those three events alone produced five titles, including singles, doubles, and mixed doubles hardware at both the South OLC and the Capital NE OLC. Her seven career championships, all won within the past several months, make her one of the most decorated active U11 competitors in the country.

Sanghani's doubles game is her signature strength, and her partnership with Arudh Sundar in mixed doubles has been the backbone of her rise. The pair have logged 19 matches together, competing at every major event on Sanghani's schedule, and they claimed the XD U11 title at the 2026 DFW South ORC. She has also developed a productive doubles pairing with Thea Yuan, with seven matches together yielding a GD U11 title at the 2026 SoCal ORC over Elina Yang and Yvonne Chen Yang. That win over Yang is part of an emerging three-way rivalry at the top of U11 girls' badminton between Sanghani, Yuan, and Yang that promises to define the age group for years to come. With her ability to collect titles across all three disciplines and a ranking trajectory that shows no sign of leveling off, Sanghani has positioned herself as the most well-rounded threat in her age group heading into the second half of the season.
