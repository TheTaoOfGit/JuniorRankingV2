<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Alyssa Kao's for dinner and honestly, the experience was... inconsistent. The menu promised so much variety - singles, doubles, mixed doubles - but the execution left a lot to be desired. Out of 10 visits, I only walked away satisfied 23% of the time, which is about the same odds as finding a good meal at a gas station.</p>

<p>The 2025 SOUTH CRC special was probably their best offering - managed to serve up 3 wins alongside 6 losses, which is like getting a decent appetizer but then having the kitchen burn your entree. The staff rotation is wild too - one day you're paired with Advik Ganesh Bhat (solid choice, been there 6 times), next day it's Dhyuti Aruru (reliable for 4 rounds), and sometimes you get the Aradhana Santhosh experience for a quick 3-course meal. The inconsistency is honestly impressive.</p>

<p>Credit where credit's due though - this place somehow got invited to cater the 2024 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS, which is like a food truck getting a Michelin star consideration. Sure, they went 2-5 that day, but hey, at least they made it to the big leagues! Most restaurants in the #155 GS U15 bracket don't even get that opportunity.</p>

<p>The trajectory seems promising - they've climbed from #125 to #83 in the GS U15 category, which suggests the chef is actually learning. But that XD ranking dropped from #188 to #313, so maybe stick to the singles menu for now. I'll probably come back because the potential is there, plus the prices are reasonable for someone still figuring out their signature dishes. Just don't expect a five-star experience... yet.</p>
