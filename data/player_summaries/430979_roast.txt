<p>★★☆☆☆ "The Justin Liu Story" is a sports drama that desperately wants to be "Rocky" but plays more like "The Room" with rackets. Director Reality has crafted a meandering four-year epic that follows our protagonist from his disastrous 2022 debut (0-6 at Northeast ORC) through a career trajectory flatter than a shuttlecock after Liu's attempted smash. The plot lacks coherence, jumping erratically between weight classes and age groups while maintaining the consistency of a 27% win rate — which, coincidentally, is also this film's Rotten Tomatoes score.</p>

<p>Liu's character development is puzzling at best. The script teases a redemption arc when he reaches the BD U15 finals at 2024 NE CRC, finally giving audiences hope for a satisfying climax. Instead, we get another loss, because apparently this director believes in "subverting expectations" to the point of narrative masochism. His chemistry with co-star Yiyang Xu (11 matches together) suggests a buddy comedy, but their on-court performances read more like a tragic miscommunication between method actors who forgot they were supposed to win points.</p>

<p>The cinematography is particularly brutal in the doubles scenes, where Liu's positioning often resembles a lost tourist rather than a badminton player. His current "unranked" status serves as this film's ultimate plot twist — going from ranked #104-112 in various categories to complete statistical invisibility. It's like watching a character literally fade from the screen, which would be artistic if it weren't so unintentional.</p>

<p>That said, there are glimmers of competence in recent scenes (3-2 at 2025 NE OLC), suggesting the sequel might have potential. Liu is still young enough that this could be an extended origin story rather than a complete disaster. One can only hope the next installment involves hiring a better director — perhaps one named "Consistent Training" or "Match Strategy." Until then, "The Justin Liu Story" remains a curiosity for badminton cinema completists only.</p>
