Sophia Liu's trajectory over the past two years reads like a case study in steady, relentless climbing. Born in 2015, she was barely a blip on the national radar at the end of 2023, ranked number 79 in GS U11 and number 156 in XD U11. By the end of 2024, she had vaulted into the top ten in multiple U11 categories, and as of March 2026 she sits at number two in GS U13 and number four in GD U13 -- a rise of more than 75 ranking spots in singles in just over two years. Her career record of 133-55 across 20 tournaments gives her a 70-percent win rate, a figure that, while lower than the very top tier, understates how competitive she has been in the biggest moments. Liu has eight tournament titles to her name, including signature wins like the GS U11 gold at the 2024 NorCal ORC over Claire QiuChen Li, the GS U13 title at the 2025 Can-Am NorCal OLC over Zelenia Shen, and a GD U15 title at the 2026 Bintang NorCal OLC that shows she can compete above her age group.

The defining challenge of Liu's current career is Krishnavi Kasu. Three of her five runner-up finishes have come in girls' doubles finals against Kasu and Claire QiuChen Li -- at the 2026 SoCal ORC, the 2026 DFW South ORC, and the 2025 NorCal ORC -- making that pair her most persistent obstacle on the doubles side. In singles, Liu's number-two ranking behind Kasu sets up what should be a compelling head-to-head rivalry for the rest of the U13 cycle. On the doubles court, Liu has cycled through several partners, with Grace Cheng (19 matches) emerging as her most frequent recent collaborator after earlier stints with Ana Paula Almirola and Yian Yang. Her mixed doubles game has been more of a secondary pursuit, with a U13 ranking of number 14 and a U15 ranking of number 51 through partnerships with Vignan Chandrasekaran and others. The 9-1 performance at the 2025 Can-Am NorCal OLC, where she won both GS U13 and XD U15 titles in the same weekend, may represent her peak form to date and hints at the kind of multi-discipline threat she could become if she continues to close the gap on Kasu at the top of the rankings.
