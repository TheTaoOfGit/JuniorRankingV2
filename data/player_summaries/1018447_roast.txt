<p><b>IN THE MATTER OF: The People v. Charity Lam — Case No. GS-U19-001</b><br>
ALL RISE. The court is now in session to examine the meteoric and occasionally turbulent career of one <b>Charity Lam</b>, who stands before us ranked <b>GS U19 #1, GD U19 #1, and XD U19 #5</b> — a triple threat so dominant the prosecution honestly isn't sure what charges to bring. The defendant's career record of <b>165-80 (67% win rate)</b> across 29 tournaments is entered into evidence. The court notes that 67% is not exactly a number that screams invincibility, and yet here she is, sitting atop two entire divisions. Clearly, Ms. Lam wins when it matters and treats regular-season losses as community service.</p>

<p>EXHIBIT A: The ascent. From GS U17 #18 and GD U17 #3 in December 2023 to the literal top of both GS U19 and GD U19 by March 2026. The prosecution calls this "suspicious improvement." The defense calls it "hard work." The court calls it <i>impressive</i>. Eight titles including the crown jewel — <b>GS U19 at the 2025 Junior National Championships</b>, defeating Lydia Chao in the final. Yes, that Lydia Chao — her own doubles partner of 28 matches. Imagine winning a national title against the person who knows your game better than anyone. That's not just winning; that's psychological warfare disguised as sportsmanship.</p>

<p>EXHIBIT B: The doubles partnerships. <b>Lydia Chao</b> (28 matches) is the primary accomplice in the GD operation, and together they've been effective if not always dominant — the 2024 NorCal ORC saw them go a combined <b>1-3</b>, which the court considers a misdemeanor at best. <b>Suraj Rajesh Kumar</b> (24 matches) serves as the mixed doubles co-conspirator, with the XD U19 titles at the 2024 Northeast ORC and 2025 South CRC proving the partnership's legitimacy. <b>Ansen Liu</b> (17 matches) is the newer associate, brought on for the 2026 campaign with promising early returns including a GD title at DFW South ORC.</p>

<p>EXHIBIT C: The runner-up file, which is thicker than the defense would like. Eight second-place finishes including losses to Sophia Wu, Micah Cruz, Sophia Jia, and Vivian Feng — a who's-who of "players who gave Charity Lam problems." The GS U19 runner-up at the 2026 DFW South ORC to Vivian Feng is particularly notable: you cannot claim total dominion over girls' singles while also losing to Vivian Feng in back-to-back tournament cycles. The GD U19 runner-up at the 2025 Midwest ORC — losing to Sophia Wu and Jennifer Xu — suggests the doubles crown, while currently held, has been contested.</p>

<p>THE VERDICT: Charity Lam is found <b>guilty</b> on all counts of excellence, with a suspended sentence for inconsistency. The 67% career win rate is the one number that doesn't match the resume of a double #1, but the court acknowledges that the defendant has learned to peak at the right moments — notably Nationals, where she collected <i>two</i> titles in 2025. She is sentenced to continued dominance with the expectation that the win rate will eventually catch up to the rankings. Court is adjourned.</p>
