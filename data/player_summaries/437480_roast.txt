<p>The name's Detective, and I've seen some cold cases in my time, but nothing quite like the mystery of Jacob Garcia's badminton career. Born in 2005, this kid stepped onto the courts of the Midwest circuit like a lamb walking into a slaughterhouse - except the lamb kept coming back for more. The evidence was damning from the start: 2022 Midwest CRC, a clean 0-6 shutout that would make a professional mourner weep.</p>

<p>I followed the trail through 2023, watching Garcia bounce between partners like a pinball in a machine that only pays out in disappointment. Oliver Chung took four matches with him - four matches of what I can only assume was therapeutic doubles therapy. Sarina Fang and Valarie Jiao each gave him three chances, probably out of the goodness of their hearts, while Meghana Dixit and Nishchal Aradhya managed two each before their survival instincts kicked in.</p>

<p>The breakthrough came at the 2023 Midwest OLC - and by breakthrough, I mean Garcia actually won two matches out of eight. Two! It was like finding a diamond in a coal mine, if diamonds were participation trophies and coal mines were badminton tournaments. For a brief, shining moment, his win percentage climbed from the depths of statistical hell to a respectable 8% career average.</p>

<p>By December 2023, the rankings told the whole sordid story: Boys' Doubles U19 #91, Boys' Singles U19 #90, Mixed Doubles U19 #118. These numbers painted a picture clearer than a mugshot - Garcia was the kind of player who made #92 feel pretty good about themselves. The case was closed, the verdict was in: some mysteries are better left unsolved, and some badminton careers are better left unranked.</p>
