<p><b>Courtroom Trial: The People vs. Nihal Kadamba</b> — The defendant stands accused of having one of the most impressive resumes in junior badminton history yet somehow failing to convert it into sustained dominance. With 8 titles, a 70% win rate (160-67), and BS U17 #1 on his permanent record, the evidence of talent is overwhelming.</p>

<p>The prosecution's star exhibits: 6 runner-up finishes, including the crushing BS U17 loss at the 2023 Junior Nationals to David Zuo and a string of BD U19 near-misses at the 2024 NorCal, Northwest, and 2025 South ORCs. The defendant's partnership with Justin Kan (73 matches) — the longest-running doubles act in this docket — produced hardware but also witnessed many of these heartbreaks.</p>

<p>The defense counters with the 2024 Junior Nationals BS U17 title over Abhi Mandanapu, the 2022 SPBA MW ORC (8-1), and an imperial 2023 Northwest ORC (8-2). The trajectory from BS U17 #1 to BS U19 #12 and BD U19 #4 to BD U19 #34 does raise questions about whether the golden era has passed or merely paused.</p>

<p>Verdict: guilty of being a generational talent with an allergy to closing out the biggest doubles finals. Sentenced to continue being excellent while watching rivals hoist trophies he dropped.</p>
