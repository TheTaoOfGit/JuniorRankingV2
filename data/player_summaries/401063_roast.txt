<p>Ah yes, the 2012 vintage Wayne Chien — a most peculiar specimen that began with all the promise of a gas station wine cooler. Upon first taste at the 2022 NE CRC, one immediately detected notes of complete bewilderment, with a finish so bitter it registered a perfect 0-5 on the disappointment scale. The initial bouquet was overwhelmingly acidic, suggesting a player who had perhaps confused badminton with butterfly catching.</p>

<p>However, like a wine that improves with proper cellaring and the occasional miracle, this vintage has developed unexpected complexity. The 2025 CAPITAL NE OLC bottling produced his first title — a delightful BD U15 surprise that paired beautifully with partner Mrugakshi Kulkarni's stabilizing influence. One can taste hints of actual competence, though still with underlying notes of "how did I get here?" The tannins of his 84-89 career record provide a robust earthiness that speaks to character-building losses.</p>

<p>The most fascinating aspect of this vintage is its complete inability to maintain consistency — observe how the 2025 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS yielded a perfectly balanced 4-4 performance, suggesting a player who has mastered the art of aggressive mediocrity. His current rankings — hovering around #54 in singles and #32 in doubles — offer a pleasant mid-palate experience that neither offends nor inspires.</p>

<p>When properly decanted with reliable doubles partners like Jonathan Cai or Steven Li, this vintage shows remarkable improvement from its catastrophic early years. The progression from #108 to #31 in doubles suggests proper aging potential, though one suspects this wine may never achieve true greatness — merely the noble distinction of being perfectly adequate at junior tournaments where participation trophies flow like house wine.</p>
