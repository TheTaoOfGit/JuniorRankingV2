<p><strong>Grace Liu</strong> (born 2009) is an American junior badminton player best known for her remarkable consistency in achieving mediocrity across multiple tournament formats.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> With a career win rate of 27%, Liu has demonstrated an almost supernatural ability to lose approximately three-quarters of her matches while maintaining what can only be described as admirable persistence.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> Sports analysts have noted that her 27-73 career record represents a mathematical achievement in its own right, as few players manage to maintain such precise underperformance across 19 tournaments.<sup>[citation needed]</sup></p>

<p>Liu's tournament career began in spectacular fashion at the 2021 HPBC NW ORC, where she achieved a flawless 0-6 record, immediately establishing herself as a player to watch for all the wrong reasons.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> Her follow-up performance at the 2021 SoCal ORC (0-5) confirmed that her initial showing was no fluke, leading badminton historians to declare her early career "impressively consistent in its inconsistency."<sup>[citation needed]</sup> The pinnacle of her achievements arguably came at the 2023 Midwest ORC, where she posted a near-.500 record of 7-8, a performance so shocking that several spectators were reportedly seen checking their scorecards twice.<sup>[citation needed]</sup></p>

<p>In doubles play, Liu has formed what experts call "mutually beneficial partnerships" with players including Hiraani Hew Nam Leung and Callie Chen, though "beneficial" may be a relative term given the outcomes.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> Her 8-match partnership with Leung has been described by one unnamed coach as "like watching two people try to solve a Rubik's cube while blindfolded and wearing oven mitts."<sup>[citation needed]</sup> Despite rotating through partners with the frequency of a seasonal wardrobe change, Liu has maintained her trademark approach of turning potential victories into learning experiences.<sup>[citation needed]</sup></p>

<p>Currently unranked after briefly holding positions in multiple junior categories in 2023-2024, Liu represents the rare athlete who has achieved the difficult feat of becoming less ranked over time.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> Her ranking trajectory from #47 in Girls' Doubles U15 to complete invisibility has been called "an impressive reverse career arc" by badminton statisticians.<sup>[citation needed]</sup> At age 16, Liu still has plenty of time to potentially win her first tournament title, though Las Vegas oddsmakers have reportedly stopped taking bets on the timeline.<sup>[citation needed]</sup></p>
