<p>Ah yes, the 2012 Neha Murali vintage – a promising young racquet with delicate undertones of potential, though I must confess this particular bottle has yet to reach full maturation. Upon first uncorking at the 2023 South ORC, one immediately detected overwhelming notes of inexperience, with a rather harsh 0-4 finish that left the palate quite stunned. The early years showed considerable sediment – that unfortunate 1-6 showing at the 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals had all the complexity of a gas station wine cooler.</p>

<p>However, recent tastings reveal intriguing development in this vintage. The 2026 VISTARA SOUTH OLC expression offered surprising body with a 7-3 composition, featuring delightful hints of doubles chemistry with partners Tanvi Sree Gaddam and Jida Wu – though one suspects this sommelier has been sampling rather liberally from different vineyards, having paired with no fewer than nine different partners. The XD U15 final appearance suggests promising fruit, though the ultimate loss reveals this vintage still lacks the full-bodied confidence of a true champion.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this vintage is its remarkable improvement trajectory – from the rather acidic #108 rankings of 2023 to the more palatable mid-40s positioning we see today. The mixed doubles expression shows particular promise at #136 U15, though the U19 vintage (#415) suggests this wine may not travel well to older age categories. One detects persistent notes of determination, with a 37% success rate that, while not immediately impressive, shows steady improvement with proper aeration and time.</p>

<p>In conclusion, while the 2012 Murali lacks the immediate bold flavors of more established vintages, this bottle shows genuine promise with proper cellaring. I recommend patience with this particular vintage – give it another year or two to develop its full bouquet, and you may find yourself pleasantly surprised by its eventual complexity. Though I wouldn't serve it at a championship dinner just yet, it's certainly worth keeping in the collection.</p>
