<p>Ah yes, the 2015 vintage Polineni—a surprisingly bold offering from the Northeast terroir that has aged remarkably well in just eleven short years. Upon first inspection, this young player presents with an assertive bouquet of U11 dominance, featuring top notes of National Championship mixed doubles gold and a robust finish of eleven career titles that would make even the most seasoned sommelier raise an eyebrow.</p>

<p>The palate reveals unexpected complexity for such a young vintage. Notice how she pairs exquisitely with the 2015 Satish—their 44-match collaboration producing a harmonious blend that has conquered everything from humble CRCs to the prestigious YONEX Nationals stage. There's a certain effervescence here, a 68% win rate that bubbles up consistently across tournaments, though one detects just a hint of tannins in those seven losses at the 2024 Junior Nationals that suggest this vintage may have been uncorked slightly before its time.</p>

<p>What's most fascinating about this particular bottle is its remarkable ability to maintain its character while transitioning terroirs—from U11 where she reigned supreme as the #1 mixed doubles player, to her current U13 expression where she's settled into a respectable top-15 position. The progression shows remarkable balance; not too aggressive, not too conservative.</p>

<p>The finish is where this vintage truly shines—crisp, clean, and leaving you wanting more. While some might argue that eleven titles before age twelve suggests this wine peaked early, I'd counter that we're witnessing the early stages of what promises to be a legendary career vintage. Best enjoyed now, but with proper cellaring, could develop into something truly extraordinary by 2030.</p>
