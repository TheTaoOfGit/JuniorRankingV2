Clarinda Jin is the reigning U17 girls' singles national champion and arguably the most well-rounded player in the junior ranks today. Ranked No. 1 in GS U17, No. 4 in both GD U17 and XD U17, and No. 18 in XD U19, the 2010-born Jin competes at an elite level across all three disciplines -- a rarity at any age. Her career numbers are impressive: 195 wins against 88 losses over 30 tournaments, but it is her trajectory over the past two years that truly stands out. In December 2023, she was ranked No. 40 in GS U15 and No. 21 in GD U15. By March 2026, she had climbed to the summit of U17 singles and become a top-four presence in both doubles events. That kind of across-the-board ascent speaks to a player who has refined every aspect of her game.

Jin's 2025 campaign was nothing short of dominant. She claimed the GS U17 title at the U.S. Junior National Championships, defeating Adonia Anny Chandran, and swept both GD U17 and XD U17 at the Northeast ORC with an 11-1 record. She added doubles titles at the 2025 Midwest ORC and Midwest CRC, and an XD U17 title at the Midwest CRC as well. Her partnership with Ishi Reddy is the backbone of her doubles game -- the two have played 68 matches together, an extraordinary volume that has clearly produced deep on-court chemistry. In mixed doubles, Srinivas Raghav Subash has been her primary partner with 63 shared matches, though Noah Chien has emerged as a newer option, particularly at the 2025 Northeast ORC where they won the XD U17 title. Jin's rivals -- Vivian Feng in singles, and the Feng sisters in doubles -- have pushed her to several finals, but she has more often than not come out on top. With her XD U19 ranking already at No. 18 after winning the 2026 DFW South ORC title in that age group, Jin is clearly setting her sights on the next level of competition.
