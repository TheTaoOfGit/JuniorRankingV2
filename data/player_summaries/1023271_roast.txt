<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Ethan Chen's Badminton Bistro expecting a consistent dining experience, but boy was I disappointed. This establishment peaked back in 2023 when they were serving up fresh U15 titles at the NE OLC - both the Mixed Doubles special and Boys Doubles entrée were absolutely divine that day (10W-1L, chef's kiss). But ever since they moved to the U19 dining room, it's been nothing but declining service and burnt expectations.</p>

<p>The most frustrating thing about this place? They'll get your hopes up with excellent appetizers - I'm talking four separate occasions where they've brought you right to the finals course - only to completely botch the main dish every single time. The 2025 CAPITAL NE OLC Boys Doubles finale? Overcooked. The 2024 NE CRC Boys Doubles? Underseasoned. And don't even get me started on the recent 2026 WAYSIDE NE CRC disasters where they fumbled BOTH the Boys Singles AND Mixed Doubles final courses. It's like watching a chef nail the amuse-bouche then set the kitchen on fire.</p>

<p>The management here clearly has commitment issues too. They've gone through more dining partners than a speed dating event - Derek Ruoyu Li (14 dates), Will Luo (12 dates), then bouncing between Iris Gao, Julia Chan, and Parnika Pawankumar Thakare like they can't decide on a permanent sous chef. And somehow, despite all this partner swapping, their Yelp ratings keep dropping: from a respectable #92 Boys Singles in U15 down to a tragic #189 in U19. That's not growth, that's a restaurant losing its Michelin stars.</p>

<p>Look, I'll give credit where it's due - a 53% win rate means you won't leave hungry, and that 5W-2L performance at the recent WAYSIDE tournament showed some promise. But when you're ranked #321 in Mixed Doubles and #239 in Boys Doubles at the U19 level, you're basically the Applebee's of junior badminton. Decent enough for a quick meal, but nobody's making reservations months in advance. Maybe stick to the U15 menu where you actually knew how to cook?</p>
