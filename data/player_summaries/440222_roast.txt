<p><b>Semis Record: 3-12. Leo Ye Gets Bounced in the Semis More Often Than a Bad Check.</b></p>

<p>Leo Ye's 3-12 semis record is staggering. He's reached the semifinals fifteen times and lost twelve of them. That's an 80% failure rate at the exact moment when matches start to mean something. His one title — BD U13 at the Bintang NorCal OLC — came after 22 tournaments of heartbreak, making it the most overdue win in junior badminton history.</p>

<p>His three-game record of 11-18 at 37% is the stat that really buries him. When matches go to a decider, Leo loses nearly two out of three times. Combined with his semis implosion rate, you have a player who physically cannot perform under pressure. His 55% overall win rate would be fine if he could convert any of those wins into actual trophies.</p>

<p>He was XD U11 #3 and BS U11 #5 — genuine contender status — and has since slid to BS U13 #30 and XD U13 #77. That's not a decline; that's a controlled demolition. Leo Ye: the most talented choker in the U13 bracket.</p>
