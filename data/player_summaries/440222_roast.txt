<p>⭐⭐⭐ "Leo Ye's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for a consistent dining experience</p>

<p>Went to Leo's establishment expecting championship-level service after hearing about his big win at the 2025 Junior Nationals, but boy was I disappointed. The doubles menu with Grace Cheng was decent - they've clearly got their 21-dish partnership down to a science - but don't even think about ordering from the mixed doubles section. Watched him crash and burn from #16 to #77 in XD U13 faster than a soufflé in a thunderstorm. The 2025 Synergy NorCal special was particularly tragic: 0 wins, 3 losses, like watching Gordon Ramsay have a complete meltdown.</p>

<p>The atmosphere is confusing - sometimes you get premium BD U13 champion vibes, other times it feels like you wandered into a budget singles joint where he's ranked #213 in U17. The service is wildly inconsistent too. One day he's serving up a 10W-5L feast at SoCal ORC, the next he's giving you a measly 1W-4L appetizer at ABC OLC. It's like the kitchen can't decide if they're a Michelin star operation or a gas station hot dog stand.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due - that BD U13 National Championship was genuinely impressive, like finding a perfect truffle in an otherwise mediocre meal. But with recent performances sliding faster than his XD ranking, I'm starting to think that title was more lucky timing than actual skill. The boy's got 1791 points in singles spread across four age groups like he's trying to run four different restaurants simultaneously and failing at three of them.</p>

<p>Would I come back? Maybe for doubles if Grace is working that shift, but I'm definitely skipping the mixed doubles disaster and anything involving Harrison Zhang (sorry Harrison, but 13 matches of evidence don't lie). Two and a half stars - shows potential but needs serious consistency training before I'd recommend to friends.</p>
