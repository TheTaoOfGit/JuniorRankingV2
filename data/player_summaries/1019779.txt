<p>Alex Song has emerged as one of the most formidable doubles forces in junior badminton, rocketing from #40 to #10 in Boys' Doubles U19 rankings in just over two years. The 2008-born athlete currently holds impressive rankings across all three disciplines: #10 in BD U19, #16 in BS U19, and #18 in XD U19, showcasing the versatility that has become his trademark on court.</p>

<p>With six tournament titles already under his belt, Song has proven his championship mettle across multiple age groups and events. His breakthrough performances include capturing the Boys' Doubles U17 crown at the prestigious 2024 YONEX NORCAL ORC and claiming Mixed Doubles U17 titles at both the 2023 NW OLC and 2024 OBA NW OLC. His early success in U15 competition was equally impressive, sweeping both Boys' Doubles and Boys' Singles titles at the 2022 NW Oregon OLC.</p>

<p>Song's 54% career win rate across 128-107 matches demonstrates his consistency at the highest levels of junior competition. His standout tournament performance came at the 2024 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, where he posted a stellar 10-6 record against the nation's elite juniors. His partnership chemistry is particularly noteworthy, having formed a dominant duo with Zimeng Zhou across 41 matches, while also finding success with regular partners Ethan Ao and Daniel Seungchan Park.</p>

<p>The young star's tactical intelligence and court awareness have made him equally dangerous in mixed doubles, where his 25,500 ranking points reflect his ability to adapt his aggressive net play to complement female partners. His recent finals appearances at the 2023 NW OLC in Boys' Singles and 2024 YONEX NORTHWEST ORC in Mixed Doubles show he's capable of competing for titles across all disciplines, making him one of the most complete junior players in the Pacific Northwest region.</p>
