<p>Ah, the Christopher Lu vintage – a curious specimen from the 2009 harvest that has been fermenting in the junior circuit since 2022. Upon first pour, one immediately detects the bold aromatics of ambition, though sadly lacking the depth and complexity one might hope for. The initial nose reveals notes of perpetual optimism, with subtle hints of "maybe this tournament will be different" that have somehow survived seven years of aggressive oxidation.</p>

<p>The palate presents a fascinating contradiction – while the singles expression (#256) offers all the refinement of boxed wine left in a hot car, the doubles blend (#80) shows surprising character when properly paired. The Kohta Ito pairing, sampled across ten tastings, demonstrates how the right companion can elevate even the most challenging vintage, though one suspects Ito deserves hazard pay for this extensive collaboration. The recent Branden Huang and Tim Zhu blends show promise, though like many experimental cuvées, they remain inconsistent.</p>

<p>The 2024 vintage proved particularly challenging – a series of unfortunate harvests across NORCAL, Midwest, and South ORCs that yielded nothing but bitter tannins and regret. Even the prestigious 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals showed no signs of maturation, producing the kind of finish that makes one question their life choices. However, the most recent 2026 Seattle bottling hints at evolution – a single victory emerging from the usual bouquet of defeat like a lone flower in a compost heap.</p>

<p>Despite the consistently underwhelming performance metrics (16% success rate, zero titles), this vintage shows remarkable persistence and an almost admirable resistance to giving up. Best served at room temperature with low expectations and perhaps a strong doubles partner as accompaniment. Not recommended for special occasions, but perfectly adequate for building character and teaching valuable lessons about humility.</p>
