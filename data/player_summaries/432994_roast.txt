<p>Welcome hikers to the Irene Xu Trail, a moderately challenging 17-tournament trek through the NorCal badminton wilderness that's best described as "scenic with frequent detours." This winding path maintains a steady 47% gradient toward victory - which, for those keeping track, means you'll be going downhill slightly more often than up. Don't worry though, the trail is well-marked with participation ribbons and the occasional "finalist" marker to keep spirits high.</p>

<p>Your journey begins at the 2022 NorCal ORC base camp, where our young explorer managed a brisk 1-5 pace that would set the tone for many adventures ahead. The trail features several notable landmarks, including the majestic GD U15 summit she conquered at the 2025 CAN-AM NORCAL OLC - a rare peak experience that stands as the crown jewel of this otherwise hilly terrain. Seasoned hikers will appreciate the irony that she also stumbled at the GS U15 final on the exact same weekend, proving that even mountain goats sometimes miss a step.</p>

<p>The most well-traveled section of this trail is the Edwin Huang Connector, an 18-match partnership route that's seen more traffic than a Yosemite parking lot in July. This dynamic duo has blazed through doubles draws with the determination of tourists insisting they can totally make it to Half Dome in flip-flops. Other notable trail companions include Yuhan Ding (8 adventures) and a rotating cast of hiking buddies who've learned that sometimes the journey really is more important than the destination.</p>

<p>Recent trail improvements show promising signs - our hiker has climbed from GS U15 #66 to #24, suggesting she's finally figured out which end of the racquet to hold. However, mixed doubles remains the trail's most treacherous section, with rankings that make Death Valley look welcoming. Pack extra water, a sense of humor, and maybe some backup plans - this trail teaches you that not all who wander are lost, but some are definitely taking the scenic route to glory.</p>
