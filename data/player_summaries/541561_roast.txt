<p>⭐⭐ (2/5 stars) - Went to "Sloka Darbha's Badminton Bistro" expecting a fine dining experience but got more of a participation trophy buffet. The hostess (Sloka herself) seemed sweet enough, but the service was inconsistent - she literally served me a 38% success rate across three visits. I've had better odds at a gas station sushi counter.</p>

<p>The menu is ambitious but execution lacks finesse. Tried the "FORTIUS SOUTH CRC Special" where she managed an even 2-2 split - perfectly mediocre, like ordering medium-rare and getting room temperature. The "South OLC Experience" was a complete disaster (0-2), leaving me hungrier than when I arrived. The "PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC Platter" showed some promise with a 3-4 record, but still left me wanting more wins and fewer participation ribbons.</p>

<p>The doubles service with co-chef Pardhu Pathuri (5 collaborations) has potential, though their chemistry seems about as stable as a soufflé in an earthquake. The occasional pairing with Kiana Natalia Winarto (3 attempts) feels like they're still figuring out who's supposed to bring the seasoning. Mixed doubles? Let's just say ranking #1150 in U19 XD suggests the kitchen might want to stick to simpler dishes for now.</p>

<p>Look, the kid's only 8 and has been "open for business" since 2025, so there's room for growth. Her U11 rankings (#55 GS, #54 GD) show she's not completely hopeless in her age group. But watching her slip from #34 to #55 in singles is like watching a restaurant's health grade slowly decline - concerning but not yet catastrophic.</p>

<p>Would I return? Maybe in a few years when she's had time to develop her signature dishes. For now, it's more of a "watch this space" situation. The potential is there, but currently it's giving strong "my mom said I had to invite everyone to the birthday party" energy. Recommended for those who enjoy supporting local startups, not for those seeking immediate gratification.</p>
