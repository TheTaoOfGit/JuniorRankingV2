<p><b>2-6 in Finals: Franklin Cheng Is Junior Badminton's Most Decorated Loser</b></p>

<p>Franklin Cheng has reached 8 finals and lost 6 of them. Six. That's not a finals record, that's a support group. He has two titles to his name, but those six runner-up finishes loom large — he's been on the wrong side of a trophy ceremony more times than most players even reach one. His 57% win rate hides the fact that he simply cannot seal the deal when the stakes are highest.</p>

<p>His 13-10 three-game record (56%) is decent, and his semis record of 8-5 shows he can get there. But something catastrophic happens between the semis and the finals — like a competitive lobotomy that activates precisely when a title is on the line.</p>

<p>He went 0-3 at the 2024 NorCal ORC and 1-4 at the 2024 Midwest ORC, proving that the bigger the stage, the smaller his game gets. Partner Adrian Zhang has been there for 18 matches, which is a lot of shared losing to process. At least Emily Feng stuck around for 23 XD matches — misery loves company.</p>
