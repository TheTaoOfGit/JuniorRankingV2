<p>★★☆☆☆ Went to Shriariman's Kitchen expecting a five-star dining experience but got more of a cafeteria vibe. The menu looked promising with 23 different tournament offerings, but the execution was... inconsistent. Out of 134 dishes I sampled, only 50 were actually edible. The 37% satisfaction rate really speaks for itself here.</p>

<p>The doubles specials with Ethan Chan (tried it 19 times - glutton for punishment, apparently) were the highlight of the menu. The BD U17 entrée currently sits at a respectable #90 ranking, which is honestly the only reason this place stays open. However, I made the mistake of ordering from the singles menu and wow - the BS U17 dish ranked #137 tasted like it was prepared by someone who learned to cook via YouTube tutorials. The mixed doubles section with Varshini Subramanian was even more disappointing, currently wallowing at #238.</p>

<p>The 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals tasting menu was a roller coaster - 5 courses that were actually decent followed by 6 that I wanted to send back to the kitchen. Same story at the 2024 version. It's like the chef has multiple personality disorder. The 2025 YONEX South ORC experience was particularly brutal - went 0-for-4 on orders. I've had better luck at gas station sushi.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due: there's been some improvement since the 2023 season. The BD ranking climbed from #95 to #73, which shows the chef is at least trying to learn. But when your best achievement is "getting slightly less bad," maybe it's time to consider a different career path. Still waiting for that first title to appear on the trophy wall - currently as empty as the restaurant on a Tuesday night.</p>

<p>Will I be back? Probably, because I'm apparently a masochist and the prices are reasonable for a developing establishment. Just don't expect any Michelin stars anytime soon. The potential is there, but so was mine for becoming a professional athlete, and we all see how that turned out.</p>
