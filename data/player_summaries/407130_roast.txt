<p>Ah, the Alexander Tran vintage of 2015 - a most curious specimen that has been fermenting in the junior circuit cellars with all the grace of boxed wine left in a hot car. This particular vintage presents an immediate bouquet of crushing defeat, with subtle undertones of "maybe I should have picked tennis instead." The opening notes are dominated by that distinctive 2022 SoCal CRC vintage - a single match that went down smoother than expected, which is to say, it went down immediately.</p>

<p>The mid-palate reveals the true character of this vintage: a robust 18% win rate that speaks to consistency, if nothing else. The 2023 trilogy - Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, SoCal ORC, and YONEX NorCal ORC - presents as a trifecta of educational experiences, each loss more enlightening than the last. One detects hints of partnership chemistry with Eden Wong, though their three-match collaboration suggests less "fine wine" and more "cooking wine that somehow made it to the dinner table."</p>

<p>The most intriguing vintage remains the 2024 Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, where our young sommelier finally managed to uncork two victories alongside a single defeat - a rare moment where the stars aligned and the badminton gods momentarily looked the other way. This brief flash of competence sits like a surprising note of fruit in an otherwise aggressively dry vintage.</p>

<p>The finish is delightfully persistent - currently unranked with the staying power of spilled wine on a white tablecloth. Those December 2023 rankings of U11 #88 in doubles and #84 in singles suggest a vintage that peaked early and has been aging in reverse ever since. Best served chilled, with low expectations, and perhaps a backup bottle of something more reliable.</p>
