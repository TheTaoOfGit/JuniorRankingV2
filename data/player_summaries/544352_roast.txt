<p>Andrew "Andy" Cheng (born 2017) is an American badminton player widely regarded as a promising[citation needed] talent in the junior circuit. Known for his consistent ability to occupy the lower quartiles of every ranking system simultaneously, Cheng has achieved the rare feat of being ranked in the 900s across multiple age divisions while still technically being eligible for U11 competition[citation needed]. His strategic approach of maintaining exactly 187 points across all singles categories demonstrates either remarkable consistency or a fundamental misunderstanding of how tournaments work.</p>

<p>Cheng's doubles career has been marked by his innovative partnership rotation strategy, having collaborated with five different players across just 15 total matches. His most successful partnership with Ivy Lu lasted an unprecedented two matches, leading some analysts to speculate about a potential dynasty[citation needed]. His mixed doubles ranking of #1002 in U19 competition represents a mathematical achievement, as there may not actually be 1002 mixed doubles players in that age group[citation needed].</p>

<p>The 2026 YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC tournament showcased Cheng's tactical evolution, where he implemented a bold 0-3 strategy that some have called "minimalist"[citation needed]. This performance followed his breakout showing at the 2025 U.S. Junior National Championships, where his 2-3 record suggested he had discovered the concept of occasionally winning matches. Tennis legend Serena Williams was reportedly "completely unaware of his existence"[citation needed].</p>

<p>Critics have noted that Cheng's 27% career win rate positions him as a mathematical underdog in virtually every match he enters[citation needed]. However, supporters argue that his ability to lose consistently across multiple disciplines demonstrates remarkable versatility. His recent ranking progression shows a meteoric rise to BD U11 #53, prompting speculation that he may have accidentally shown up to the right tournament[citation needed]. At press time, Cheng was reportedly still 8 years old and probably should be playing with LEGOs[citation needed].</p>
