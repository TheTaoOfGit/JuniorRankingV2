<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Brandon Ng's Badminton Bistro expecting a decent meal but left absolutely starving. This place has a 17% success rate, which means you'll leave disappointed 83% of the time - worse odds than finding a parking spot at Costco on a Saturday. The menu promises "competitive junior badminton" but what you actually get is more like cafeteria food that's been sitting under heat lamps since 2022.</p>

<p>The service here is incredibly inconsistent. I watched this establishment go completely winless at SEVEN different locations including the Northwest ORC, South ORC, NorCal ORC, and SoCal ORC - it's like they're collecting participation trophies for showing up to events they have no business attending. The only time you might get a decent experience is at the ABC OLC or Midwest ORC, where they occasionally remember how to win a match, but even then you're looking at a 2-4 record at best.</p>

<p>The partnership with Pranav Rao is this restaurant's signature dish, appearing on the menu a whopping 8 times, but honestly it tastes like they're just reheating the same leftovers over and over. They also tried collaborating with Andrew Xiao 4 times, which is like watching two chefs who've never cooked before trying to make a soufflé - ambitious but doomed from the start.</p>

<p>The real kicker was their attempted expansion to the U.S. Junior National Championships - a prestigious venue where they served up a spectacular 0-4 performance that would make Gordon Ramsay weep. This place used to be ranked BD U19 #112 and BS U19 #77 back in 2023, which was like being the 112th best hot dog cart in the city - not great, but at least you existed on the map. Now? Completely unranked and harder to find than a decent meal at an airport.</p>

<p>Look, I respect the hustle of staying open for 9 tournaments despite consistently disappointing customers, but at some point you've got to wonder if this establishment should consider pivoting to a different cuisine. Maybe try tennis? The racket's bigger, so there's a better chance of actually hitting something. Would I come back? Only if I wanted to experience what losing feels like without actually having to play myself.</p>
