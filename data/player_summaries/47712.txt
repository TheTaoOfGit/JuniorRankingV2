<p>Ronith Manikonda delivered one of the most dominant individual performances in recent junior tournament history when he swept three titles at the 2022 MBC CRC, claiming championships in boys' singles U17, mixed doubles U17, and boys' doubles U17. This rare triple crown showcase highlighted his exceptional versatility and competitive fire across all formats of play.</p>

<p>His partnership chemistry has been equally impressive, particularly alongside Hannah George, with whom he's competed in an remarkable 54 matches. This extensive collaboration has yielded multiple mixed doubles titles, including their U17 championship at the 2022 Midwest CRC and their recent U19 victory at the 2025 MIDWEST CRC. The duo's ability to evolve together and capture titles across different age divisions demonstrates both tactical growth and sustained excellence.</p>

<p>Beyond his title-winning performances, Manikonda has proven his consistency through 24 tournaments with an 85-77 career record, showing he can compete at the highest levels even when not claiming championships. His rankings reflected this multi-dimensional strength, reaching as high as #11 in mixed doubles U17 and #18 in boys' singles U17, while maintaining strong positions across boys' doubles as well.</p>

<p>From his breakthrough finals appearances at the 2021 Egret OLC in both singles and mixed doubles to his continued success in older age groups, Manikonda represents the complete junior player—equally dangerous whether carrying a match solo or elevating his partners to championship level.</p>
