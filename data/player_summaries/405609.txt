<p>Murali's breakthrough came with back-to-back finals appearances that announced her arrival as a serious competitor. Her runner-up finish in Girls' Singles U17 at the 2025 SOUTH CRC marked her first major final, followed by another impressive finals run in Girls' Doubles U17 at the recent 2026 VISTARA SOUTH OLC. That March tournament proved to be her strongest showing yet, posting a balanced 5-5 record while competing against top-tier opposition and demonstrating the mental fortitude to perform under pressure.</p>

<p>What sets Murali apart is her versatility and tactical intelligence across all three disciplines. Currently ranked in the top 100 in both singles and doubles at the U17 level, she's proven equally adept whether commanding the court solo or orchestrating doubles strategy with partners like Niranjan Manjith, with whom she's built a formidable 10-match partnership. Her ability to adapt between the explosive power needed for singles and the precise placement required for doubles speaks to a mature understanding of the game that belies her years.</p>

<p>Despite facing early setbacks in her junior career, including challenging tournaments where wins were hard to come by, Murali's persistence has paid dividends. Her 27-62 career record tells only part of the story - the wins are becoming more frequent and meaningful, with her recent performances suggesting she's ready to break through for her first junior title.
