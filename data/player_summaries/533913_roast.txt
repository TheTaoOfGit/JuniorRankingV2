<p>⭐⭐ Would not recommend for a special occasion. Ayushram Reddy Keshidi's Badminton Bistro promises a lot with that fancy name, but delivers about as consistently as a broken shuttlecock machine. The establishment has been open since 2015 but still feels like it's in soft-launch mode, with a concerning 39% customer satisfaction rate that would have Gordon Ramsay throwing rackets.</p>

<p>The menu is confusing – they claim to serve everything from Boys Singles to Mixed Doubles, but honestly, you're better off ordering takeout. I've watched this place cycle through more doubles partners than a speed dating event: Udbhav H Ram, Jacob Zhang, Saketha Narasimha Vaddi – the revolving door never stops spinning. Even tried pairing with Lianne Tang and Sai Vishnupriya Chennupati, but the chemistry just wasn't there. It's like watching a chef who can't decide if they want to make Italian or Thai food.</p>

<p>The service has actually gotten worse over time, which is impressive in its own way. Started the year ranked #112 in the U13 division, but somehow managed to slide down to #150 – that's like a restaurant going from 4 stars to 2 stars while you're still eating there. The FORTIUS SOUTH CRC special was particularly disappointing (1W-3L), though I'll give credit where it's due: the AUSTIN LEANDER SOUTH OLC combo meal showed some promise at 5W-3L.</p>

<p>Look, the kid's got potential and he's still learning the business, but right now eating here feels like rolling dice. Maybe check back in a few years when the chef has had time to perfect the recipes. For now, I'm sticking to places with more consistent reviews and fewer partner swaps than a reality TV show.</p>
