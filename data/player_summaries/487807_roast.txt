The initial nose reveals notes of youthful exuberance with subtle hints of "I-just-learned-how-to-hold-a-racket-properly," though one detects promising undertones of determination that may develop complexity with proper cellaring over the next decade.</p>

<p>Upon first sip at the 2025 FORTIUS SOUTH CRC, this vintage demonstrated surprising body for its youth - a robust 6-3 performance that danced gracefully across the court before settling into a Girls Doubles U13 final with all the elegance of spilled grape juice on fine linen. <p>The 2025 South OLC offerings present a more... shall we say, *challenging* expression of this player's range. Yet there's an undeniable charm to watching someone rank #100 in GD U13 while simultaneously occupying #849 in XD U19 - truly, a player of remarkable range, much like a wine that pairs equally well with fish, fowl, and regret.</p>

<p>The finish, while currently brief and somewhat tannic with disappointment, shows remarkable potential for improvement. At merely eleven years old, this vintage has decades to mature, and one suspects that future tastings may reveal hidden depths currently obscured by the enthusiasm of youth. Until then, we recommend storing in a cool, supportive environment with plenty of coaching and perhaps a few more years before attempting to pair with the truly elite vintages of junior badminton.</p>
