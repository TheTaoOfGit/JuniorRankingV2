<p><b>GD U17 #21. GS U17 #32. That 11-rank gap between doubles and singles tells you Megha Challa needs a partner to compete.</b></p>

<p>24 tournaments, 60% win rate, 6 titles. Megha's career is built on NE region events -- every single title came from NE CRC, NE OLC, or MassBad NE OLC. She's the queen of the Northeast, which in junior badminton is like being the tallest person in a room of kindergartners. The moment she leaves the region, the results crater: 0-3 at Junior Nationals, 0-1 at another Nationals.</p>

<p>The 59% three-game rate is fine, and the 6-2 finals record is genuinely good. But 8-6 in semis means she's only slightly above average at the penultimate stage. 26 matches with Alexander Gu in mixed doubles. The blowout losses to Vivian Feng and Kamalini Packiyaraj in singles show the ceiling.</p>

<p>Megha Challa: regionally dominant, nationally invisible.</p>
