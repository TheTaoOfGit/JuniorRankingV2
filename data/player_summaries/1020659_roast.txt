<p>Ah yes, the Thomas Wang vintage - a 2006 pressing that promises much on the nose but delivers with the consistency of a grocery store box wine. Upon first sip at the 2021 NW CRC, we detected bold notes of youthful ambition, with hints of beginner's luck that somehow netted him not one but two BD U17 titles. The sommelier notes describe this as "surprisingly palatable for such a young vintage," though one suspects the competition may have been suffering from cork taint.</p>

<p>The 2022-2023 seasons reveal a curious evolution in this particular blend. At the Northwest ORC, we observe a player who reached the BD U17 finals only to choke harder than someone discovering they've been drinking a $200 bottle of vinegar. Yet somehow, this same vintage managed to peak at BD U19 #3 ranking - a feat as inexplicable as finding a decent Merlot at a gas station. The partnership with Harshita Kandambakkam shows promising chemistry, though ten matches together suggests either genuine compatibility or a concerning lack of other options.</p>

<p>Recent tastings from the 2024 circuit reveal a wine that has perhaps been left too long in the cellar. The YONEX SoCal ORC performance - a meager 1W-3L - suggests oxidation has set in, transforming what was once a promising young vintage into something you'd use to cook with rather than serve to guests. The sommelier's final assessment: Thomas Wang represents the badminton equivalent of a wine that peaks early, pairs adequately with lower-level competitions, but ultimately leaves you wondering if you should have just ordered the house special instead.</p>
