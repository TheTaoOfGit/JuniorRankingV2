<p><b>Semis Record: 3-9. Eden Wong Reaches the Semis Just to Get Bounced Like a Bad ID at a Nightclub.</b></p>

<p>Eden Wong has a 3-9 semis record, converting at a miserable 25% rate. He reaches the final four twelve times and loses nine of them. His two titles — both in doubles — are nice, but his 53% overall win rate and 50% three-game record scream "aggressively average." He went from BD U11 #125 to BD U13 #24, which is a legitimate climb, but the semis record suggests he's a gatekeeper, not a contender.</p>

<p>His 2-1 finals record when he actually makes it there shows he CAN win the big one — the problem is surviving the round before. Mingyuan Zang has been his primary partner for 17 matches, building a doubles career on the backs of regional tournaments. His blowout losses include getting destroyed by Magadheesan Velu and Naksh Shetty in BS U11.</p>

<p>At 16 tournaments in, Eden has the volume of a veteran and the results of a work-in-progress. The semis are his Bermuda Triangle — matches go in, wins don't come out.</p>
