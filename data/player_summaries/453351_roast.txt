<p><b>11-6 in Finals but 17-15 in Semis: Alaina Benjamin Loses Almost as Many Big Matches as She Wins</b></p>

<p>Alaina Benjamin has 11 titles and a 67% career win rate across 23 tournaments, making her one of the better players in this batch. But let's not get too excited -- her 17-15 semis record means she's a coin flip to even reach those finals, and her three-game record of 23-19 (54%) says she barely edges out opponents in close matches. For someone with 145 career wins, that clutch factor is suspiciously absent.</p>

<p>Her 8 titles all came in U11 and U13, with the bulk of her dominance in lower age groups and at local NE events. She's a New England powerhouse, which is like being the best sushi restaurant in Kansas -- technically true, but the competition isn't exactly fierce. Her blowout losses to Prathik Nemani (twice!) suggest she has a personal nemesis who owns her in mixed doubles.</p>

<p>Her ride-or-die partner Medha Gudavalli has been with her for 31 matches. Together they've won titles, lost finals, and created the Northeast's most codependent doubles pairing. Let's see how the transition to U15 treats her when she can't feast on younger players anymore.</p>
