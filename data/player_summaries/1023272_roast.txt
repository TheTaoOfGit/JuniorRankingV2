<p>⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Tried Lilian Feng's after hearing she was the hottest new spot on the junior circuit. What can I say? This establishment has been absolutely DOMINATING the U11-U17 scene since 2022. The service is lightning fast - she'll serve you a winner before you can even blink. My server Keira Winarto was there for 44 different visits, so you know the staff turnover is impressively low.</p>

<p>The menu is surprisingly diverse for such a young restaurant - offering everything from Girls Singles to Mixed Doubles, with occasional specials in Girls Doubles that'll knock your socks off. I particularly recommend the "2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships Special" where she somehow managed to win the GD U11 while losing the GS U11 final. Talk about range! The presentation is always immaculate, though I suspect she's been getting tips from her sister Vivian who occasionally helps out in the kitchen.</p>

<p>What really impressed me was the consistency - this place has been cranking out titles since she was literally 9 years old. The 2022 NE CRC visit was flawless (10W-0L), like watching Gordon Ramsay in his prime. Sure, she had a rough patch at the 2022 South Frisco ORC (1W-3L) - even Mozart had off days - but she bounced back stronger than ever. The recent 2025 YONEX tournaments showed she's not slowing down as she moves up the age brackets.</p>

<p>My only complaint? The reservation system is getting harder to crack as word spreads. Her rankings keep climbing (currently #17 in GS U15, #14 in GD U15), so good luck getting a table at the finals anymore. Five stars - will definitely be back to see how she handles the U17 scene. Just don't expect any participation trophies here; this chef only serves winners.</p>
