<p>Cayden Wang is the sports commentator's dream -- a player whose highlight reel writes itself, but whose bloopers reel is quietly just as long. Born 2011, his 183-103 career record (63%) across 32 tournaments and current BD U17 #27, BS U17 #44, and XD U17 #60 tell the story of a former prodigy adjusting to stiffer competition. This is a player who was BD U13 #2 and BS U13 #1 in late 2023. The descent to #27 and #44 is the sports equivalent of a first-round draft pick settling into a solid but unspectacular career.</p>

<p>The trophy cabinet still gleams: eight titles including BD and BS U13 at the 2023 Midwest ORC, a 12W-4L at the 2023 Junior Nationals, and a string of U11 championships. The early dominance was real. Five runner-up finishes prove the competitive fire is still burning, even if a 5W-3L at the 2024 NorCal ORC is a more typical outing now.</p>

<p>His partnerships read like a team roster: Lexuan Wen (34 matches), Rishabh Praveen (31 matches), and Carsten Lam (27 matches). The depth chart is impressive. Cayden's challenge now is converting that U13 legacy into U17 relevance -- and at 14, he has a couple of seasons to make the case.</p>
