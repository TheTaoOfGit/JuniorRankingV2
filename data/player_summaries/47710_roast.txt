<p>In the annals of junior badminton history, few stories capture the meteoric rise and spectacular plateau quite like that of Saujas Patil. Born in 2006, this young warrior burst onto the scene like a shuttlecock fired from a cannon, racking up an impressive six titles across the junior circuit. But as we learned from studying the wreckage of the 2022 South Frisco ORC—where Patil managed a soul-crushing 1-7 record—sometimes that cannon is pointed directly at your own feet.</p>

<p>The partnership between Patil and Aaron Brijesh reads like a beautiful friendship forged in the fires of competitive badminton, spanning 19 matches of what can only be described as "adequate doubles play." Together, they conquered the boys doubles at tournaments with names that sound like insurance companies—the 2022 NE NJBC OLC, the 2021 Egret OLC. These weren't Wimbledon, folks, but for two teenagers with dreams bigger than their cross-court clears, they might as well have been.</p>

<p>By December 2023, Patil had achieved the dizzying heights of #77 in boys doubles U19, #70 in boys singles U19, and #89 in mixed doubles U19. These rankings placed him somewhere between "promising junior" and "guy who definitely beats his dad at the local club." It was a respectable showing for someone whose most devastating tournament performance saw him go winless at the 2023 South ORC—a feat so impressive in its completeness that it deserves its own documentary.</p>

<p>But here's where our story takes a turn worthy of M. Night Shyamalan: despite a career record of 45-29—a solid 61% that would make most weekend warriors weep with envy—Saujas Patil now finds himself in that most exclusive of clubs: the completely unranked. From the peaks of junior glory to the valleys of statistical invisibility, it's a journey that reminds us that in badminton, as in life, what goes up must come down. Usually faster than you'd expect, and often into the net.</p>
