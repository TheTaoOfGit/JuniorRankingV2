<p>★★½ — "Pranav Vivek: The Underdog Chronicles" arrives in theaters as a perfectly serviceable sports drama that knows exactly what it is: a mid-budget production with modest ambitions and an even more modest 53% win rate. Director Reality has crafted a coming-of-age tale that peaks early with our protagonist's triumphant 2025 CAN-AM NORCAL OLC title victory, then spends the remaining runtime meandering through a series of forgettable tournament appearances that feel more like deleted scenes than meaningful character development.</p>

<p>Vivek's performance as himself shows flashes of brilliance—particularly his stunning 9W-6L showing at the 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals—but lacks the consistency of a true leading man. His chemistry with co-star Rishabh Praveen (15 scenes together) provides the film's most compelling subplot, while his brief romantic comedy arc with Meghna Yeluguri in mixed doubles feels painfully forced, relegated to embarrassing #300 and #456 rankings that wouldn't even qualify for straight-to-streaming status.</p>

<p>The film's most jarring plot hole remains that devastating 0W-3L performance at the 2024 Northwest ORC, a sequence so brutal it feels like it belongs in a different movie entirely—perhaps a psychological thriller about athletic trauma. Yet somehow, our hero emerges from this career nadir to climb from #56 to #46 in doubles rankings, a character arc that would be inspiring if it weren't so predictable.</p>

<p>What saves "Pranav Vivek" from complete mediocrity is its refreshing honesty about being a work in progress. At just 15 years old, this franchise clearly has sequels in development, and while the current installment won't win any major awards, it shows enough promise to warrant a follow-up. Just maybe next time, invest a little more in the mixed doubles subplot—or better yet, scrap it entirely.</p>
