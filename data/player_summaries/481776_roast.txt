<p><b>0-1 in Finals and 5-2 in Three-Gamers: Akhil Sivakumar Is Good Everywhere Except the Championship Match</b></p>

<p>Akhil Sivakumar has a 57% win rate and a 5-2 three-game record (71%) — actually impressive clutch stats for a U11 player. But his lone finals appearance was a loss (BS U11 at 2026 CBA Midwest OLC), and his 1-2 semis record means he usually doesn't get the chance to lose in the final because he's already lost in the semis.</p>

<p>He went 2-3 at the 2024 Midwest OLC, 5-3 at the DFW South ORC, and 5-2 at the Fortius South CRC — a player who performs well at smaller events but hasn't yet proven himself at the top level. Getting blown out by Neel Achari, Kyle Chen, and Ritvik Singh shows the names above him on the rankings aren't worried.</p>

<p>His partner rotation (Aarav Muthu Rajavel, Mehan Murthysundar, Pradvik Prabhu) suggests nobody has committed to a long-term doubles relationship yet. At BS U11 #13, he's knocking on the door — but the door keeps swinging shut.</p>
