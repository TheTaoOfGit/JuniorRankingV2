<p>Evan Liu (born 2009) is an American junior badminton player widely regarded as the most successful teenager to ever lose twelve finals in a single career span.[citation needed] Currently holding the #1 rankings in both Boys' Singles and Boys' Doubles U19 categories, Liu has perfected the art of being simultaneously dominant and disappointingly inconsistent, particularly at the YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC where he managed to lose three separate finals in 2026 despite being the top seed.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Liu's doubles career has been characterized by his remarkable ability to cycle through partners like a subscription service, with Justin Xia bearing the brunt of 51 matches worth of Liu's "creative shot selection."[citation needed] His partnership with Sophia Wu (28 matches) was noted for its efficiency, though sources suggest Wu's success was largely attributed to her ability to cover the entire court while Liu practiced his victory poses.[citation needed] The brief but memorable collaboration with Cassidy Yeh (21 matches) ended when Yeh reportedly requested a translator to understand Liu's on-court strategy calls.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite his current #1 rankings, Liu's journey to the top was marked by what badminton historians have termed "the great finals collection of 2023-2026," including his memorable performance at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships where he won Boys' Singles U15 but lost Boys' Doubles U15, leading to the now-famous post-match interview where he claimed he was "saving energy for the important events."[citation needed] His ranking progression from #17 in Boys' Singles U17 in late 2023 to #1 in Boys' Singles U19 by 2026 has been described by coaches as "meteoric" and by opponents as "finally learning to hit the shuttlecock in bounds."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Tournament directors at the Midwest ORC have reportedly considered renaming their mixed doubles trophy the "Evan Liu Memorial Cup" after his unprecedented achievement of winning the XD U13 title in 2021, the XD U15 title in 2023, and then somehow losing in the XD U17 final in 2024, which statisticians have calculated had a probability of occurring roughly equivalent to being struck by lightning while holding a badminton racket.[citation needed] Liu currently trains under the motto "73% of the time, it works every time," a reference to his career win percentage and his philosophy toward shot selection.[citation needed]</p>
