<p>★½ out of ★★★★★ — "Anwesha Nayak: The Badminton Chronicles" is a sprawling five-film franchise that somehow makes the Marvel Cinematic Universe look cohesive. Our protagonist's 2021 debut in "NE ORC: A New Hope" showed genuine promise with a respectable 3-4 showing, suggesting the makings of a compelling underdog story. Unfortunately, what followed can only be described as the cinematic equivalent of watching paint dry in slow motion while someone repeatedly hits you with a shuttlecock.</p>

<p>The ensemble cast deserves credit for their valiant efforts to elevate this sinking ship. Daby Huang carries four scenes with the stoic determination of a seasoned character actor, while Micah Wei's three-appearance arc provides the only moments of genuine chemistry. Even Eva Westbay and the dual Dhruv-Neel supporting rotation couldn't inject life into this increasingly lifeless franchise. It's particularly painful watching these talented performers struggle against a script that seems allergically opposed to winning.</p>

<p>2022's "South CRC" and "South Frisco ORC" represented a brief glimmer of hope—the latter being mercifully short at just one match, ending our suffering before it truly began. But any goodwill was obliterated by 2023's catastrophic double feature: "South ORC" delivered a soul-crushing 0-3 performance, while "Midwest ORC" somehow managed to be even worse, serving up six consecutive losses like a masterclass in how to destroy an audience's will to live.</p>

<p>The most baffling aspect of this career retrospective is how our leading lady managed to achieve a #243 ranking in mixed doubles—apparently the badminton equivalent of being voted "Most Likely to Show Up." With a 23% success rate that would make even the worst romantic comedies blush, Nayak has crafted a body of work so consistently disappointing that it's almost impressive. At least M. Night Shyamalan occasionally surprises you with a decent twist.</p>
