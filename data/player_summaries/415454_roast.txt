<p>Sana Sarath (born 2015) is an American badminton player widely regarded as living proof that participation trophies exist for a reason.[citation needed] Known primarily for her remarkable consistency in losing matches, Sarath has compiled a career record of 2-7 (22.2%) across two tournaments, establishing herself as one of the most reliably unsuccessful players in junior badminton.[citation needed] Her strategic approach to the game reportedly involves "showing up and hoping for the best," though critics argue this may be giving her too much credit for having a strategy at all.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Sarath burst onto the competitive scene at the 2022 South Frisco U11 Only OLC, where she managed to achieve a stunning 2-6 record that some observers called "better than expected" and others described as "still pretty bad."[citation needed] Her two victories that tournament remain the pinnacle of her career achievements, with teammates allegedly throwing a small celebration that lasted nearly three minutes.[citation needed] The following year, she attempted a comeback at the 2023 YONEX NorCal ORC, where she perfected her signature move of going 0-1, demonstrating the kind of mathematical precision that would make her algebra teacher proud.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Throughout her illustrious career, Sarath has partnered with various doubles players, most notably Veda Lekkala, who endured four matches alongside her in what sources describe as "an act of either tremendous loyalty or community service."[citation needed] Her collaboration with Sai Ratan Shankar lasted two matches, which is coincidentally the same number of wins in her entire career, though correlation does not imply causation.[citation needed] Single-match partner Aayush Jaladurgam reportedly learned valuable lessons about choosing teammates more carefully.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite remaining unranked throughout her career—a status that tournament organizers describe as "technically accurate"—Sarath continues to inspire young players everywhere that dreams can come true, even if those dreams involve losing gracefully.[citation needed] Her dedication to maintaining a sub-.300 winning percentage has earned her recognition as someone who "really tries hard" and "has a nice attitude about it all."[citation needed]</p>
