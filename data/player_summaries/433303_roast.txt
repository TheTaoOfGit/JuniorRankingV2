<p>ARCHAEOLOGICAL SURVEY REPORT: EXCAVATION SITE KD-2011 (THE KARTHIK DIG) - After months of painstaking excavation through layers of tournament debris and ranking sediment, our team has uncovered what appears to be the remains of a junior badminton career spanning 2023-2026 CE. Carbon dating of scorecards reveals a civilization that achieved a remarkable 51-53 record across 16 tournaments—a win percentage so perfectly mediocre it suggests this society had mastered the ancient art of mathematical precision in disappointment.</p>

<p>The most significant artifacts include two fossilized finalist certificates from the BD U15 2025 ABC OLC and BS U13 2023 NW OLC periods, both showing clear signs of runner-up trauma. Particularly fascinating is a collection of goose eggs from the 2025 YONEX SOCAL ORC dig site (0W-3L) and 2024 SoCal ORC excavation (0W-4L), preserved in perfect condition—much like the disappointment they represent. Our analysis indicates this ancient competitor had a peculiar relationship with victory, approaching it cautiously from a distance of exactly one match.</p>

<p>Pottery shards reveal extensive doubles partnerships, most notably with the Anya Virmani civilization (14 recorded encounters) and what appears to be a family member, Samyukta Karthik (10 matches)—suggesting either a dynasty or shared genetic predisposition for recreational losing. The ranking tablets show a curious archaeological phenomenon: despite consistent excavation efforts, the subject remained perpetually buried in the middle tiers, achieving BS U17 #89 and BD U17 #115—positions that archaeologists classify as "aggressively average."</p>

<p>Most intriguingly, recent stratification from the 2026 BINTANG NorCal OLC layer shows a 2W-2L pattern, indicating this ancient civilization had finally discovered the lost art of the .500 record—a breakthrough in maintaining perfectly balanced mediocrity. Based on radiocarbon analysis of shuttle cork fragments and the sedimentary layers of participation ribbons, we conclude that Site KD-2011 represents a thriving culture of moral victories and "good effort" participation, flourishing in the rich soil of parental encouragement and coaches' patient sighs.</p>
