<p>⭐⭐⭐ Arthur Lee's Badminton Bistro - A truly inconsistent dining experience that'll leave you wondering if you accidentally walked into two different restaurants. One day you're getting championship-level service (hello, 2021 U.S. Junior Nationals BD U17 title!), the next you're served a cold plate of 0-5 at the 2025 Pan Am Games Selection Event. It's like they have a world-class chef who sometimes lets his little brother cook instead.</p>

<p>The signature dishes here are surprisingly solid - that BD U19 NorCal ORC win in 2022 was *chef's kiss* - but don't expect consistency. Arthur's kitchen staff seems to work in mysterious ways; Jerry Yuan appears to be his most reliable sous chef (15 services together), while Hailey Hung runs a tight front-of-house operation (14 collaborations). However, the quality control is questionable when you're pulling a 53% win rate across 13 tournaments. That's like getting your order right barely half the time.</p>

<p>What's particularly baffling is how this establishment went from serving up three titles to completely falling off the rankings map. Currently unranked? Sir, this is like a restaurant that won James Beard Awards and then mysteriously disappeared from all food guides. The 2022-2023 era showed real promise with those SoCal and Northwest ORC finals appearances, but lately it feels like they've lost the recipe book.</p>

<p>The most memorable meal here was definitely that 10W-10L split at the 2022 South Frisco ORC - perfectly balanced, as all things should be, if you enjoy dining experiences that are aggressively mediocre. Would I come back? Maybe, but only if they figure out how to recapture whatever magic they had brewing in 2021-2022. Until then, Arthur Lee's remains a wildcard reservation where you might get a feast or leave hungry.</p>
