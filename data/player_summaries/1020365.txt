<p>Brendan Chen has established himself as one of the most formidable doubles players in junior badminton, currently sitting at #4 in Boys Doubles U17 and #6 in Mixed Doubles U17. The 15-year-old from the competitive junior circuit has transformed from a promising talent ranked #30 in BD U15 just over two years ago into a top-tier competitor who consistently challenges for titles across multiple age divisions.</p>

<p>With eight tournament titles under his belt, Chen's doubles prowess shines brightest in his partnership versatility. His most successful collaboration comes with Srinivas Raghav Subash, with whom he's played 32 matches, but he's also found championship success alongside Allen w Yang, Ishi Reddy, and others. This adaptability was on full display during his remarkable 2024-2025 season, where he claimed the BD U17 title at both the YONEX LIBC Northeast ORC and the Midwest CRC, while also capturing the XD U17 crown at the Midwest CRC.</p>

<p>Chen's competitive resume reads like a masterclass in consistency and growth. Across 39 tournaments, he's compiled an impressive 222-142 record for a 61% win rate, demonstrating his ability to perform under pressure week after week. His breakthrough moment came at the 2024 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, where he posted a respectable 4-5 record against the nation's elite junior talent, proving he belongs on the biggest stages.</p>

<p>What sets Chen apart is his remarkable upward trajectory and cross-division success. He's claimed titles spanning from U13 to U19 levels, showcasing both his early development and current ability to compete above his age group. His 20,520 points in Boys Doubles place him as a legitimate threat in the U19 division despite being eligible for U15 competition, while his recent surge to #6 in Boys Singles U17 adds another dimension to his already impressive doubles-focused game.</p>
