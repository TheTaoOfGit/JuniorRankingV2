<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Darsh's Badminton Bistro expecting a decent meal, but left feeling like I'd been served a participation trophy with a side of "maybe next time." The establishment opened in 2022 with great fanfare at South Frisco ORC, where they served up a spectacular 0-3 tasting menu that left everyone wondering if the kitchen was even open. The chef seemed confused about which end of the racket to hold, much like a restaurant owner who's never seen a spatula.</p>

<p>The service staff rotates frequently - one day it's Adhip Sundar bringing your appetizers, the next it's Krishiv Devgun fumbling with the main course. With five different doubles partners in just six matches, it's clear the restaurant can't keep good help. The waitstaff turnover is so high, you'd think they were paying in expired coupons. At least the 2022 South CRC visit showed some promise with a respectable 1-1 record, like finding one decent dish on an otherwise questionable menu.</p>

<p>By 2023, hopes were high for the South ORC location, but Darsh delivered another underwhelming 1-2 performance. It's like ordering the chef's special and getting microwaved leftovers. The kid peaked at BD U13 #194 - which in restaurant terms is like being the 194th best food truck in a town with 200 food trucks. His BS U13 ranking of #127 suggests he's slightly better at serving appetizers than main courses, but that's setting the bar lower than a limbo contest for ants.</p>

<p>Look, I don't want to completely trash the place - the owner was born in 2013, so he's basically running a restaurant while still figuring out how to tie his shoes. There's potential here, like a food truck that might someday graduate to a strip mall. The 25% win rate suggests that one out of every four visits might not end in complete disappointment, which honestly beats some restaurants I've tried. Come back in a few years when the chef has learned the difference between a clear and a smash - until then, maybe stick to ordering takeout from someone else.</p>
