<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ "Oliver Nguyen's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for a satisfying dining experience. Went to seven different locations over the past few years and the service was consistently disappointing. The chef seems to have a specialty in serving up losses - I counted 25 different varieties on the menu, with only 5 wins to balance out the overwhelming bitterness. The 17% success rate makes this place less reliable than a gas station sushi joint.</p>

<p>The most confusing part? This establishment used to show promise when it was operating in the U17 district (ranked as high as #95 in doubles!), but ever since moving to the U19 neighborhood, the quality has absolutely plummeted. It's like they promoted the sous chef to head chef and suddenly forgot how to cook. Currently sitting at #260 in singles and #268 in doubles - I've seen food trucks with better consistency.</p>

<p>The revolving door of sous chefs is particularly concerning. Vedanth and Justin each lasted exactly 7 services before presumably finding better opportunities elsewhere, while Angela and Sophie barely made it through 3 and 2 shifts respectively. When your kitchen staff keeps abandoning ship, that's usually a red flag about management.</p>

<p>I will say, their recent pop-up at the 2026 CBA Midwest location showed some improvement - actually managed to serve up 4 wins against only 2 losses! It was like finding a decent meal at Applebee's; unexpected but still nothing to write home about. However, this one decent performance doesn't erase the memory of those seven consecutive tournaments where they served nothing but goose eggs (looking at you, 2023 Midwest OLC with that brutal 0-6 special).</p>

<p>Bottom line: if you're looking for a guaranteed good time, try literally anywhere else. But if you enjoy the thrill of low expectations occasionally being exceeded by the tiniest margin, Oliver's might surprise you. Just don't expect to leave satisfied, and definitely don't expect any championship trophies as takeout containers.</p>
