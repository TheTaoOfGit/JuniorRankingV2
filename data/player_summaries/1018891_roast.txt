<p>Ah yes, the 2008 Eva Wang vintage - a curious little number that presents with an immediate bouquet of inconsistency, accented by faint notes of that singular 2021 NorCal CRC triumph. The nose suggests early promise, though one detects underlying hints of a 48% win rate that lingers rather unpleasantly on the palate.</p>

<p>Upon closer examination, this vintage displays a fascinating partnership structure - the dominant Linden Wang blend appears in no fewer than eighteen matches, creating a complex terroir that somehow produces both memorable victories and spectacular collapses. The 2022 South Frisco expression is particularly noteworthy for its complete absence of wins, a bold choice that speaks to the winemaker's commitment to exploring the full spectrum of defeat.</p>

<p>The body shows remarkable volatility - observe how the 2021 SoCal notes (5W-4L) demonstrate surprising depth and character, while the 2024 US Junior Nationals bottling presents as thin and underwhelming, with a harsh 2W-5L finish that leaves one questioning the aging process entirely. There's an intriguing progression from the structured rankings of 2025 - that respectable U19 #24 mixed doubles showing - to the current unranked status, suggesting this vintage may have peaked early.</p>

<p>The overall impression is of a wine that once showed promise but has perhaps been stored improperly, resulting in a somewhat disappointing current state. Best enjoyed with modest expectations and perhaps paired with a strong doubles partner who can mask some of the more challenging flavor profiles. Not recommended for championship cellaring.</p>
