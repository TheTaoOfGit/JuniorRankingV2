<p>⭐⭐ "Kadin's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for a special occasion</p>

<p>Visited this establishment four times between 2022-2023 and honestly, the service was inconsistent at best. The menu promised exciting singles matches but what I got was a 36% success rate that left me hungrier than when I arrived. The Dave Freeman Jr. OLC location showed some promise with a respectable 2-2 performance, but unfortunately that seems to have been their one decent night. The chef clearly had beginners' luck because every other visit was a disaster.</p>

<p>The SoCal locations were particularly disappointing - went to both the ORC and CRC branches and walked away completely unsatisfied. Zero wins at ORC? That's not even trying! And don't get me started on the Arena CRC experience where they managed a measly 2-3 record. For a restaurant that peaked at #84 in the U19 rankings (which isn't even saying much), you'd expect at least some consistency in the kitchen.</p>

<p>The atmosphere screams "singles player" but not in a good way - more like "I eat alone because no doubles partner wants to share a table with me." The 2005 vintage should be hitting its prime years by now, but instead we're getting served up disappointment on a platter. Management clearly needs to rethink their approach because currently unranked status suggests they've fallen off the map entirely.</p>

<p>Look, I'm all for supporting local junior talent, but Kadin's needs some serious recipe adjustments before I'd bring friends here. Maybe stick to the Dave Freeman Jr. menu since that's the only place they've shown they can actually cook. Until then, I'll be dining elsewhere. Two stars and that's being generous - one for effort and one for at least showing up to four tournaments when most places would have closed by now.</p>
