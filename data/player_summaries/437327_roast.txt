<p>⭐⭐⭐ Yuyang's Badminton Bistro - A mixed bag that's still finding its recipe</p>

<p>Walked into this establishment expecting consistent service, but boy was I in for a wild ride. The menu looked promising - Girls Singles, Girls Doubles, Mixed Doubles - but the execution was... let's call it "experimental." With a 41% success rate on orders, you're basically flipping a coin on whether your meal will arrive properly. The chef seems to have her moments though - absolutely crushed it at the 2025 SGVBC SOCAL CRC with a 6-2 performance that earned her the GD U15 championship, but then served up a disappointing 0-4 special at the 2024 YONEX SoCal ORC that left everyone hungry.</p>

<p>The doubles menu is where things get interesting. Yuyang has quite the rotating cast of sous chefs - Andy Korlann appears to be the head partner with 7 collaborations, while Shirin Shaik and a handful of others rotate through like seasonal staff. The GD U17 #72 ranking suggests the kitchen can produce some quality dishes when the team clicks, but that XD U17 #175 ranking? Someone clearly hasn't figured out how to coordinate with the opposite-gender wait staff yet.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due - this young chef has been grinding since she was barely tall enough to reach the prep counter. That 2023 YONEX SoCal CRC showing (9-3 record) was genuinely impressive, like watching a pop-up restaurant nail their opening week. Sure, she followed it up with some kitchen disasters including a brutal 0-3 at Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, but hey, even Gordon Ramsay burned some steaks learning his craft.</p>

<p>Will I be back? Probably. At 15 years old, Yuyang's Badminton Bistro is still working out the kinks in their service model. The potential is there - you can see it in flashes - but right now it's the kind of place where you might get an incredible meal or walk away wondering what just happened. Reservations recommended for when she hits her stride in a few years.</p>
