<p>★★☆☆☆ Visited Rubie Liu's Badminton Bistro multiple times over the past few years and wow, what a rollercoaster of inconsistency. The 2023 Northwest ORC experience was actually decent - finally got the GD U17 title I ordered after waiting forever. But then I made the mistake of returning for the U.S. Junior Nationals that same year and they completely botched my order in the finals. Left hungry and disappointed.</p>

<p>The service here is wildly unpredictable. Sometimes you get an 11-4 performance like at South ORC that makes you think this place has potential, other times you're staring at a 0-3 disaster like NorCal ORC where they clearly weren't even trying. The kitchen staff (looking at you, Kathleen Li and William Ye) changes constantly - Rubie's been through more doubles partners than a speed dating event. At least Kathleen stuck around for 19 matches before presumably finding somewhere better to eat.</p>

<p>What really gets me is how this establishment went from being ranked #1 in December 2023 to completely unranked now. That's like going from Michelin starred to food truck in the parking lot of a failing mall. Sure, the 78-71 career record looks okay on paper, but that 52% success rate means you're basically flipping a coin every time you walk through those doors.</p>

<p>I keep coming back hoping they'll recapture that brief moment of glory, but after watching four finals losses pile up like overcooked rice, I'm starting to think this place peaked early and hard. The 2024 Junior Nationals visit was particularly painful - six wins felt promising until five losses reminded me why I should've just ordered takeout. Would maybe try again if they get their act together, but I'm not holding my breath.</p>
