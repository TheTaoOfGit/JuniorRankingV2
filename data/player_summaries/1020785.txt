Terence Cai has established himself as one of the most formidable boys' singles players in the U17 age group, currently ranked second nationally with 18,954 points. Born in 2011, Cai has compiled a remarkable 274-88 career record across 32 tournaments, a 75 percent win rate that speaks to his consistency at every level he has entered. His trajectory tells a story of relentless upward movement: from third in BS U13 and fourteenth in BS U15 in late 2023, he climbed to the top of BS U15 by mid-2025 before settling into his current position as the second-ranked U17 singles player. His most recent outing, the 2026 YONEX Arena SoCal ORC, saw him go 11-3 and reach the BS U17 final, where he fell to Abhiram Tripuraneni in what appears to be an emerging rivalry at the top of the age group. Rithik Rasamsetti has been another recurring nemesis, having denied Cai titles at the 2023 Junior Nationals in BS U13 and at the 2021 Nationals in BS U11.

Cai is far more than a singles specialist, however. He ranks eighth in BD U17 and eleventh in XD U17, making him a genuine triple-threat competitor. His doubles resume is anchored by a deep partnership with Michelle Yourong Zhang, with whom he has logged 43 matches, and Seth Li, his most frequent boys' doubles partner at 34 matches. The Cai-Li pairing has produced titles and finals alike, while his mixed doubles work has yielded championships at events ranging from the 2022 Northwest ORC to the 2024 ABC OLC. His eight career titles span all three disciplines, a rare versatility that few juniors can claim. With Nationals season approaching, Cai's combination of singles firepower, doubles instincts, and big-match experience makes him one of the players most likely to challenge for multiple gold medals in U17.
