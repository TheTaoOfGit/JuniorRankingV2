<p>Ah yes, the 2008 vintage Jaleesa Yu—a delightfully inconsistent offering that peaked early and has since developed some rather unfortunate characteristics. The initial nose presents bright notes of promise, with that lovely 2022 SoCal CRC double-vintage where she somehow managed to cork-pop both Girls' and Mixed Doubles U17 titles. One detects hints of partnership with the reliable Sophia Wu and Justin Kan estates, though the terroir seems to shift dramatically depending on court conditions.</p>

<p>Upon closer examination, this particular vintage exhibits troubling signs of oxidation. The 2022 NorCal and Synergy offerings were completely undrinkable—0-5 and 0-4 respectively—suggesting serious flaws in the fermentation process. While the body showed promise with that respectable 63-49 overall composition, the finish has been disappointingly flat. The 2023 Midwest final loss left a bitter aftertaste that seems to linger still.</p>

<p>Most concerning is the rapid deterioration from cellar to table. Once ranked a respectable #14 in Girls' Doubles U17, this vintage has now completely fallen off the charts—literally unranked, as if the sommelier association has collectively decided to pretend it never existed. The aging process has been unkind, with her U19 rankings plummeting faster than a cheap Beaujolais in August heat.</p>

<p>In conclusion, the Yu vintage represents a cautionary tale of early promise squandered through inconsistent handling. While there were moments of brilliance—particularly that 2022 harvest—I'm afraid this bottle is best left unopened until further aging reveals whether there's any hidden complexity beneath that increasingly sour finish. Pairs well with modest expectations and a healthy dose of patience.</p>
