<p>Welcome, fellow hikers, to the Pradyun Rasamsetti Memorial Trail—a winding path through junior badminton that perfectly captures the essence of getting lost despite having a map. This moderately challenging 25-tournament trek begins in 2021 and meanders through various regional championships with the determination of a hiker who keeps insisting they know where they're going while clearly heading deeper into the wilderness.</p>

<p>Our trail starts promisingly enough at the 2021 USAB South Open, where young Pradyun managed a respectable 1-2 record—think of it as successfully setting up camp but forgetting to bring matches. The path then winds through a scenic detour at the 2022 NorCal ORC, where he achieved the rare feat of a perfect 0-2 record, demonstrating that sometimes the journey really is more important than the destination. Like a true trail enthusiast, he collected partners along the way—Nathan Chien, Afraz Muhammed Baig, and even family member Rithik Rasamsetti—because nothing says "I'm confident in my abilities" like needing to recruit your own relatives.</p>

<p>The trail reaches its singular summit at the 2023 South CRC, where Pradyun finally planted his flag with a Boys Singles U17 title—a moment as rare and beautiful as spotting a unicorn, if unicorns existed and had a 45% win rate. This peak experience was followed immediately by a steep descent into the valley of the 2023 NE OLC finals loss, proving that what goes up in badminton must come down, preferably with style but often with the grace of a tourist in hiking boots on wet rocks.</p>

<p>The trail concludes at present day, where our intrepid explorer finds himself unranked—a testament to the circular nature of hiking trails and badminton careers. After 64 wins and 78 losses across this scenic route, Pradyun has achieved what many thought impossible: making a sport played with feathered projectiles somehow less graceful than it sounds. Remember to pack extra water, comfortable shoes, and perhaps lower expectations when attempting this trail yourself.</p>
