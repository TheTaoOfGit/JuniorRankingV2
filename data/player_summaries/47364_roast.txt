<p>Michael Xu (born 2005) is an American badminton player best known for his uncanny ability to reach finals and then immediately forget how to play the sport.[citation needed] Despite maintaining a respectable 54% win rate across eight tournaments, Xu has achieved the remarkable feat of converting exactly zero of his three finals appearances into actual victories, leading some experts to theorize that he may be allergic to trophy ceremonies.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Xu's tournament career began with promise at the 2021 USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships, where he posted an impressive 18-9 record before losing the U17 Boys Doubles final, establishing what would become his signature move of "getting tantalizingly close to success and then not."[citation needed] His performance at the 2021 SoCal ORC, where he achieved a perfect 0-4 record, has been described by badminton historians as "a masterclass in how to pack your bags efficiently."[citation needed]</p>

<p>In doubles play, Xu has demonstrated remarkable commitment to the "try everyone until something works" strategy, cycling through partners like a speed dating event gone wrong.[citation needed] His most frequent collaborator, Neil Ganguly, endured 26 matches with Xu, though court records suggest Ganguly may have requested a restraining order after their partnership ended.[citation needed] Xu's brief three-match stint with Samuel Jiaoyang Li was reportedly terminated when Li discovered that Xu's idea of "communication" involved interpretive dance.[citation needed]</p>

<p>By December 2023, Xu had achieved rankings of #63 in U19 Boys Doubles, #86 in U19 Boys Singles, and #86 in U19 Mixed Doubles, placing him in the exclusive "top 100 but not really" category that junior players' parents love to mention at dinner parties.[citation needed] Despite his current unranked status, Xu continues to compete, presumably in the hope that one day he will remember what to do when handed a trophy.[citation needed]</p>
