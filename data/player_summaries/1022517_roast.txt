<p>What if I told you that in the hallowed halls of Southern California badminton, there exists a player whose mere presence at a tournament bracket sends opponents into fits of overconfidence? This is the story of Swanika Liang, a competitor so consistently generous with victories that charity organizations should issue her tax receipts.</p>

<p>The year was 2022. A young Swanika stepped onto the SoCal CRC courts with dreams as high as her shuttlecock trajectory—which, according to witnesses, was often straight into the net. That inaugural 1-4 record would prove to be the high-water mark of what experts now call "The Liang Era." But Swanika wasn't finished. Oh no, she was just getting started.</p>

<p>Enter 2023: the Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, where Swanika achieved something truly remarkable—a perfect 0-3 record, the kind of mathematical precision that would make NASA engineers weep with envy. Not content with mere failure, she followed this masterpiece with a 2-6 showing at the YONEX SoCal CRC, proving that sometimes lightning does strike twice, and sometimes it strikes your shuttlecock directly into the floor.</p>

<p>Through it all, a rotating cast of doubles partners—Zoe Sun, Fay Chao, Max Lee, Dylan Ji Daniels—learned valuable life lessons about patience, perseverance, and the importance of having a good therapist. Her mixed doubles ranking of #236 stands as a testament to her ability to make even the most optimistic partner question their life choices, while her #127 rankings in girls' events suggest that gender-specific competition might be her kryptonite's kryptonite.</p>

<p>Today, Swanika Liang sits unranked, a statistical ghost haunting the badminton databases of Southern California. But in a sport obsessed with winners, perhaps we need someone to remind us that losing, when done with such artistic consistency, is its own form of greatness. This has been the story of a player who turned defeat into an art form, one shuttlecock at a time.</p>
