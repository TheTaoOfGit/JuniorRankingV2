<p><b>0-1 in Finals and 29% Career Win Rate: Ellis Ho Is SoCal's Most Persistent Underperformer</b></p>

<p>Ellis Ho has a 21-51 career record (29%) across 15 tournaments with one runner-up finish (GD U15 at 2026 Dave Freeman Socal OLC). That's 15 events and 72 matches to produce a single meaningful result. She went 1-6 at the SoCal CRC, 0-3 at the SoCal ORC, and 3-6 at Junior Nationals, where she lost twice as many as she won.</p>

<p>Her three-game record of 3-3 (50%) is average, and her 1-1 semis record shows she can occasionally compete in elimination rounds. But the overall 29% win rate drags everything into the mud. Her primary partner Eddie Sun (20 matches) has been remarkably committed to a partnership that's produced one runner-up in 20 attempts.</p>

<p>Getting blown out by Jocelyn Chiang and Iris Kuan in singles shows her ceiling is firmly below the elite tier. She's ranked in four categories (GD U15, GS U15, XD U15, XD U17), which is the "spread your losses across multiple disciplines" approach to competitive badminton.</p>
