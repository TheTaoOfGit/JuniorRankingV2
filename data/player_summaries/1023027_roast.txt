<p>Ah yes, the 2012 Henry Tan vintage - a curiously ambitious little number that promises more complexity than it ultimately delivers. This particular specimen exhibits an intriguing bouquet of early promise, with delicate notes of BD U11 championship at the 2022 NE NJBC OLC, though one detects a certain oxidation setting in as we progress through the age categories. The initial nose suggests bold potential, but upon closer inspection, we find a wine that peaked rather early in its development cycle.</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage presents a medium-bodied 58% win rate with surprising tannins of inconsistency - smooth victories at regionals paired with the harsh bite of multiple finals losses. The 2024 NE CRC offers perhaps the most telling tasting notes: simultaneously producing both a BD U13 title and a BD U13 finals defeat, creating a peculiar contradiction that even the most seasoned sommelier struggles to explain. It's rather like drinking a wine that's both corked and brilliant in the same glass.</p>

<p>The doubles blend shows particular character when paired with the Justin Dong varietal - 27 matches of what can only be described as "enthusiastic mediocrity." The partnership produces a serviceable table wine, though one suspects it lacks the refinement for truly prestigious occasions. Recent vintages with Alaina Benjamin and Rachel Yao suggest our young vintner is experimenting with mixed doubles blending, though the results remain... educational.</p>

<p>Most concerning is the aging trajectory - where one expects improvement with time, our 2012 vintage appears to be moving backwards through the rankings like a wine that's developed an unfortunate sediment problem. From BD U13 #45 to BD U15 #133, we observe a vintage that's somehow becoming less distinguished with age. Still, at barely 14 years old, there remains hope this bottle simply needs more time in the cellar - though I wouldn't recommend serving it at any championship dinners just yet.</p>
