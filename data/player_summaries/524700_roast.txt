<p><b>5-9 in Three-Game Matches: Devaansh Surpur, the Third-Game Disaster</b></p>

<p>Devaansh Surpur is BD U11 #8 and BS U11 #10, a top-10 player who goes 5-9 (35%) in three-game matches. When it's close, he loses two-thirds of the time. His 2-6 semis record is particularly damning -- six semifinal losses for a top-10 player is the definition of underperforming on the big stage.</p>

<p>His 41-39 career (51%) is barely above .500 for a supposedly elite player. One title at the Fortius South CRC and a runner-up elsewhere is thin for someone with his ranking. Partner Avyukth Reddy Pati has been along for 8 matches, and their combined results suggest two talented players who can't quite figure out how to be great together.</p>

<p>The blowout losses to Pradhyumn Surendran and Neel Achari are especially painful -- those are his direct rivals, and they're treating him like cannon fodder.</p>
