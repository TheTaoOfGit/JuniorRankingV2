<p><b>9-12 in Three-Gamers Despite 67% Win Rate: Mokshagna Surpur Dominates Weak Fields, Crumbles Against Real Competition</b></p>

<p>Mokshagna Surpur has 7 titles and a 67% win rate, which looks great on paper — until you see that 9-12 three-game record (42%). When matches actually get competitive, this kid loses more often than he wins. He's a front-runner who steamrolls the early rounds and then wilts like an overwatered plant when someone fights back.</p>

<p>His 7-6 finals record means he's lost nearly half his championship matches. His doubles partner Amogh Lanka has endured 47 matches with him — that's basically a full-time job. They've won together, they've lost together, and at this point they probably finish each other's sentences and each other's chokes.</p>

<p>He's ranked in five different categories across U13 and U15, which just means he enters everything and loses to the best in all of them. Getting blown out by Vignan Chandrasekaran and Melvin Cui shows the real contenders have his number. BS U15 #27 is nice, but when Adhip Sundar blows you off the court, the ranking feels decorative.</p>
