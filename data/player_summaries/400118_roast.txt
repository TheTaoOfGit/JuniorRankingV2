<p>Welcome, hikers, to the Rucha Rajadhyax Memorial Trail, a challenging 29% grade that's been disappointing outdoor enthusiasts since 2021. This particular path starts promisingly at the Egret OLC Trailhead, where our young explorer immediately took a wrong turn and plummeted into a 0-2 ravine, setting the tone for what naturalists call "a consistently downward trajectory."</p>

<p>As we continue along this winding route, you'll notice the frequent partner-swapping stations - first Nethmi Hewawitharana for 8 grueling miles, then Arthur Yan for another 8, followed by Anna Choi for a brief 5-mile stretch. Local guides theorize that Rucha's hiking companions keep mysteriously disappearing, though whether they're lost in the wilderness or simply found better trails remains unclear. The scattered remains of partnerships with Cali Bennett and Arunabh Ganguli serve as cautionary markers for future expeditions.</p>

<p>The trail's most treacherous section runs through the notorious SPBA MW ORC canyon, where hikers regularly experience a devastating 1-8 altitude drop. Legend has it that many who attempt this passage emerge speaking in whispers about "the great descent." Similarly challenging is the MBC CRC overlook, offering spectacular views of another 1-5 tumble down the mountainside.</p>

<p>Our journey concludes at the Midwest OLC vista, where despite years of experience, our trail remains stubbornly at base camp elevation - currently unranked and showing no signs of reaching the summit. Park rangers recommend this route only for masochists and those seeking a humbling reminder that not all paths lead upward. Please remember to pack extra first-aid supplies and perhaps a map to literally any other trail in the system.</p>
