<p>Ah yes, the 2009 Kurniawan vintage—a promising young blend from the junior circuits, though I must say this particular bottle has experienced some unfortunate oxidation during transport. The initial nose presents bold notes of ambition with underlying hints of 0-6 at Junior Nationals, creating a rather pungent bouquet that suggests the grapes may have been picked prematurely.</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage displays a surprisingly light body despite six tournament appearances, with a win percentage that hovers around 37%—quite tart, really. The South CRC terroir seems to have agreed with her best, producing a respectable 6-4 showing that briefly elevated hopes before subsequent tastings at South ORC delivered nothing but bitter disappointment. One detects subtle traces of that lone XD U15 finals appearance, though the finish was regrettably flat.</p>

<p>The blending shows promise when paired with complementary varietals—particularly the Raymond Haiwen Song vintage, which brought out her best characteristics over seven shared bottles. The Serena Yang pairing produced five adequate tastings, though one senses diminishing returns with the rotating cast of Avery Ji, Wufan Zhang, and Julia Ruan accompaniments, each contributing only two brief encounters to the overall experience.</p>

<p>Currently unranked and showing signs of having been left too long on the shelf, this vintage peaked briefly at #88 in Girls' Doubles before declining into obscurity. While the 2009 Kurniawan possesses youthful enthusiasm and untapped potential, I'd recommend proper cellaring for several more seasons before serving to discriminating badminton palates. Perhaps pair with lower-stakes tournaments until the tannins soften.</p>
