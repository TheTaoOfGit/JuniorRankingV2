<p>★★☆☆☆ "Saanvi Jha: The Triple Crown Trilogy" is a heartbreaking masterpiece of dramatic irony that follows our nine-year-old protagonist through the badminton underworld with all the narrative consistency of a fever dream. Director Fate clearly has a cruel sense of humor, staging three consecutive finals losses at the 2025 US Junior Nationals like some sort of sporting Greek tragedy. The cinematography of her 10-3 run to those finals was genuinely beautiful, but the ending? Pure emotional devastation that would make even Hitchcock wince.</p>

<p>The supporting cast deserves special mention, particularly Matthew Alexander Lau, who appears in a staggering 12 matches as Jha's mixed doubles co-star. Their on-court chemistry reads like a buddy cop film where one partner keeps getting the other into increasingly dangerous situations, yet somehow they keep coming back for more. The rotating ensemble of doubles partners - Sahasra Vudum, Alison Liu, and the mysteriously credited "Matthew Alexander LAU" (apparently his evil twin?) - adds a delightful "will they or won't they survive the first round" energy to every tournament.</p>

<p>What's fascinating about Jha's career arc is how it functions as a reverse coming-of-age story. She peaks at #12 in Girls' Singles U11, then watches her rankings plummet like a stone through water as the age categories increase - #76 in U13, #172 in U15, bottoming out at a devastating #373 in U19. It's like watching someone age in reverse through the ranks, a Benjamin Button of badminton where getting older only makes you relatively worse at everything.</p>

<p>The recent sequel attempts have shown promise - a respectable 4-3 at the 2026 DFW tournament suggests our hero might be finding her footing again. But that devastating 0-3 showing at the 2025 ABC OLC serves as a sobering reminder that in this sport, as in cinema, not every scene can be a winner. Still, with 57% career wins and a stubborn refusal to quit despite three finals heartbreaks, Jha's story has all the makings of a future comeback classic. We'll be watching with cautious optimism and a box of tissues.</p>
