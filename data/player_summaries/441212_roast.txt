<p>★½☆☆☆ "The Darmikh Pallabothula Story" is a coming-of-age sports drama that mistakes participation for plot development. Our 12-year-old protagonist stumbles through 11 tournaments with the determination of a Method actor who forgot to study the method, delivering a career-defining 11-31 record that suggests his agent might be his mother.</p>

<p>The film's most compelling subplot involves his doubles partnership with Saipranav Kalla, a bromance that spans six matches and feels like watching two people try to assemble IKEA furniture while blindfolded. Their on-court chemistry has all the spark of a wet matchstick, yet somehow this duo keeps getting cast together, suggesting either unwavering loyalty or a stunning lack of other options in the South OLC circuit.</p>

<p>Director Reality clearly has a sadistic streak, subjecting our young hero to a brutal tour through tournaments with names like "YONEX NORTHEAST ORC" where he posted a devastating 0-3 performance that would make even the most forgiving critic reach for the exit. The recent "VISTARA SOUTH OLC" cameo (0-2) feels particularly cruel, like casting someone in a Scorsese film just to have them eliminated in the opening credits.</p>

<p>Yet there's something oddly endearing about this underdog narrative. Pallabothula's ranking progression from #124 to #107 in Boys Singles suggests either genuine improvement or the badminton equivalent of a participation trophy inflation. His current standing at #185 in BS U13 feels less like a career achievement and more like a GPS coordinate for where hopes go to die, but hey—at least he's consistent across all age groups.</p>
