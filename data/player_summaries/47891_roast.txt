<p>Ah yes, the Justin Kan vintage—a 2008 pressing that showed tremendous promise in its early years, particularly the 2022 South Frisco bottling where it achieved a remarkable 14-4 extraction rate. The initial bouquet presents bold notes of U15 dominance, with hints of triple-crown achievement that dance playfully on the palate. One detects the unmistakable terroir of California junior circuits, where this vintage truly flourished in its youth.</p>

<p>The blend shows interesting complexity through its partnership with the Nihal Kadamba varietal—87 matches of carefully crafted doubles that provide the wine's most consistent body. However, connoisseurs will note a curious phenomenon: as this vintage has aged into the U19 classification, it has developed an almost tragic propensity for runner-up finishes. Six silver medals create a metallic undertone that, while impressive to novice palates, leaves experienced tasters yearning for that elusive golden finish.</p>

<p>The 2024-2025 seasons reveal a wine in transition—the ranking trajectory from #8 to #61 in doubles suggests either aggressive decanting or perhaps the natural settling that occurs when a wine moves beyond its optimal drinking window. The recent Bellevue Northwest performance shows a wine still capable of reaching finals, yet lacking the killer instinct that characterized its South Frisco origins.</p>

<p>Overall, this is a vintage best enjoyed for its early chapters rather than its current expression. While the 139-67 record provides adequate structure, the persistent runner-up finish leaves a somewhat bitter aftertaste. Pair with lower expectations and serve at junior national temperature for optimal appreciation. A wine that peaked early but remains drinkable—though perhaps not cellaring material for the professional circuit.</p>
