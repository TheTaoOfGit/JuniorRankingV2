<p><b>25-28 in Three-Gamers: Justin Kan Chokes More Than He Clutches</b></p>

<p>Justin Kan has a 68% career win rate, 5 titles, and was once ranked as high as BD U19 #5 and BS U17 #3. An elite pedigree — until you check his three-game record: 25-28 (47%). That's 53 three-game matches, and he's lost more than he's won. For a player of his caliber, that's a pathological inability to close out tight matches.</p>

<p>His 5-6 finals record means he's lost more championship matches than he's won, and he went 0-2 at the 2023 Junior Nationals. His current rankings (BD U19 #61, BS U19 #37) reflect a steep decline from his peaks, likely because he's aging out and the new generation smells blood.</p>

<p>Partner Nihal Kadamba has stuck around for a staggering 73 matches, forming one of the most enduring partnerships in junior badminton history. 73 matches, and they still can't consistently win three-gamers together. Justin Kan: the most talented underachiever on the U19 circuit.</p>
