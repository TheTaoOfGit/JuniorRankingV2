<p><b>1-11: Howard's Law of Diminishing Returns</b></p>

<p>Howard Law has played four tournaments and won exactly one match. One. His 8% win rate could be a typo for his age if he weren't born in 2017. He went 0-3, 0-1, 1-4, and 0-3 -- a collection of results that reads less like a career and more like a series of unfortunate events.</p>

<p>The blowout losses are a who's who of players who used Howard as target practice: Neel Achari, Aaditya Lanka, Daniel Xiao, and Matthew Alexander Lau. He does have a 50% three-game record (1-1), which technically makes three-gamers his best format. Unfortunately, his opponents seem determined not to let it get that far.</p>

<p>Anvitha Krishnan has been his partner for five of eleven matches. That's not a doubles partnership, that's a shared trauma bond.</p>
