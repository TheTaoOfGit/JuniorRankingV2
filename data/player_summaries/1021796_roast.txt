The initial bouquet presents bold notes of U11 dominance, with a delightfully aggressive 13-1 finish at the 2022 NorCal ORC that suggested this young vintage might age magnificently. However, like a Beaujolais nouveau that peaked too early, our subject has since developed some troubling complexity.</p>

<p>The palate reveals a fascinating contradiction—while the singles expression maintains a respectable BS U15 #17 ranking with 4899 points, the doubles blend tells a more nuanced story. Paired with the reliable Sophia Li terroir across 33 matches, this vintage shows remarkable consistency, though one detects hints of over-dependence that may limit long-term cellaring potential. This suggests a vintage that may have been bottled too early, with insufficient tannins to withstand the pressure of aging up through the junior divisions. <p>In conclusion, the Prathik Nemani 2012 remains an intriguing vintage for collectors of junior badminton prospects, though I'd recommend immediate consumption rather than extended cellaring.
