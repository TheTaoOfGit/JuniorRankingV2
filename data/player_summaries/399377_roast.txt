<p>⭐⭐ "Kelly Hu's Badminton Experience" - Would not recommend for special occasions</p>

<p>Came to Kelly Hu's establishment with high hopes after hearing she made it to a GD U13 finals back in 2021 at the Egret OLC, but what a disappointment that turned out to be. The service was inconsistent at best - sure, she had that one decent performance at the 2024 Midwest CRC (7W-3L), but most visits left me hungry for more. The 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals was particularly brutal - went 0W-6L, which is like ordering the chef's special and getting six consecutive food poisoning incidents.</p>

<p>The doubles menu is extensive - she's worked with everyone from Zoey Tian to Samuel Burton to Aaron Fu - but the execution never quite delivers on the promise. It's like she keeps changing business partners hoping someone will finally make the recipes work. The atmosphere feels scattered, and you can tell she's still figuring out her signature dishes after 14 different restaurant locations.</p>

<p>What's really concerning is the decline in quality over time. Back in early 2025, she was running a respectable operation (GD U17 #39, GS U17 #39), but recent visits show she's dropped to #326 and #399 in her age group. That's like going from a solid neighborhood bistro to whatever's left open at 2 AM in a strip mall. The 39% win rate means you're literally more likely to leave unsatisfied than happy.</p>

<p>Look, Kelly's got potential - she's still young and clearly passionate about the badminton hospitality business. But until she figures out how to consistently deliver results instead of just showing up, I'll be taking my tournament dining dollars elsewhere. Maybe try again in a few years when she's had more time to perfect her craft.</p>
