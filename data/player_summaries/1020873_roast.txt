<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Anson Chen's Badminton Bistro expecting a solid meal, but left feeling like I'd been served a participation trophy with a side of false hope. The establishment has been operating since 2021, and while the ambiance shows promise, the execution is about as consistent as a broken shuttlecock machine - you never know if you're getting a winner or watching it sail into the net.</p>

<p>The chef clearly has his go-to sous chef in Tsun Him Lam (they've collaborated on 8 dishes together), but even their signature combo can't seem to nail the recipe for success. I tried the "Junior National Championships Special" in 2022 - came out 6-6, which is restaurant speak for "we have no idea what we're doing but at least we showed up." The "Dave Freeman Jr. OLC Final" was actually quite promising (made it to the championship round!), but they choked harder than someone trying to swallow a whole birdie.</p>

<p>Service has been spotty across all locations - absolutely brutal 0-4 showing at the SoCal ORC location in 2021, though they seem to have figured out how to at least break even at most spots since then. The kitchen staff keeps rotating (Benson Liang, Savannah Kam, Yalan Zhu all taking turns), which explains why nothing tastes the same twice. Currently sporting a 22-29 lifetime record, which in restaurant terms means "your food arrives cold more often than hot."</p>

<p>Look, this place has potential - anyone who can make it to a U15 final clearly knows where the kitchen is located. But with rankings that peaked at #91 in boys doubles (think "we're technically listed in the phone book"), you're rolling the dice every time you walk through those doors. Come for the heart, stay because you're too polite to leave before the check arrives. Maybe try again in a few years when they've had time to perfect their craft.</p>
