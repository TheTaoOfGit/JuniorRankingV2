<p>Yichen Li has emerged as one of the most versatile and determined competitors in junior badminton, seamlessly competing across age groups with remarkable consistency. At just 15 years old, he's already established himself in both U17 and U19 divisions, currently ranked #115 in BS U17, #106 in BS U19, and an impressive #83 in BD U17. His ability to maintain competitive rankings across multiple categories while still being one of the younger players in these divisions speaks to his exceptional talent and work ethic.</p>

<p>The young athlete's breakthrough moment came early in his career with a dominant doubles title at the 2022 NW Oregon OLC in BD U13, where he also reached the singles final. More recently, Li demonstrated his continued growth by advancing to the BS U19 final at the 2025 DAVE FREEMAN JR. SOCAL OLC, a remarkable achievement that showcased his ability to compete with players up to four years older. His 6-6 record at the prestigious 2024 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS further proves he belongs among the nation's elite junior players.</p>

<p>Li's ranking trajectory tells a story of steady, impressive improvement. His boys' singles rankings have shown particularly strong growth, climbing from #46 in U15 to #49 in U17 and #81 in U19, demonstrating his ability to adapt and excel as he faces older, more experienced opponents. In doubles, his partnerships have been equally impressive, particularly his 23-match collaboration with Cynthia Wang and 20-match partnership with Clay Yang, showing his ability to build chemistry and succeed in team-oriented play.</p>
