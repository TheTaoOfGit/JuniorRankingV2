<p><b>9-15 With Zero Finals: The Forever Middle of the Pack</b></p>

<p>Bingru Huang is the platonic ideal of "fine." Five tournaments, 37% win rate, zero finals, zero semis. She exists in that uncomfortable zone where she wins just enough to keep showing up but never enough to actually threaten anyone important. Her 66% three-game record is genuinely decent, but it's hard to celebrate clutch play when you never clutch your way to a meaningful match.</p>

<p>The move from U11 to U13 hasn't changed the narrative. At the 2026 YONEX Arena SoCal ORC she went 0-3, which is a bold way to introduce yourself to a new age group. Blowout losses to Deepika Vaishnavi Venkatraman, Eesha Vikram, and Royi Huang (no relation, presumably, but the name similarity makes it funnier) round out the picture.</p>

<p>Five different doubles partners across five tournaments. Bingru is the badminton equivalent of a blind date -- everyone tries once, nobody comes back.</p>
