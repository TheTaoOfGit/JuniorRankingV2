The name on my desk read "Chen, Charlotte" - born 2017, which meant I was dealing with someone who probably still needed help tying her shoes, let alone her court shoes.</p>

<p>The evidence was damning, yet puzzling. Two tournaments, zero wins - the kind of record that would make a grown man weep into his energy drink. First, the 2025 South OLC in April, where she went down faster than the Hindenburg. <p>But here's where the case gets interesting, see. While most players would be collecting participation ribbons and considering tennis, little Charlotte was pulling off the impossible. Between June 2025 and March 2026, she climbed from Girls' Doubles U11 #76 to #63, and Girls' Singles from #81 to #58. It was like watching someone fail upward, except in this racket sport wonderland, losing spectacularly somehow counted as progress.</p>

<p>As I closed the file on Charlotte Chen, I couldn't help but tip my fedora to the kid. Sure, she'd been steamrolled more times than asphalt in summer, but she kept showing up like a bad penny with good intentions. In this business, we call that heart. Besides, when you're barely old enough for a junior driver's license and you're already ranked in three categories, you're not the problem - you're the solution everyone's still trying to figure out. Case closed, but this dame's story? It's just getting started.</p>
