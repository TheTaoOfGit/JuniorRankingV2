<p><b>25% Three-Game Rate and 1-3 in Finals: Kaishi Yang Loses Close Matches at an Alarming Rate</b></p>

<p>Kaishi Yang has a 4-12 three-game record (25%), meaning he loses three-quarters of his close matches. That's not a clutch problem -- that's a structural failure. His 1-3 finals record and 4-3 semis record show he can occasionally compete in elimination rounds, but the three-game curse follows him everywhere. One title (BD U13 at 2026 Seattle NW CRC) and three runner-ups paint a picture of a player who's always close but can't seal the deal.</p>

<p>His 56% career win rate (64-49) is solid, making the 25% three-game rate even more puzzling. He wins matches handily in two games but falls apart the instant it goes to a decider. His partner Kira Chu (16 matches) and Nina Kuan (12 matches) have been along for these third-game collapses.</p>

<p>He went 6-4 at the SoCal ORC and 6-3 at the Midwest ORC, proving he can compete at the ORC level. But his blowout losses to Vignan Chandrasekaran and David Zhang in XD U13 reveal he has a mixed doubles weakness that no amount of singles and doubles success can paper over.</p>
