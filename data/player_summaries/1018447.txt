Charity Lam sits atop U.S. junior badminton in a way few players manage, holding the number one ranking in both Girls Singles U19 and Girls Doubles U19 as of March 2026. The dual crown is the culmination of a steady three-year climb: in December 2023 she was ranked number 18 in GS U17 and number three in GD U17, and by mid-2025 she had broken into the top ten in both U19 categories before ascending to the summit. Her 165-80 career record across 29 tournaments reflects a player who has logged serious mileage on the national circuit, and her 2025 season was nothing short of spectacular -- she captured the Girls Singles U19 title at Junior Nationals by defeating Lydia Chao, then paired with a partner to win the Girls Doubles U19 national championship as well. Titles at the 2025 Midwest ORC, 2025 South CRC, and the 2024 Northeast ORC further padded a resume that now boasts eight career finals victories.

Lam's game is defined by her ability to compete at the highest level in all three disciplines. Beyond her singles and doubles crowns, she ranks number five in Mixed Doubles U19, having won XD titles at the 2025 South CRC, the 2024 Northeast ORC, and the 2024 South OLC. Her most enduring partnership has been with Lydia Chao in doubles, a pairing that has logged 28 matches together -- though the two have also faced each other in singles finals, adding a fascinating layer of rivalry to their collaboration. Suraj Rajesh Kumar has been her primary mixed doubles partner with 24 matches shared, while Ansen Liu has stepped into that role more recently with 17 matches. Lam's recent form shows a player still hungry despite her top ranking: a 9-2 record at the 2026 DFW South ORC produced a doubles title, even as a singles final loss to Vivian Feng reminded her that challengers are always close behind. At seventeen, she has already accomplished what most juniors spend entire careers chasing, and her consistency across disciplines makes her the standard-bearer for the girls' game in the United States.
