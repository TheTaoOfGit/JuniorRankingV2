<p>Welcome hikers to the Chloe Ho Trail, a challenging 12-tournament expedition through the treacherous terrain of junior badminton that's somehow managed to maintain a 72% success rate despite several questionable route choices. Our journey begins in 2021 at the NorCal CRC basecamp, where young Chloe first learned that going 4-2 is like completing a nature walk—technically an accomplishment, but nothing to write home about.</p>

<p>As we ascend to the more demanding peaks of her career, you'll notice the trail is well-marked with victories, particularly around the U19 Girls' Doubles summit where she's practically set up a permanent camp. The Ella Yishan Lin Memorial Partnership spans an impressive 21 matches of this trail—think of it as Chloe's favorite hiking buddy who's been dragging her up mountains since they figured out that two rackets are better than one. The scenic overlook at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals offers breathtaking views of what happens when you win Girls' Doubles but somehow stumble off a cliff in Mixed Doubles finals.</p>

<p>Trail conditions become particularly interesting around the Isaac Yang Junction, where our protagonist discovered that switching hiking partners every few tournaments is like changing boots mid-hike—sometimes it works, sometimes you get blisters. The Mihir Raja and Jeffrey Chang rest stops each lasted exactly 10 matches, suggesting Chloe treats doubles partners like energy bars: consume quickly, discard when empty.</p>

<p>The most puzzling landmark on this trail remains the Current Ranking Vista Point, where despite reaching #1 in two categories just over a year ago, Chloe now enjoys the serene view of being completely unranked—a zen-like state that's either transcendent enlightenment or what happens when you graduate from junior competition and discover the adult tour is basically switching from bunny slopes to black diamonds.</p>
