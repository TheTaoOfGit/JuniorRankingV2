<p>⭐⭐⭐ Harry Ng's Badminton Bistro - Mixed experience, would maybe return</p>

<p>Went to Harry's place last weekend after hearing he was the hottest new spot since 2024. The ambiance was promising - clean courts, decent lighting - but honestly the menu is pretty limited. Like, REALLY limited. Dude's only been open since the 2024 NORTHWEST CRC and hasn't expanded since. One location, one weekend, that's it. I've seen food trucks with more extensive operations.</p>

<p>The doubles special with Daniel Seungchan Park was actually fire though - they absolutely demolished the competition in the U17 category. Perfect chemistry, great presentation, left everyone satisfied. These two clearly know how to work together in the kitchen. But then Harry tried to go solo with the singles entrée and... yikes. Made it all the way to the U19 finals which got my hopes up, but then completely choked when it mattered. Like ordering the chef's special and getting served a participation trophy.</p>

<p>Look, a 5-1 record sounds impressive until you realize it's literally his entire career. It's like a restaurant bragging about their perfect health inspection when they've only been inspected once. Sure, an 83% success rate is solid, but my guy needs to expand beyond one tournament before I'm writing five-star reviews. Currently unranked and it shows - this place has potential but needs more time in the business before becoming a destination spot.</p>

<p>Will I be back? Maybe when he opens a second location. But for now, Harry's Badminton Bistro feels more like a promising pop-up than an established restaurant. Great when he's on, but the sample size is smaller than their portion sizes.</p>
