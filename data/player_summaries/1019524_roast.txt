<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Visited Abraham Xu's Badminton Bistro during the 2022 Junior Nationals and boy, was I disappointed. Ordered the "Championship Special" but got served a lukewarm 4-7 record instead. The service was inconsistent - sometimes you'd get a decent meal like at SoCal CRC (4-1, not bad!), but then they'd completely botch your order like at NorCal ORC where I left hungry and 0-3. Management clearly has no idea what they're doing.</p>

<p>The doubles menu is where things get really confusing. They keep pairing Abraham with Matthew Li and Ashton Reyes Prieto like they're some kind of signature combo, but honestly? It's like ordering surf and turf and getting fish sticks with hamburger helper. Sure, they managed to win that XD U19 title at SoCal Arena CRC, but let's be real - that's like winning "Best Gas Station Sushi" at a strip mall food court.</p>

<p>What really gets me is the false advertising. This place claims to be a "rising star" establishment, but their current ranking is literally "unranked" - that's like a restaurant not even being listed in the phone book. I mean, Abraham peaked at #94 in singles and #97 in doubles, which in badminton terms is basically being the 94th best McDonald's in your state. Technically an achievement, I guess?</p>

<p>Look, I'll give credit where it's due - a 55% win rate isn't terrible for a junior player, and those two U19 titles show potential. But when you're bouncing between 6-1 performances and 0-3 disasters faster than a short-order cook on Red Bull, maybe it's time to reassess the menu. Might come back in a few years to see if the chef has figured things out, but for now, I'm sticking to more reliable establishments.</p>
