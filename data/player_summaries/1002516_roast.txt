<p>Ah yes, the 2008 vintage Prutha Ramaprakash - a delightfully inconsistent blend that opens with promising aromatics but delivers a surprisingly tart finish. Upon first inspection at the 2021 SoCal ORC, this young vintage exhibited bold ambition with notes of 3-5 defeat, followed by an intriguing complexity at the 2024 US Junior Nationals where she managed a perfectly balanced 7-7 record - not quite acidic enough to be undrinkable, yet lacking the structure for true excellence.</p>

<p>The body reveals fascinating partnerships, particularly the dominant Ansen Liu blend that appears in no fewer than 23 pairings - clearly this sommelier knows which grape to lean on when crafting her doubles portfolio. There are hints of Kayosh Kumar Mohapatra (appearing twice in different spellings, perhaps indicating some confusion in the cellar), with supporting notes of Justin Han and Reina Subramanian that add modest depth but fail to elevate the overall composition.</p>

<p>What's particularly amusing about this vintage is its remarkable ability to maintain mediocrity across multiple terroirs - whether it's the prestigious YONEX estates or the humble ORC vineyards, Ramaprakash consistently delivers a 44% satisfaction rate. The 2025 YONEX SYNERGY bottling was especially memorable for its complete absence of victories, a bold zero-win statement that speaks to either artistic courage or fundamental fermentation issues.</p>

<p>The finish is where this vintage truly shines in its ordinariness - 68 wins against 86 losses creates a lingering aftertaste of "almost but not quite." Her current U19 rankings (#142 GS, #62 GD, #126 XD) suggest this wine has found its proper shelf placement: not premium enough for the top tier, but certainly drinkable enough to avoid the clearance bin. Best enjoyed with modest expectations and perhaps a stronger vintage for comparison.</p>
