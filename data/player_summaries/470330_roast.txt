<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Prarthana's Badminton Bistro expecting a quaint little U11 establishment, but this place has serious delusions of grandeur. The owner, a 10-year-old with an attitude bigger than her racquet, keeps bragging about being ranked #2 in doubles when half her customers can't even see over the net. The "Arudh Sundar Special" - her signature dish with her most frequent doubles partner - is served at literally every table (20 times and counting), making you wonder if this kitchen has any other recipes.</p>

<p>The service is wildly inconsistent. One day she's serving up an immaculate 11-0 performance at the PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC like she's Gordon Ramsay, the next she's fumbling through a 1-5 disaster at the NE CRC that would make a food truck blush. And don't get me started on her "US Junior Nationals Tasting Menu" - went 6-6 like she couldn't decide if she wanted to be fine dining or fast food. Pick a lane, Prarthana!</p>

<p>What really grinds my gears is how she's expanded from a humble U11 joint to trying to compete in the U13, U15, and even U17 markets. Honey, just because you can technically serve those age groups doesn't mean you should - dropping to #155 in U17 singles is like a pizza place trying to do molecular gastronomy. Stick to what you know!</p>

<p>I'll give credit where it's due: the recent renovation has been impressive. Going from #17 to #4 in singles shows some real kitchen improvements, and that 12-1 performance at the DFW tournament was chef's kiss perfection. But until she stops treating every doubles match like it requires her bestie Arudh as sous chef, this establishment will remain inconsistently ambitious rather than consistently great. Would I come back? Probably, but only for the U11 specials.</p>
