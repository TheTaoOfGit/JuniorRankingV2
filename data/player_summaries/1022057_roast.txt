<p>Ethan Wu (born 2010) is an American junior badminton player best known for his remarkable transformation from a serial finals choker to an actual winner[citation needed]. Wu's early career was defined by an almost supernatural ability to reach tournament finals and then spectacularly combust, losing seven finals in 2022 alone at the SoCal Arena CRC, including an impressive double-header of XD U13 finals losses on the same day[citation needed]. Sports psychologists have theorized that Wu's 2022 season single-handedly funded several therapists' vacation homes[citation needed].</p>

<p>Wu's partnership with Gavin Le has become the stuff of legend, with the duo playing an astounding 39 matches together[citation needed]. Le, who presumably drew the short straw in partner selection, has shown remarkable patience in tolerating Wu's early tendency to treat finals like a participation trophy ceremony[citation needed]. Their relationship has been described by anonymous sources as "Stockholm Syndrome, but with shuttlecocks"[citation needed].</p>

<p>The turning point in Wu's career came in 2025 when he apparently remembered that winning tournaments was more enjoyable than losing them[citation needed]. His 10-1 record at the 2025 SGVBC SOCAL CRC was hailed as a "minor miracle" by observers who had grown accustomed to watching him snatch defeat from the jaws of victory[citation needed]. Wu's doubles ranking subsequently skyrocketed to BD U17 #5, leading some to question whether the ranking system had malfunctioned[citation needed].</p>

<p>Despite his doubles success, Wu's singles game remains as consistent as his early finals record—consistently mediocre[citation needed]. Currently ranked BS U17 #27, Wu has proven that while he can carry a partner, carrying himself remains a work in progress[citation needed]. Critics note that his singles strategy appears to involve hitting the shuttlecock really hard and hoping for the best, a technique that has been scientifically proven to work approximately 63% of the time[citation needed].</p>
