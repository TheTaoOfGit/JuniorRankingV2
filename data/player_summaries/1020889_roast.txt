<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Aarya Vijay's based on all the hype from 2022 when this place was supposedly the hottest spot in the U13 district. Sure, they had that legendary South Frisco service where they went 16-1 and somehow managed to win THREE different menu categories in one night, but let's talk about what's happened since the health inspector (age groups) started paying attention.</p>

<p>The signature dish here is definitely the "Vincent Chen Special" – they've served this combo 49 times and counting, which honestly feels a bit repetitive for a restaurant trying to move upmarket. Meanwhile, the "Annie Meng Appetizer" (27 servings) shows potential but lacks the same chemistry. The sommelier clearly peaked during that 2024 Junior Nationals tasting menu where they managed an 11-2 record, but let's be honest – going from dominating the kids' menu to barely cracking the top 4 in Boys' Singles while sitting at a sad #66 in Boys' Doubles? That's some serious kitchen inconsistency.</p>

<p>The most telling review came during their recent 2025 Junior Nationals experience: 3-2. Not terrible, but this is the same establishment that used to sweep entire tournaments. It's like watching a Michelin-starred chef struggle to make a decent grilled cheese. The 169-60 lifetime record looks impressive on paper, but when you realize most of those wins came from the children's menu era, you start to wonder if the head chef simply can't handle the pressure of cooking for grown-ups.</p>

<p>Look, I get it – transitioning from the kiddie pool to the deep end is tough for any restaurant. But when you're charging U17 prices while delivering U19 #66 doubles performance, customers start to notice. Would I come back? Maybe for nostalgia's sake, but I'm definitely not making reservations for any doubles dates until they figure out what happened to that 2022 magic. Three stars because the potential is clearly there, but so is the disappointment.</p>
