<p>★★☆☆☆ Went to Nathan Chien's Badminton Bistro expecting a consistent dining experience, but this place serves up more disappointment than a gas station sushi counter. The 53-55 record on the menu should have been my first warning sign – apparently this establishment operates on the "flip a coin" method of quality control.</p>

<p>The service was wildly inconsistent. One day you get the premium "Midwest ORC Special" where they somehow delivered 9 wins out of 12 orders, but then you walk into the 2022 NE CRC location and they literally couldn't complete a single order successfully (0-6, are you kidding me?). It's like they forgot how to cook between locations. The head chef Pradyun Rasamsetti seems to be the only thing keeping this place afloat, having to cover Nathan's shifts 15 different times.</p>

<p>I'll give them credit for the 2025 NE OLC grand opening where they somehow managed to win both the doubles AND mixed doubles specials in one night – clearly they hired a competent sous chef that week. But then they followed it up by choking at the YONEX Nationals finals, serving up a loss when everyone was expecting the signature dish. The inconsistency is maddening.</p>

<p>Look, the rankings show this place has improved from #112 to #83 in doubles, so maybe there's hope. But when your win percentage hovers around coin-flip territory and your customers never know if they're getting a feast or food poisoning, it's hard to recommend. Come for the occasional surprise victory, but don't expect fine dining. More like a food truck that sometimes parks in the right neighborhood.</p>
