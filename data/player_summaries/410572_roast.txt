<p>What if I told you that in the unforgiving world of junior badminton, there exists a player whose very presence on court serves as a beacon of hope... for his opponents? This is the story of Oliver Chung, a young man who has turned losing into an art form so refined, so consistent, that statisticians weep at the beauty of his 6-44 career record.</p>

<p>Born in 2007, Oliver entered the badminton scene like a meteorite - burning bright, creating a crater, and leaving everyone wondering what just happened. Across 11 tournaments from the YONEX MIDWEST ORC to the humble MBC CRC, Oliver has maintained a win percentage so low - just 12% - that MIT mathematicians have contacted him to study what they're calling "The Chung Constant."</p>

<p>But perhaps most impressive is Oliver's gift for partner rotation. Nathan Park lasted six matches. Lohith Raj Vuppala made it four. Jacob Garcia, also four. It's like musical chairs, except the music is the sound of shuttlecocks hitting the net, and when it stops, everyone realizes they're paired with Oliver. Some say he doesn't lose partners - he sets them free to discover their true potential elsewhere.</p>

<p>Yet here's where the story takes an unexpected turn: somehow, impossibly, Oliver's rankings have actually improved. From #158 to #110 in boys doubles, from #126 to #97 in boys singles. It defies logic, physics, and several laws of competitive sports. Perhaps what we're witnessing isn't failure at all, but the slow-burn brilliance of a player who understands that in badminton, as in life, the real victory is showing up... even if you leave immediately afterward.</p>

<p>This is Oliver Chung: proof that in a sport played to 21 points, sometimes the most important number is simply... one more tournament.</p>
