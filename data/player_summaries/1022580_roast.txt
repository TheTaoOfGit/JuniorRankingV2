<p>Ah, what we have here is a 2010 vintage Renee Liu – a somewhat uneven offering from the junior badminton vineyards that displays the characteristic inconsistency of a player still finding her terroir. The initial 2021 NE ORC vintage was frankly undrinkable, presenting a harsh 1-5 bouquet with notes of overwhelming defeat and hints of "perhaps tennis would be better." However, like a wine that benefits from patient cellaring, this vintage has shown remarkable improvement with age.</p>

<p>The palate reveals fascinating complexity – while her singles game offers the subtle sophistication of a #48 ranking (much like a decent house wine you wouldn't be embarrassed to serve), her doubles partnership with Ariston Yu Feng Goh creates a blend that occasionally achieves genuine excellence. The 2024 SOUTH CRC represents her finest vintage to date, a magnificent 13-2 expression that swept the XD U15 field twice, though one suspects this may have been more about favorable growing conditions than inherent quality.</p>

<p>The finish, however, tells the true story – a 74-94 record that lingers on the palate like a wine with unresolved tannins. Her performances at nationals consistently deliver the disappointing aftertaste of a 3-6 showing, as if she saves her most pedestrian efforts for the most prestigious occasions. The 2025 YONEX SOUTH ORC was particularly regrettable, offering nothing but a swift 0-3 evacuation that left spectators wondering if they'd accidentally ordered the house swill.</p>

<p>Still, this vintage shows promise for future harvests. The ranking progression from #67 to #33 in girls' doubles suggests proper aging techniques are finally being employed, and her partnership portfolio continues to diversify with promising collaborations. While she may never achieve grand cru status, Renee Liu 2010 offers adequate value for casual consumption – just don't expect miracles when the pressure is on.</p>
