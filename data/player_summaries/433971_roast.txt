<p>⭐⭐ "Ksheera's Kitchen: Where Consistency Goes to Die" - Went to this establishment 14 times hoping for a satisfying meal, but left hungry 41% of the time. The 2026 CBA Midwest OLC special was absolutely phenomenal though - a perfect 6-course tasting menu with zero complaints! Unfortunately, that seems to be their only successful catering event because everything else has been... inconsistent.</p>

<p>The South ORC 2023 experience was a complete disaster - didn't even get an appetizer before being asked to leave. Same story at the Midwest ORC later that year where they served me five consecutive courses of disappointment. The chef clearly hadn't figured out the recipe yet. However, I will say the Yonex Junior Nationals 2025 showed some promise with their signature singles dish, even if the doubles platter was still undercooked.</p>

<p>Speaking of doubles, this place really struggles with their mixed offerings - currently ranked #468 in mixed cuisine, which explains why the Vedanth Menon collaboration tasted so confused. The Esha Mehta partnership shows more potential, but they've only managed 6 successful services together. At least the singles menu has improved from #86 to #44 in the GS category, so the kitchen is learning!</p>

<p>Look, this is clearly a young establishment still finding its footing. The 2009 vintage shows promise and the recent Midwest CRC performances suggest they're finally getting their act together. Would I recommend it for a special occasion? Not yet. But if you're in the neighborhood and want to watch a restaurant grow into something special, it might be worth a visit. Just don't expect the mixed menu to be ready anytime soon.</p>
