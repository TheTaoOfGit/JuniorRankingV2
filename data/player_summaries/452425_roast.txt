<p>Mihika Bapat (born 2013) is an American badminton player widely regarded as having one of the most extensively documented single-tournament careers in junior sports history[citation needed]. Bapat burst onto the scene in 2023 with a commanding 2-3 record at the NW OLC, establishing herself as a formidable force in the "players who have attended exactly one tournament" category[citation needed]. Her meteoric rise to complete anonymity has been called "inspiring" by her mother and "who?" by everyone else[citation needed].</p>

<p>Bapat's partnership with doubles specialist Revati K Gadre produced what badminton historians describe as "two matches of tennis that happened"[citation needed]. The dynamic duo's chemistry was so potent that they immediately decided never to play together again, a strategic move that some analysts believe preserved their perfect record of having played together[citation needed]. Critics argue that calling two matches a "partnership" is generous, but supporters counter that it's technically more matches than most people have played with Revati K Gadre[citation needed].</p>

<p>At her peak ranking of #150 in Girls Doubles U15 and #118 in Girls Singles U13, Bapat dominated the bottom half of regional youth rankings with the consistency of a participation trophy[citation needed]. Her current unranked status represents either a strategic withdrawal from competitive play or the natural consequence of not playing any tournaments since 2023[citation needed]. Sports scientists have noted that her 40% win rate places her firmly in the "could beat your aunt but probably not your cousin who played in high school" tier of amateur athletics[citation needed].</p>

<p>Bapat's legacy in junior badminton remains largely theoretical, though she has successfully maintained her perfect record of never losing a final by the innovative strategy of never reaching one[citation needed]. Her retirement from active competition at the age of 10 marked the end of what can only be described as a career that definitely happened[citation needed].</p>
