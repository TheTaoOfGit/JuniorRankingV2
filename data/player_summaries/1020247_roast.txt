<p>Ah yes, the Lauren Cho vintage—a delicate 2021-2023 pressing that opens with bold promises of Southern California terroir but delivers a surprisingly thin body. Upon first sip at the SoCal ORC, one detects hints of ambition with a 1-4 finish, though the palate quickly realizes this wine lacks the depth and structure for sustained consumption. The nose is pleasant enough, but the finish is regrettably short—much like her tournament runs.</p>

<p>The 2022 bottling shows interesting complexity, with appearances at both the SoCal Arena CRC and Junior National Championships creating a fascinating duality. While the Arena CRC vintage managed to squeeze out a single victorious note, the Junior Nationals expression was completely overwhelmed by the competition, offering a harsh 0-4 tannin structure that left even the most generous judges reaching for their spittoons. One appreciates the winemaker's courage in attempting such an ambitious blend, though the execution suggests perhaps more time in the cellar was needed.</p>

<p>The doubles pairings show a sommelier still searching for the perfect food match—cycling through Jordan Lau, Elle Zeng, Elise Wenxin Xu, Nathan Cao, and Ella Mak like a confused diner asking the waiter to recommend something, anything, that might complement this challenging vintage. Each pairing brings out different characteristics, though none manage to elevate the overall 12% alcohol content that defines this label's market position.</p>

<p>The 2023 vintage briefly showed promise with rankings appearing like unexpected fruit notes—GD U19 #89, GS U19 #78, XD U19 #148—suggesting this wine might finally develop some commercial viability. However, the current unranked status indicates this was merely a temporary oxidation, and the wine has returned to its natural state of being perfectly suitable for those with very modest expectations and an appreciation for the underdog story in every bottle.</p>
