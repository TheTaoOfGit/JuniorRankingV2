<p><b>Four Titles But a 50% Three-Game Record. Iniyan Arun Can Win Championships but Can't Win Coin Flips.</b></p>

<p>Iniyan Arun has four titles and a 4-2 finals record, which is genuinely impressive. But his 12-12 three-game record at exactly 50% means when matches go the distance, it's literally a coin flip whether he wins. For a player with championship hardware, that's embarrassingly random. His 6-4 semis record is decent, but his 0-6 at the 2023 Junior Nationals is the kind of catastrophic debut that follows you forever.</p>

<p>His blowout losses to Nihal Reddy Gajjala and Daiwik Reddy in BD U11 show the top players viewed him as a snack, not a rival. He went 0-3 at the 2026 SoCal ORC recently, proving that his worst habit — getting blanked at big tournaments — hasn't gone away. His 22 matches with Grace Li have produced exactly zero titles together.</p>

<p>Four titles, all from ABC OLC, OBA NW OLC, and Dave Freeman Jr. OLC — the participation-level regional circuit. Iniyan wins where it's easy and vanishes where it's hard.</p>
