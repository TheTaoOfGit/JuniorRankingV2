<p><b>BS U15 #1. 48% three-game win rate. Jason Gu: the #1 seed who can't close.</b></p>

<p>Let that sink in. The number one ranked boys singles player in U15 loses more three-game matches than he wins. 21W-22L in tight matches. That's not a top seed, that's a top seed with an asterisk the size of a shuttlecock. He's 11W-9L in finals, which is good but not dominant for someone whose ranking says he should be untouchable.</p>

<p>Jason rocketed from BS U13 #60 to BS U15 #1 in two years, which is genuinely impressive. But those blowout losses -- getting demolished by Brendan Chen, Melvin Cui, Kayden Hossain -- reveal a player who occasionally gets absolutely exposed. His partner Iris Kuan has played 38 matches with him, making her the most patient woman in junior badminton.</p>

<p>Eleven titles is real. Nine finals losses is also real. Jason Gu is a dominant player who, in the moments that define legacies, becomes aggressively mortal.</p>
