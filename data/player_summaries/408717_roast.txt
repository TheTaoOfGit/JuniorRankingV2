<p>Michael Sun (born 2014) is an American junior badminton player widely regarded as the most feared shuttlecock slinger in the U13 division, a title he has held since approximately never losing a tournament that mattered [citation needed]. Sun's meteoric rise to dominance began in 2023 when he swept the YONEX NorCal ORC with a perfect 15-0 record, presumably leaving a trail of devastated 9-year-olds in his wake who are still in therapy [citation needed].</p>

<p>Known for his ruthless efficiency on court, Sun has amassed an impressive collection of 20 titles across various age groups, though critics argue this is merely the result of showing up to tournaments where half the competition is still learning to tie their shoes [citation needed]. His partnership with Siddhi Thorat (28 matches) has been described by unnamed sources as "unstoppable" and "the reason other kids fake injuries," while his collaboration with Vignan Chandrasekaran (26 matches) allegedly caused three different tournaments to implement new rules about "excessive celebration" [citation needed].</p>

<p>Sun's dominance reached peak absurdity in 2024 when he achieved the rare feat of going undefeated (13-0) at the ABC OLC, a performance so dominant that tournament organizers reportedly considered implementing a handicap system where Sun would play with one hand tied behind his back [citation needed]. However, his career has not been without controversy, including a shocking finals loss at the 2025 YONEX SYNERGY NORCAL ORC that left spectators wondering if the 11-year-old had finally met his match or simply gotten distracted by the snack bar [citation needed].</p>

<p>Currently holding the #1 ranking in both BD U13 and XD U13 divisions with 7,087 points, Sun continues to terrorize junior badminton circuits across America, though his recent expansion into U15 and higher age groups has resulted in more humble rankings, suggesting that puberty may be his greatest opponent yet [citation needed]. His coaching staff has reportedly started a support group for the parents of children who have lost to Sun, which meets every Tuesday at the local community center [citation needed].</p>
