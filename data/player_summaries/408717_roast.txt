<p><b>CHARACTER SELECT: MICHAEL SUN — CLASS: DOUBLES SPECIALIST / MIXED DOUBLES ARCHMAGE.</b> If junior badminton were an RPG, Michael Sun would be the player who dumped every stat point into Doubles and Mixed Doubles, unlocked the #1 ranking in both BD U13 AND XD U13, then realized he'd accidentally left Singles on the default build. With a career record of 205-49 (80% win rate) across 20 tournaments, this is a max-level character in two out of three skill trees. The third one — BS U13 #21 — looks like that one ability you keep meaning to level up but never get around to because you're too busy dominating everywhere else.</p>

<p>The partner synergy stats are absurd. Siddhi Thorat (34 matches) was the original co-op partner — the Player 2 who helped clear the early-game content including the dominant 2024 South ORC where Sun went 14-0 and collected BS U11, BD U11, AND XD U11 titles in a single tournament. A 14-0 perfect run. That's not a tournament performance, that's a cheat code. Vignan Chandrasekaran (26 matches) is the current meta partner, powering the BD U13 titles at both the 2026 SOCAL ORC and the 2026 DFW South ORC. And Grace Cheng (24 matches) has become the XD partner of choice — together they've claimed XD U13 titles at those same two events, making the Sun-Cheng combo the final boss that nobody in U13 mixed doubles wants to encounter.</p>

<p>But let's talk about the singles skill tree, because this is where the build gets questionable. BS U13 #21 on a character with #1 rankings in two other categories is the gaming equivalent of a Level 99 Paladin who somehow still can't cook. The BS U15 #12 ranking is respectable — clearly someone remembered to allocate a few points there — but the runner-ups to Yian Yang at both the 2024 NORCAL ORC (BS U13) and the 2025 South ORC (BS U13) suggest Yang has figured out the counter-strategy. When the same opponent keeps beating you in the same event category, that's not bad luck — that's a matchup problem, and your build guide needs an update.</p>

<p>The Kevin Xie rivalry is this game's most compelling PvP storyline. Sun beat Xie in the BS U11 final at the 2023 Northwest ORC, then proceeded to defeat Xie's teams in the BD U13 and XD U13 finals at BOTH the 2026 SOCAL ORC and the 2026 DFW South ORC. That's four finals wins against the same rival across two tournaments — the kind of dominance that makes the other player consider rerolling their character. The BD U15 runner-up at the 2026 Bintang NorCal OLC (losing to Andy Korlann and Steven Li) is a reminder that the U15 dungeon has higher-level mobs that don't care about your U13 achievements.</p>

<p>The progression log tells the story: from BD U11 #7 and XD U11 #4 in late 2023 to double #1 rankings in U13 by March 2026. Michael Sun's character build may be hilariously lopsided — an absolute raid boss in doubles, a mid-tier DPS in singles — but in a game where you can queue for three events, being the undisputed champion in two of them is a pretty broken build. The only patch note this character needs: please, for the love of the leaderboard, invest some XP into singles before the U15 bracket arrives in force.</p>
