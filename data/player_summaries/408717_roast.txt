<p><b>BS U13 #21. BD U13 #1. Apparently Michael Sun needs a buddy system to succeed.</b></p>

<p>An 80% career win rate sounds impressive until you realize this kid has cycled through five different partners like he's swiping through a doubles dating app. Siddhi Thorat, Vignan Chandrasekaran, Grace Cheng -- everyone gets a turn carrying Michael to his BD and XD titles. But put a singles racket in his hand? He plummets from #1 to #21. That's not a ranking gap, that's a canyon with its own weather system.</p>

<p>And let's talk about those finals: 20 wins, 9 losses. Nearly a third of the time he chokes when it matters most. His 61% three-game win rate means in tight matches he's basically a coin flip with extra steps. Got blown out by his own age group peers -- Yian Yang, Harrison Zhang -- the guys he's supposedly ranked above.</p>

<p>Eight titles looks great on paper, but strip away the doubles and mixed and you're left with two lonely singles titles, both at U11. He hasn't won a singles title since he was ten. At this rate, Michael's career arc is "doubles specialist" -- which is just a polite way of saying he can't win alone.</p>
