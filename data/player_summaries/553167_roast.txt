In this city of racket swingers and net rushers, some players shine like streetlights in the fog, others flicker and fade. Karis? She was more like a blackout during rush hour.</p>

<p>Two tournaments, nine losses—the math was as cold as a January morning at the YONEX LIBC NORTHEAST ORC, where she first made her mark by not making any marks at all. 0-3, clean as a whistle, sharp as a butter knife. In Karis's case, those people were her opponents—and they were telling her exactly where she belonged: somewhere south of competitive and north of recreational.</p>
