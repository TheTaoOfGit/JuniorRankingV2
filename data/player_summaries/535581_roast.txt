<p>★★☆☆☆ Went to Anna Dai's Badminton Bistro expecting a fine dining experience, but got more of a "kids eat free" situation. The establishment is clearly still in its soft opening phase (born 2017, if you can believe the permit), and boy does it show. The service is enthusiastic but inconsistent - sometimes you get a perfectly executed girls doubles appetizer (#26 in the U11 category, not bad!), other times you're served a singles entrée that tastes like it was ranked #51 out of pure charity.</p>

<p>The menu is ambitious - they offer everything from U11 to U19 specialties - but the execution varies wildly by dish. Their mixed doubles offering is particularly questionable, currently sitting at #82 in U11 and somehow plummeting to #1049 by U19. It's like watching a soufflé collapse in real time. The chef clearly hasn't figured out how to scale recipes yet, which explains why the same 277-point seasoning tastes completely different depending on what age group ordered it.</p>

<p>I've dined here twice now - once at the YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC location and again at their U.S. Junior Nationals pop-up. Both times I left with the same experience: one satisfying dish followed by three disappointing courses. The waitstaff (shoutout to Meera Patel, who seems to be carrying this place) works hard, but you can't polish a 25% success rate no matter how much doubles chemistry you bring to the table.</p>

<p>Look, I get it - new restaurants take time to find their footing. The potential is there (that girls doubles ranking doesn't lie), and the owners are clearly passionate about what they're doing. But until Anna's Badminton Bistro figures out how to consistently deliver across all menu categories, I'd recommend sticking to their specialty dishes and maybe waiting a few years for them to work out the kinks. Come back when they've got more than 277 points worth of seasoning to work with.</p>
