<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Vedaant's Badminton Bistro expecting a fine dining experience, but left feeling like I'd been served at a mediocre chain restaurant. The establishment advertises itself as "BD U19 #51" but honestly, that's like claiming you're the 51st best pizza place in Chicago – technically an achievement, but nobody's writing home about it.</p>

<p>The service was wildly inconsistent. Some nights you get a stellar performance (like that BD U17 title at 2024 MIDWEST OLC – the one dish on the menu they actually perfected), but other times you're left wondering if the chef even showed up. Case in point: the recent disaster at Play N Thrive MW CRC where they somehow managed to lose BOTH the BS and XD finals on the same weekend. It's like ordering the surf and turf and having them burn both the lobster AND the steak.</p>

<p>Speaking of mixed doubles, their XD ranking of #371 is basically the restaurant equivalent of serving ketchup as marinara sauce. I've seen more chemistry between a vegan and a steakhouse menu than between Vedaant and his mixed doubles partners. Poor Charlotte Richards and Ksheera Karthik deserved better appetizer pairings.</p>

<p>The regulars seem to enjoy the Felix Zhang combo meal (16 matches served together), which I'll admit has some decent flavor, but the overall 28-24 record screams "we're trying our best with frozen ingredients." Sure, 54% isn't terrible, but it's not exactly Michelin star material either. Would maybe return if they were the only option in a badminton food desert, but there are definitely better establishments in the U19 district.</p>
