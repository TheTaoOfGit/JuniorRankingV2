<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Visited Medha's Badminton Bistro six times over the past few years, and I have to say, the consistency is remarkable — consistently disappointing. With a 15% success rate, this establishment has mastered the art of serving up losses with the reliability of a McDonald's ice cream machine. The signature dish appears to be "Defeat Flambé" which arrives at your table roughly 85% of the time you order.</p>

<p>The venue rotation is interesting — they've got locations at Midwest ORC (multiple visits, mixed results), Midwest OLC (batting zero), and even expanded to the prestigious U.S. Junior Nationals where they served up a perfect 0-4 shutout special. The ambiance at that location must have been particularly intimidating because our chef didn't manage to plate a single win. Props for shooting their shot at the big leagues though!</p>

<p>The doubles menu features rotating partnerships with Ava Lee, Daksha Ravindhar, and Tatthva Sharma, though none of these collaborations seem to have found their rhythm in the kitchen. It's like watching a cooking show where every episode ends with Gordon Ramsay yelling about raw chicken. The ranking has actually slipped from #102 to #170 in doubles, which suggests either the competition got stiffer or the recipe needs some serious adjustments.</p>

<p>Look, Medha's still in the early stages of building her restaurant empire, and at 15, she's got plenty of time to perfect her craft. The fact that she keeps showing up to compete despite the rough streak shows real grit — most people would have hung up their apron by now. Sometimes the best restaurants start with terrible Yelp reviews, and who knows? Maybe next year we'll be writing about her Michelin star instead of her participation trophy collection.</p>
