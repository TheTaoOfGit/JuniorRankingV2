<p>Cayden Wang burst onto the junior badminton scene with a statement-making performance at the 2021 NorCal CRC, where he claimed an impressive triple crown by winning boys singles, boys doubles, and mixed doubles titles at just 10 years old. This early display of versatility across all three disciplines set the tone for what has become a remarkable junior career spanning six years and 32 tournaments.</p>

<p>Wang's greatest strength lies in his doubles play, where he currently sits at #29 in the U17 boys doubles rankings with 10,617 points. His partnership chemistry is exceptional, particularly with Lexuan Wen, with whom he has competed in 36 matches, and Rishabh Praveen (31 matches). This collaborative approach has yielded multiple doubles titles, including back-to-back boys doubles championships at the 2023 YONEX Northwest ORC and 2023 Midwest ORC.</p>

<p>The 2023 Midwest ORC stands as Wang's career masterpiece, where he delivered a flawless 14-0 match record while sweeping all three events - boys singles, boys doubles, and mixed doubles. This perfect tournament performance exemplifies his ability to peak when it matters most, contributing to his impressive 62% career win rate (189-117) across highly competitive regional and national events.</p>

<p>Now competing at the U17 and U19 levels, Wang has successfully transitioned through age groups while maintaining his competitive edge. His current #57 ranking in U17 boys singles and top-30 position in U17 boys doubles demonstrate his continued growth as he faces older, more experienced opponents. With experience at prestigious events including the 2024 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, Wang continues to refine his game while building toward even greater achievements in the years ahead.</p>
