<p>★★☆☆☆ "The Ishan Goswami Chronicles: A Racquet Tragedy" arrives in theaters as an ambitious but ultimately uneven sports drama that follows its 11-year-old protagonist through seven tournaments of determined mediocrity. Director Reality has crafted a coming-of-age tale where our hero's 32% win rate serves as both character flaw and inexplicable source of optimism, like watching someone confidently order the fish at a gas station restaurant.</p>

<p>The film's most compelling subplot involves Goswami's revolving door of doubles partners—a casting carousel that would make even the most promiscuous Hollywood agent blush. Aadvik Vakicherla and Sai Nakshatra Prabhu each endure four matches of this partnership roulette, while Kavish Balaji and Kabir Dubey wisely limit their screen time to two matches each. It's a masterclass in diplomatic exits, like watching passengers politely decline to sit next to someone eating tuna on an airplane.</p>

<p>The climactic sequence at Seattle NW CRC provides the film's most heartbreaking moment: Goswami actually reaches a final! The BD U13 championship match unfolds with all the tension of watching someone try to fold a fitted sheet—you admire the effort, but the outcome was never really in doubt. Still, this lone final appearance towers over his resume like the Space Needle towers over the rest of Seattle's modest skyline.</p>

<p>Perhaps most remarkably, our protagonist maintains rankings across an impressive eleven different categories, spreading himself thinner than cream cheese on a Everything bagel. His XD U19 ranking of #1066 is particularly inspired—it takes real dedication to achieve four-digit obscurity while still technically being ranked. The film's greatest achievement is making us root for someone whose greatest victory was that time he went 3-4 instead of his usual 0-3.</p>

<p>While "The Ishan Goswami Chronicles" may not win any awards this season, it succeeds as an endearing portrait of youthful determination in the face of overwhelming statistical evidence. Like a participation trophy with delusions of grandeur, it's impossible not to admire the pluck, even if you wouldn't bet money on the sequel.</p>
