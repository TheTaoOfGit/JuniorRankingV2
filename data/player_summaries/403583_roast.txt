<p>Ah, the 2012 vintage Medha Pediredla—a promising young bouquet that initially showed remarkable potential with its U11 Frisco expression, delivering a surprisingly balanced 6-5 performance that suggested notes of future greatness. However, like a wine that peaked too early and has since turned, this particular vintage has undergone a rather unfortunate oxidation process, transforming from a respectable U13 #50 ranking into something more reminiscent of a box wine left open too long.</p>

<p>The palate reveals a complex blend of disappointment and persistence, with dominant notes of 0-4 defeats at the recent DFW tournament creating a rather harsh finish that lingers unpleasantly. <p>In conclusion, this vintage appears to be going through an awkward adolescent phase—much like a Beaujolais that's lost its youthful charm but hasn't yet developed the sophistication of maturity.
