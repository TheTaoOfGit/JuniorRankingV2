<p>★★☆☆☆ "Miura Goh: The Early Years" arrives as an uneven coming-of-age sports dramedy that shows flashes of promise but suffers from inconsistent pacing and a protagonist still searching for her signature style. Director Life has given us a 12-year-old lead whose 43% win rate suggests she's still learning her lines, though her recent 7-2 performance at the South CRC hints at untapped potential lurking beneath the surface.</p>

<p>The film's most devastating sequence remains the 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals chapter, a brutal 0-7 massacre that plays like a psychological horror film where our heroine gets systematically dismantled by every opponent. It's the kind of career-defining disaster that would end most biopics, but Goh's story takes an unexpectedly resilient turn with her 2025 comeback arc, featuring solid showings at Austin Leander (4-2) and Schafer SLG (4-1) that suggest our protagonist learned something from her earlier failures.</p>

<p>Where this narrative truly shines is in its ensemble casting. Goh's rotating gallery of doubles partners reads like a United Nations summit, with frequent collaborator Chaturveda Samhitha Shanigaram serving as her most reliable co-star across 12 matches. The chemistry varies wildly depending on the pairing, and the mixed doubles subplot remains painfully underdeveloped, ranking 369th in her age group like a direct-to-streaming release nobody asked for.</p>

<p>What saves this biographical effort from complete mediocrity is its authentic portrayal of a young athlete climbing the mountain one small step at a time. Goh's progression from #66 singles ranking in U11 to #115 in U15 may not scream "blockbuster," but there's something genuinely compelling about watching someone build their craft methodically. This isn't a superhero origin story—it's more like a thoughtful indie film where the real victory is simply showing up and getting better, one tournament at a time.</p>
