<p>What if I told you that in the hallowed halls of Midwest badminton, where champions are forged and legends are born, there existed a player so consistently underwhelming that his very presence on a tournament bracket became a mathematical certainty for his opponents' advancement? This is the story of Ethan Zheng, a young man who turned losing into an art form across four tournaments and thirteen soul-crushing defeats.</p>

<p>Born in 2012, Zheng burst onto the junior circuit with all the explosive impact of a wet shuttlecock. The 2023 MIDWEST OLC marked his inauspicious debut—a perfect 0-3 performance that would become his calling card. But Ethan was nothing if not persistent, returning to the same tournament in 2024 to achieve the exact same result, proving that some people really do learn nothing from history.</p>

<p>Perhaps most telling is Zheng's revolving door of doubles partners: Akshita Tripathi, Evelyn Huang, Andrew Wu, Steven Mei, and even someone named Sunny Zheng—presumably a family member who couldn't legally refuse to play with him. Like a badminton version of musical chairs, each partner seemed to discover after two matches maximum that their championship dreams lay elsewhere. Even his own apparent sibling managed just one tournament appearance alongside him.</p>

<p>The 2024 MIDWEST CRC offered a glimmer of hope—Zheng's lone tournament with a winning record at 2-1. For one shining moment, it seemed like the underdog story we all wanted to believe in. But the badminton gods are cruel, and his immediate return to form at the 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC (1-3) reminded everyone that miracles are temporary, but mediocrity is eternal.</p>

<p>Today, Ethan Zheng sits unranked in current standings, though February 2025 briefly saw him grace the rankings at a lofty #179 in boys' doubles, #128 in boys' singles, and #211 in mixed doubles. These numbers represent not just rankings, but a testament to the beautiful democracy of youth sports—where hope springs eternal, even when winning does not.</p>
