<p>Welcome to the Jocelyn Zhang Trail, a moderately confusing 28-tournament hike through the Pacific Northwest junior badminton circuit. This trail is famous for its perfectly balanced difficulty rating of 98 wins and 99 losses, making it ideal for hikers who enjoy the philosophical question of whether they're actually making progress. Rangers recommend bringing extra water, as you'll likely experience the same dehydration that comes from watching someone lose three singles finals in two years.</p>

<p>The trail begins in 2022 at the scenic NW Oregon overlook, where our young hiker first learned that 3-3 records are a way of life, not just a temporary setback. As you ascend through the age divisions, you'll notice the terrain becomes increasingly treacherous - observe how the GS rankings mysteriously hover in the mid-30s like morning fog that refuses to lift. The trail's only significant landmark is a small monument at the 2023 NW OLC, commemorating the one and only GD U15 title victory, which serves as both inspiration and a reminder of what peak performance looks like.</p>

<p>Hikers frequently report getting lost in the doubles section of this trail, where you'll encounter a rotating cast of trail companions including Shuoan Xiang and Zimeng Zhou, who've bravely attempted to guide our protagonist through 28 and 27 matches respectively. Wildlife experts note this as unusual behavior, as most experienced doubles players typically flee after witnessing someone achieve exactly 50% success rate with the mathematical precision of a Swiss timepiece.</p>

<p>The trail concludes at the 2025 Yonex U.S. Junior Nationals, where visitors can experience the full Zhang hiking experience: a perfectly balanced 6-6 record that somehow feels both triumphant and utterly predictable. Park services warn that this trail may cause existential questioning about the nature of competitive progress, though the views of consistent mediocrity are quite spectacular from this elevation.</p>
