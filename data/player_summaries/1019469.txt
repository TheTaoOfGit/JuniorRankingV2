Vincent Chen's rise through the U17 ranks has been nothing short of dramatic. As recently as December 2023, he sat at 52nd in BS U15 and 70th in BD U17, buried deep enough in the rankings that few would have predicted what came next. By March 2026, the 2010-born Chen has surged to second in both BD U17 and XD U17, fifth in BS U17, and has even cracked the U19 boys' doubles rankings at 30th. That kind of multi-discipline ascent, powered by a 227-89 career record across 34 tournaments, reflects a player who has figured out how to compete and win at increasingly elite levels. His recent form has been outstanding: a perfect 7-0 sweep at the 2024 Midwest OLC, where he claimed both the BS U19 and BD U19 titles, followed by a BD U17 championship at the 2026 SoCal ORC over Brendan Chen and Yifan Liu.

What sets Chen apart is his exceptional doubles pedigree. His primary partner, Aarya Vijay, has been his most frequent collaborator with 45 matches together, forming a pairing that has been tested at Junior Nationals and multiple ORCs. In mixed doubles, Chen has reached the finals at both the 2025 Junior Nationals and the 2025 Midwest ORC in XD U17, falling to Vivek Rai and Sasha Suman at Nationals and to Noah Chien and Ishi Reddy at the Midwest. Those near-misses suggest that a major mixed doubles title is within reach. His singles game remains potent too, with a National Championship in BS U13 from 2022 on his resume. Chen's career arc, from mid-pack anonymity to top-five status in three disciplines, marks him as one of the most improved players in the country, and at just fifteen years old, his ceiling may still be well above where he stands today.
