<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Advith Karnam's Badminton Bistro expecting a feast, but left hungrier than when I arrived. The menu promised exciting dishes like "South OLC Surprise" and "CBA Midwest Delight," but what I got was a 38% success rate that would make Gordon Ramsay weep. The chef clearly has ambition—I mean, who else would serve the same disappointing entree across U13, U15, U17, AND U19 categories simultaneously?</p>

<p>The signature dish, "827-Point Special," lacks any real flavor despite being featured prominently across multiple age groups. I watched him prepare the "2026 CBA Midwest Final," and for a moment it looked promising—he actually made it to the final course! But then he fumbled the presentation worse than a server dropping soup on a first date. His sous chef Ritvik Singh has been remarkably patient, sticking around for 6 services, though I suspect Stockholm Syndrome.</p>

<p>The restaurant's track record speaks volumes: 8 attempts at service, 23 disappointed customers, and zero five-star reviews from the trophy case. The "FORTIUS SOUTH CRC" tasting menu was particularly brutal—a complete shutout that left me questioning my life choices. Even the "YONEX DFW" appetizer was a two-course disaster that barely registered on my palate.</p>

<p>To be fair, recent visits show signs of improvement—the "VISTARA SOUTH OLC" experience delivered 4 solid courses despite 3 kitchen mishaps, and there's clear effort in the preparation. The young chef is still learning his craft, and honestly, at his age, just keeping the restaurant open is impressive. Would I recommend it to friends? Not yet. Would I come back in a few years? The potential is there, marinating slowly.</p>
