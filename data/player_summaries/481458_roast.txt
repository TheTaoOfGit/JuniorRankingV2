<p><b>7-9 in Three-Gamers Despite Being Top 30: Vishaant Harish Crumbles When Matches Go the Distance</b></p>

<p>Vishaant Harish has a 66% win rate, 2 titles, and is ranked BD U15 #27 and BS U15 #20. Impressive — until you see his 7-9 three-game record (43%). When matches go three games, this supposed top-20 player loses more often than he wins. That's like being a firefighter who's scared of smoke.</p>

<p>His 2-3 finals record means he's lost more championship matches than he's won. Getting blown out by Jacob Zhou twice (in different events!) is the kind of targeted humiliation that suggests a specific player has completely figured him out. His 5-1 semis record is excellent, which makes the finals and three-game collapses even more bewildering.</p>

<p>He went 10-1 at the 2025 Schafer SLG South OLC and then lost to Arjun Aditya Hariharan at the Austin Leander South OLC. The South region is where he feasts; the national stage is where he starves. Partner Kayden Hossain has been along for 10 matches of this Jekyll-and-Hyde act.</p>
