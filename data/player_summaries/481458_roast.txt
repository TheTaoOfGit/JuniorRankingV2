<p>⭐⭐⭐ "Vishaant's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for fine dining</p>

<p>Went to Vishaant's establishment expecting consistent quality, but this place is more unpredictable than a gas station sushi joint. Sure, he had that ONE magnificent weekend at the 2025 SCHAFER SLG SOUTH OLC where everything was perfectly seasoned (10-1 record, double titles), but don't let that fool you - it was clearly a fluke, like finding a Michelin star meal at a Denny's. The rest of the time, you're rolling the dice on whether you'll get a decent meal or straight-up food poisoning.</p>

<p>The head chef Kayden Hossain deserves credit for keeping this kitchen afloat through 10 service shifts together, because without him, this place would've closed faster than Vishaant's tournament runs at 2024 ABC OLC (0-2) and 2025 YONEX SOCAL ORC (0-1). Speaking of which, those performances were so bad they should come with a health department warning. I've seen more fight in a soggy pretzel.</p>

<p>What really gets me is the false advertising - this kid went from being ranked #279 in doubles to #27, which sounds impressive until you realize he's still getting schooled by 26 other restaurants in his age bracket. And don't even get me started on his mixed doubles game (#160) - it's like ordering the surf and turf and getting fish sticks with a side of disappointment. The poor girls he partners with (Mihika, Ashni, Sophia) deserve hazard pay for carrying that dead weight.</p>

<p>Look, I'll give credit where it's due - the boy's got a 67% win rate and has climbed the rankings faster than a health inspector fleeing a roach infestation. But until he stops serving up more finals losses than victories and learns to cook with something other than beginners' luck, I'm sticking to more reliable establishments. Two stars only because that SCHAFER performance was genuinely impressive, and because I feel bad roasting a kid who's clearly trying his best to master the recipe.</p>
