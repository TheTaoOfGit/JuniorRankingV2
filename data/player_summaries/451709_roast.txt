<p><b>0-6 at Junior Nationals, Then 4-4 Finals: Derek Huang Started From the Bottom and He's... Still Kind of There</b></p>

<p>Derek Huang's career opened with a picture-perfect 0-6 at the 2023 U.S. Junior National Championships. Six matches, six losses, zero dignity. He's since clawed his way to 4 titles and a 55% win rate across 24 tournaments, which is the badminton equivalent of going from F student to C+ student and expecting a scholarship.</p>

<p>His 4-4 finals record is the most perfectly mediocre stat in junior badminton. He wins exactly half the time when it matters most, making every final a literal coin flip. His three-game record of 21-13 (61%) is solid, but let's be real -- he's in 34 three-gamers out of 167 matches, which means a fifth of his career is spent in overtime because he can't close anyone out cleanly.</p>

<p>His most frequent partner is Seanna Yang with 38 matches together -- at this point that's not a partnership, it's a common-law marriage. Four titles split across local OLCs and CRCs means he's king of the small ponds in the Pacific Northwest. Try winning something that requires a plane ticket, Derek.</p>
