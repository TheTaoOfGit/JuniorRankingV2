<p>★★☆☆☆ "Koyu(Tara) Chou: The Rise and Fade" - In what can only be described as a cautionary tale about peaked-too-early syndrome, director Chou delivers a junior career that starts with promise before dissolving into the ether of unranked obscurity. The opening act shows real potential - a breakout performance at 2021 SoCal ORC where she swept to double gold, establishing her as a fresh face in the U19 circuit. But like so many promising debuts, the sequel syndrome quickly sets in.</p>

<p>The middle act becomes a frustrating exercise in "almosts" and "what-ifs," with Chou racking up an impressive seven finals losses that feel like watching the same tragic ending on repeat. Her 2022 SPBA MW ORC performance stands out as her magnum opus - capturing both singles and mixed doubles titles while losing the girls' doubles final, a bittersweet trifecta that perfectly encapsulates her career's theme of "so close, yet so far." The supporting cast, led by frequent co-star Andrew S. Wang across 17 matches, provides solid chemistry, though one wonders if this prolific partnership was more quantity than quality.</p>

<p>The film's climax at 2022 Junior Nationals promised blockbuster status with an impressive 14-3 record, but like many overhyped sequels, the follow-up releases have been disappointingly sparse. Her 2023 South ORC showing - managing just one title from three finals - reads like a director losing their creative spark. The final act sees our protagonist vanishing entirely from the rankings, a fade-to-black ending that leaves audiences wondering if they witnessed a brief shooting star or just another case of junior circuit burnout.</p>

<p>While Chou's 71% career win rate suggests technical competence behind the camera, her inability to maintain momentum beyond the U19 circuit transforms what could have been a compelling sports biopic into a melancholy meditation on unfulfilled potential. A career that promised franchise potential ultimately delivers a forgettable straight-to-streaming experience.</p>
