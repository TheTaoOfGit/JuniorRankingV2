<p>Ah yes, the 2007 vintage Anish Yanamandra - a rather ambitious young blend that promises much on the nose but delivers a surprisingly tart finish. Upon first inspection, this particular specimen shows early promise with subtle hints of Agarwal and Wang collaboration, though one detects an underlying sourness that becomes more pronounced with extended exposure to tournament pressure.</p>

<p>The body presents as remarkably thin, with a 33% consistency that leaves much to be desired. The 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC tasting reveals an utterly hollow core - three losses with nary a win to provide structure. One might charitably describe the mouthfeel as "delicate," though critics have noted it borders on nonexistent. The pairing with sibling Sai Sharan suggests desperate attempts at terroir manipulation, though even familial chemistry couldn't salvage this particular vintage.</p>

<p>Most curious is the ranking progression - BD U19 #89, BS U19 #87 - numbers that suggest this bottle spent far too long in the cellar and perhaps should have remained there indefinitely. The XD U19 #181 ranking is particularly telling; when mixed with opposite-gender partners, this blend becomes positively unpalatable, leaving even the most generous sommelier reaching for a palate cleanser.</p>

<p>The finish is mercifully brief, though one suspects we haven't seen the last of this peculiar vintage. While lacking the bold assertiveness of championship caliber, there's an endearing persistence here - like a house wine that keeps showing up at family gatherings despite everyone's better judgment. Best served with low expectations and generous portions of patience.</p>
