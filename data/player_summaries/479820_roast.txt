<p>★★☆☆☆ Had high hopes for Rachel Chan's Badminton Bistro after hearing she made it to the finals at 2025 NE OLC, but what a letdown! The service was absolutely dreadful - she's got a 27% success rate, which means you're more likely to leave hungry than satisfied. I've been to nine of her "establishments" across different locations from Midwest ORC to WAYSIDE NE CRC, and the experience is consistently inconsistent.</p>

<p>The menu variety is impressive, I'll give her that - she serves up Girls Singles, Girls Doubles, and Mixed Doubles across multiple age groups. But quantity doesn't make up for quality! Her signature dish seems to be the "Disappointing Doubles Platter" which she's been perfecting with her business partner Alicia Chang (6 collaborations and counting). The Mixed Doubles section is particularly concerning - ranked #619 in U19? That's not even bottom shelf, that's basement level!</p>

<p>What really gets me is the false advertising - she went from being ranked #69 in GS U13 to #141 in GS U15. That's not growth, that's regression! It's like a restaurant promising farm-to-table freshness and serving you day-old leftovers. Her recent performance at WAYSIDE where she went 1-5 was like ordering a seven-course meal and only getting one edible appetizer.</p>

<p>Look, I want to support young entrepreneurs in the badminton restaurant business, and that GD U13 finals appearance shows she's got potential ingredients. But until she figures out how to actually win matches instead of just participating in them, I can't recommend Rachel Chan's establishment. Maybe try again in a few years when she's had time to develop her recipes - er, shots.</p>
