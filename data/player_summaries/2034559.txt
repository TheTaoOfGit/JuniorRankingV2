<p>Weslie Chen burst onto the national badminton scene with authority, capturing the boys' doubles title at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships and establishing himself as one of America's premier junior talents. The 2006-born athlete has dominated the Olympic Regional Championship circuit, claiming an impressive five titles across multiple events and proving his versatility by excelling in both boys' doubles and mixed doubles competition.</p>

<p>Chen's partnership chemistry has been a defining characteristic of his career, particularly his dynamic collaboration with Jack Yin, with whom he's competed in 22 matches. This formidable duo has been the cornerstone of Chen's success in boys' doubles, helping him achieve a peak ranking of #2 in the BD U19 category by December 2023. His ability to seamlessly transition between partners—from Kyle Wang in boys' events to standout mixed doubles combinations with Savanna Lee, Veronica Yang, and Annabelle Zhang—showcases remarkable court awareness and adaptability.</p>

<p>What sets Chen apart is his remarkable consistency and competitive drive, evidenced by his impressive 92-52 overall record (64% win rate) across 13 tournaments spanning from 2021 to 2024. His breakthrough 2023 season was particularly stellar, highlighted by ORC victories at both the South and NorCal events, plus his mixed doubles triumph at the YONEX NorCal ORC where he also reached #9 in XD U19 rankings. Even when facing setbacks, like back-to-back finals losses at the 2024 YONEX South ORC, Chen demonstrated resilience by bouncing back to claim the 2024 YONEX SoCal ORC title.</p>

<p>Chen's tournament performances reveal a player who rises to the occasion when it matters most, with his 10-6 record at the prestigious U.S. Junior National Championships and dominant 9-2 showing at the 2023 YONEX NorCal ORC exemplifying his ability to perform under pressure. As he transitions beyond junior competition, Chen's proven track record of excellence, strategic partnership building, and unwavering competitive spirit position him as a rising star ready to make his mark on senior-level badminton.</p>
