<p>Ah yes, the Hannah George vintage—a 2007 pressing that initially showed tremendous promise in its early cellaring years. The 2022 Midwest CRC offers our first proper tasting notes: an assertive 8-1 body with hints of championship oak, displaying remarkable depth for such a young vintage. One detects the subtle terroir of seven U17 titles, with a particularly robust GS/XD double at the MBC CRC that suggests this bottle understood the fundamentals of proper aging.</p>

<p>However, I'm afraid the sommelier must report some troubling oxidation in recent years. The 2022 Northwest ORC presents as completely corked—a devastating 0-5 that leaves only the bitter aftertaste of unforced errors. Similarly, the Synergy NorCal ORC vintage suffers from severe tartrate precipitation, another 0-4 that suggests poor storage conditions or perhaps an unfortunate pairing with the wrong doubles partner. These are the sort of bottles one quietly removes from the cellar before guests arrive.</p>

<p>The most intriguing aspect of this vintage remains her partnership blend with Ronith A Manikonda—48 matches of what can only be described as a complex marriage, sometimes producing championship-worthy bouquets, other times resulting in early spoilage. The ranking progression tells a melancholy tale: from a respectable GD U17 #12 in 2023 to an almost undrinkable GS U19 #99 by 2025. It's rather like watching a promising Bordeaux turn to vinegar in real time.</p>

<p>In conclusion, the Hannah George collection offers moments of genuine brilliance—those seven titles sparkle like properly aged Champagne—but suffers from inconsistent vintage quality and what appears to be premature peaking. At 96-75 overall, it's a perfectly serviceable table wine, but one suspects the cellar master may have rushed the bottling process. Perhaps best enjoyed young, before the full complexity of unranked mediocrity has time to develop.</p>
