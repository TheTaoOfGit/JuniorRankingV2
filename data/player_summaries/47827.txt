<p>Garret Tan burst onto the national scene in spectacular fashion at just 13 years old, capturing the boys doubles U15 title at the 2021 USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships with an outstanding 18-3 tournament record. That breakthrough performance established him as one of the most promising young talents in American junior badminton, showcasing the versatility and competitive fire that would define his career.</p>

<p>What sets Tan apart is his remarkable adaptability across all three disciplines and age groups. He has claimed titles in boys singles (U19), boys doubles (U15, U17), and mixed doubles (U17), demonstrating rare tactical intelligence and court awareness. His dominant 11-1 performance at the 2022 SoCal Arena CRC, where he swept both the boys doubles U17 and mixed doubles U17 titles, exemplified his ability to peak when it matters most.</p>

<p>The California native has built an impressive 87-34 career record across 11 tournaments, maintaining a stellar 72% win percentage while consistently reaching the sport's biggest stages. His seven finals appearances, including memorable runs to the title match at prestigious events like the YONEX NorCal ORC and South ORC, reflect his mental toughness and clutch gene under pressure.</p>

<p>Tan's partnership chemistry has been equally impressive, particularly his 21-match collaboration with Tian Qi Alan Zhang that has become one of junior badminton's most formidable doubles combinations. As he continues developing his craft, this multi-talented competitor has all the tools to make a significant impact at the senior level, with his national championship pedigree and cross-discipline excellence positioning him as a player to watch in American badminton's bright future.</p>
