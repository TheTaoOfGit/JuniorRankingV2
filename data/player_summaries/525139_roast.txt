<p>Ah, the Aaditya Lanka vintage - a most intriguing specimen from the 2016 harvest that has been aging for precisely a decade. Upon first nosing, one detects the bold opening notes of a 3-1 performance at the YONEX LIBC Northeast, followed immediately by the sharp, acidic finish of multiple 0-2 showings that leave the palate quite... challenged. This is clearly a wine still finding its legs, much like young Lanka himself navigating the court with Tanush Jaltar, his most frequent doubles partner and fellow participant in this grand experiment we call competitive badminton.</p>

<p>The body presents as surprisingly light yet persistent - a 24-41 record that speaks to remarkable endurance if not exactly refinement. Notice how the tannins of defeat have been expertly balanced by just enough sweetness to keep things interesting, particularly that delightful 6-4 showing at the Vistara South that suggests this vintage may yet develop some complexity. The sommelier notes a certain youthful exuberance in the way Lanka approaches each tournament, completely unburdened by the weight of expectations or, indeed, any titles whatsoever.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this offering is its remarkable consistency across age groups - maintaining that signature 37% win rate with the dedication of a Swiss timekeeper. The progression from #85 to #17 in U11 doubles shows promise, though one wonders if this wine might benefit from a bit more time in the cellar before attempting to compete with the more mature vintages in the U17 and U19 categories, where it currently ranks somewhere between "ambitious" and "adorably optimistic."</p>

<p>In conclusion, the Lanka 2016 is best enjoyed now while still young and hopeful, paired perhaps with understanding parents and a good sports psychologist. While it may not have the championship finish of more celebrated vintages, it possesses an undeniable charm and the potential to surprise - assuming one's expectations are appropriately calibrated to "participation trophy" levels. A solid B+ for effort, though the scorecard might suggest otherwise.</p>
