<p>Ah, the 2009 vintage Sandra Chen - a delightfully ambitious young offering that presents with an intriguing bouquet of promise and a distinctly unrefined finish. Upon first sampling at the 2023 Northeast OLC, this particular vintage displayed early notes of raw potential, though with a somewhat astringent 2-3 record that left the palate yearning for more developed complexity.</p>

<p>The mid-career tasting reveals fascinating inconsistencies - one detects hints of excellence in the 2024 NE MASSBAD OLC blend, where she achieved a respectable 5-4 body alongside partner Hannah Cheng, even managing to reach the mixed doubles final before the inevitable oxidation set in. However, this promising vintage was immediately followed by the rather unfortunate 2024 NE CRC bottling, a harsh 1-5 composition that suggested the grapes may have been picked prematurely.</p>

<p>Most intriguingly, this Chen vintage pairs exceptionally well with Hannah Cheng across 19 separate tastings, creating a doubles blend that shows remarkable improvement over time. The partnership exhibits notes of youthful exuberance with undertones of strategic naivety, though recent samplings suggest this combination is aging gracefully from #82 to #36 in the girls' doubles rankings.</p>

<p>While the overall 25-37 vintage profile suggests this is not yet a championship-worthy selection, there's an undeniable charm to its 40% success rate - rather like a plucky pinot grigio that knows it's not Dom Pérignon but refuses to apologize for its modest aspirations. The finish is surprisingly long, with lingering notes of determination and just a hint of "maybe next tournament," making this a vintage worth watching as it continues to mature.</p>
