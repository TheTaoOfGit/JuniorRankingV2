<p>★★☆☆☆ — "Carsten Lam: The Buddy Comedy" opens promisingly with our young protagonist discovering his true calling isn't in the lonely world of singles badminton, where he currently languishes at a forgettable #65 ranking. Instead, this is a heartwarming tale about the power of friendship, as Lam transforms from mediocre solo act into a legitimate doubles threat. The character development is genuinely impressive — watching him climb to #17 in boys doubles while simultaneously plummeting to #140 in boys singles is like witnessing Clark Kent become Superman, except in reverse and only when he has a sidekick.</p>

<p>The film's most compelling subplot involves Lam's partnership with Sophia Sun, a 28-episode romantic comedy that unfortunately keeps getting cancelled in the mixed doubles finals. Their chemistry is undeniable on paper, but the execution suggests they should probably stick to being friends. Meanwhile, his bromance with Cayden Wang produces the kind of boys doubles magic that won them the 2026 VISTARA SOUTH OLC title, proving that sometimes the best relationships are the ones where nobody expects you to understand the opposite sex.</p>

<p>The movie's middle act drags considerably during the infamous 2023 sequence, where our hero manages to reach four finals and lose them all like some sort of badminton groundhog day. The Dave Freeman Jr. OLC serves as the film's lowest point, where Lam somehow found three different ways to lose finals at the same tournament — a hat trick of heartbreak that would make even the most optimistic sports movie critic reach for the tissues.</p>

<p>What saves this production from complete mediocrity is Lam's unwavering commitment to the "doubles specialist" character arc. His 13,290 points in boys doubles prove he's found his niche, even if his 5,214 singles points suggest he should avoid any solo scenes. The supporting cast of partners — from Varin Karri to Jacob Ma — all play their roles admirably, though none quite match the on-court magic he creates with his leading men. Ultimately, "Carsten Lam: The Buddy Comedy" succeeds because it knows exactly what it is: a feel-good story about finding your strengths and sticking with them, even if those strengths require bringing a friend along for the ride.</p>
