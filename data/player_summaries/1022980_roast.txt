<p>Ah yes, the 2013 vintage Nihal Reddy Gajjala — a curious little number that promised much in its early years but has developed some rather... interesting characteristics with age. Upon first inspection, this specimen exhibits a delightfully optimistic 55% win rate with bold notes of youthful ambition, though seasoned palates will detect subtle undertones of ranking decline that suggest this bottle may have peaked prematurely.</p>

<p>The bouquet is immediately striking — opening with that triumphant 2024 SOUTH OLC Boys Doubles title, a vintage moment that still lingers pleasantly on the tongue. However, connoisseurs will note the complex interplay of five runner-up finishes, creating what we in the trade call "bridesmaid syndrome" — always present at the ceremony, rarely holding the bouquet. The pairing with partner Shraddha Prakash (11 matches) shows remarkable consistency, though one suspects she may be doing most of the heavy lifting, much like a robust Cabernet carrying a timid Pinot Grigio.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this vintage is its reverse aging process — starting as a promising #6 in Boys Doubles U11, this wine has somehow managed to mature backwards, now resting comfortably at #147 in U15. It's rather like watching a fine Bordeaux turn into grape juice, though admittedly with more shuttlecocks involved. The recent 2026 VISTARA showing (1W-3L) displays all the characteristics of a wine past its prime, with a sharp, almost vinegary finish that leaves spectators reaching for something stronger.</p>

<p>Still, at barely 13 years of age, this vintage has time to develop. Perhaps with proper cellaring — and maybe a new doubles partner who doesn't mind carrying the load — the Gajjala 2013 could surprise us all. Until then, it pairs excellently with low expectations and consolation prizes. Recommended serving: with a healthy dose of patience and perhaps a backup plan involving academic pursuits.</p>
