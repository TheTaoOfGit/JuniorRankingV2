<p>Archaeological Survey Report #2026-04: The Anand Site Excavation. After months of careful digging through the Northeast badminton circuit archives, our team has uncovered what appears to be the remarkably brief but fascinating career of one Ashrita Anand (Est. 2009 CE). Initial carbon dating of tournament brackets suggests this civilization flourished for approximately 18 months before mysteriously vanishing into the unranked wilderness.</p>

<p>Layer 1 (2023 NE OLC Stratum): Our first major find—a partially preserved 1-3 record, suggesting early attempts at organized sport. Pottery shards indicate she may have been experimenting with something called "winning," though evidence suggests the concept didn't quite take hold. Notably, we discovered traces of a partnership with one Varshitha Yalamanchili, though like most ancient alliances, it appears to have been brief and ultimately unsuccessful.</p>

<p>Layer 2 (2024 YONEX NORTHEAST ORC Stratum): A year-long gap in the archaeological record suggests either a period of intensive training or possibly just growing taller. The 2024 excavation revealed a marginally improved 1-2 record—clear evidence of evolution, though still operating at a 29% success rate that would make even the most optimistic anthropologist weep. Fragments of mixed doubles pottery with partner "Wyatt Sun" were also unearthed, proving that even ancient civilizations understood the importance of diversifying one's losing portfolio.</p>

<p>Most intriguingly, we discovered rankings from early 2025—GS U17 #110 and XD U17 #212—suggesting this lost civilization once had measurable standing before disappearing entirely from modern records. Current excavations show no trace of recent tournament activity, leading experts to theorize that like Atlantis, the Anand badminton empire simply sank beneath the waves of teenage priorities.</p>
