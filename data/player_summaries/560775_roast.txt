<p>⭐☆☆☆☆ (1/5 stars) - Went to Avni Pawar's Badminton Bistro expecting a decent meal, but left hungrier than when I arrived. The establishment has only been open for 2 seasons and it shows. The menu is extremely limited - she's only serving at two locations so far: FORTIUS SOUTH CRC and PEAK SPORTS SOUTH OLC.</p>

<p>The service at FORTIUS was marginally better than PEAK SPORTS, where I witnessed a complete disaster. At PEAK SPORTS, every single order came back to the kitchen (0-4 record), while FORTIUS at least managed to get one dish out correctly before the kitchen caught fire again (1-4). The wait staff seems confused - she keeps switching between servers Om Kane and Aarav Juven Prabhu, but neither seems to know what they're doing either.</p>

<p>The presentation is amateur hour. Despite being ranked #372 in her age group for singles, she somehow thought she could handle the mixed doubles section too, where she's currently sitting at a shocking #676. It's like watching someone try to run a sushi bar when they can barely make a sandwich. The ambition is admirable, the execution is... well, let's just say there's room for improvement.</p>

<p>Look, I don't want to completely trash this place - the owner is clearly just getting started in the restaurant business and everyone has to begin somewhere. But with an 11% success rate, you'd have better luck ordering from a food truck operated by blindfolded toddlers. Maybe give it a few more years before making a reservation. The potential is there, but right now you're better off bringing your own lunch.</p>
