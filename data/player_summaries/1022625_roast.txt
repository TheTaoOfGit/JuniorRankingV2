<p>Avery Ji (born 2008) is an American badminton player widely recognized for her groundbreaking contributions to the art of competitive losing[citation needed]. Currently ranked #193 in Girls' Singles U19 with a commanding 2700 points, Ji has established herself as a formidable presence on court, particularly when viewed from the perspective of her opponents who rarely need to break a sweat[citation needed]. Her career winning percentage of 34% represents what experts call "consistently consistent inconsistency," a rare talent that allows spectators to plan their bathroom breaks with remarkable precision[citation needed].</p>

<p>Ji's meteoric descent from #38 in Girls' Doubles U17 to her current #193 ranking has been described by badminton analysts as "Newton's Law of Gravity applied to junior rankings"[citation needed]. Her partnership history reads like a revolving door of hope and disappointment, with Ethan Ao courageously enduring 8 matches alongside Ji, followed by Savannah Kam's 6-match stint, presumably before both sought witness protection[citation needed]. The 2024 OBA NW OLC tournament marked Ji's career peak, where she managed to lose not one, but two finals in the same event—a feat that tournament organizers called "impressively thorough"[citation needed].</p>

<p>Ji's tournament record showcases her remarkable ability to transform promising starts into spectacular collapses, exemplified by her 0-3 performance at the 2025 YONEX SOUTH ORC, where she achieved what mathematicians call "perfect failure"[citation needed]. Her appearance at the 2024 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS resulted in a 1-6 record, leading one anonymous coach to comment that "at least she showed up"[citation needed]. Despite never winning a title across 22 tournaments, Ji continues to inspire underdogs everywhere by proving that participation trophies exist for a reason[citation needed].</p>
