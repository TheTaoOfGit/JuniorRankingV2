<p>At just 12 years old, Rylan Tan announced himself as a force in junior badminton with one of the most dominant performances in recent U.S. Junior National Championships history. Capturing both the Mixed Doubles U13 and Boys Doubles U13 titles while reaching the Boys Singles U13 final, he compiled an extraordinary 19-1 record at the 2021 nationals—a feat that showcased his remarkable versatility across all three disciplines.</p>

<p>Tan's explosive 78% career win rate (51-14) reflects a player who consistently rises to the occasion when it matters most. His partnership with Zicheng Xu has been particularly formidable, spanning 11 matches and forming the backbone of his doubles success. But it's Tan's adaptability that truly sets him apart—equally comfortable commanding the court in singles or orchestrating complex doubles strategies with partners like Micah Cruz and Cassidy Yeh.</p>

<p>After a competitive hiatus, Tan's return to tournament play in 2025 demonstrated his continued evolution as a player. Competing in the older U19 division, he swept through the YONEX SOCAL ORC without dropping a single match (6-0), claiming the Boys Singles title and proving that his game had matured during his time away from competition.</p>

<p>What makes Tan exceptional isn't just his impressive title collection or sterling win percentage—it's his ability to perform under pressure at the highest levels of junior competition. From his breakthrough 2021 season where he went 9-1 at the SoCal ORC to his recent perfect tournament performance, Tan has consistently shown the mental fortitude and technical skill that separate good players from great ones. At 17, with his combination of proven championship pedigree and renewed competitive hunger, Tan represents one of the most intriguing talents in junior badminton today.</p>
