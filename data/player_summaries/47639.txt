<p>Jasmine Yeung's 248-49 match record speaks to a level of dominance rarely seen in junior badminton, with her 84% win rate across 19 tournaments establishing her as one of the most consistent performers on the circuit. The 18-year-old has captured an astounding 31 titles across all three disciplines, including back-to-back mixed doubles U17 championships at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships and multiple victories at the prestigious Junior National Championships.</p>

<p>Her versatility sets her apart from her peers, with title victories spanning from U15 to U19 age groups in singles, girls doubles, and mixed doubles. Yeung's breakthrough came early, winning her first national titles in girls doubles U15 at the 2022 Junior National Championships, but she truly announced herself with a flawless 19-0 performance at the 2022 Synergy NorCal ORC, where she swept titles in all three events she entered.</p>

<p>The foundation of Yeung's doubles success lies in her chemistry with key partners, particularly Zicheng Xu and Angela Ye, with whom she's played 34 matches each. This partnership stability has been crucial to her mixed doubles and girls doubles dominance, including her historic climb to #1 in GD U19 and #2 in XD U19 rankings in early 2025. Her ability to excel with multiple partners demonstrates exceptional court awareness and adaptability.</p>

<p>Yeung's tournament consistency is perhaps her most impressive attribute, with multiple near-perfect campaigns including a 17-1 record at the 2021 SoCal ORC and 18-2 at the 2021 HPBC NW ORC. Even as she transitions through age groups and faces increasingly competitive fields, her fundamental excellence and multi-discipline threat make her a formidable opponent who has already established herself as a force in American junior badminton.</p>
