<p>Field Report #2024-001: Excavation of the Ramesh Site reveals a fascinating glimpse into early 21st century junior badminton civilization. Carbon dating places the initial settlement at approximately 2023, when our subject first emerged from the primordial ooze of recreational play. The lowest stratum contains two pristine artifacts from 2024: a GD U13 trophy from NE CRC and a GS U13 championship from South OLC, suggesting this was once a thriving U13 empire before mysterious forces led to its gradual decline.</p>

<p>The middle layers tell a more complex story of territorial expansion gone awry. As our archaeological subject attempted to colonize the U15 territories, we discovered a curious pattern of three final appearances at various sites, yet only empty-handed returns. Most notably, the 2025 South OLC expedition appears twice in the historical record—once as a GD U15 defeat and again as a GS U15 failure, suggesting either poor strategic planning or an alarming case of archaeological déjà vu. The site shows evidence of a 47% success rate, placing it firmly in the "mediocre but trying their best" category of ancient civilizations.</p>

<p>Perhaps most intriguing is the discovery of multiple partnership contracts, with the Ruohan Joy Du alliance dominating the historical record with 13 collaborative ventures. Lesser partnerships with Olivia Liu, Tanvi Sree Gaddam, and others suggest a diplomatic approach to doubles warfare, though the XD rankings (#245, #423, #585 across age groups) indicate that mixed doubles remained about as appealing to our subject as a plague of locusts. The artifact labeled "2025 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONALS: 2W-6L" serves as a particularly humbling reminder that not all expeditions end in glory—sometimes you just get thoroughly pillaged by superior civilizations.</p>

<p>Current excavations place the Ramesh civilization at GS U15 #67 and GD U15 #41, suggesting a modest recovery from the great U15 transition crisis. While not quite the dominant empire of her U13 golden age, our subject appears to have stabilized her kingdom somewhere between "respectable local power" and "still figuring out how to handle players who've hit puberty." Future archaeological missions may reveal whether this civilization can reclaim its former glory or will remain a cautionary tale about the perils of aging up in junior badminton.</p>
