<p>★☆☆☆☆ Tried Ayur Bayal's Badminton Bistro during the 2025 Peak Sports South OLC weekend and honestly, I've had more satisfying meals at a gas station. The service was incredibly consistent though - he delivered the same disappointing experience three times in a row! Zero stars for variety, but you have to admire that level of commitment to mediocrity.</p>

<p>The menu seemed promising with options like "Boy's Doubles with Izaac Schuenemann" and "Mixed Doubles featuring Sahasra Parasa," but every dish came out completely undercooked. I watched him serve up loss after loss with the enthusiasm of a 9-year-old who just discovered that shuttlecocks don't actually contain chickens. The presentation was adorable, I'll give him that, but the execution left me hungrier than when I arrived.</p>

<p>Currently ranked #107 in Boy's Doubles and #117 in Boy's Singles on the U11 menu - which honestly feels generous considering the quality of service I received. It's like rating a lemonade stand in the Michelin Guide. The kid's got heart, showing up to compete in December 2025 like a food truck brave enough to park outside a five-star restaurant, but the results speak for themselves.</p>

<p>Look, I get it - every great chef starts somewhere, and this little guy's probably still figuring out which end of the racket to hold. The 0-3 record at Peak Sports South OLC was basically an appetizer sampler of "Things That Didn't Go According to Plan." But hey, at least he's consistent! If you're looking for a guaranteed loss with a side of youthful optimism, Ayur's your guy. Just don't expect to leave satisfied - unless you're rooting for his opponents.</p>
