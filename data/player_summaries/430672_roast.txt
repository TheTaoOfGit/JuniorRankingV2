<p>⭐⭐⭐ Went to Agastya's Kitchen expecting consistent service, but got the most unpredictable dining experience of my life. One day you're getting a championship-worthy meal (shoutout to that BD U17 title at CBA MIDWEST OLC), the next you're served six straight losses at Nationals like it's some kind of punishment buffet. The house specialty seems to be "coin flip cuisine" - with a 50-49 record, you literally have a better chance of getting good food by flipping a quarter.</p>

<p>The waitstaff situation is absolute chaos. Agastya has gone through more doubles partners than a restaurant goes through line cooks during a health inspection. Started the evening with Rudransh Majumdar (solid choice), then suddenly Vivaan Khullar is taking my order, followed by Shloka, Pranav, and Shradha in rapid succession. Sir, this is supposed to be fine dining, not speed dating with badminton rackets. Pick a partner and stick with them!</p>

<p>The menu is wildly inconsistent across age groups. Ordered the "U17 Special" and got respectable rankings (#52 BS, #75 BD), but when I asked about the U19 options, they served me #125 and #164 like I'm dining at a completely different establishment. And don't get me started on the mixed doubles section - #352 in U17, #510 in U19. At this point I'm wondering if they're even using the same kitchen.</p>

<p>Credit where credit's due - those recent wins at Play N Thrive MW CRC show the chef can cook when the pressure's on. But then I remember that 0-6 disaster at 2024 Nationals and wonder if someone accidentally unplugged the fryer that week. The ranking progression from #98 to #59 in boys singles shows promise, but until Agastya's Kitchen figures out how to deliver the same quality meal twice in a row, I'm sticking to more reliable establishments. Will consider returning when they stop treating every tournament like they're experimenting with a new recipe.</p>
