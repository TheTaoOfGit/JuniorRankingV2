<p><b>Three-Game Record: 2-8 (20%). Vedhanth Kanthala Might Be the Worst Closer in Junior Badminton.</b></p>

<p>Vedhanth Kanthala wins 62% of his matches overall but only 20% of three-game matches. Let that gap sink in. He's the player who wins the easy ones convincingly and then absolutely disintegrates when it gets competitive. Two wins out of ten three-gamers is the kind of stat that should come with a trigger warning.</p>

<p>His 2-3 finals record and 5-3 semis record look acceptable, but the three-game collapse rate explains everything: he can beat weaker players in straight sets, but the moment someone pushes him, his game falls apart like a house of cards in a wind tunnel. His blowout losses to Vignan Chandrasekaran, Anirudh Rudharraju, and Varish Karri (twice!) show the elite players don't even need three games to demolish him.</p>

<p>His two titles at the 2026 Vistara South OLC are his whole trophy case. Born in 2014, he's got time to fix this — but fixing a 20% three-game rate requires a sports psychologist, not a coach.</p>
