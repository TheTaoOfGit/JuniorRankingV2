The doubles appetizer menu is absolutely stellar - this kid is serving up some serious BD U15 #8 ranked delicacies that'll make your taste buds dance. That's like being the 58th best microwave burrito in the freezer aisle.</p>

<p>The service here is wildly inconsistent. One day you're getting a 14W-6L performance at Nationals that makes you think this place deserves a Michelin star, then the next year you're served a tragic 3W-6L disaster that tastes like disappointment with a side of regret. The waitstaff (looking at you, partner rotation of Sahasra Reddy and Kavya Goyal) tries their best to carry the operation, but even they can't save a sinking ship when the head chef decides to phone it in.</p>

<p>What really gets me is the false advertising. The menu promises BD U15 championship quality after that 2026 DAVE FREEMAN JR. SOCAL OLC title win, but then you see the XD U15 #117 ranking and realize this kitchen can't handle more than two ingredients at once. It's like they're amazing at making grilled cheese but completely fall apart the moment you ask them to add tomato.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due - the NorCal CRC BD U11 title from 2021 was genuinely impressive for such a young establishment, and that recent jump from BD U15 #23 to #8 shows real potential for improvement. But until this place figures out how to make a decent singles dish or coordinate a mixed doubles special without burning down the kitchen, I'll stick to ordering takeout from more consistent competitors. Might revisit in a year or two when the chef hits puberty and hopefully develops some consistency.</p>
