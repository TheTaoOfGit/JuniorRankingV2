<p>Mia Zhang (born 2006) is a former American junior badminton player best known for her remarkable ability to transform promising tournament runs into spectacular mediocrity.[citation needed] Zhang achieved her career pinnacle in 2022 by reaching the Girls' Singles U17 final at the Junior National Championships, where she promptly discovered that "runner-up" is just a fancy term for "first loser."[citation needed] Her subsequent career trajectory has been described by badminton analysts as "a masterclass in gravitational physics," falling from a respectable GS U19 #11 ranking in 2023 to her current status of completely unranked.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Zhang's most enduring partnership has been with Alice Y Wang, with whom she has played 29 matches in what tennis commentators would call "a beautiful friendship" and badminton statisticians call "co-dependent failure."[citation needed] Their doubles partnership has been characterized by consistent inconsistency, managing to lose more finals than most players reach in their entire careers.[citation needed] Zhang has also experimented with other partners including Jack Yin (5 matches), though this brief mixed doubles venture ended when Yin reportedly requested a transfer to singles play "for his mental health."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Her tournament record reads like a choose-your-own-adventure book where every choice leads to disappointment.[citation needed] Notable performances include her 0-2 showing at the 2023 SoCal ORC, which badminton historians rank among the "most efficient eliminations in junior tournament history."[citation needed] Zhang bounced back admirably at the 2024 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships with an 8-3 record, proving that even a broken clock is right twice a day, though in her case it appears to be significantly less frequent.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Currently maintaining an overall record of 45-31 across 12 tournaments, Zhang has achieved the rare distinction of having a winning percentage that somehow feels like a losing one.[citation needed] Sports psychologists theorize that her ability to consistently underperform expectations despite decent statistics represents a new phenomenon they've termed "Zhang's Paradox."[citation needed] At age 20, she continues to compete with the determination of someone who hasn't yet realized that "former junior champion" is not actually a career path.[citation needed]</p>
