<p><b>0-4 in Finals: Ritvik Singh Is the Best Player Who Can't Win the Big One</b></p>

<p>Ritvik Singh has four runner-up finishes and zero titles. He's made it to the final four times and lost every single one — including losing to Lohith Reddy Arava and Adithya Yeluguri, who must feel like Ritvik's personal championship gatekeepers. A 0-4 finals record is genuinely impressive in its futility; most players don't even get that many chances to choke.</p>

<p>His 8-12 three-game record (40%) explains everything. When it matters most, Ritvik finds a creative new way to lose. He's got the talent to go deep — 57% overall win rate, 4-4 in semis — but the moment a trophy is on the line, his game evaporates like morning dew on a hot court.</p>

<p>Getting blown out by his own doubles partner Adithya Yeluguri in BS U13 is the kind of humiliation that haunts your dreams. Ritvik is the bridesmaid of junior badminton: always dressed up, never walking down the aisle.</p>
