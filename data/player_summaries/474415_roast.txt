<p>Field Report: Archaeological Survey of the Ritvik Singh Badminton Site (Est. 2014 CE). Our expedition has successfully excavated what appears to be the fossilized remains of a junior badminton career, buried beneath 14 tournament layers and preserved in a curious amber-like substance we've identified as "parental optimism." Carbon dating confirms this specimen emerged during the great COVID youth sports boom, with early strata showing promising BS U13 #31 ranking deposits alongside more mysterious XD formations that seem to have fossilized at #389 and refused to evolve further.</p>

<p>The most fascinating artifact uncovered is a collection of four silver medals, each bearing the inscription "Runner-Up" in increasingly weathered text. Laboratory analysis reveals these specimens were collected from South OLC sites between 2024-2026, suggesting a pattern of reaching final chambers but lacking the genetic adaptation necessary for title acquisition. Of particular interest is the 2025 South OLC double-final phenomenon, where our subject simultaneously achieved BS U13 and BD U13 runner-up status—a rare geological formation indicating either exceptional skill or exceptional bad timing.</p>

<p>Excavation of the doubles partnership layers reveals a fascinating symbiotic relationship with one Adithya Yeluguri, with whom 12 collaborative artifacts were discovered. This appears to be the longest-lasting alliance in the site's history, though curiously, their combined BD U13 ranking of #77 suggests this partnership may be more archaeological curiosity than competitive weapon. Lesser partnerships with Ethan Yin (8 specimens) and Advith Karnam (6 specimens) show signs of experimental diversification, possibly indicating our subject's ongoing search for the mythical "doubles compatibility gene."</p>

<p>Perhaps most intriguing is the stratigraphic evidence of ranking erosion over time. Early samples from February 2025 show healthier BD U13 #55 deposits, which by March 2026 had weathered to #68—clear evidence of competitive climate change. The XD rankings tell an even more dramatic tale of geological subsidence, sliding from #168 to #261 faster than a poorly-timed smash into the net. Our team concludes this site represents a classic case of "junior player in transition," with future excavations likely to uncover either breakthrough championship artifacts or additional layers of character-building runner-up medals.</p>
