<p>What if I told you that in the summer of 2023, a young warrior named Zhenbang Yang stood on the precipice of badminton greatness, racket trembling in his 12-year-old hands? At the YONEX SoCal CRC, he didn't just participate—he dominated his way to not one, but TWO finals. The crowd held its breath. The shuttlecocks seemed to fly in slow motion. And then... he lost both. Boys' Singles U13. Mixed Doubles U13. Two silver medals that might as well have been participation ribbons, because in badminton, as in life, close only counts in horseshoes and hand grenades.</p>

<p>This is the story of a player who achieved the most mathematically perfect mediocrity in junior sports history: a career record of 14 wins, 14 losses. Not 15-13. Not 13-15. Exactly, precisely, beautifully average. Zhenbang Yang didn't just find the middle ground—he pitched a tent there, roasted marshmallows, and invited his doubles partner Molly Chen to stay for seven matches of exquisite ordinariness.</p>

<p>By December 2023, Yang had climbed to the dizzying heights of #88 in Boys' Doubles U13 and #97 in Boys' Singles. These weren't rankings—they were suggestions. Gentle hints from the badminton gods that perhaps he should consider a sport with lower expectations, like professional bowling or competitive checkers.</p>

<p>But the cruel mistress of time showed no mercy. By 2024, at the Dave Freeman Jr. OLC—a tournament that sounds like it was named after someone's accountant—Yang achieved the rare badminton hat trick: three losses, zero wins, and a one-way ticket from "ranked" to "unranked" faster than a shuttlecock to the face. Sometimes the sport doesn't choose you back, Zhenbang. Sometimes it just chooses to forget you existed.</p>
