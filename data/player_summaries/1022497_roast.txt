<p>Welcome, fellow hikers, to the Brianna Wang Memorial Trail, a moderately challenging 5-14 kilometer route known for its steep descents and occasional glimpses of hope before plummeting back into disappointment. This trail was first blazed in 2022 at the SPBA MW ORC, where our young explorer managed an impressive 3-7 expedition before discovering that badminton, much like wilderness survival, requires more than just showing up with a racket and good intentions.</p>

<p>As you continue along the path, you'll notice the trail markers become increasingly sparse - much like Brianna's wins after that initial burst of optimism. The 2022 MBC CRC section is particularly treacherous, featuring a notorious 0-2 cliff face that has claimed many a junior player's confidence. Local wildlife includes her rotating cast of doubles partners, with Katie Moy serving as her most faithful hiking companion through 4 matches of shared suffering, and Eric Zhang gamely attempting 3 mixed doubles adventures before presumably requesting a trail transfer.</p>

<p>The terrain becomes especially unforgiving as you reach the 2023 Midwest ORC overlook, where despite achieving the lofty heights of GD U17 #121 and GS U17 #100 rankings, our trail blazer managed just 1 victory in 3 attempts. Park rangers report that this particular vista offers stunning views of what could have been, if only the shuttle had landed inbounds more often.</p>

<p>Please note that this trail has been temporarily closed since 2023, with no current maintenance schedule available. Rangers suspect our trailblazer may have migrated to less challenging recreational activities, possibly involving sports where the net is lower and the opponents less skilled at making her look like she's swinging at invisible butterflies. Remember to pack plenty of water, realistic expectations, and perhaps a backup hobby, as this trail is not recommended for those seeking consistent elevation gains or trophy photo opportunities.</p>
