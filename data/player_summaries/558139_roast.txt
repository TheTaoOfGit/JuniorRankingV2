<p>What if I told you that in the winter of 2025, at the SCHAFER SLG SOUTH OLC, a young warrior stepped onto the court with dreams bigger than her racquet? This is the story of Millie Mistry, born in 2017, whose maiden voyage into competitive badminton would become the stuff of legend—just not the kind she was hoping for.</p>

<p>Partnered with Diya Vikram in what would become their one and only collaboration, Millie entered the tournament ranked nowhere because, well, you have to play tournaments to get ranked. But armed with the confidence that only an 8-year-old can possess, she was ready to take on the world. The world, however, had other plans.</p>

<p>Over two days in late November, Millie would compile a record that mathematicians call "undefined"—three matches, three losses, zero victories. It's the kind of start that would make Charlie Brown's baseball team say "Hey, at least we got close sometimes." But here's the thing about rock bottom: it gives you a solid foundation to build on.</p>

<p>By March 2026, Millie had clawed her way to #65 in Girls Doubles U11 and #76 in Girls Singles—proof that sometimes losing everything is just the beginning of your story. Because in badminton, as in life, the only real failure is not stepping onto the court. And Millie Mistry? She stepped up, got knocked down, and lived to shuttle another day.</p>
