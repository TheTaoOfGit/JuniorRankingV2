<p>Emily Tang (born 2011) is an American junior badminton player best known for her remarkable ability to peak at ages that no longer apply to her current competitive category.[citation needed] Tang achieved the rare distinction of winning multiple U13 and U15 titles before discovering that aging is, unfortunately, mandatory in competitive sports.</p>

<p>Tang's partnership portfolio reads like a badminton speed-dating service, having cycled through doubles partners with the efficiency of a revolving door.[citation needed] Her most enduring relationship appears to be with Seth Li (26 matches), though critics note this may simply reflect Li's inability to find alternative transportation to tournaments.[citation needed] Her 19-match stint with Jessica Zhang reportedly ended when Zhang realized Tang's strategy of "hit it harder" wasn't actually a tactical innovation.</p>

<p>The 2024 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS proved particularly educational for Tang, where her 6W-6L record demonstrated a mathematical precision in mediocrity that would make statisticians weep.[citation needed] This performance was preceded by her inspiring 1W-4L showing at the 2022 NE CRC, which Tang's supporters describe as "character building" and her detractors describe as "painful to watch."</p>

<p>Currently ranked #13 in Girls Singles U17 with 12,437 points, Tang occupies the prestigious position of "almost really good but not quite there yet."[citation needed] Her ranking progression from GS U15 #5 to GS U17 #14 suggests that either the competition improved dramatically or Tang discovered that her opponents also have access to YouTube coaching videos. Tang continues to compete with the unwavering optimism of someone who hasn't yet realized that trophies don't automatically resize themselves to match her age category.[citation needed]</p>
