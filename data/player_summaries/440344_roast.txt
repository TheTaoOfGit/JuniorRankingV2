<p><b>Career Win Rate: 34%. Niya Chen's Racket Should Come with a Sympathy Card.</b></p>

<p>Niya Chen has won 34 matches and lost 64 across 18 tournaments. That's a 34% win rate — worse than most players' three-game percentages. She's racked up zero titles, a 0-2 finals record, and a 2-7 semis record. She can barely make the semis, and when she does, she loses 78% of the time. It's consistent, at least.</p>

<p>Her two finals appearances both ended in losses, meaning the closest she's come to a trophy is watching someone else lift one. She went 0-3 at the 2023 South OLC and 1-3 at multiple other tournaments, establishing a pattern of paying entry fees to pad other people's records.</p>

<p>The one bright spot? Her 60% three-game record. But with so few matches going to three games (because she's getting beaten in straight sets), it's like being proud of your free throw percentage when you never get to the line. Partner Ananya Mishra has stuck through 18 matches of this, which is either loyalty or Stockholm syndrome.</p>
