<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Logan Lei-Chong's Badminton Bistro - "Consistently Disappointing Since 2021"</p>

<p>I've been following this establishment since their SoCal ORC debut in 2021, and wow, what a journey it's been. Going 0-1 in your grand opening? That's like serving undercooked chicken on day one. The chef clearly wasn't ready for prime time, but hey, we all start somewhere. Fast forward to 2023 and Logan's is still struggling with basic service - a 22% satisfaction rate would shut down any respectable eatery, but somehow this place keeps getting tournament reservations.</p>

<p>The menu is extremely limited. They specialize in both boys' singles and boys' doubles, but honestly, neither dish is particularly well-executed. Their doubles partnership with Max Zhang shows some promise - at least someone's willing to share a table with them repeatedly - but the chemistry just isn't there. It's like ordering a surf and turf and getting fish sticks with a hamburger patty. The 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals experience was particularly memorable: 1 win, 4 losses. That's like getting one decent appetizer followed by four courses of pure disappointment.</p>

<p>The ambiance peaked briefly in December 2023 when they managed to crack the top 100 in both categories (BD U19 #84, BS U19 #52), which is basically like getting a health department grade of C+. You're technically allowed to stay open, but nobody's writing home about it. Since then, they've apparently fallen off the rankings entirely - the restaurant equivalent of losing your liquor license.</p>

<p>Look, I want to root for the little guy, and Logan's Badminton Bistro has heart, I'll give them that. Three years in business despite consistently underwhelming reviews takes determination. Maybe they're just a late bloomer, or maybe they're destined to be one of those hole-in-the-wall places that somehow survives on the loyalty of Max Zhang and a few other regulars. Either way, I'll check back in a year to see if they've figured out how to cook.</p>
