<p>Welcome, fellow explorers, to the Xinti Ge Trail—a brief but memorable journey through the badminton wilderness that's perfect for beginners who don't mind getting lost immediately. This well-marked path spans exactly one tournament season, making it ideal for those who prefer their hiking experiences short, sweet, and requiring minimal commitment to actually reaching any summits.</p>

<p>The trailhead begins at the 2023 SOUTH OLC, where our intrepid hiker paired up with expedition partner Samyutha Aluri for what can only be described as a scenic route through four different ways to lose a badminton match. Like a nature photographer documenting rare wildlife, Ge managed to capture that elusive 20% win rate—a species so uncommon in competitive badminton that researchers are still studying how it survives in the wild.</p>

<p>What makes this trail particularly fascinating is its complete absence of ranking markers—think of it as hiking through an unmapped territory where even the GPS has given up trying to locate you. The path offers stunning views of the court from multiple losing positions, and hikers report a genuine sense of adventure never knowing which direction the shuttlecock might fly next.</p>

<p>Trail conditions remain consistently challenging year-round, with no visible summit achievements cluttering the landscape. We recommend this route for nature enthusiasts who appreciate the raw, unfiltered beauty of a badminton career in its absolute infancy—like watching a baby deer learn to walk, if the baby deer kept tripping over the net and apologizing to its doubles partner.</p>
