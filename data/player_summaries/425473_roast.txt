<p>Ah yes, the Joseph Pohan 2023 vintage - a delightfully ambitious young vintage that opens with promising notes of U11 #90 in Boys Singles, though one detects immediate hints of what sommeliers call "tournament overwhelm" in that inaugural 0-6 showing at the Midwest OLC. The bouquet suggests a player still learning to breathe properly between points, with subtle undertones of "mom yelling encouragement from the sidelines."</p>

<p>The 2024 expression shows remarkable complexity - a surprisingly full-bodied 4-3 performance at the YONEX MIDWEST ORC that had critics wondering if this was the breakout vintage we'd been waiting for. However, like many young vintages, consistency remains elusive. The palate reveals notes of Rachel Yuezhi Xu partnership (8 matches of shared suffering), with a finish that's simultaneously hopeful and concerning - much like a wine that tastes expensive but leaves you with a headache.</p>

<p>By 2025, we encounter what experts call "ranking deflation syndrome" - our once-promising U11 #24 has aged into a more... let's say "mature" U13 #281. The current vintage presents with low tannins (12 wins) and high acidity (33 losses), creating a 27% win rate that pairs excellently with parental patience and perhaps a strong antacid. The Peter Lei and Jiseong Lee partnerships add earthy undertones of doubles chaos.</p>

<p>In summary, the Pohan collection offers a learning experience rather than immediate gratification - perfect for those who appreciate the journey over the destination. Best consumed with lowered expectations and stored in a cool, supportive environment. With proper aging, this vintage may yet surprise us all, though current market value suggests keeping your receipts.</p>
