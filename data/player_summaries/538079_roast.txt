<p>Ah yes, the 2017 vintage Ella Kung - a remarkably young pressing that's still developing its character, though one must admire the ambitious attempt to compete across five age divisions simultaneously. The nose presents immediate notes of youthful optimism, with subtle undertones of "my mom signed me up for this." At first sip, one detects a crisp 17% win rate that finishes with lingering hints of character-building defeats.</p>

<p>The body reveals a fascinating complexity - while most wines improve with age, this particular vintage seems to dilute as it moves up the age brackets, going from a respectable U11 #28 to a more... shall we say, adventurous U19 #465. The 2026 YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC offers a perfect example of her terroir: one win beautifully balanced by three losses, creating that distinctive bitter-sweet flavor profile that defines her entire collection.</p>

<p>One cannot discuss this vintage without noting the remarkable partner rotation - from Ethan Yichen Zhou to Vincent Xiaoyuan ZHANG to a delightful parade of doubles companions, each pairing lasting just long enough to share in the educational experience of defeat. It's rather like a wine flight, if every glass in the flight contained the same disappointed expression and a 1-3 tournament record.</p>

<p>The 2025 collection shows remarkable consistency across venues - whether it's the ABC OLC's complete shutout or the CAN-AM NORCAL OLC's generous 1-5 offering, there's a reliable thread of perseverance that runs through each tournament like a fine vintage that's been left open too long. The sommelier appreciates the dedication to the craft, even if the craft doesn't quite appreciate her back yet.</p>

<p>In conclusion, this is a vintage best appreciated for its potential rather than its current expression. With 553 points spread thin across more ranking categories than a wine has flavor notes, Ella Kung represents the kind of ambitious overreach that would make even the most optimistic sommelier suggest perhaps focusing on fewer varietals until the primary ones are properly developed. Still, one must admire the courage - not everyone would attempt to conquer five age divisions while still requiring a booster seat at the awards banquet.</p>
