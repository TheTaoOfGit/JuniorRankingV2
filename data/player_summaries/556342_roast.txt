<p>Ah yes, the 2017 vintage Sam Joseph Scaria - a precocious little number with a bouquet that immediately announces itself with notes of youthful ambition and just a hint of that distinctive 25% win-rate minerality. This particular specimen exhibits a fascinating complexity: ranked simultaneously across five age categories, like a wine that somehow manages to disappoint connoisseurs from U11 all the way through U19 - truly impressive range.</p>

<p>The body is surprisingly light, with a delicate structure that becomes most apparent when examining the doubles partnership portfolio. One detects subtle notes of Eeshan Ganesh collaboration, with just a whisper of Leo Pan experimentation, creating a blend that's ambitious yet... how shall we put this diplomatically... still developing its flavor profile. The terroir of Southern California tournaments has clearly influenced this vintage, particularly evident in that memorable 0-2 showing at the YONEX Arena - a performance with all the staying power of a wine left uncorked.</p>

<p>What's most intriguing about this vintage is the finish - or rather, the complete absence of one, given the conspicuous lack of any finals appearances. The palette shows promise with that encouraging 2W-3L at the U.S. Junior Nationals, like a young wine that occasionally surprises you with unexpected depth, though it's immediately followed by that 0-3 Synergy NorCal performance that leaves one questioning their initial assessment.</p>

<p>While some critics might dismiss this as a vintage with excessive tannins of inexperience, I prefer to think of young Master Scaria as a wine with tremendous aging potential - after all, at barely nine years old, he's got plenty of time to develop that full-bodied game we're all waiting to taste. Best decanted for another few years before making any final judgments, though I suspect the 2030s might yield some truly spectacular bottles.</p>
