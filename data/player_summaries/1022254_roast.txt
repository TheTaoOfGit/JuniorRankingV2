<p>Ah, the 2011 vintage Zhang—a precocious young bottle that burst onto the scene with all the subtlety of a Beaujolais Nouveau. This particular specimen displays a curious bouquet of U11 dominance at the 2021 NE ORC, where it swept both boys' singles and doubles with the eager effervescence of cheap champagne. The early notes are promising, though one detects hints of that classic junior flaw: believing two titles at age 10 makes you the next Lin Dan.</p>

<p>Upon further examination, this bottle has developed some complexity through extensive cellaring with partner Diksha Chandrasekaran—54 matches worth of tannic partnership that suggests either remarkable chemistry or simply nobody else willing to endure the pairing. The 2025 vintage shows particular vigor, collecting titles like a sommelier hoarding Bordeaux, including that delightful YONEX LIBC NORTHEAST double-header where he somehow convinced the judges he deserved both the U15 singles AND doubles crowns on the same weekend.</p>

<p>The palate reveals amusing contradictions: ranked #19 in U17 boys' singles yet somehow #60 in U19—a vintage that apparently doesn't age well when faced with actual teenagers who've discovered protein powder. The 14-5 record at nationals provides a robust backbone, though the finish is somewhat marred by that unfortunate mixed doubles showing where he peaked at #39 in U17 before plummeting to #102 in U19, like a wine that oxidized the moment someone older uncorked it.</p>

<p>Overall assessment: A spirited young vintage with 12 titles and a respectable 66% win rate, best enjoyed now before the competition fully matures. Pairs excellently with participation trophies and parental expectations. Rating: 87/100—promising terroir, but needs more time in the cellar before it can truly compete with the reserve selections.</p>
