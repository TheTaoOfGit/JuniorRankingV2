<p>⭐⭐⭐ "Zelenia's Shuttlecock Bistro" - Would I return? Maybe for doubles, definitely skip the mixed menu.</p>

<p>Went to this establishment 25 times over the past few years and boy, what a rollercoaster of culinary experiences. The doubles department is clearly where Chef Zelenia shines - her GD U13 #6 ranking is like a perfectly seasoned dish that keeps you coming back. She's got this amazing partnership with Grace Cheng (39 services together!) that's smoother than butter, and when they served up that GD U13 title at the 2025 CAN-AM NORCAL OLC, it was *chef's kiss*. The U11 doubles menu from 2024 was also spectacular - multiple titles including that YONEX SoCal ORC victory.</p>

<p>However, I made the mistake of ordering from the mixed doubles section and... yikes. XD U13 #29? That's like ordering the house special and getting served cafeteria food. The partnership chemistry just isn't there - it's like watching someone try to make a soufflé with a sledgehammer. Kevin Xie tried his best as sous chef for 26 matches, but even he couldn't salvage that trainwreck of a menu item.</p>

<p>The singles offerings are decent but nothing to write home about - GS U13 #11 is solid comfort food, though watching her lose that 2025 CAN-AM NORCAL OLC singles final after winning doubles was like ordering dessert and getting served humble pie instead. The service has been consistently good with a 62% success rate, and the 2025 Junior Nationals performance (13W-6L) showed she can handle the big leagues.</p>

<p>Atmosphere is energetic and the owner clearly has potential, but someone really needs to either remove mixed doubles from the menu entirely or completely revamp that section. Would recommend for doubles dining, but maybe call ahead to confirm Grace Cheng is working that shift.</p>
