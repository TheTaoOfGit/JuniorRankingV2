<p>At just 13 years old, Egbert Zhao has already established himself as one of Southern California's most determined young competitors, logging seven tournaments across three seasons with an unwavering commitment to improvement. His partnership with Eric Yu has become a cornerstone of his doubles strategy, with their six-match collaboration representing more than half of his competitive doubles experience and showcasing his ability to build chemistry with a consistent playing partner.</p>

<p>Zhao's tournament journey began in 2022 with a promising debut at the SoCal CRC, where he posted a balanced 2-2 record that hinted at his competitive potential. His resilience shines through in challenging tournaments like the 2023 YONEX SoCal CRC, where despite facing a tough 0-4 result, he bounced back to compete in three more events that same year, demonstrating the mental fortitude that separates dedicated players from casual participants.</p>

<p>The transition from Under-11 to Under-13 competition marked a significant milestone in his development, with his December 2023 rankings placing him at #61 in boys' singles and #76 in boys' doubles at the U11 level. By February 2025, he had successfully adapted to the increased competition in U13 play, maintaining competitive rankings of #107 in singles and #97 in doubles, proving his ability to evolve his game against older and more experienced opponents.</p>

<p>What sets Zhao apart is his versatility across all three disciplines - singles, doubles, and mixed doubles - a rare quality among junior players who often specialize early. His willingness to compete in mixed doubles, including matches with partner Riya Prasanna, demonstrates both his tactical adaptability and his confidence in playing alongside different partner styles, skills that will serve him well as he continues climbing the junior rankings.</p>
