<p><b>47% career win rate. 46% three-game rate. 4-0 finals record. Anya Virmani is somehow unbeaten in finals but loses everything else.</b></p>

<p>4-0 in finals is genuinely impressive. The problem is the 4-5 semis record, 13-15 three-game record, and 80-90 career record that surround it. Anya is like a student who aces the final exam but fails every quiz -- she shows up when the trophy is on the line and sleepwalks through everything else.</p>

<p>27 tournaments and 4 titles, all from OBA NW OLC and Dave Freeman Jr. OLC -- local events where the draw is thin enough to see through. 15 matches each with Jaden D'Souza and Kavya Goyal, neither of whom appear to be elevating her game. The blowout losses to Mihika Jaiswal and Zaph J-Din Bayona show where the real ceiling is.</p>

<p>From GD U13 #37 to GD U15 #34 across three years is the competitive equivalent of treading water. Anya isn't declining, but she's not improving either.</p>
