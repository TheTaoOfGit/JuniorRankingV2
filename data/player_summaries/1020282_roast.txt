<p><b>Movie Franchise: Abhi Mandanapu</b> — This was supposed to be the billion-dollar franchise. BS U15 #1. BD U15 #4. XD U15 #2. A 79% career win rate (131-34) that reads like peak Marvel. Eight titles including the BD U15 crown at the 2023 Junior Nationals — defeating Evan Liu and Justin Xia, no less. The 15-3 record at the 2023 Nationals was the opening weekend that shattered records.</p>

<p>Then the sequel stalled. BD U19 #56 and BS U19 #27 represent a franchise that went from Avengers: Endgame to a straight-to-streaming release. The 2024 Junior Nationals BS U17 runner-up to Nihal Kadamba was the moment the reviews started to slip. The ride-or-die partnership with Arush Shyam Chitgopkar (49 matches) still produces, but five runner-up finishes suggest the plot armor is wearing thin.</p>

<p>The fundamentals remain terrifying: 79% across 17 tournaments is otherworldly efficiency, and the BS U17 title at the 2025 SoCal ORC (over Justin Xia) proves the franchise still has blockbuster moments. Only 17 tournaments is conspicuously few — is this a quality-over-quantity strategy or a sign of limited availability? Either way, the brand recognition built on that 79% will keep this name on every marquee.</p>
