<p>Ah yes, the 2023 Saki Konishi Nip—a rather ambitious vintage from the junior courts, though I'm afraid this particular bottle has yet to fully mature. Upon first pour, one detects promising aromatic notes of youthful enthusiasm, with subtle hints of that Northwest OLC terroir where she achieved her finest expression, a 9-3 bouquet that briefly fooled the palate into expecting greatness. Alas, the finish revealed itself to be somewhat... unrefined, as she stumbled at the final hurdle in that GS U13 final, leaving connoisseurs with a lingering aftertaste of "what could have been."</p>

<p>The body of this vintage presents curious inconsistencies—while the NorCal ORC pressing yielded a respectable 6-3 extraction, the National Championships bottling was frankly undrinkable, a harsh 1-6 that suggested serious oxidation issues under pressure. One suspects the winemaker (if we may call young Saki that) may have rushed this vintage to market before proper aging, resulting in a rather acidic 18-19 overall composition that lacks the smooth complexity of more seasoned offerings.</p>

<p>The most intriguing aspect of this vintage lies in its blending potential—the Iris Kuan pairing produced twelve separate tastings, suggesting a certain compatibility of terroir, while the brief Ayaaz Muhammed Baig collaboration (six tastings) hinted at untapped potential for mixed varietals. However, the fleeting Akhil Chendur and Marissa Zhang pairings were mere sips—hardly enough to judge the blend's true character.</p>

<p>Currently, this vintage remains unranked in our cellar, a curious bottle that peaked early with those December 2023 rankings (#36 GD, #42 GS, #76 XD) before mysteriously disappearing from our inventory entirely. Perhaps this wine simply needs more time in the cellar—after all, 2011 was an excellent birth year, and sometimes the finest vintages take longer to reach their full potential. I'd recommend cellaring this one for a few more seasons before the next serious tasting.</p>
