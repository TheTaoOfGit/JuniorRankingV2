<p>Ah yes, the 2006 vintage Andrew Xiao - a most curious specimen indeed. Upon first examination, this junior presents with an intriguing bouquet of ambition and inexperience, with subtle notes of defeat lingering throughout. The initial nose suggests promise, having achieved rankings of #65 in boys' doubles and #80 in singles, yet the palate reveals a rather astringent 27% win rate that leaves one puckering with concern.</p>

<p>The terroir of his career shows remarkable consistency - consistently disappointing, that is. From the harsh climate of the 2022 NE NJBC OLC where he managed a pristine 0-4 record, to the slightly more forgiving conditions of 2024 NE MASSBAD OLC where he achieved a rare 3-2 vintage. One detects hints of partnership with the lovely Kamalini Packiyaraj, though even her influence couldn't prevent the wine from turning rather sour over their five encounters together.</p>

<p>The 2024 NE CRC presents this vintage's most fascinating contradiction - a triple finalist finish that somehow manages to taste entirely of defeat. Three finals, three losses, creating what wine experts call a "hat trick of heartbreak" with complex notes of "so close yet so far." The finish here is remarkably short, much like his celebration time between reaching the final and losing it.</p>

<p>Currently unranked after once gracing the junior charts, this vintage has clearly not aged well in the cellar. The oxidation process appears to have stripped away whatever ranking points he once possessed, leaving behind a somewhat flat profile with lingering hints of what might have been. I recommend decanting this player for several more years before expecting any significant improvement in quality.</p>
