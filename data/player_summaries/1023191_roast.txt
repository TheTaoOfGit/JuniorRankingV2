<p>⭐⭐ (2/5 stars) - Went to Aashreeti's Badminton Bistro expecting a decent meal, but left hungrier than when I arrived. The menu promised exciting dishes from venues like "YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals" and "South ORC Specials," but somehow every order came back with a big fat L. Out of 84 attempts, only 13 were edible - that's like a 15% success rate, which would get most restaurants shut down by the health department.</p>

<p>The staffing situation is absolutely chaotic. I've seen this place cycle through partners faster than a mall food court changes employees. Poor Manuel Melwyn Alleluia got stuck working 12 shifts with management, while Wei Yee Pua only lasted 6 before presumably finding better opportunities. Even Hitansh Shah and Afraz Muhammed Baig couldn't stick around long. When your longest-tenured employee is someone whose name sounds like a religious proclamation, you might want to examine your workplace culture.</p>

<p>The most insulting part? This establishment was actually ranked back in 2023 and 2025 - #71 in Girls Doubles U17, #97 in Girls Singles U17. But somehow they've managed to completely disappear from the rankings, like a restaurant so bad it gets removed from Google Maps. It's actually impressive how you can go from being a documented business to being completely unranked. That takes real commitment to mediocrity.</p>

<p>Look, I respect the hustle of staying open for 14 different tournaments since 2021, but at some point you have to wonder if maybe the kitchen just isn't cut out for this. The 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals was particularly brutal - went 0-6, which is restaurant equivalent of running out of every menu item before noon. Would maybe recommend for masochists or people who enjoy the authentic experience of disappointment, but otherwise, plenty of better options in the area.</p>
