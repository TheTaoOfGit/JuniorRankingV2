<p>*swirls racquet thoughtfully* Ah, the 2021-2024 Ishaan Panda vintage—a boldly ambitious collection that promises much on the label but delivers a surprisingly austere experience. This particular blend opens with subtle notes of optimism from the Northwest circuits, though one immediately detects the overpowering tannins of a 1-13 career record that leaves the palate rather... how shall we put this... wanting.</p>

<p>The 2023 U.S. Junior Nationals expression showcases Panda's signature style—a crisp 0-4 finish with absolutely no body whatsoever. One appreciates the consistency here; like a reliable house wine, you know exactly what you're getting. The partnership with Rudra Gaikwad adds interesting complexity to the blend, though even their four-match collaboration couldn't elevate this vintage above its natural terroir of early-round eliminations.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this offering is its complete absence of finals notes—not even a hint of championship fruit. The 2024 Northwest ORC provides a delightfully brief tasting experience, a single-match amuse-bouche that leaves one craving absolutely nothing more. Currently unranked, this vintage has achieved the sommelier's dream of complete transparency—you can see right through it.</p>

<p>While some critics might call this a 7% success rate "disappointing," I prefer to think of it as "efficiently concentrated failure." The Panda collection may lack the robust victories of premium players, but it offers an authentic, unfiltered taste of the junior circuit's more humble expressions. Best served immediately forgotten, pairs well with low expectations.</p>
