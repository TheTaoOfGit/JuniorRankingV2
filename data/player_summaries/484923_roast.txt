<p><b>4-4 in Finals But 4-7 in Three-Gamers: Luke Wen Wins Big and Loses Small</b></p>

<p>Luke Wen has 4 titles, a 4-4 finals record, and a 64% overall win rate — solid numbers that hide a devastating flaw: his 4-7 three-game record (36%). When matches go to a deciding game, Luke loses nearly two-thirds of the time. He's a frontrunner who dominates easy matches and crumbles in tight ones, which makes his titles look like they came from favorable draws rather than competitive dominance.</p>

<p>His 8-3 semis record is excellent, meaning he reaches finals consistently. But those four finals losses prove that he peaks in the penultimate round. Going 1-4 at the 2025 Midwest ORC and 2-4 at the 2025 Midwest CRC shows the Midwest circuit has figured him out.</p>

<p>His 9-1 blitz at the 2026 CBA Midwest OLC (4 titles!) is impressive — but it's one event at a local level propping up otherwise inconsistent results. Partner Pearl Li (11 matches) has been there for the highs and lows. Mostly the lows, when three-gamers are involved.</p>
