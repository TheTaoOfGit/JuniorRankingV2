<p><b>3-6 in Finals and 43% Three-Game Rate: Jeremy Wang Chokes When It Counts</b></p>

<p>Jeremy Wang is 3-6 in finals, which means he loses two-thirds of the championship matches he reaches. His three-game record of 13-17 (43%) confirms what everyone already suspected: when matches get tight, Jeremy finds a way to lose. He's played 19 tournaments with a 62% win rate, so he's clearly talented -- he just has the clutch gene of a houseplant.</p>

<p>His BD U13 #10 ranking makes him a top-10 player, but his 6 runner-up finishes paint a different picture. He lost finals at NorCal ORC, SoCal CRC, Dave Freeman OLC, and more. His primary partner Leslie Wang has been with him for a staggering 50 matches -- that's not a partnership, it's a small business with a terrible P&L statement.</p>

<p>Three titles against six runner-ups means for every time he hoists a trophy, he watches someone else do it twice. His blowout losses include Anirudh Rudharraju and Chiranjeev Deepak from his U11 days, but even now he's getting dismantled by Andy Korlann at the U13 level. Aging up hasn't fixed the finishing problem.</p>
