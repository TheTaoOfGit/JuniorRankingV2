<p>Kylie Ou (born 2016) is an American junior badminton player best known for her mathematically impressive ability to lose exactly as many matches as she wins [citation needed]. With a career record of 34-33 across seven tournaments, Ou has achieved the rare distinction of maintaining a 51% win rate, proving that sometimes the house edge in badminton is approximately zero percent [citation needed]. Her strategic approach to competitive play has been described by analysts as "aggressively mediocre" and "courageously committed to statistical parity" [citation needed].</p>

<p>Ou's tournament career began with a memorable 0-6 performance at the 2024 Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, where she pioneered the "reverse sweep" by losing every single match [citation needed]. However, she demonstrated remarkable character development by returning to the same tournament in 2026 and posting a 10-2 record, leading some observers to theorize that she may have actually been playing a different sport entirely in 2024 [citation needed]. Despite this dramatic improvement, Ou has maintained her perfect record of zero tournament titles, having reached four finals and lost them all with the consistency of a Swiss timepiece [citation needed].</p>

<p>Her most frequent doubles partner, Bowen Wang, has played 15 matches alongside Ou, leading to speculation about whether this constitutes the badminton equivalent of a long-term relationship or a form of competitive masochism [citation needed]. The pair's chemistry has been described as "functional" and "existing," with their partnership enduring through multiple tournaments despite what statisticians call "aggressive normalcy" in their results [citation needed]. Ou has also partnered with Elena Yang, Adalyn Puah, Bella Guo, and Aurora Liu, suggesting either exceptional social skills or a talent for convincing others to share in her precisely calibrated disappointment [citation needed].</p>

<p>Currently ranked as high as #7 in Girls' Singles U11, Ou represents the new generation of American junior players who have mastered the art of being almost good enough [citation needed].
