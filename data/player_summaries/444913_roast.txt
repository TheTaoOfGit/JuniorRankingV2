<p><b>Five Titles and 5-6 Finals Record — But an 11-3 Semis Record? Bryce Xie Only Chokes on the Biggest Stage.</b></p>

<p>Bryce Xie has a fascinating split personality: he's 11-3 in semis (elite) and 5-6 in finals (losing record). He dominates the penultimate round, then enters the final and promptly forgets how to play. His five titles include wins at the NE MassBad OLC, NE CRC, NE OLC, and Wayside NE CRC — all Northeast events, making him the king of a kingdom nobody else is fighting over.</p>

<p>His 56% career win rate and 52% three-game record are unremarkable for a player with five titles. The gap between his semis dominance and finals fragility is the most psychologically interesting stat in the dataset. His primary partner Zechariah Wang (33 matches) at least provides stability, even if that stability sometimes results in a runner-up finish.</p>

<p>The blowout losses to Kevin Xie and Vignan Chandrasekaran in singles show that when he faces genuinely elite players, the semis survival skills mean nothing. Bryce Xie: untouchable in the semis, unreliable in the finals.</p>
