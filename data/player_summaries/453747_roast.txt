<p><b>1-2 in Finals and 3-5 in Semis: Shloka Edunuri Peaks at the Wrong Time</b></p>

<p>Shloka Edunuri has 18 tournaments under her belt with a 49-57 career record -- she's literally below .500. Her one title came at the 2023 South OLC in GS U13, beating Haziqa Mathani, which is approximately the badminton equivalent of winning a pickup game at the park. Her two runner-up finishes show she can get to finals; she just can't win them.</p>

<p>Her three-game record of 15-10 (60%) is actually respectable, but her 3-5 semis record reveals that when the pressure turns up a notch, she turns into a pumpkin. She's been blown out by Kimaya Krishna in both doubles and mixed -- apparently Kimaya has a standing appointment to demolish her in every discipline.</p>

<p>Ranked GS U17 #47, she's firmly in "good but not good enough" territory. The kind of player who makes quarterfinals consistently and makes the audience say "oh right, she's still in this." Background music with a racket.</p>
