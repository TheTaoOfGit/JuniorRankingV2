<p>★☆☆☆☆ - "Atharva Vaishnav: The Underdog Chronicles" is a sports drama that mistakes quantity for quality, featuring our young protagonist's ambitious six-tournament tour de force that somehow manages a 14% success rate. Director Reality clearly had a vision here, but unfortunately that vision appears to have been filmed through a broken shuttlecock.</p>

<p>The film's opening act at the 2023 YONEX Northwest ORC sets the tone with a crisp 0-1 debut that screams "method acting." Vaishnav's commitment to the struggling everyman character is so convincing, you'll genuinely believe he's never held a racquet before. His follow-up performances across the Midwest and South ORCs maintain this dedication to mediocrity with the consistency of a Swiss watch—if that watch only worked 14% of the time.</p>

<p>The supporting cast of doubles partners reads like a revolving door of hope and disappointment. Isha Haldankar, Noah Chien, and the intriguingly named Varad V Vaishnav (nepotism hire, perhaps?) all try valiantly to elevate the material, but even the most generous co-star can't carry a lead who treats the court like it's made of quicksand. The chemistry between Vaishnav and victory remains frustratingly elusive throughout the entire runtime.</p>

<p>While "The Underdog Chronicles" occasionally shows flashes of potential—that miraculous 2-3 performance at the 2024 SOUTH OLC suggests our hero might have accidentally wandered onto the right set—the overall narrative arc feels more like a slow-motion montage of missed opportunities. At 15, Vaishnav still has time for a sequel, but perhaps it's worth investing in a new script first. Currently streaming nowhere near the winner's podium.</p>
