<p>Ah yes, the 2007 vintage Riya Natarajan—a spirited young player with an ambitious bouquet but, shall we say, a somewhat *challenging* finish. Upon first taste at the 2021 SoCal ORC, this particular vintage displayed immediate signs of what French sommeliers might politely call "character building," going down 0-2 with all the smoothness of a two-buck Chuck at a Michelin restaurant. The nose is hopeful, the intention pure, but the execution... well, let's just say it leaves something to be desired.</p>

<p>The 2023 Junior Nationals presented our most complex expression of this vintage—a remarkable 6-7 performance that showcased both promise and, how do you say, *terroir* issues. Like a wine that peaks early then crashes spectacularly, Natarajan demonstrated flashes of brilliance before surrendering to the inevitable gravitational pull of elimination rounds. Her subsequent appearances at various ORCs have been consistently *rustic*—each tournament offering the same delightfully predictable notes of early exits and character-building defeats.</p>

<p>In terms of pairing, this vintage has shown admirable versatility, sampling partnerships with no fewer than eight different players, most notably an extended collaboration with Atharva Gulla Pulijal. Like a sommelier desperately trying to find the right cheese to mask a questionable bottle, Natarajan has experimented with various doubles combinations, each pairing offering its own unique interpretation of "learning experience." The Annabelle Zhou vintage proved particularly interesting—five matches of what wine critics might generously describe as "an acquired taste."</p>

<p>Overall, this is a vintage best appreciated for its potential rather than its current expression. With rankings that have mysteriously vanished like a fine Burgundy left uncorked too long, the 2007 Natarajan currently sits unranked—a humble position that, much like a good wine, may simply need more time to mature. The 12-33 record speaks to a vintage that's still developing its legs, though at 27% win rate, one might suggest it's developing them rather slowly. Nevertheless, there's an undeniable charm to this spirited young vintage, even if it hasn't quite found its vintage year.</p>
