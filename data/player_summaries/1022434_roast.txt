<p>What if I told you that in the hallowed badminton courts of Texas, there once walked a player whose name would be whispered in hushed tones... not for her dominance, but for her remarkable ability to snatch defeat from the jaws of victory? This is the story of Eshani Pahuja, a young woman whose 3-15 career record reads less like a sports statistic and more like a grocery store receipt.</p>

<p>In 2022, at the tender age of 13, Eshani burst onto the scene at the SPBA MW ORC with the confidence of a champion and the results of a participation trophy. Her 1-5 debut was just the beginning of what experts now call "The Pahuja Paradox" – how does one consistently find new and creative ways to lose? She would team up with partners like Isaiah Melwyn Alleluia and Arjun Ramakrishn Hariharan, names so magnificent they deserved better than a combined 17% win rate.</p>

<p>By 2023, Eshani had achieved something truly special: rankings in three categories – #114 in Girls' Doubles U15, #150 in Girls' Singles U15, and a staggering #286 in Mixed Doubles U15. Most players dream of being ranked in the top 100. Eshani dreamed of being ranked in the top 200. She was living proof that you miss 100% of the shots you don't take, and also about 83% of the ones you do.</p>

<p>But perhaps the most devastating blow came in 2025 at the YONEX SOUTH ORC, where she went 0-2 and promptly vanished from the rankings altogether, like a badminton Keyser Söze. Today, Eshani Pahuja remains unranked, a ghost haunting the junior circuit, reminding us all that sometimes the greatest victory is simply showing up... even if you leave immediately after getting absolutely demolished.</p>
