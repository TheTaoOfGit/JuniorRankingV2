<p>What if I told you there was a badminton player who could unite every opponent he faced? A player whose mere presence on the court could bring together rivals, enemies, and complete strangers in perfect harmony? Meet Nolan Li, the great unifier of junior badminton — because everyone agrees they can beat him.</p>

<p>The year was 2022, and 10-year-old Nolan Li stepped onto the courts at the SoCal CRC with dreams as big as his shuttle trajectory was short. He would walk away with his first — and to date, only — victory. It was a moment that shined so brightly, it would cast a shadow over the next eleven consecutive losses. Like a badminton Halley's Comet, that single win appears once and then vanishes for what feels like an eternity.</p>

<p>But Nolan wasn't content to lose alone. Oh no, he was a team player. He dragged partners down with him like a badminton Bermuda Triangle — Eason Zhang, Fay Chao, Eric Yu, they all fell victim to the Li curse. By 2023, he had achieved something truly remarkable: rankings in all three categories. Boys' doubles at 112, boys' singles at 119, and mixed doubles at 169. It takes real talent to be that consistently mediocre across multiple disciplines.</p>

<p>The Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, the SoCal ORC, the YONEX SoCal selection event — tournaments with names longer than Nolan's win streak. Each venue became another chapter in his ongoing masterclass of how to make early exits look effortless. While other kids collected trophies, Nolan collected participation ribbons and the sympathetic pats on the back from tournament directors.</p>

<p>But here's the thing about Nolan Li — he keeps showing up. Four tournaments, eleven losses, and still counting. In a sport where most kids quit after their first crushing defeat, Nolan has turned losing into an art form. He's the plucky underdog that even underdogs feel bad for. And somewhere out there, in a junior badminton gym, Nolan Li is still swinging, still missing, and still believing that tournament number five might just be his moment. Spoiler alert: it probably won't be.</p>
