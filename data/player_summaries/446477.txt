Naksh Shetty has authored one of the most dramatic ascents in recent U.S. junior badminton memory. Born in 2016, the nine-year-old sat outside the top 100 in both boys' singles and boys' doubles at the close of 2023. Twelve months later he had cracked the top 80 and top 53, respectively, and by March 2026 he holds the number-one ranking in U11 boys' singles, U11 boys' doubles, and U11 mixed doubles simultaneously -- while also sitting at number four in U13 boys' singles. His career win rate of 84 percent across 15 tournaments and 138 matches is exceptional by any standard, and his recent run of results tells the story: a 14-1 demolition at the 2025 Midwest ORC where he claimed both the BS U11 and XD U11 titles, followed by a 10-1 performance at the 2025 ABC OLC with another pair of golds, and a dominant 9-1 outing at the 2026 DFW South ORC that added two more titles to his growing collection. Eight tournament titles in barely two years of serious competition is a pace few juniors can match.

What sets Shetty apart is his versatility across all three disciplines. He is equally dangerous in singles, where he has repeatedly beaten Virat Satish and Shrihayagrivan Karthigaya in finals, and in doubles, where he has built a prolific partnership with Xiner Hu -- his most frequent collaborator at 27 matches together. Hu has been his primary mixed doubles partner, and the pair have proven nearly unbeatable at the U11 level. He has also shown he can win with different partners, capturing the BD U11 title at the 2026 DFW South ORC alongside Neel Achari and taking the XD U11 crown at the same event with Prarthana Sanghani. His only real stumbles have come in boys' doubles, where he fell in the finals of the 2025 Midwest ORC and the 2023 Dave Freeman OLC, but even those losses feel like minor footnotes in a trajectory that points unmistakably upward. With his U13 singles ranking already at number four, Shetty is not merely dominating his age group -- he is knocking hard on the door of older competition.
