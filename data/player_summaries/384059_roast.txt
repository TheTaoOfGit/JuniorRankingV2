<p>Ah, the 2016 vintage Hanwen Xiong—a delightfully young specimen that presents with all the complexity of grape juice left in the sun. This particular bottle opens with robust notes of unforced errors and a surprisingly persistent finish of 0-7, creating what can only be described as a perfectly balanced losing streak across three carefully curated tournaments.</p>

<p>The palate reveals layers of vulnerability, beginning with a crisp YONEX LIBC NORTHEAST ORC where our young vintner contributed two losses with the efficiency of a well-oiled disappointment machine. The mid-palate transitions beautifully into a WAYSIDE NE CRC experience, maintaining the same consistent 0-2 profile that has become his signature characteristic. One detects hints of determination, though they remain largely overshadowed by the dominant notes of "still figuring out which end of the racket to hold."</p>

<p>In doubles, this vintage pairs adequately with both Virat Satish and Javen Wu, though like a cheap wine mixed with orange juice, the combination doesn't elevate any of the individual components. The partnership produces a bouquet reminiscent of two people enthusiastically running into each other while the shuttlecock sails past, untouched and victorious.</p>

<p>The ranking progression tells a story of ambitious reach—simultaneously occupying positions from #139 to #914 across various age categories, like a wine that's trying to be everything from a breakfast blend to an after-dinner digestif and succeeding at neither. This 2016 vintage shows tremendous aging potential, which is fortunate because at 0% win rate, it's clearly not ready for consumption yet. Store in a cool, dry place with plenty of coaching for optimal development.</p>
