<p>Ah yes, the 2023 Audrey Li vintage - a delightfully young offering from the MassBad NE OLC terroir. Upon first sip, one immediately detects the bold notes of inexperience, with a crisp 17% win rate that positively sparkles on the palate. The bouquet reveals hints of promise, though perhaps still fermenting in the U11 cellars at a respectable #78 in singles and #83 in doubles.</p>

<p>The body presents as refreshingly light, with a 1-5 finish that leaves you wanting more - specifically, more wins. One can appreciate the partnership notes with Gowri Ashrita Potnuru, creating a doubles blend that, while not yet award-winning, shows potential for future vintages. The tannins are still quite sharp, suggesting this player needs more time to mature in the competitive barrel.</p>

<p>What strikes the discerning palate is the courage of this vintage - diving headfirst into tournament play with the fearlessness only an 8-year-old can possess. The aftertaste lingers with determination, and while the current vintage may not grace the winner's podium, there's something charmingly ambitious about a player who shows up, gets thoroughly outplayed, and presumably asks when the next tournament is.</p>

<p>In conclusion, this is a vintage to cellar and revisit in a few years. The 2023 Li shows promise but lacks the complexity of more seasoned players. Best enjoyed with lowered expectations and the understanding that sometimes the most entertaining wines are the ones still learning how to pour themselves properly. Rating: Two shuttlecocks out of five, with room for improvement.</p>
