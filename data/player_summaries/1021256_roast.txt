<p><b>Video Game Boss: Kimaya Krishna</b> — GD U17 #1 (22,545pts). Let that sink in. This boss has controlled the girls' doubles map since the U13 era and shows no signs of relinquishing the throne. The 72% win rate (196-73 across 27 tournaments) and 8 titles make this the hardest encounter in the U17 division.</p>

<p>The Joanne Li co-op partnership (57 matches) is the game's most dominant duo. Together they've claimed GD titles at the 2023 Junior Nationals, 2022 SPBA MW ORC, 2023 South ORC, 2022 NE ORC, 2023 NE OLC, and the 2021 NE ORC. The Seth Li partnership (51 matches) handles mixed and BD duties. The 2022 Junior Nationals (13-4) was the boss fight that wiped out most challengers.</p>

<p>The GS stats are the one vulnerability in this boss's armor: GS U17 #20 and GS U19 #29 suggest the singles skill tree was neglected in favor of the doubles build. Six runner-up finishes, including the GD U13 loss at the 2022 Junior Nationals to Annie Meng and Maggie Zang and the GD U15 loss at the 2024 NorCal ORC to Joanne Li (her own partner!), are the rare moments when the health bar drops. But with GD U17 #1, 8 titles, and the most feared doubles partnership in junior badminton, Kimaya Krishna is the raid boss everyone prepares for and few defeat.</p>
