<p>Welcome hikers to the Arjun Rajkumar Trail, a winding 12-tournament path through the junior badminton wilderness that's equal parts scenic overlook and treacherous ravine. This moderately difficult trail boasts a 47% success rate, which in hiking terms means you'll reach your destination slightly less often than you'll find yourself lost in the woods wondering how you ended up here. Our trail begins at the 2023 South OLC basecamp, where young Arjun managed to bag not one but TWO BD U11 titles—a rare double summit that remains the highest peak of his expedition to date.</p>

<p>As we venture deeper into the trail system, you'll notice the terrain becomes increasingly challenging. The path winds through the treacherous 2024 U.S. Junior National Championships, a particularly brutal section where our intrepid explorer posted a harrowing 2-6 record, proving that some mountains are best admired from a distance. Wildlife spotters will appreciate Arjun's ability to attract various hiking companions, most notably the faithful Surya Peechara (spotted on 9 separate expeditions) and the reliable Tushaar Praveen (8 sightings), though like most trail partnerships, these tend to end when someone suggests taking the more difficult route.</p>

<p>The trail's most fascinating feature is its unique elevation profile—what rangers call "the ranking rollercoaster." Starting at respectable #77 BD U11 and #49 BS U11 peaks in late 2023, the trail then meanders through some lower elevations before settling into its current comfortable valley positions of #239-546 across various age categories. It's the kind of gentle downhill slope that's easy on the knees but leaves experienced hikers wondering if they took a wrong turn somewhere around the 2024 South OLC junction.</p>

<p>Park Service recommends this trail for beginners who want to experience the full spectrum of junior badminton topography without the risk of altitude sickness from ascending too quickly. While the Arjun Rajkumar Trail may not offer the breathtaking vistas of championship peaks, it provides a solid foundational hike with plenty of character-building switchbacks and the occasional pleasant surprise when you least expect it.</p>
