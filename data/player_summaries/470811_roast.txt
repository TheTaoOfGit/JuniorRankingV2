<p>⭐⭐ "Jaden Ni's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for special occasions</p>

<p>Went to Jaden's establishment twice in 2025 expecting consistent quality, but left disappointed both times. The YONEX MIDWEST ORC experience was particularly underwhelming - ordered the "championship special" but got served a 1-3 loss platter instead. The South OLC visit showed some improvement with a 2-2 sampler, but honestly, going .500 at a mid-tier venue isn't exactly fine dining. Service was enthusiastic but clearly still learning the ropes.</p>

<p>The doubles menu seems to be where this place shines relatively speaking, though "shines" is generous when you're ranked #177 in U13 but somehow #733 in U19 - it's like a restaurant that makes decent appetizers but completely falls apart on the entrees. Koushal and Shubhan seem to be the regular sous chefs here, each working two shifts, though the kitchen chemistry still needs work. William and Jason apparently tried the place once each and decided not to return.</p>

<p>Look, I get it - this is clearly a young establishment (opened around 2014) still finding its identity. The 6-10 lifetime record suggests they're experimenting with the menu, and the 346 points in singles shows they're at least... participating. But when your ranking gets progressively worse as the competition ages up, it's like advertising "We get less appetizing the longer you stay!"</p>

<p>Credit where it's due: they show up consistently and haven't closed down despite never winning a major event or even reaching a finals. That takes dedication. But until Jaden's Bistro figures out how to turn those 1-3 YONEX performances into something more palatable, I'll be dining elsewhere. Two stars for effort and the fact that he's probably still growing into the business.</p>
