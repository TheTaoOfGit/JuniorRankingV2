<p>Luna Han burst onto the junior badminton scene in spectacular fashion at the 2021 Egret OLC, achieving something few players ever accomplish - sweeping all three events she entered. The then-12-year-old claimed titles in Girls' Doubles U15, Mixed Doubles U15, and Girls' Singles U15, announcing herself as one of the most versatile young talents in American badminton. This triple crown performance set the tone for what would become a remarkably consistent career spanning multiple age divisions.</p>

<p>What truly sets Luna apart is her exceptional doubles prowess and ability to forge winning partnerships. Her chemistry with Eli Cui has been particularly noteworthy, with the pair competing together in 18 matches and helping Luna secure multiple finals appearances. She's also found success alongside Aditi Gummadi (16 matches), Justin Xia (12 matches), and others, demonstrating her adaptability and court intelligence. Her 2022 Mixed Doubles U15 title at MBC CRC further solidified her reputation as a doubles specialist who elevates her partners' games.</p>

<p>Luna's competitive fire shines brightest on the biggest stages, evidenced by her impressive tournament record of 78-43 (64% win rate) across 15 major competitions. She's reached finals in both Girls' and Mixed Doubles at the U17 level, including back-to-back appearances at the 2023 Midwest OLC and 2022 MBC CRC. Her ranking progression tells the story of steady growth - from breaking into top-35 in Girls' Doubles U15 to currently sitting at #135 in the U19 division, she continues to compete against increasingly elite competition.</p>

<p>Now competing primarily in the U19 ranks with over 6,250 points in Girls' Doubles, Luna represents the next generation of American badminton talent. Her journey from that breakthrough 2021 season to her current status as a nationally-ranked player showcases not just natural ability, but the dedication and mental toughness required to excel at the highest levels of junior competition.</p>
