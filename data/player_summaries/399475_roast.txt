<p>★★☆☆☆ "Cali Bennett: The Badminton Chronicles" is a plucky underdog story that unfortunately forgets to include the part where the underdog actually wins. Director Reality serves up a five-year epic spanning from 2021's disastrous debut at Egret OLC (a brutal 2-6 opening that would make M. Night Shyamalan proud) to this year's scoreless swan song at MIDWEST CRC. Bennett's 31% win rate suggests she's either playing badminton or auditioning for a very depressing sports documentary.</p>

<p>The film's casting choices are particularly fascinating—Bennett cycles through doubles partners like a romantic comedy protagonist who can't commit. Pratham Bansal gets top billing with 10 matches, followed by Arushi Panda's 8-match supporting role, while Emma Yin and Rucha Rajadhyax make brief cameos. It's giving "Love Actually" vibes, if Love Actually were about consistently losing badminton matches instead of finding romance in London.</p>

<p>The plot reaches its dramatic peaks in two finals appearances—2023 MIDWEST OLC and 2024 MIDWEST CRC—where Bennett masterfully builds tension by getting tantalizingly close to glory before remembering she's the protagonist of a tragedy, not a triumph. These runner-up finishes serve as the film's climactic moments, though "climactic" might be generous when describing someone who went 0-2 in her most recent tournament appearance.</p>

<p>To Bennett's credit, the character development arc shows genuine promise: her rankings improved from U17 #95 to U19 #53 in girls' doubles, proving that sometimes the best stories are about small victories against overwhelming odds. "Cali Bennett: The Badminton Chronicles" may not be a blockbuster, but it's an honest portrayal of athletic perseverance—even if that perseverance mostly involves showing up to lose matches with admirable consistency.</p>
