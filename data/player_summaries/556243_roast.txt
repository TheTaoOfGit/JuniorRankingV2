<p>⭐☆☆☆☆ Visited Katherine's Badminton Bistro twice this year and honestly, I'm not sure how this place is still open. The YONEX ARENA SOCAL ORC location in January served up a disappointing 0-3 tasting menu, while the SGVBC SOCAL CRC branch somehow managed an even more ambitious 0-4 course disaster. The consistency is actually impressive – consistently empty-handed.</p>

<p>The kitchen staff seems confused about their roles. Katherine pairs herself with different sous chefs like Leo Huang and Sahir Bhuiyan, but the chemistry is all wrong. It's like watching someone try to make a soufflé with a toaster. Even when Emily Chiang (possibly a family member trying to help?) joined the team, the results were the same: a perfectly executed recipe for disappointment, served fresh with a side of 0% success rate.</p>

<p>I have to admire the ambition though – Katherine's is trying to compete in FOUR different dining categories simultaneously (U13, U15, U17, U19), which explains why she's spread so thin. Currently ranked #141 in the GS U13 division and somehow #926 in XD U19, it's like a food truck trying to serve both kids' meals and fine dining to adults. Pick a lane!</p>

<p>The most baffling part is watching her rankings slide backwards – she was #102 in March but has somehow worked her way down to #141. That's like a restaurant getting worse reviews the longer it stays open. At this rate, Katherine's Badminton Bistro might achieve the rare feat of negative stars. But hey, she's only been in business since 2015, so there's plenty of time to perfect the recipe. Maybe try focusing on one dish at a time?</p>
