<p>Ah yes, the 2014 vintage Mariia Filimonova - a charmingly unpredictable offering from the Midwest terroir that presents with all the consistency of a bottle that's been stored sideways in direct sunlight. Upon first inspection, this young vintage displays a modest 57% clarity, though connoisseurs will note the curious phenomenon where her rankings actually *descend* with age - dropping from U13 #83 to #103 in girls doubles, as if she's aging in reverse like a Benjamin Button shuttlecock.</p>

<p>The nose reveals complex notes of partner rotation, having sampled no fewer than five different doubles combinations across her brief four-tournament portfolio. There's a particularly intriguing pairing with Annabeth Pan that appears twice on the palate, though neither collaboration produced anything approaching a vintage worth cellaring. The bouquet carries undertones of the 2025 MIDWEST CRC, where she achieved the rare feat of going 0-2, a finish so clean it left no trace whatsoever.</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage oscillates wildly between extremes - the 2025 EGRET MIDWEST OLC presents as a surprisingly bold 4-0 sweep, full-bodied and confident, while the very next tournament sees her crashing back to earth like a poorly executed clear shot. It's the kind of inconsistency that would make a sommelier question their taste buds, or perhaps suggest the cork has gone bad.</p>

<p>The finish is notably absent - zero titles, zero finals, just a pleasant aftertaste of mediocrity with hints of unrealized potential. While this vintage shows promise for future development, current market conditions suggest it's best enjoyed immediately rather than saved for special occasions, as extended cellaring appears to diminish rather than enhance its qualities.</p>
