<p>★★☆☆☆ Went to Japjit's Badminton Bistro hoping for a quality dining experience, but left feeling like I ordered the tasting menu and got served a single sad appetizer. The 2025 Bellevue location had a soft opening that went about as well as you'd expect - zero courses served, one very disappointed customer (me). The chef seemed overwhelmed by the kitchen pressure and folded faster than origami in a hurricane.</p>

<p>Decided to give the February 2026 Seattle location another shot, and honestly, there was some improvement! The kitchen managed to plate up 3 decent dishes before completely running out of steam and burning the next 5. The sous chef Mausam Darji seems to be carrying most of the workload here - bless that partnership because without it, this place would've shuttered after the Bellevue disaster. They did try bringing in Marissa Zhang for one shift, but that collaboration was shorter than a TikTok video.</p>

<p>The most baffling thing about this establishment? They somehow have three different locations (U15, U17, U19) but serve the exact same 729-point prix fixe menu at all of them. It's like having a McDonald's, a Burger King, and a Wendy's that all mysteriously serve identical sad desk salads. The rankings suggest she's #211 at the flagship location but drops to #422 at the upscale spot - which tells you everything about consistency here.</p>

<p>Look, the chef is young and clearly still learning the ropes, so there's potential for growth. The 33% satisfaction rate isn't great, but hey, at least she showed up to work in Seattle and actually served some food. Would I recommend? Maybe in a few years when she's had time to develop her signature dishes. For now, it's giving "food truck that just got its permit" energy.</p>
