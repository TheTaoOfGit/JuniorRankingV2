<p>Jeffrey Chang's explosive rise through junior badminton has been powered by an exceptional doubles game that has netted him six titles across multiple disciplines. The 2006-born athlete made his mark early, capturing the coveted boys doubles U17 championship at the 2022 Junior National Championships while simultaneously reaching the singles final—a rare feat that showcased his versatility at the sport's highest junior level.</p>

<p>Chang's tournament resume reads like a masterclass in consistency and clutch performance. His dominant 2021 campaign included a near-perfect 9-1 record at the SoCal ORC, where he swept both boys doubles and singles titles, followed by an impressive 16-7 showing at the NorCal CRC that yielded two more championships. Even when facing setbacks, Chang has demonstrated remarkable resilience, bouncing back from tough losses to claim the boys doubles title at the 2022 Synergy NorCal ORC.</p>

<p>The foundation of Chang's success lies in his exceptional partnership chemistry, particularly with frequent doubles partner Kyle Wang, with whom he has contested 27 matches. This collaboration has been complemented by successful pairings across mixed doubles with Alice Y Wang and Kai Chong, highlighting his adaptability and court awareness. His seven finals appearances across different events underscore his ability to elevate his game when it matters most.</p>

<p>By December 2023, Chang had established himself as a force in U19 competition, earning rankings of #29 in boys doubles, #7 in boys singles, and #10 in mixed doubles. With a career win percentage of 66% across 99 victories, Chang represents the new generation of American badminton talent—technically sound, mentally tough, and ready to make his mark on the senior circuit.</p>
