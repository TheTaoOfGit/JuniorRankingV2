<p>★★½ "Devansh Ponnapalli: The Almost Champion" - In this coming-of-age sports dramedy, our 10-year-old protagonist delivers a performance that's equal parts promising and maddeningly incomplete. Director Junior Tennis clearly had high hopes for this production, but what we get is a film that knows how to build toward a climax but fumbles every landing. Think "Rocky" if Rocky kept making it to the final bell only to trip over his own shoelaces.</p>

<p>The plot centers around Ponnapalli's uncanny ability to reach the mountaintop in boys' doubles, only to discover he's brought a parachute instead of climbing gear. Four separate BD U11 finals - at Fortius South, Yonex Arena SoCal, South OLC, and Peak Sports South - all end with our hero in the runner-up position, like a badminton Groundhog Day where Bill Murray keeps double-faulting at match point. His #4 national ranking in the division suggests serious talent, but those silver medals are piling up faster than rejected script revisions.</p>

<p>The supporting cast is led by frequent co-star Arudh Sundar, who appears in 23 scenes alongside Ponnapalli in what can only be described as the most productive buddy-cop partnership since Lethal Weapon. Together they've created a doubles dynamic that's simultaneously dominant and cursed - capable of dismantling opponents with surgical precision, yet apparently allergic to championship ceremonies. Supporting players Elina Xiao-En Yang and Bhavanyaa Arunkumar provide solid performances in their limited mixed doubles roles, though the script seems to forget about them whenever the real drama begins.</p>

<p>Where this film really loses steam is in its ambitious multi-timeline narrative, attempting to juggle storylines across five different age divisions simultaneously. While Ponnapalli shines in his natural U11 habitat, his ventures into older age groups feel like a child actor trying to play Hamlet - technically proficient but clearly out of his depth. His U19 rankings (#498 in singles, #399 in doubles) suggest the casting director may have gotten a bit ahead of themselves.</p>

<p>Despite its flaws, there's undeniable charm in watching this young performer grow into his role. The character arc from #73 to #15 in singles and #58 to #4 in doubles over the course of a year shows genuine development, even if the final act keeps getting rewritten. "Devansh Ponnapalli: The Almost Champion" may not win any awards this season, but it's definitely worth watching for the inevitable sequel where he finally learns to close.</p>
