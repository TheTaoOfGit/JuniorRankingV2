<p>Ah yes, the 2007 vintage Aryan Cimala - a curious specimen that arrived with modest expectations and has largely delivered on them. Upon first pour, one detects immediate notes of inconsistency, with a 44% win rate that suggests a wine still finding its legs. The initial bouquet carries hints of that glorious 2023 NE OLC triumph in boys' doubles U17, where he peaked at 6-1 with partner Srikrishna Sriganesh - a fleeting moment of brilliance that teases the palate before dissolving into mediocrity.</p>

<p>The mid-palate reveals a fascinating complexity of partnerships, with young Cimala seemingly unable to settle on a doubles companion the way a sommelier can't decide between a Bordeaux and a Burgundy. After 17 matches with Sriganesh, he's dabbled with Puneeth Kumar Anumala for 8 outings, then flirted briefly with mixed doubles partners Ahana Bhat and Ravya Senthilkumar - a romantic portfolio that suggests commitment issues more suited to a college freshman than a developing vintner.</p>

<p>The 2024 vintage shows particular character flaws, with that heartbreaking final loss at SOUTH OLC in boys' doubles U19 - close enough to taste greatness but finishing with all the satisfaction of a corked bottle. His journey to the US Junior Nationals produced a respectable 4-6 showing, though one suspects the tannins of defeat lingered longer than the sweetness of those victories.</p>

<p>Currently unranked and aging toward his expiration as a junior, this vintage shows limited complexity but surprising persistence. While lacking the bold finish of champions, Cimala offers an honest, workmanlike character - the kind of reliable house wine you'd serve at a family barbecue, knowing it won't offend anyone but certainly won't be the conversation piece either. Best consumed now, as the aging potential appears... limited.</p>
