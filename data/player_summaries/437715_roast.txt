<p><b>87% Three-Game Win Rate. Sahana Samarthy Thrives in Chaos — Because Her Regular Play Isn't Good Enough to Win in Two.</b></p>

<p>Sahana Samarthy has a stunning 7-1 three-game record, which sounds elite until you ask why so many of her matches go to three games in the first place. She wins 87% of tight matches because she can't win 87% of easy ones — her overall career record is 41-46, a losing 47%. She needs the drama of a decider to activate whatever competitive gene is dormant during normal play.</p>

<p>Her 2-3 finals record and 5-2 semis record show she can survive the semis gauntlet but crumbles on the biggest stage. Two titles at the CBA Midwest OLC are nice, but they came in a single tournament — so she basically had one good weekend and has been dining out on it ever since. Her 0-3 at the 2026 South ORC is the more representative result.</p>

<p>The blowout losses to Erica Chen Lin, Vindhya Chekuru, and Mihika Jaiswal reveal the truth: against actually good players, the three-game magic vanishes because those matches never make it to a third game.</p>
