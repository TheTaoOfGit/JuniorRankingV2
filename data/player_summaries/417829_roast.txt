<p>Ah, the Hanyi Lei vintage - a precocious 2013 pressing that burst onto the scene with remarkable early promise. The initial bouquet reveals notes of U11 dominance, with a particularly robust 2022 Midwest CRC and those delightful 2023 YONEX Northwest twin victories creating an almost overwhelming aroma of youthful confidence. However, much like a wine that peaks too early, one detects the faintest hint of sulfur from those seven finals losses - a rather unfortunate oxidation that suggests this vintage may have been uncorked before proper aging.</p>

<p>On the palate, we discover a complex partnership blend, most notably the Daisy Xu varietal that appears in no fewer than 26 matches - clearly this sommelier's preferred mixing companion. The texture shows remarkable consistency with that 61% win rate, though connoisseurs will note the curious tang of a 4W-6L performance at the 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals, which leaves one wondering if perhaps the terroir wasn't quite ready for such prestigious soil.</p>

<p>The current rankings present an interesting study in vertical tasting - U15 #20 in singles shows promise, while that U19 #227 in mixed doubles suggests this vintage definitely benefits from age-appropriate consumption. The finish is surprisingly long, with echoes of those early U11 triumphs still lingering, though seasoned tasters might detect just a whisper of "what might have been" had those seven silver medals been properly converted to gold.</p>

<p>In summary, the Lei 2013 is a perfectly serviceable table wine - not quite ready for the cellar of elite junior champions, but certainly pleasant enough for casual consumption at your local ORC gathering. Pairs well with modest expectations and a strong doubles partner.</p>
