<p>Sophie Pan (born 2008) is an American badminton player widely recognized for her groundbreaking contributions to the field of competitive losing.[citation needed] Pan has established herself as a dominant force in the art of early tournament exits, boasting an impressive 3-17 career record that has inspired mathematicians to question whether win percentages can actually achieve negative values.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Pan's tactical brilliance is perhaps best demonstrated by her revolutionary "0W Strategy," which she has successfully implemented at three separate tournaments including the 2023 Midwest OLC, 2024 MIDWEST OLC, and 2025 YONEX MIDWEST ORC.[citation needed] This groundbreaking approach to tournament participation has led badminton analysts to propose renaming the strategy "The Pan Protocol" in honor of her consistent execution.[citation needed] Her only deviation from this winning formula occurred at the 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC, where she inexplicably won three matches, an aberration she quickly corrected at subsequent tournaments.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Known for her diverse partnership portfolio, Pan has mastered the art of spreading disappointment across multiple doubles partners, most notably collaborating with Avyukt Iyer on three separate occasions to explore new frontiers in synchronized failure.[citation needed] Her strategic partner rotation system, featuring Sophie Hou, Oliver Nguyen, Veda Sivakumar, and Callie Wu, has been praised by sports psychologists as an innovative method for ensuring that no single player bears the full burden of her unique playing style.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite critics questioning her methods, Pan has achieved remarkable consistency in her rankings, maintaining positions that hover comfortably in the triple digits across all disciplines.[citation needed] Her current standings of GS U19 #212, GD U19 #205, and XD U19 #406 represent what her coaching team calls "strategic market positioning" in the competitive badminton landscape.[citation needed] Remarkably, Pan has managed to rank 201 spots lower in mixed doubles than girls doubles, a feat that has led to speculation about the existence of previously unknown laws of physics.[citation needed]</p>
