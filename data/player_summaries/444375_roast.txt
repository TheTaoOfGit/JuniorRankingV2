<p>Welcome to the Aarav Gupta Memorial Trail, a moderately challenging 20-tournament trek through the badminton wilderness that's left more hikers confused than inspired. This scenic route, established in 2023 by a then-12-year-old trail blazer, features an impressive 63% success rate in reaching designated checkpoints, though seasoned guides note an alarming tendency for our young pathfinder to pitch camp just short of the summit six different times.</p>

<p>The trail begins promisingly at the 2023 South OLC base camp, where young Aarav claimed his first BD U13 trophy before the terrain turned treacherous. Hikers will encounter the notorious "False Summit Ridge" section, a cruel geological formation that has claimed six finals appearances—including back-to-back peaks at the 2025 South OLC where he managed to lose both the BS U17 and BD U17 finals like a hiker who packed two left boots.</p>

<p>The most perilous section remains the 2024 U.S. Junior Nationals Valley of Despair, where our intrepid explorer posted a catastrophic 4-8 record that left even the local wildlife feeling embarrassed. However, environmental conditions improved dramatically by 2025, when Aarav returned to the same location with his trusty trail companion Saee Thorat (spotted together on 20 separate expeditions) and managed a respectable 13-4 recovery, proving that even the most lost hikers can eventually find their compass.</p>

<p>The trail concludes at the current scenic overlook, where Aarav has established comfortable base camps at BS U17 #18 and BD U17 #16—solid middle-tier elevations that offer pleasant views without the oxygen deprivation of true peak performance. Park rangers recommend this trail for intermediate adventurers who enjoy a journey with plenty of "almosts" and the occasional genuine triumph, though they advise bringing extra provisions for those inevitable extended stays at "Runner-Up Ridge."</p>
