Jacob Ma has rocketed up the boys' rankings over the past two years and now stands as one of the most versatile male players in the U15 division, ranked No. 2 in singles, No. 3 in doubles, and No. 7 in mixed doubles. Born in 2012, Ma carries a career record of 181-69 across 22 tournaments -- a 72 percent win rate that is among the best in the country for his age group. His progression has been remarkable: in December 2023 he was ranked No. 30 in BS U13 and a distant No. 92 in BD U13. By December 2024 he had claimed the BS U13 No. 1 ranking and won the national championship in that event, defeating Srineet Atluri. Now, at the U15 level, he continues to climb, with his BS U15 ranking jumping from No. 5 to No. 2 and his BD U15 from No. 8 to No. 3 in the span of nine months.

Ma's doubles game is particularly fascinating because of the sheer number of high-quality partnerships he maintains. He has logged 25 matches with Sophia Sun in mixed doubles, 22 with Lexuan Wen, 19 with Steven Lei, 18 with Jacob Zhou, and 16 with Carsten Lam -- rotating partners across events while remaining effective with all of them. His BD U15 title at the 2026 SoCal ORC, won alongside Jacob Zhou, and his XD U17 title at the 2026 Midwest OLC highlight his ability to win with different partners at different levels. The rivalry with Srineet Atluri has become the defining matchup of his career: Atluri and his partners have beaten Ma in three consecutive XD U15 finals (Northeast ORC, NorCal ORC, and SoCal ORC) and edged him in the BD U15 final at the 2026 DFW South ORC. Ma has also tested himself against U17 and even U19 competition, reaching the BD U17 final at the 2025 Midwest CRC and the BD U19 final at the 2026 Midwest OLC. With his 11-2 performance at the 2026 SoCal ORC and consistent double-digit win totals at major events, Ma is building the kind of resume that suggests a national title in U15 may not be far away.
