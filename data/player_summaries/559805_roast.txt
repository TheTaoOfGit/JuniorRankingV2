<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Ethan Li's Badminton Bistro based on the hype, but honestly? The service was slower than a drive-thru during lunch rush. This establishment has been operating since 2017 but somehow still feels like they're figuring out the basic menu. The signature dish appears to be "Participation Points" - they serve exactly 474 of them in singles and 499 in doubles, no matter what you order.</p>

<p>The atmosphere is... enthusiastic? Ethan really commits to the experience, bouncing around the court like he's trying to personally greet every shuttlecock in the building. His doubles partnerships with Thomas Ho were decent - they had that "two kids sharing a Happy Meal" energy that was actually kind of charming. The collaboration with Zelvin Kim was more of a limited-time special that didn't quite catch on.</p>

<p>Location-wise, they've tried expanding to multiple venues - YONEX ARENA, DAVE FREEMAN JR., and SGVBC - but the consistency is questionable. At the Dave Freeman location, they literally served up a goose egg (0 wins, 1 loss). The SGVBC branch showed some promise with a 2-3 record, but that's like saying the appetizers were better than the main course when the whole meal still left you hungry.</p>

<p>Look, I get that this is a young establishment trying to find its footing in a competitive market, but when you're ranked #711 in the U19 category, maybe it's time to revise the business plan? The potential is there - recent rankings show slight improvement from #48 to whatever black hole of badminton rankings exists beyond that. Would I come back? Maybe in a few years when they've had time to perfect their craft. Currently accepting reservations for 2030.</p>
