<p>⭐⭐ "Matthew Wu's Badminton Bistro" - Would not recommend for a special occasion. Walked into this establishment with high hopes after seeing they'd been operating since 2022, but left feeling like I'd been served a 23% success rate on a silver platter. The menu promises exciting matchups across multiple age divisions, but consistently delivers disappointment with a side of "maybe next tournament."</p>

<p>The service is painfully slow - took 17 visits just to rack up 14 wins, and don't even get me started on the complete absence of any signature dishes (zero titles, zero finals). The chef keeps experimenting with different kitchen partners like Derik S Li and Enming Cao, but no matter who's helping in the doubles department, the final product always seems to fall flat. At least they're consistent - consistently ranked in the #400s when you order from the U19 menu.</p>

<p>The atmosphere varies wildly depending on which location you visit. The 2025 SCHAFER SLG SOUTH OLC was actually decent (3W-2L - their best performance!), but every other venue from YONEX South ORC to the various regional locations has been a complete disaster. The 2022 Northeast ORC was particularly traumatic - went 0-4 and left hungry for literally any kind of victory.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due: the establishment has shown some improvement since opening. The rankings have actually climbed from #89 to #124 in doubles, which is like going from "completely inedible" to "technically food." The owner seems determined to keep trying new recipes and partnerships, which is admirable if not effective. Maybe stick to the U15 menu where they're only #159 - it's their least disappointing option.</p>

<p>Overall: If you're looking for a guaranteed win, eat literally anywhere else. But if you enjoy the thrill of very low expectations and want to support a young business that's still figuring out the recipe for success, Matthew Wu's Badminton Bistro might serve up just enough hope to keep you coming back. Just don't expect to leave satisfied.</p>
