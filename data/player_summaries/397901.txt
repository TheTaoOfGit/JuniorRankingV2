Kevin Xie is the top-ranked U13 boys' singles player in the country and one of the most battle-tested competitors in the junior circuit, with 25 tournaments and a career record of 194-64 under his belt. Born in 2014, Xie's rise from U11 number nine in late 2023 to U13 number one in singles and number two in doubles by March 2026 reflects a player who has consistently leveled up as the competition around him has intensified. His 6,495 points in BS U13 lead the division, and his 7,334 points in BS U15 place him seventh nationally among players two years his senior -- a clear signal that he belongs at the higher age group. Yet Xie's path has not been a straight line of dominance. His recent tournament history includes a string of finals losses: runner-up finishes at the 2025 Midwest ORC in both BS U13 and BD U13, a BS U13 loss at the 2025 Northeast ORC, and dual runner-up results at the 2026 SoCal ORC and 2026 DFW South ORC in doubles and mixed. The recurring obstacle in many of those defeats has been the partnership of Vignan Chandrasekaran and Michael Sun, a duo that has become Xie's defining rivalry at the U13 level.

The constants in Xie's game are his doubles partnerships and his ability to collect points across all three disciplines. Anirudh Rudharraju has been his primary doubles partner for 68 matches -- by far his most significant collaboration -- and together they swept through the U11 circuit in 2023-24, taking BD titles at the Northwest, Midwest, NorCal, and Northeast ORCs. As both players have aged into U13, that partnership has run into stiffer resistance, particularly from Sun and Chandrasekaran, but the volume of their shared experience gives them a tactical cohesion that few pairs can replicate. In mixed doubles, Xie has rotated through several partners, with Claire QiuChen Li (30 matches) and Zelenia Shen (25 matches) seeing the most court time alongside him. His XD U13 ranking of number three reflects solid but not dominant returns in that discipline, and the gap between his singles ceiling and his mixed results suggests that finding the right long-term XD partner could be the key to a complete sweep of the U13 rankings. At a career 75-percent win rate and with a 10-3 showing at the most recent SoCal ORC, Xie remains firmly in the conversation as one of the most complete young players in the country.
