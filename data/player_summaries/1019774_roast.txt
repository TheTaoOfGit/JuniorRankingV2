<p>Sydney Li (born 2009) is an American badminton player best known for her uncanny ability to win mixed doubles tournaments while simultaneously proving that two X chromosomes don't automatically make good doubles partners[citation needed]. Currently ranked #1 in XD U19 with an intimidating 44,000 points, Li has perfected the art of being carried by male partners to such an extent that her girls doubles ranking sits at a modest #39, leading many to wonder if she's allergic to playing with her own gender[citation needed].</p>

<p>Li's tournament career began with a bang at the 2021 USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships, where she won GD U13 before apparently discovering that boys exist and promptly abandoning any pretense of same-sex success. Her 2022 NE CRC performance remains legendary, going a perfect 13-0 while collecting titles in both girls singles and mixed doubles, though sources suggest she spent the entire tournament asking male competitors for their phone numbers[citation needed]. This was followed by her dominant 22-2 showing at the 2022 South Frisco ORC, where she won multiple titles and established herself as the undisputed queen of "I'll take mixed doubles, you can have girls doubles."</p>

<p>Perhaps most telling is Li's partnership history, where Rocky Yang leads with 26 matches played together, presumably because he's strong enough to literally carry her across the court[citation needed]. Her collaboration with Chloe A Kai (24 matches) represents her most sustained attempt at same-gender cooperation, though their results suggest they spent more time braiding each other's hair than practicing their serves. With 19 career titles and a 74% win rate, Li has proven that strategic gender selection in doubles can overcome any technical deficiencies, though her 10 finals losses serve as a reminder that even the best male partners can't win everything[citation needed].</p>

<p>Currently residing at #10 in girls singles, Li continues to mystify coaches who can't understand how someone so dominant in mixed doubles can struggle when deprived of testosterone-fueled assistance[citation needed]. Her recent performances at the 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships (5-6 record) and 2025 YONEX SYNERGY NORCAL ORC (4-5 record) suggest that as the competition gets fiercer, even the most reliable male partners can't compensate for what appears to be a fundamental aversion to winning without masculine support.</p>
