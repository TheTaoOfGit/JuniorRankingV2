<p>Ah yes, the 2007 vintage Linden Wang—a promising junior bouquet that opened with remarkable early notes but has since developed some concerning oxidation. Upon first nosing this specimen in 2021, one detected the bright, effervescent qualities of a U15 National Champion, with delightful hints of doubles mastery alongside Eva Wang. The initial palate was exceptionally clean, boasting six titles across various age divisions with the confident structure one expects from premium terroir.</p>

<p>The 2022-2023 period reveals a wine in transition, displaying the complex tannins of someone aging out of junior categories. While the South Frisco ORC vintage yielded an impressive 22-5 showing, connoisseurs noted a slightly bitter finish in those U17 singles finals losses. The partnership with Emily Liu—22 matches of harmonious blending—provided excellent balance, though one detects hints of over-reliance on familiar varietals rather than bold experimentation with new court positions.</p>

<p>By 2024, this once-promising vintage had developed a peculiar character—still capable of producing the occasional gem like that YONEX Midwest ORC U19 singles title, yet showing disturbing signs of decline. The YONEX Northwest ORC performance (4-5) suggested a wine past its prime, with the crispness of youth replaced by the murky complexity of someone discovering that U19 competition doesn't taste quite as sweet as those U15 glory days.</p>

<p>The current unranked status represents a fascinating study in terroir degradation. What began as a medal-worthy vintage with #3 U19 singles ranking has aged into something more suitable for casual consumption—still drinkable at local club level, but hardly the championship caliber one might serve at formal competitions. A cautionary tale of early promise versus sustained excellence, best enjoyed with modest expectations and perhaps a strong mixed doubles partner as accompaniment.</p>
