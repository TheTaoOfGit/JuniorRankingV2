<p><b>Two Titles in 13 Tournaments and a BS U15 #65 Ranking. Nithesh Madishetti: Firmly Mid.</b></p>

<p>Nithesh Madishetti has two titles — both from the Midwest CRC and Midwest OLC circuits, the minor leagues of junior badminton. His 58% win rate is respectable, and his 2-2 finals record shows he can compete when he makes it to the big stage. The problem is he rarely makes it, with a 4-3 semis record that's not bad but not exactly dominant.</p>

<p>His 53% three-game record places him squarely in "average" territory, and his progression from BS U11 #115 to BS U15 #65 suggests a long, slow climb toward mediocrity. His 18 matches with Bavish Reddi Dornala make them the Midwest's most committed middling doubles team.</p>

<p>The blowout losses to Kevin Xie, Anirudh Rudharraju, and Leon Luo confirm that whenever Nithesh faces an elite player, it's not a match — it's an execution. At least he beat Tinghao Tan for one of his titles. Living the dream, one small Midwest tournament at a time.</p>
