<p>⭐⭐ "Vincent Zhang Badminton Experience" - Would not recommend for a special occasion. Went to this establishment 11 times hoping for consistent service, but left disappointed 37 times out of 53 attempts. The menu promises exciting matches across multiple categories (U11 through U19), but delivery is spotty at best. Their signature "win percentage" dish comes in at a measly 30% - I've had better odds at gas station sushi.</p>

<p>The doubles experience with partner Ethan Yichen Zhou (tried it 12 times!) shows some chemistry, but the final product still lacks that championship flavor. Andy Liu collaboration was brief but unremarkable - only 4 attempts before they moved on to other ventures. The mixed doubles with Yvonne Chen Yang had potential with 3 visits, but again, no memorable finishes. It's like they're still working out the recipe.</p>

<p>Location seems to be all over the map - from the 2026 Dave Freeman Jr. SoCal (mediocre 2W-2L showing) to the prestigious U.S. Junior Nationals where they served up a forgettable 4W-4L, then an even more disappointing 2W-4L on the return visit. The Yonex Bellevue Northwest location was particularly rough - 0W-4L is basically the restaurant equivalent of serving you an empty plate and still charging full price.</p>

<p>Credit where credit's due: there has been some improvement from the early days. Rankings jumped from #60 to #16 in BD U11 and #79 to #19 in BS U11, so maybe the kitchen is finally finding its rhythm. At 9 years old, this establishment is clearly still figuring out its identity. The potential is there, but right now you're paying premium tournament prices for what feels like a work-in-progress. Maybe check back in a few years when they've had time to mature their offerings.</p>
