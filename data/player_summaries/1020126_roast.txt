<p>Committee Notes: Justin Han - Application for Admission to "Players Who Actually Win Sometimes" University. Initial review by Dr. Patricia Shuttleworth, Professor of Athletic Mediocrity Studies. Mr. Han's academic transcript in badminton presents a fascinating case study in consistency - specifically, his consistent ability to hover just below the break-even point with a remarkable 42% career win rate. One might call it mathematical precision if it weren't so mathematically depressing.</p>

<p>His extracurricular involvement is noteworthy for its breadth rather than depth. Like a social butterfly with commitment issues, Mr. Han has partnered with no fewer than seven different doubles partners in his quest to find someone who might actually help him win a tournament. Yelang Lee, Tsun Wang Lam, Martin Wu, Prutha Ramaprakas Bharadwaj, and Arida Narayan have all had the privilege of sharing court time with our applicant - though none apparently long enough to develop any sort of championship chemistry. We admire his optimism in continuing to believe the next partnership will be "the one."</p>

<p>Mr. Han's performance at the 2021 USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships deserves special mention, where he achieved a 5-6 record that perfectly encapsulates his entire career philosophy: "So close to .500, yet so far." His subsequent appearances at various ORCs (Oregon Regional Championships, for those keeping score at home) read like a tour of the Pacific Northwest's most scenic losing venues. The 0-5 showing at the 2022 Synergy NorCal ORC was particularly impressive in its thoroughness.</p>

<p>Current status shows Mr. Han as "unranked," which the Admissions Committee interprets as either a clerical error or a merciful act by the ranking officials. His peak achievement of #34 in U17 Boys Singles suggests he was once within shouting distance of relevance, though shouting distance in badminton is admittedly quite limited given the sport's emphasis on quiet concentration.</p>

<p>Recommendation: CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE to our "Participation Trophy" program. While Mr. Han may never trouble the trophy engravers, his dedication to showing up and losing with dignity is exactly the kind of reliable mediocrity our institution values. Plus, his extensive experience in defeat should make him an excellent doubles partner for our other admittees - someone has to lose, after all.</p>
