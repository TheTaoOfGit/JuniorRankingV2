<p>Arundhathi Mohan made badminton history at the 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, becoming one of the rare players to capture a triple crown by sweeping the Girls' Singles, Girls' Doubles, and Mixed Doubles titles in the U13 division. Her perfect 12-0 match record at nationals showcased the dominance that has propelled her to #7 in the nation for U13 Girls' Singles and #9 in Girls' Doubles.</p>

<p>At just 11 years old, Mohan demonstrates remarkable versatility across all three disciplines, currently ranking in the top 20 nationally in U13 Mixed Doubles at #19. Her 66% career win rate (100-51) across 16 tournaments reflects not just talent, but the consistency that separates good players from great ones. She's proven equally dangerous whether controlling the court in singles or orchestrating strategic doubles play.</p>

<p>The California-based player has forged a particularly successful partnership with Shravan Chockalingam, competing together in 45 matches and developing the kind of court chemistry that elevates both players' games. Her ability to adapt her style with different partners—from her 20 matches with Ayushi Raheja to her work with Grace Li and others—demonstrates tactical intelligence beyond her years.</p>

<p>Mohan's trajectory tells the story of a player hitting her stride at exactly the right time. Her rankings have surged dramatically from #22 in U11 Girls' Singles in December 2023 to #7 in U13 by early 2025, while simultaneously climbing in doubles from #24 to #9. With titles spanning from the 2024 ABC OLC to her historic nationals performance, she's building a resume that suggests even greater achievements lie ahead as she continues her ascent through junior badminton's ranks.</p>
