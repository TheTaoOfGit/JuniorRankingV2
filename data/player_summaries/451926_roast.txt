<p>⭐⭐⭐ Sara Biju's Badminton Bistro - A pop-up restaurant that appeared once in 2023 and then vanished into thin air. The menu was extremely limited (literally just "2023 NW OLC Special") but I'll give credit where it's due - 4 out of 5 dishes were actually pretty decent! The one failure was catastrophic enough that the health inspector probably showed up, but hey, 80% success rate isn't terrible for a place run by someone born when YouTube was still a baby.</p>

<p>The staff situation is... interesting. Zimeng Zhou seems to be the head chef who actually knows what they're doing (worked 2 shifts), while Shivali Panda and Julia Ruan were clearly just filling in part-time (1 shift each). The teamwork was surprisingly solid considering this feels like a high school culinary arts project that got out of hand. I kept waiting for Gordon Ramsay to burst through the doors screaming about raw chicken, but somehow they pulled it together.</p>

<p>Here's the thing though - this place has been "temporarily closed for renovations" since 2023, and their Yelp page doesn't even have a star rating yet. It's like that trendy ramen shop that had one amazing night, got written up in the local paper, and then the owner immediately retired to "focus on their studies." Are they coming back? Did they move locations? Did they get absorbed into a food truck empire? Nobody knows!</p>

<p>Would I recommend Sara Biju's Badminton Bistro? Well, if you can find it (good luck with that), and if they ever decide to reopen (even better luck), you might actually have a decent time. Just don't expect a full dining experience - more like a food cart that shows up once every few years when the owner feels like it. Still waiting for that sophomore season, Sara!</p>
