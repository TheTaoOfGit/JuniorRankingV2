<p>★★☆☆☆ "Andy Trinh: The Beginning" is a mercifully short biographical drama clocking in at exactly two tournaments – a runtime so brief it makes most TikTok compilations look like extended director's cuts. Our protagonist, a fresh-faced 2006 model, stumbles onto the badminton scene with all the fanfare of a film released straight to the bargain bin at Walmart.</p>

<p>The plot follows young Trinh through his meteoric rise from complete unknown to... still complete unknown, but now with a 40% win percentage that suggests either admirable persistence or a concerning inability to read the room. His debut at the 2024 ABC OLC delivers a perfectly balanced 1-1 record – the cinematic equivalent of a movie that's neither good enough to recommend nor bad enough to be interesting.</p>

<p>The sequel, "YONEX SoCal ORC & SHONAI SELECTION EVENT" (a title so long it needs its own intermission), sees our hero team up with Joshua Wong in what can only be described as badminton's most fleeting bromance. Their partnership lasted exactly one match, making it shorter than most pharmaceutical commercials and roughly as memorable.</p>

<p>What's most remarkable about this career retrospective is its ambitious minimalism – Trinh has managed to remain completely unranked while maintaining the confidence to show up to tournaments with names longer than his entire win streak. It's the sporting equivalent of an indie film with no budget, no script, and somehow even less plot development.</p>

<p>While "Andy Trinh: The Beginning" may lack the explosive action sequences of established stars, there's something charmingly optimistic about a career so determinedly modest. Sure, he's currently ranked somewhere between "Who?" and "I think I've seen him in the parking lot," but every epic saga needs an opening chapter – even if this one reads more like a grocery list than literature.</p>
