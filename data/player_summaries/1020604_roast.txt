<p>Welcome, fellow adventurers, to the Yalan Zhu Memorial Trail - a moderately challenging 7-year hike through the rugged terrain of junior badminton. Our journey begins at the scenic 2022 SoCal CRC basecamp, where our young explorer first claimed the GS U15 summit at age 13. Note the gentle elevation gain here - she was clearly packing light, unburdened by the weight of expectations that would later slow her ascent.</p>

<p>As we continue along the winding path, you'll notice several impressive peaks dotting the landscape - the 2023 Dave Freeman Jr. double summit (GD and GS U15), and the towering 2025 U.S. Junior National Championships GD U17 peak. However, seasoned hikers will observe the curious phenomenon known as "False Summit Syndrome" - note the seven silver-medal viewpoints where our trailblazer thought she'd reached the top, only to discover someone else had already planted their flag. The 2024 NORTHEAST ORC and 2025 SOCAL ORC overlooks offer particularly stunning views of what might have been.</p>

<p>The trail's most treacherous section features the notorious "Mixed Doubles Marsh" - a boggy wasteland where even experienced guides like Kelvin Zhang and Vivek Rai couldn't prevent our hiker from sinking to #46 in the XD U19 rankings. Many brave souls have been swallowed whole by this unforgiving terrain, though Sasha Suman has proven to be the most reliable sherpa, accompanying Zhu on 33 expeditions without getting completely lost.</p>

<p>As we approach the trail's end at the 2025 NORCAL ORC rest stop (4W-5L - clearly she needed the break), veteran hikers will notice the gradual decline from her glory days at GS U17 #2. The path ahead remains uncertain, but take heart - at only 17, there's plenty of trail left to explore. Just maybe invest in better navigation equipment for those mixed doubles expeditions.</p>
