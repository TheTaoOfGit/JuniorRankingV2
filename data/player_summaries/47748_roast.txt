<p>The case file landed on my desk like a shuttlecock in a hurricane—Rylan Tan, born 2009, currently unranked, and somehow that felt like the most honest thing about him. The kid had the numbers: 51-14 career record, 78% win rate, three titles collecting dust on his shelf. But in this racket, numbers lie easier than a crooked referee, and I've seen enough junior careers to know when something doesn't add up.</p>

<p>It all started in 2021 when Tan was just a U13 pup with dreams bigger than his backhand. Kid swept the USA Badminton U.S. Junior Nationals like he owned the place—19 wins, 1 loss, two titles in the bag, and a finals appearance in singles for good measure. Then came the HPBC NW ORC, where reality hit him like a cross-court smash to the face: 7 wins, 8 losses, and suddenly the golden boy was bleeding on the court. That's when I knew we had ourselves a classic case of the sophomore slump meets Northwest weather.</p>

<p>His partner in crime? Zicheng Xu, who rode shotgun for 11 matches of this wild ride. Kid cycled through doubles partners like a detective changes coffee filters—Micah Cruz, Cassidy Yeh, Vaughn Zhao, even poor Abhi Mandanapu got one match of this chaos. But here's the thing about Tan: he bounced back harder than a plastic bird on concrete, going perfect 6-0 at the 2025 YONEX SOCAL ORC and snagging a BS U19 title like he was picking up dry cleaning.</p>

<p>So why's a kid with three titles and a winning record sitting unranked like yesterday's newspaper? Maybe he got bored, maybe he found something better to do, or maybe—just maybe—he realized that in junior badminton, you're only as good as your last tournament, and everybody's got to grow up sometime. Case closed, but the mystery of what could have been? That's still wide open.</p>
