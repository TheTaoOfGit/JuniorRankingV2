<p>⭐⭐☆☆☆ Went to Dylan Yang's Badminton Bistro expecting a decent meal, but left hungrier than when I arrived. The menu promised exciting dishes like "U.S. Junior Nationals Special" and "Dave Freeman Jr. Double Feature," but the execution was... inconsistent. Out of 18 orders placed across 7 visits, only 6 came out properly cooked. The 25% success rate makes this place less reliable than a gas station sushi counter.</p>

<p>The chef seems to specialize in doubles cuisine, particularly when paired with sous chefs Evan Mu and Shishir Bokka, though even then the results are hit-or-miss. I watched him completely botch a 5-course tasting menu at the February Dave Freeman Jr. location - not a single dish made it to the table! Ironically, the same establishment served up a respectable 3-out-of-4 success rate just months earlier. Talk about inconsistent kitchen management.</p>

<p>The mixed doubles section of the menu is particularly tragic - currently ranked #669 out of presumably everyone who's ever held a racquet. It's like they're trying to serve gourmet food but keep burning the toast. His partnership with Tamara Valueva shows promise, but two shared meals isn't enough data to recommend the collaboration special.</p>

<p>What's most concerning is the declining quality over time. Earlier reviews had him ranked much higher (#74 in doubles, #91 in singles), but recent visits suggest the kitchen has lost its way. The YONEX Arena SoCal experience delivered one decent course out of three attempts, while the Northeast LIBC outing was a complete shutout. At 17 years old, there's still time for this establishment to find its footing, but right now I'd recommend ordering takeout from literally anywhere else in the U19 district.</p>
