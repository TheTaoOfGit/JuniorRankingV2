<p>Welcome, fellow adventurers, to the Darlene Axella Purwanto Memorial Trail — a modest 3-tournament trek that's less "challenging Alpine ascent" and more "scenic neighborhood loop your grandmother could handle in flip-flops." Our young hiker, born in 2017 and still figuring out which end of the racket to hold, has blazed this trail with the determination of a lost tourist insisting they don't need GPS while walking in circles.</p>

<p>The trail begins at Austin Leander South OLC, where our intrepid explorer posted a respectable 4-5 record — think of it as that first steep hill that makes you question your life choices before you realize the rest of the hike is downhill. From there, she meandered through Schafer SLG South OLC with a perfectly balanced 3-3 performance, the hiking equivalent of stopping at every scenic overlook to take Instagram photos instead of actually making progress.</p>

<p>Our trail guide particularly enjoys pointing out the "Potter Han Schafer Observation Deck," named after Darlene's most frequent hiking companion who's been dragged along for 6 matches of what can only be described as "enthusiastic wandering." Together, they've discovered that mixed doubles is like that unmarked trail that looked promising on the map but turns out to be a swamp — hence Darlene's impressive #958 ranking in XD U19, a number so high it might actually be her altitude above sea level.</p>

<p>The trail's crown jewel is the recent Vistara South OLC summit, where our hiker achieved her personal best 5-3 record and somehow stumbled into two finals despite what can only be described as "beginner's luck meets questionable seeding." She lost both finals, of course, but hey — at least she found the finish line, which is more than we can say for her mixed doubles ranking progression.</p>

<p>Trail difficulty: Beginner-friendly with occasional moments where you'll wonder if the trail markers were placed by someone who's never actually been hiking.
