<p><b>0-2 in Finals: Darlene Purwanto Gets to the Big Stage Just to Lose on It</b></p>

<p>Darlene Axella Purwanto has a 52% career win rate, which sounds respectable until you realize she's 0-2 in finals. She made it to the championship match twice at the 2026 Vistara -- in both GD and GS -- and lost both times. That's like getting invited to prom twice in one night and getting rejected at the door both times. She can beat the semis (2-0) but apparently the sight of a final-round scoreboard triggers a complete system shutdown.</p>

<p>Her three-game record is 1-2, meaning when matches get tight she usually crumbles. Four blowout losses including getting destroyed by Adalyn Puah in BOTH singles and doubles suggests Adalyn has taken up permanent residence in Darlene's head. The fact that she lost to the same player she then faced in a GD final is the kind of poetic suffering you can't make up.</p>

<p>Three tournaments in and she's collected two runner-up trophies, zero titles, and a growing collection of psychological scars from Adalyn Puah. At this rate, her specialty is bridesmaiding.</p>
