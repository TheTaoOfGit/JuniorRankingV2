<p>★★☆☆☆ "Varshini: The Beginning" is a brief but ambitious sports drama that clocks in at just two tournaments—making it shorter than most movie trailers. Our plucky protagonist, a fresh-faced 12-year-old, bursts onto the badminton scene with the confidence of someone who clearly hasn't read the script. The plot is refreshingly simple: young hopeful discovers that winning matches is significantly harder than it looks on YouTube.</p>

<p>Sivaramakrishnan's performance is charmingly uneven, delivering her strongest work in the doubles category where she's managed to crack the top 200 in multiple age groups—a respectable showing that suggests she's learned the ancient art of "letting your partner do most of the work." Her supporting cast rotates frequently (five different doubles partners in nine total matches), giving the production a reality TV feel where alliances shift faster than shuttle speeds. Mihika Jaiswal and Soudamini Reddy each get two-episode arcs, though neither partnership has produced any hardware for the trophy cabinet.</p>

<p>The film's highlight reel centers on the DFW tournament, where our heroine managed a respectable 4-2 showing—proof that sometimes the sequel actually improves on the original. Unfortunately, this peak performance is bookended by the Capital NE disaster, a three-match losing streak so thorough it makes "The Room" look like "Citizen Kane." Her mixed doubles subplot remains largely unwatched, hovering in the 600-1000 ranking range like a direct-to-streaming release nobody talks about.</p>

<p>While "Varshini: The Beginning" lacks the polish of seasoned veterans, there's undeniable charm in watching someone discover that badminton rankings are not, in fact, participation trophies. The character development from #109 to #105 in girls' singles shows promise, though at this rate, reaching the top 50 would require more sequels than the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Critics are cautiously optimistic about future installments, provided the script includes more wins and fewer dramatic three-set losses.</p>
