<p>**Anish Bhandary** (born 2011) is an Indian-American badminton player widely regarded by absolutely no one as a rising star in junior competitive badminton.[citation needed] Known for his groundbreaking 48% career win rate, Bhandary has revolutionized the concept of "moral victories" by consistently losing just enough matches to maintain perfect mediocrity across nine tournaments.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Bhandary's career highlight remains his 9W-3L performance at the 2022 South CRC, a tournament so prestigious that it doesn't even warrant a full name expansion.[citation needed] This remarkable achievement was immediately followed by a humbling 0W-2L showing at the 2023 South ORC, proving that consistency is indeed his greatest weakness.[citation needed] His 2022 Junior National Championships debut of 2W-4L established him as the kind of player who makes participation trophies feel like legitimate recognition.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Perhaps most notably, Bhandary has formed what can only be described as badminton's least dynamic duo with doubles partner Aarav Bhandary, presumably his brother, across 16 matches.[citation needed] This partnership has redefined sibling rivalry by demonstrating that blood may be thicker than water, but neither is thicker than a losing record.[citation needed] His brief one-match collaboration with Mi Michael Liu suggests that even random doubles assignments couldn't salvage his tournament prospects.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Currently unranked in all competitive categories, Bhandary continues to pursue his badminton career with the determination of someone who has never let mathematical probability discourage him.[citation needed] His strategic approach of going 0W-2L in multiple tournaments (2023 South ORC, 2023 YONEX NorCal ORC, and 2024 YONEX SoCal ORC) has earned him recognition as junior badminton's most reliable source of first-round exits.[citation needed]</p>
