<p><b>0-4 in Finals: The Best Player Who Can't Win the Big One</b></p>

<p>Aadya Patil is ranked GS U11 #3, GD U11 #6, and XD U11 #9 -- elite numbers that make her 0-4 finals record absolutely devastating. Four finals. Four losses. She's made it to the championship match FOUR TIMES and choked every single time. Runner-up to Thea Yuan in GS, to Prarthana Sanghani (twice -- once in GD, once in GS), and to Arudh Sundar's team in XD. This isn't bad luck. This is a clinical condition.</p>

<p>Her 60% overall win rate and 4-1 semis record prove she can beat anyone to reach the final. Then she arrives and forgets how to play. The 66% three-game rate shows she's clutch in regular matches, which makes the finals collapses even more inexplicable. Blowout losses to Thea Yuan, Jake Shi, Isha Suresh, and Arundhathi Padma Mohan add color to the humiliation palette.</p>

<p>Vihaa Reddy Keesara has been her partner for 12 matches and Abhinav Praveen for 10 -- long-suffering teammates who've witnessed the full spectrum of "great player, terrible closer." If Aadya were a stock, she'd be a company with amazing revenue and no profit.</p>
