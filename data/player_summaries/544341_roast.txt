<p>Aadya Patil is the Michelin-starred restaurant that keeps getting two stars when it clearly deserves three. Born in 2017, she sits at GD U11 #6, GS U11 #3, and XD U11 #9 -- triple top-10 rankings that scream "franchise player" louder than a smoke alarm in a five-star kitchen. Her 38-25 career record (60%) across 8 tournaments is the win rate of an establishment that's permanently booked out, and her 2,337 singles points make her the third-best U11 girls' singles player in the country.</p>

<p>The trophy case, however, is conspicuously empty. Four runner-up finishes -- at SoCal ORC, DFW South ORC, and two at Peak Sports -- form a collection of silver medals that would fill a jewelry store display case. Losing to Thea Yuan in the SoCal ORC singles final, falling to Prarthana Sanghani at Peak Sports -- Aadya keeps getting to the final course of the tasting menu and then the dessert arrives cold.</p>

<p>The 7W-3L at SoCal ORC and 6W-3L at DFW show she's averaging roughly seven wins per major event, which is extraordinary volume. With Vihaa Reddy Keesara (12 matches) as her doubles soulmate and Abhinav Praveen (10 matches) running the mixed doubles kitchen, the staff is elite. At 8, ranked #3 in the country with zero titles is the most maddening stat line in U11 badminton. The coronation is coming -- it just keeps getting rescheduled.</p>
