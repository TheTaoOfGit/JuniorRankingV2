<p>Ah yes, the 2005 vintage Neel Chandra - a curious case study in junior badminton terroir. Upon first inspection, this specimen presents with an immediately promising bouquet of regional dominance, particularly the 2022 MBC CRC where our subject achieved an intoxicating 11-2 record. The initial sip reveals bold notes of Boys Singles supremacy, with a surprisingly robust finish that captured not one, but two U19 titles. However, seasoned palates will detect underlying tannins of inconsistency that become more pronounced with aeration.</p>

<p>The midpalate tells a more complex story, as this vintage seems to have peaked early in smaller appellations before struggling with the full-bodied nationals. The 2022 Junior Nationals tasting reveals a rather astringent 4-6 performance - clearly this bottle was opened before reaching proper maturity. By 2023, we observe some improvement with a 10-5 showing, suggesting better aging potential, though still lacking the sophisticated complexity of true championship caliber.</p>

<p>Most intriguingly, our sommelier notes detect a fascinating partnership dynamic with one Matthew Jing - 23 matches of what can only be described as a well-balanced blend. The Chandra-Jing pairing exhibits remarkable consistency in doubles play, though one suspects this vintage may have become overly dependent on this particular vintage companion. Secondary partnerships with Angie Huang and others show promise but lack the same depth of character development.</p>

<p>The finish, regrettably, is where this vintage reveals its limitations. Currently sitting unranked - presumably having aged out of the junior classification - we're left with a wine that peaked at a respectable #5 in Boys Singles but ultimately lacks the structure for long-term cellaring. A pleasant regional vintage with moments of brilliance, but one suspects the Chandra label may struggle to maintain relevance in more competitive adult markets. Recommended pairing: realistic expectations and a solid backup career plan.</p>
