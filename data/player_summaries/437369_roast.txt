<p>⭐⭐⭐ "Leslie Wang's Badminton Bistro" - Went here expecting a fine dining experience but got more of a "promising food truck that sometimes burns the main course." The ambiance has improved dramatically though, especially after that triple crown performance at 2025 SGVBC SOCAL CRC where he somehow managed to win BS, BD, AND XD U13. Kid went full Gordon Ramsay that weekend.</p>

The mixed doubles offerings with Regina Lee (25 collaborations) are also solid, though the XD rankings suggest this pairing could use some more seasoning. Pro tip: avoid ordering from the singles menu when he's having an off day - those U17 and U19 rankings (#122, #224) taste like he's still learning the recipe.</p>

<p>Here's where things get spicy: this establishment has a serious case of "runner-up syndrome." Eleven finals appearances that ended in defeat? That's more silver than a fancy steakhouse's cutlery drawer. The 2024 season was particularly brutal - losing BD U11 finals at both YONEX NORCAL ORC AND YONEX MIDWEST ORC. At some point, you've got to wonder if he's collecting second-place trophies like some people collect restaurant loyalty cards.</p>

<p>The service has definitely improved with time - his ranking trajectory from #78 XD U11 to #30 XD U13 shows real growth, and that 144-84 career record (63% win rate) suggests the kitchen knows what it's doing most of the time. Would I come back? Absolutely, especially since this young chef is clearly still perfecting his craft. Just maybe avoid the busy tournament weekends when he's trying to juggle too many age divisions at once - nobody needs to see a 12-year-old get overwhelmed trying to compete in U19s.</p>
