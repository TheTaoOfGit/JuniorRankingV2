<p>★★☆☆☆ "Shaunak Misra: The Career" is the kind of sports drama that makes you wonder if the screenwriter confused "inspirational underdog story" with "elaborate practical joke." Our protagonist, armed with nothing but youthful optimism and a 47% win rate, stumbles through a decade-long odyssey that feels less like "The Karate Kid" and more like "The Bad News Bears" if the Bears never quite figured out how to play baseball.</p>

<p>The film's lone highlight comes early in act one, when our hero claims the BD U15 crown at the 2021 USAB South Open Local Championships with a commanding 6-1 performance. For a brief, shining moment, it seems we might be watching the birth of a badminton dynasty. Instead, what follows is a masterclass in dramatic irony as Misra proceeds to collect losses like they're going out of style, including a particularly brutal 2-5 showing at the South Frisco ORC that plays like a deleted scene from a sports blooper reel.</p>

<p>The supporting cast reads like a rotating door of doubles partners—Aditya Kotaru, William Ye, Suraj Koneru—each presumably hoping to be the sidekick who helps turn this ship around. Spoiler alert: they don't. The chemistry between Misra and his various co-stars ranges from "awkward first date" to "witness protection program," culminating in recent performances where he's managed to post three consecutive 0-2 tournaments, a feat that requires a special kind of dedication to mediocrity.</p>

<p>Perhaps most tellingly, our protagonist has managed the impressive trick of going from ranked (#65 BD U19, #89 BS U19) to completely unranked, like a reverse superhero origin story where the hero discovers his power is making rankings disappear. "Shaunak Misra: The Career" is the rare sports film that makes you root for the opponent, if only to put this poor soul out of his misery and let him pursue a career in something less physically demanding, like competitive chess or professional Netflix watching.</p>
