<p>Rishita Tubati made her mark on the junior badminton scene with an unforgettable performance at the 2021 Egret OLC, where she captured both the Girls' Doubles U17 and Girls' Singles U17 titles in a dominant display of versatility. This dual-title achievement showcased her rare ability to excel in both individual and team formats, setting the foundation for what would become a consistently strong junior career spanning multiple age groups and competitive regions.</p>

<p>With a stellar 66-42 match record across 14 tournaments, Tubati has maintained an impressive 61% win rate while competing against top-tier opposition throughout the South, Northeast, and Midwest circuits. Her tactical intelligence and court awareness have been particularly evident in doubles play, where she has forged successful partnerships with standout players like Stella Pan, with whom she has contested 12 matches, and Siva Rajesh across 10 encounters, demonstrating her adaptability with different playing styles.</p>

<p>Tubati's competitive maturity shone through her seamless transition to U19 competition, where she achieved a remarkable #3 national ranking in Girls' Singles and reached the finals at the 2023 SOUTH OLC. Her ability to compete effectively across all three disciplines—singles, girls' doubles, and mixed doubles—has earned her rankings in each category, with her #3 GS U19 position highlighting her exceptional individual skills against older, more experienced opponents.</p>

<p>From her breakthrough season in 2021 through her continued excellence in higher age divisions, Tubati has proven herself as one of the most well-rounded junior players in the country. Her consistent tournament performances across diverse geographic regions, combined with her natural leadership in doubles partnerships and her ability to elevate her game in championship moments, mark her as a player with both immediate impact and significant long-term potential in American badminton.</p>
