Claire Li has established herself as one of the most dangerous doubles players in the U13 age group, currently ranked No. 2 in girls' doubles and No. 4 in mixed doubles. Born in 2014, she has risen at a staggering pace -- from No. 29 in GD U11 in December 2023 to U11 national champion by the summer of 2024, and now a dominant force at the U13 level with a career record of 138-62 across 21 tournaments. Her doubles prowess is unmistakable: she has claimed four GD U13 titles in the past year alone, winning at the 2025 Midwest ORC, 2025 NorCal ORC, and both the 2026 SoCal and DFW South ORCs. Her singles game is catching up too, as her GS U13 ranking has surged from No. 14 to No. 6, and she recently claimed a GS U15 title at the 2026 Vistara South OLC, proving she can compete above her age group.

Li's doubles success is built on remarkably stable partnerships. She and Krishnavi Kasu have accumulated 46 matches together in girls' doubles, forming one of the most consistent pairings on the junior circuit. In mixed doubles, she has transitioned from Eli You to Kevin Xie, with whom she has played 30 matches and reached two XD U13 finals at the 2026 SoCal and DFW South ORCs -- both losses coming against the formidable tandem of Michael Yufu Sun and Grace. The recurring rivalry with Grace Cheng and Sophia Z Li in girls' doubles has defined her recent trajectory: Li has won three of their last four finals meetings. Her recent form is electric, posting an 11-2 record at the 2026 SoCal ORC and maintaining a run of double-digit win totals at every major tournament since mid-2025. At just eleven years old, Li's combination of doubles instinct and an increasingly potent singles game suggests she is only beginning to scratch the surface of her potential.
