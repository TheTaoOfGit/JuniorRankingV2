<p>Ah yes, the Vincent Lu 2021 vintage—a most peculiar specimen that presents immediate challenges upon first encounter. The nose reveals hints of early promise with that singular 3-0 SoCal triumph, yet one detects underlying notes of defeat that would come to define this particular pressing. Like a wine that peaked too early in the barrel, this vintage showed initial complexity before rapidly developing an unfortunate tendency toward oxidation.</p>

<p>On the palate, the Lu exhibits a surprisingly delicate 17% win rate that dances lightly across the tongue before vanishing entirely, leaving behind the unmistakable finish of a 6-30 record. The tannins are virtually non-existent—much like his current ranking status—while the acidity remains consistently high, particularly evident in those memorable 0-5 and 0-4 tastings at the Junior Nationals and Synergy NorCal events. One appreciates the terroir of his partnership selections: the Ryan Liu and Andrew Yu blends show promise, though they lack the necessary structure to support the vintage's inherent weaknesses.</p>

<p>The 2023 offerings present fascinating study in regression, with rankings of #197, #101, and #260 suggesting a wine that has not aged gracefully. Critics note the vintage's ambitious reach across multiple appellations—from NorCal to SoCal to the prestigious Northwest regions—yet the results consistently underwhelm even the most generous sommelier. This is decidedly a table wine masquerading as something more sophisticated.</p>

<p>While purists might dismiss this vintage entirely, I detect subtle notes of determination and youth that suggest potential for future harvests. Best served immediately with low expectations and paired perhaps with a good book on badminton fundamentals. Not recommended for special occasions, but adequate for everyday consumption by those who appreciate the journey more than the destination. Cellar for several more years and revisit when the vintage has had time to mature—preferably with a new coach.</p>
