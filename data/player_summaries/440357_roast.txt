<p>Adithya Yeluguri (born 2014) is an American junior badminton player best known for his remarkable ability to maintain exactly 2362 points across four different Boys' Singles age categories, a feat of mathematical precision that has baffled ranking officials and led some to theorize he may have discovered the secret to badminton point conservation.[citation needed] Currently ranked #23 in Boys' Singles U13, Yeluguri's ranking deteriorates with alarming consistency as age categories increase, dropping to #283 in U19, suggesting either a rapid aging process or a fundamental misunderstanding of how birthdays work.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Yeluguri's playing style has been described as "aggressively mediocre" in mixed doubles, where his U13 ranking of #97 plummets to an impressive #552 in U19 competition.[citation needed] His longtime doubles partnership with Ritvik Singh spans 12 matches, leading tennis commentators to speculate whether this constitutes a bromance or simply two players who got lost looking for their respective singles courts.[citation needed] The duo's chemistry has been compared to "two badminton rackets trying to high-five," though this comparison may be overly generous to the rackets.[citation needed]</p>

<p>His tournament resume reads like a corporate sponsor's fever dream, with victories at prestigious events including the 2025 FORTIUS SOUTH CRC and 2026 VISTARA SOUTH OLC, where he presumably received trophies shaped like airline logos.[citation needed] Yeluguri dominated U11 competition in 2024, winning four titles before the cruel reality of aging caught up with him, forcing him into the treacherous waters of U13 play where he has managed only four additional titles.[citation needed] His career win rate of 62% suggests he wins approximately three matches for every two he loses, a mathematical revelation that has revolutionized how people count to five.[citation needed]</p>
