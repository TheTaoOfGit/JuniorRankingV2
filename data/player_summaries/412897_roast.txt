<p>Ah, the Sing Nei Tong collection—a rather ambitious young vintage that premiered in 2022 with considerable promise. The inaugural 2022 SoCal CRC offers a surprisingly robust profile, with hints of determination and a crisp 3-2 finish that nearly achieved greatness in the GD U17 finals. One detects notes of youthful optimism with undertones of "maybe I actually belong here," though the final cork disappointingly popped before the championship could be properly uncorked.</p>

<p>The 2023 SoCal ORC vintage presents a more restrained character—a modest 2-2 showing that suggests the terroir may be shifting. Like a wine that's been stored improperly, there's a certain flatness to the performance, though still maintaining drinkability for those with modest expectations. The pairing with Isabelle Chen adds some complexity, though one wonders if even the finest accompaniment can elevate a fundamentally unremarkable base.</p>

<p>Moving to the more recent 2024 offerings, we encounter what can only be described as a series of unfortunate harvests. The YONEX SoCal ORC & SHONAI SELECTION EVENT delivers a thin, acidic 1-2 vintage with little body and an unpleasantly sharp finish. The Dave Freeman Jr. OLC pairs even worse—a 2-3 disaster that leaves one questioning whether this particular vineyard should perhaps consider alternative crops, like accounting or customer service.</p>

<p>The current state of this collection reveals a producer who has somehow managed to go from ranked (#83 GD U19, #93 GS U19) to completely unranked—a remarkable feat of reverse alchemy. With partnerships ranging from Cody Duan to Jordan Lau, it appears our sommelier has been experimenting with various blends, though none have managed to salvage what is fundamentally becoming a rather forgettable label. Perhaps it's time to let this vintage breathe... indefinitely.</p>
