<p>Ah yes, the Jianxi Wen 2024 vintage - a particularly ambitious young label from the NorCal region, though I must say this bottle shows all the hallmarks of premature harvesting. The initial nose presents bold notes of optimism with undertones of Kido PHAN partnership, but upon first sip, one encounters a surprisingly thin body that lacks the structural integrity one expects from even a developing player. The 8-19 record creates a harsh, almost astringent finish that lingers uncomfortably on the palate.</p>

<p>The 2023 collection, I'm afraid, reads like a series of unfortunate accidents - the Northwest ORC showing particularly flat characteristics with zero wins to balance the acidity of that singular loss. The SoCal and NorCal offerings from this period display an alarming consistency in their complete absence of victories, creating what sommeliers call "the dreaded goose egg vintage." One might charitably describe these tournaments as "learning experiences," though the learning curve appears to have the gradient of a gentle hill rather than the sharp ascent one hopes for.</p>

<p>What's most concerning about this particular label is the reverse aging process - our young vintner somehow managed to descend from a respectable U17 #137 ranking to a rather pedestrian #417, suggesting either poor storage conditions or perhaps the wine simply wasn't ready for the cellar in the first place. The doubles partnerships read like a revolving door of well-meaning collaborations, with Aaron Liao appearing twice on the manifest (once properly capitalized, once not - even the scorekeeper seems confused).</p>

<p>While the 2024 YONEX NORCAL ORC did produce a surprisingly palatable 3W-2L blend - the closest thing to a vintage year in this collection - the overall portfolio suggests a player still very much in the fermentation stage. I'd recommend storing this one in a cool, dark place for several more years before attempting to serve it at any serious gathering. With proper aging and perhaps a more consistent doubles partnership, future vintages may yet surprise us.</p>
