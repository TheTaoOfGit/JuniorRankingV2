<p>At just 15 years old, Joanne Li has already established herself as one of junior badminton's most versatile champions, capturing national titles across singles, doubles, and mixed doubles since she was barely 10. Her remarkable journey began with a stunning debut at the 2021 USA Badminton U.S. Junior National Championships, where she swept both the U11 girls' singles and mixed doubles titles, announcing her arrival on the national stage with authority.</p>

<p>Li's trophy cabinet tells the story of sustained excellence across multiple age divisions, boasting an impressive collection of 25 tournament titles spanning from U11 to U17 levels. Her crown jewel achievements include back-to-back U.S. Junior National Championships in both girls' singles and doubles at the U13 level in 2023, followed by another national singles title at U15 in 2024. With a career record of 217-55 across 24 tournaments, her 80% win rate demonstrates the consistency that separates elite competitors from the field.</p>

<p>The California native has found particular success in doubles play, where her partnership with Kimaya Krishna has become one of junior badminton's most formidable combinations. Together, they've contested 59 matches and captured multiple regional and national titles, including their breakthrough U13 girls' doubles championship at the 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships. Li's ability to seamlessly transition between different partners and events showcases her tactical intelligence and adaptability on court.</p>

<p>Currently ranked #49 in U17 girls' singles and #29 in U17 girls' doubles, Li continues to compete at the highest levels while balancing three different age divisions. Her recent performance at the 2025 YONEX SOUTH ORC, where she went undefeated (10-0) while claiming both singles and doubles titles, proves she remains a force to be reckoned with as she approaches the senior ranks. With her combination of early success, tactical versatility, and proven championship mentality, Li represents the bright future of American badminton.</p>
