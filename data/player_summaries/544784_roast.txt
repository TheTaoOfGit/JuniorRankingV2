<p>★½ out of ★★★★★ — "Prisha Rastogi: A Badminton Story" clocks in at a mercifully brief runtime of just two tournaments, though even that feels like an eternity when you're watching someone compile a 1-6 record with the dramatic tension of watching paint dry on a shuttlecock. Director Reality clearly had no script, no character arc, and apparently no understanding that protagonists are supposed to, you know, occasionally win.</p>

<p>The film's ambitious opening act at the 2025 U.S. Junior National Championships promised a breakout performance, but Rastogi's lone victory feels less like a triumphant moment and more like the cinematographer accidentally left the lens cap off. Her subsequent 4-loss streak plays out with the inexorable logic of a Greek tragedy, if Greek tragedies were about 13-year-olds learning that badminton is harder than it looks on YouTube.</p>

<p>The supporting cast is forgettable at best — Jonathan Wang appears in two scenes as her doubles partner before presumably reading the script and demanding a better role elsewhere. Even poor Raymond Wang managed just one match before what can only be described as a strategic career pivot away from this production. The chemistry between Rastogi and her various partners has all the spark of wet fireworks.</p>

<p>Most damning is the film's complete lack of character development. Despite a promising ranking of #125 in the U15 division back in March, our protagonist has somehow managed to slide backwards to #198, like a coming-of-age story told in reverse. The sequel "Prisha Rastogi 2: The Revenge" has been indefinitely postponed, though given this performance, that might be for the best.</p>
