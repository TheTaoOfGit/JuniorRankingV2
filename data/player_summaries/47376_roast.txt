<p>"Charlotte Tse: A Badminton Story" is the kind of plucky underdog sports drama that makes you root for the protagonist despite knowing deep down that the sequel probably isn't happening. Director Charlotte Tse stars as herself in this eight-tournament epic spanning 2021-2023, delivering a performance that's equal parts determination and... well, mostly determination. With a 43% win rate that would make even the most optimistic studio executive nervous about green-lighting a franchise, Tse's career reads like a script that keeps getting sent back for rewrites.</p>

<p>The supporting cast deserves special mention, particularly Tanvi Deshmukh and Ethan Paw, who each bravely appeared in six matches alongside our leading lady. One can only imagine the chemistry reads that led to these casting decisions, though the on-court results suggest their partnerships had more dramatic tension than romantic comedy. Harshita Kandambakkam, Thanik Bharani, and Hailey Hung round out the ensemble in what can only be described as a revolving door of co-stars, each bringing their own interpretation of "how to carry a doubles team."</p>

<p>The film's most compelling arc comes during the 2023 Midwest ORC, where Tse delivered her career-best 9-4 performance—a genuine crowd-pleaser that had audiences believing this might be her breakout role. Unfortunately, this triumphant third act was bookended by the 2023 YONEX Northwest ORC, a brutal 0-2 showing that felt less like a satisfying conclusion and more like the studio ran out of money mid-production.</p>

<p>While Tse's rankings peaked at a respectable U19 #50 in girls' doubles, her mixed doubles ranking of #162 suggests she might want to consider a career pivot to singles—or perhaps documentary filmmaking. "Charlotte Tse: A Badminton Story" earns two and a half stars for heart, effort, and the kind of unpolished authenticity that reminds us why we fell in love with sports movies in the first place, even if we're not quite ready to buy the director's cut.</p>
