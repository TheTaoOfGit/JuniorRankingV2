<p><b>0-3 in Finals and 72% in Three-Gamers: Yuhan Ding Wins Close Matches but Chokes in Championship Ones</b></p>

<p>Yuhan Ding has a three-game record of 8-3 (72%), which is legitimately impressive. The problem? She's 0-3 in finals. Three finals, three losses. She's clutch enough to grind through tight matches in early rounds but completely falls apart when a title is on the line. Two of those losses came at ABC OLCs, her home turf, where she should theoretically have an advantage.</p>

<p>Her 56% career win rate (36-28) across only 8 tournaments is decent, and her 3-0 semis record means she always reaches finals -- only to lose them. She went 7-6 at Junior Nationals, which is respectable, and 6-2 at ABC OLC, where she was dominant until the final match. The pattern is clear: Yuhan is a relentless fighter who transforms into a glass cannon in championship moments.</p>

<p>Her partner Irene Xu has been with her for 8 matches, and Arshiya Raheja for 7 more. She collects doubles partners the way she collects runner-up trophies -- efficiently and joylessly.</p>
