<p><b>Restaurant Review: Veronica Ornatsky</b> — A charming neighborhood bistro that's been quietly serving competent doubles since 2021. The 51% career rate (47-45 across 16 tournaments) makes this a coin-flip dining experience — you might get the special, or you might get the Tuesday leftovers.</p>

<p>The doubles menu is the draw. GD U19 #54 doesn't scream fine dining, but the GD U15 title at the 2022 MBC CRC and GD U17 title at the 2023 Midwest OLC show the kitchen can produce when it matters. Partnership with Nivedya S Nair (21 matches) is the comfort food that keeps regulars coming back.</p>

<p>The singles appetizer at GS U19 #50 is serviceable, highlighted by a GS U17 runner-up at the 2023 Midwest OLC against Ishi Reddy. But with zero titles or runner-ups in recent years and a modest tournament count of 16, this restaurant operates on limited hours. The 2021 Junior Nationals (0-4) was a kitchen fire best forgotten, while the 2022 MBC CRC (7-2) was the grand opening everyone remembers fondly.</p>
