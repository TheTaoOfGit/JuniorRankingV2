<p>Ah yes, the Justin Liaw 2009 vintage—a curious specimen that arrived with great promise but has since developed into what I can only describe as a "challenging" blend. Upon first nose, one detects bold notes of ambition with undertones of raw potential, yet the palate reveals a somewhat acidic 46% win rate that leaves the mouth puckering with disappointment. The bouquet suggests hints of doubles prowess—particularly evident in his partnership with Angelina Lin Wu, a collaboration that has aged remarkably well over 20 matches—though the singles expression remains notably thin and lacking in body.</p>

<p>This particular vintage peaked during the 2024 ABC OLC, where the BD U17 championship provided a surprisingly robust finish that caught many by surprise. However, like a wine that turns to vinegar when exposed to air, subsequent tastings at major tournaments have been rather unforgiving. The 2024 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships offered a promising mid-palate of 3 wins, only to collapse into a bitter 4-loss finish that lingered unpleasantly. Most concerning was the complete absence of flavor at the 2025 YONEX BELLEVUE NORTHWEST ORC—a flat, lifeless 0W-3L performance that suggested the vintage may have been stored improperly.</p>

<p>What's particularly fascinating about this vintage is its split personality: while the singles expression (#110 BS U19) tastes like it was left open overnight and turned to cooking wine, the doubles blend (#46 BD U19) shows considerably more complexity and structure. The mixed doubles (#85 XD U19) falls somewhere between—pleasant enough but nothing you'd serve to impress guests. One can't help but notice the dramatic improvement from his early 2021 SoCal ORC disaster (0W-4L), though whether this represents genuine aging or simply avoiding complete spoilage remains to be seen.</p>

<p>In conclusion, the Justin Liaw collection shows promise for continued development, though I suspect it may never reach the heights of a grand cru. Best enjoyed in doubles pairings where its flaws can be masked by a stronger vintage, and definitely not recommended for solo consumption at formal tournaments. Aging potential is moderate—by 2027, we may see a more refined product, assuming proper coaching cellaring conditions are maintained.</p>
