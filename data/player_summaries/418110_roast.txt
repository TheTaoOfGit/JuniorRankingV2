<p>⭐⭐ "Kuhu's Court Cuisine" - Unfortunately had to bump this down from my initial 3-star rating after multiple disappointing visits. Look, I really wanted to love this place after hearing about their 2023 grand opening at South OLC (4-4 record, not terrible for a debut!), but subsequent experiences have left me wanting. The service is inconsistent at best - went 0-4 at one South OLC location, then showed marginal improvement with a 2-3 performance at another. For a restaurant that's been operating since 2023, you'd expect more seasoning by now.</p>

<p>The biggest issue seems to be with their partnership program - they keep rotating through different sous chefs faster than a revolving door. Started with Mithun Gopinath (solid choice, 6 collaborative dishes), then moved to Haripriya Tati and Vishanth Vijay (5 each), before trying out Siya Alicia Chen and even giving Siddharth Sriganesh a brief stint. When your kitchen staff changes this frequently, it's hard to develop any signature dishes. Speaking of which, their finals presentation was gorgeous twice (South CRC 2024 GD U17 and South OLC 2023 GD U15) but somehow they just couldn't close the deal - beautiful plating, no flavor payoff.</p>

<p>Credit where it's due: there has been some improvement in their rankings system. Their Girls Singles has climbed from #94 to #88 to #72, which shows they're at least learning from their mistakes. The 2023 Midwest ORC performance (4-3) was actually quite respectable, and you can see glimmers of potential when everything aligns. But with an overall 15-20 record (43% satisfaction rate), this establishment is still very much a work in progress.</p>

<p>Would I recommend Kuhu's Court Cuisine? If you're looking for a reliable, consistent experience, probably not yet. But if you enjoy watching a young chef find their footing and don't mind the occasional undercooked result, there might be something here worth following. Just maybe wait for them to settle on a permanent kitchen staff first. The 2024 YONEX MIDWEST ORC visit (3-3) suggests they're getting closer to that elusive break-even point. Reservations recommended, expectations should be managed accordingly.</p>
