<p>After months of careful excavation at the Modi site, our team has uncovered startling evidence of a once-thriving badminton civilization that mysteriously vanished around 2024. Carbon dating reveals the specimen known as "Tvisha Modi" reached peak dominance in the ancient 2022 South CRC stratum, where artifacts show a perfect 12-0 record—a level of dominance not seen since the great Rithik Rasamsetti partnerships of that era. However, subsequent geological layers tell a darker tale.</p>

<p>The most fascinating discovery lies in the 2024 YONEX South ORC sediment, where we found only two match fragments—both losses—suggesting a catastrophic collapse of the Modi Empire. Our anthropologists theorize this represents the "Great Unranking Event," where a player who once held U13 rankings of #6 in singles and mixed doubles simply vanished from competitive records, like a lost civilization swallowed by time.</p>

<p>Perhaps most intriguing are the partnership pottery shards scattered throughout the dig site. Analysis reveals 24 matches worth of Rithik Rasamsetti ceramics, suggesting either a highly successful doubles dynasty or the badminton equivalent of an arranged marriage. The presence of 14 Anirud Veldanda artifacts and various Vivian Feng relics indicates Modi may have been something of a doubles nomad, collecting partners like archaeological specimens.</p>

<p>The trophy chamber yields seven championship artifacts from her golden age, including a rare U.S. Junior National XD crown—though archaeologists note the conspicuous absence of any singles hardware despite her #6 ranking, suggesting she may have been the badminton equivalent of a social butterfly who couldn't survive alone on court. Current expeditions seeking signs of life in the Modi ruins have yielded nothing but tumbleweeds and unranked status reports.</p>
