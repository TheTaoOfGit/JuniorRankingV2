<p>Ah yes, the 2014 vintage Sreeskandan Saravanan - a precocious little number that burst onto the scene with all the subtlety of a Two Buck Chuck at a Michelin-starred establishment. This ambitious blend shows early promise with a delightful XD U11 title at the 2023 MassBad NE OLC, offering bright notes of mixed doubles chemistry with partner Manvi Jandhyala, though one detects hints of over-reliance on a single terroir.</p>

<p>The 2024 bottling reveals concerning oxidation issues, particularly evident in the rather pedestrian 8-8 showing at Nationals - a vintage that promised complexity but delivered the sophistication of a gas station rosé. While the BD U11 title at NE CRC suggests decent body, the accompanying BS final loss at the same tournament reveals a troubling lack of consistency, like a wine that can't decide if it wants to be a Pinot or a Cab.</p>

<p>Most alarming is the rapid deterioration upon aging into U13 categories - our young vintner has somehow managed to produce a wine that gets worse with time, dropping from a respectable U11 #42 BD ranking to a rather pedestrian #59, while his XD plummeted from #40 to #97 faster than a cork falling into an open bottle.</p>

<p>The career's finish is disappointingly short, with four finals appearances yielding only two titles - a 50% conversion rate that would make even the most generous sommelier reach for the Pepto. One appreciates the effort to diversify across multiple tournaments and partners, but like a wine maker who can't commit to a single grape varietal, young Saravanan's 53-39 record suggests a vintage that's pleasant enough for everyday drinking but unlikely to age into anything approaching Grand Cru status.</p>
