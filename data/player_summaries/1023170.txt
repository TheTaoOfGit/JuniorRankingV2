Krishnavi Kasu stands alone atop both the U13 girls' singles and U13 girls' doubles rankings, a double number-one status that reflects sustained excellence across 24 tournaments and a career record of 192-55. Born in 2014, Kasu was a promising but unranked prospect in several U13 categories at the end of 2023, sitting at number 39 in GD U13 and outside the top 50 in XD U13. The transformation since then has been remarkable. By mid-2025 she had climbed to number three in doubles and number four in singles, and by March 2026 she owns the top spot in both with 7,087 and 7,244 points, respectively -- point totals high enough to place her at number 21 in GD U15 and number 15 in GS U15, confirming that she is already competitive against older girls. Her recent form has been devastating: a perfect 12-0 at the 2025 Fortius South CRC where she took both the GS U15 and XD U15 titles, a 13-1 run at the 2026 SoCal ORC capturing GS U13 and GD U13 golds, and a 10-2 outing at the 2025 Northeast ORC that included the GS U13 title over rival Trisha Mehta.

Kasu's doubles game is anchored by two long-standing partnerships. Claire QiuChen Li has been her primary girls' doubles partner across 46 matches, and together they have become the pair to beat in GD U13, winning titles at the 2026 SoCal ORC and the 2025 Midwest ORC. In mixed doubles, Anirudh Rudharraju has filled the partner role for 45 matches, giving Kasu a reliable option that currently ranks them fifth in XD U13. The rivalry that defines her career at this stage is with Grace Cheng, who has appeared on the opposite side of the net in multiple singles and doubles finals -- Kasu defeated Cheng in both the GS U11 and GD U11 finals at the 2024 Midwest ORC, and beat Cheng and Sophia Liu in the GD U13 final at the 2026 SoCal ORC. Mizuki Ikeuchi and Siddhi Thorat have also posed challenges in recent singles draws. What makes Kasu particularly dangerous is that she has no glaring weakness: her 77-percent career win rate holds steady across singles, doubles, and mixed, and her willingness to compete up in age -- taking a GS U15 title at the Fortius South CRC and already appearing in U17 doubles rankings at number 57 -- suggests that the ceiling on her trajectory is still a long way from being reached.
