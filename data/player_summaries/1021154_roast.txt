<p>Ah yes, the 2005 Arunabh Ganguli vintage—a curious specimen that initially presents with promising aromatics of potential but reveals its true character upon closer examination. The nose opens with hints of ambition and youthful enthusiasm, though one immediately detects the underlying notes of a 34% win rate that suggests this particular vintage may have been harvested a touch too early.</p>

<p>The palate delivers a complex bouquet of mixed doubles partnerships—Yuito Tao, Srikar Kovvuri, Vivian Liu, Anna Choi—each contributing their own terroir to this increasingly muddled blend. The 2022 Junior National Championships expression shows particularly harsh tannins with zero wins and four losses, creating an almost undrinkable astringency. Similarly, the SPBA MW ORC bottling from that same year presents with an aggressive 0-6 finish that would make even the most forgiving wine critic reach for a palate cleanser.</p>

<p>However, the 2023 Midwest OLC vintage shows this vineyard's potential—achieving not one but two finals appearances, though both ultimately corked by superior opponents. The 5-3 record here suggests that given proper conditions and perhaps a more favorable climate, this vintage might actually develop into something... well, let's say "approachable" rather than "distinguished."</p>

<p>The current unranked status speaks to a wine that has unfortunately lost its structure over time, though one must note the previous rankings of #87 in doubles and #62 in singles show this vintage once held promise. Best enjoyed immediately with low expectations and paired with patience—definitely not one for the cellar, as extended aging has proven... unforgiving. A wine that reminds us that not every year produces a classic.</p>
