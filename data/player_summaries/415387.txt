Aayush Jaladurgam has undergone one of the most dramatic transformations on the U.S. junior badminton circuit, rising from obscurity to elite status in barely two years. In December 2023, he was ranked number 77 in Boys Singles U11 and number 62 in Boys Doubles U11. By December 2024 he had surged to BS U11 number two and BD U11 number eight, and now, having aged into the U13 division, he holds the number two singles ranking and number five doubles ranking -- plus a number seven spot in Mixed Doubles U13. His 156-64 career record across 23 tournaments translates to a 70 percent win rate, and his recent form has been prolific: a 13-3 record at the 2026 DFW South ORC, an 11-5 mark at the 2026 SoCal ORC, and a 9-3 showing at the 2025 Northeast ORC demonstrate that he thrives in high-volume tournament weekends where depth of endurance matters as much as skill.

Jaladurgam's title collection tells the story of a player who dominates across formats. He has won singles crowns at the 2024 NorCal ORC, the 2024 Northeast ORC, the 2026 SoCal ORC, and the 2026 DFW South ORC, along with doubles titles at the 2024 Northeast ORC, the 2025 Austin Leander South OLC, and the 2025 South CRC. His mixed doubles title at the 2024 NorCal ORC rounds out an eight-title haul that spans all three disciplines. His rivalry with Vignan Chandrasekaran has become the defining subplot of U13 boys' badminton -- the two have met on opposite sides of finals brackets repeatedly, and Chandrasekaran is also one of Jaladurgam's doubles partners with 11 matches shared, creating the kind of frenemy dynamic that sharpens both players. His most frequent doubles partner has been Leo Ye at 14 matches, while Siddhi Thorat has emerged as his go-to mixed doubles teammate with 13 matches together. Jeremy Wang has also entered the rotation recently, partnering in both the 2026 SoCal and DFW events. At eleven years old, Jaladurgam's combination of volume, versatility, and competitive resilience makes him one of the most complete young prospects in the country.
