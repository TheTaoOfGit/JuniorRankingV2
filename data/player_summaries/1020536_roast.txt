<p>Krish Raja (born 2010) is an American junior badminton player best known for his remarkable ability to achieve mathematical precision in mediocrity, maintaining a career win rate of exactly 49% across 31 tournaments.[citation needed] Raja has distinguished himself as a specialist in men's doubles, where he ranks #11 nationally in U17, though this achievement is somewhat diminished by his simultaneous ranking of #516 in mixed doubles, suggesting an inexplicable inability to coordinate with anyone possessing two X chromosomes.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Raja's partnership with Alex Wen has become the stuff of legend, with the duo playing together an astounding 32 times despite never actually winning a tournament together.[citation needed] This prolific collaboration has led badminton statisticians to classify their relationship as "the most committed underachievement in junior sports history."[citation needed] His other notable partnerships include Allen w Yang (17 matches) and Yichen Li (11 matches), forming what insiders call "The Axis of Almost" for their consistent ability to reach the semifinals and then spectacularly implode.[citation needed]</p>

<p>The 2025 YONEX BELLEVUE NORTHWEST ORC marked a career low point for Raja, where he achieved the rare distinction of losing every single match (0W-2L), a performance so impressively bad that tournament organizers considered creating a special "participation trophy" just for him.[citation needed] This followed his mixed showing at the 2024 U.S. Junior National Championships, where his 9W-6L record was described by one coach as "aggressively average."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite ranking #85 in singles, Raja continues to enter singles events with the unwavering optimism of someone who clearly doesn't own a mirror or access to ranking websites.[citation needed] His mixed doubles ranking of #680 has prompted speculation that he may have accidentally wandered into the wrong sport entirely, with some suggesting he might be better suited for chess, where his opponents would be legally required to maintain social distance.[citation needed] As of March 2026, Raja remains titleless but eternally hopeful, embodying the fighting spirit of someone who has never met a tournament entry fee he couldn't waste.[citation needed]</p>
