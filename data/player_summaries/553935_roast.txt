<p>It was a cold March morning when the case of Rayan Shyam landed on my desk. Born in 2017, this kid had been making waves in the junior circuit – and by waves, I mean the kind you get when you drop a pebble in a still pond. Three tournaments, five wins, nine losses. The numbers don't lie, but sometimes I wish they would.</p>

<p>I started digging deeper into this mystery. The DFW Badminton South ORC in February – he went 2-2, not bad for a kid still figuring out which end of the racket to hold. But then came the Schafer SLG South OLC, where dreams go to die. One win, four losses. I've seen murder scenes with better ratios. The kid was getting schooled harder than a substitute teacher on the first day.</p>

<p>His partner in crime? One Shen Hao Lim, who'd been riding shotgun for six matches of what I can only describe as "educational experiences." Together, they were like Batman and Robin, if Batman forgot his utility belt and Robin showed up in flip-flops. In the shadowy world of junior badminton doubles, ranking #47 in BD U11 with 531 points, young Rayan was climbing the mountain one stumble at a time.</p>

<p>The Vistara South OLC was supposed to be his redemption arc – 2 wins, 3 losses. Still underwater, but at least he was learning to doggy paddle. At 8 years old, ranking #44 in BS U11, he's got time to crack this case. The real mystery isn't why he's losing – it's how long before this gumshoe figures out that in badminton, like in noir stories, persistence pays off. Until then, I'll be watching from the shadows, waiting for his first title to drop like the other shuttlecock never did.</p>
