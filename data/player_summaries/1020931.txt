<p>Bernice Xu has established herself as one of the most promising doubles specialists in junior badminton, climbing from #41 in Girls' Doubles U15 to an impressive #22 in GD U17 over just two years. The 16-year-old's balanced attack across multiple disciplines is evident in her current rankings: #24 in Girls' Singles U17, #22 in Girls' Doubles U17, and #27 in Mixed Doubles U17, showcasing her versatility on court.</p>

<p>Her breakthrough moment came at the 2024 Dave Freeman Jr. OLC, where she captured her first major title in Girls' Doubles U15 while posting a dominant 8-2 record across all events. This championship performance highlighted her ability to peak at crucial moments, a trait she's continued to display with strong showings at premier tournaments including a 7-6 record at the 2025 Yonex U.S. Junior National Championships.</p>

<p>Xu's doubles prowess extends beyond individual rankings to her exceptional partnership chemistry, particularly with Renee Leung across 23 matches and Daniel Xie through 22 contests. Her ability to adapt to different playing styles is further demonstrated through successful collaborations with Kingsley Chong, Christopher Gunawan, and Wendy Li, making her one of the most sought-after partners on the junior circuit.</p>

<p>With 32 tournaments under her belt and a 110-117 overall record that reflects her willingness to compete against top-tier opposition, Xu continues her upward trajectory. Her recent surge to #22 in GD U17 from #40 just over a year ago, combined with strong performances at both the Dave Freeman Jr. SoCal OLC (7-2) and multiple Yonex events, positions her as a player to watch as she approaches the upper echelons of junior badminton.</p>
