<p>★★☆☆☆ "Tailong Cheng: The Chronicles of Almost" arrives as yet another entry in the oversaturated junior badminton genre, following our 12-year-old protagonist through a meandering 19-tournament odyssey that plays like a rough cut still searching for its third act. Director Reality has given us a classic underdog narrative, complete with a 59-62 win-loss record that hovers around mediocrity with the precision of a well-calibrated GPS stuck in traffic.</p>

<p>The supporting cast delivers mixed performances, with Cyrus M Cai appearing in a staggering 16 scenes as Cheng's most frequent doubles partner—a bromance that's clearly the film's strongest subplot. Meanwhile, Deepika Vaishnavi Venkatraman's 8-match arc provides solid comic relief, though one can't help but wonder if the chemistry was there or if they were simply thrown together by lazy casting directors at various New England tournaments. The ensemble work at the 2024 OBA NW OLC (8W-5L) represents the film's peak dramatic tension, showcasing genuine promise before dissolving into the forgettable 1W-3L showing at this year's Wayside Northeast CRC.</p>

<p>What's most frustrating about this biographical sports drama is its protagonist's stubborn refusal to embrace either triumph or tragedy. Cheng's lone finals appearance—a loss in BD U11 at the 2024 NE MASSBAD OLC—feels like a deleted scene from a more interesting movie, while his steady climb from #90 to #31 in boy's doubles suggests a character arc that's moving at geological pace. The film's ranking system becomes increasingly convoluted as Cheng simultaneously holds positions from #37 to #349 depending on which age category you're watching, like a sports movie that can't decide if it's rated G or PG-13.</p>

<p>Perhaps most telling is the title drought that defines this production—zero championships across 19 tournaments feels less like dramatic tension and more like a director who's forgotten how movies are supposed to end. While there's undeniable charm in watching a young athlete navigate the junior circuit with the determination of someone who's clearly still figuring out the rules, "Tailong Cheng: The Chronicles of Almost" ultimately feels like a sports movie that's still in pre-production, promising a more compelling sequel once its star hits puberty and discovers a killer instinct.</p>
