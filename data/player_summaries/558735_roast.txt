<p><b>BS U19 Champion Who Still Gets Blown Out: Qingyuan Song's Range Is Suspicious</b></p>

<p>Qingyuan Song won the BS U19 title at Dave Freeman and has an 8-4 career record at 66%, which looks solid until you notice he got blown out by Eshan Neog in doubles and Gavin Le in U17 singles. So he can beat Aryan Dayaram for a title but gets demolished by anyone at a higher tier. His range goes from champion to punching bag depending on who's across the net.</p>

<p>Ranked #70 in BS U19 and #123 in BD U19, that doubles ranking is a cliff drop from his singles -- suggesting he's either a terrible partner or partners make him worse, which amounts to the same thing. His 1-2 record at the Yonex Arena ORC shows the title at Dave Freeman might have been more about the draw than the player.</p>

<p>With only three tournaments under his belt, Qingyuan has the smallest sample size of anyone claiming a title. One great tournament does not a career make, and those blowout losses are a reminder that the U19 field has plenty of players ready to send him back to reality.</p>
