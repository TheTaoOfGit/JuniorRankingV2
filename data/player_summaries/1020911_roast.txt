<p>Sasha Suman (born 2009) is an American badminton player best known for her revolutionary approach to singles play: avoiding it entirely in favor of doubles.[citation needed] Currently ranked #9 in U19 girls doubles but languishing at #22 in singles, Suman has perfected the art of finding talented partners to carry her to victory, a strategy that has netted her an impressive nine titles across various age divisions.</p>

<p>Suman's career breakthrough came in 2021 when she dominated the U13 circuit, winning multiple titles at events like the SoCal ORC and USAB South Open Local Championships.[citation needed] Critics noted that beating 11-year-olds as a 12-year-old hardly constituted a fair fight, but Suman reportedly responded by immediately aging up to terrorize the U15s instead.[citation needed] Her tactical genius was on full display at the 2023 Midwest ORC, where she posted a commanding 12-3 record while somehow still managing to lose the U15 girls doubles final—a feat that statisticians are still trying to understand.</p>

<p>The 2025 YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships marked the pinnacle of Suman's partnership-based empire, where she claimed both girls doubles and mixed doubles U17 titles.[citation needed] Her most frequent collaborator, Yalan Zhu (33 matches), has been described by unnamed sources as "the Robin to Suman's Batman, if Batman were significantly worse at solo missions."[citation needed] When Zhu is unavailable, Suman has demonstrated remarkable adaptability by cycling through partners like Adhyaan Balaji (29 matches) and Lydia Chao (24 matches), leading some to speculate she maintains a sophisticated spreadsheet ranking her teammates' carrying capacity.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite her obvious talent for finding competent doubles partners, Suman's singles career remains puzzlingly mediocre, with her ranking actually declining from #18 to #22 in the U19 division between February and March 2026.[citation needed] Tournament observers frequently note the stark contrast between her commanding presence at the net during doubles and her bewildered expression when forced to cover an entire court alone, though Suman's representatives have yet to comment on these allegations.[citation needed]</p>
