<p>Lisa Liu (born 2013) is an American junior badminton player widely regarded as one of the most consistently disappointing competitors in the sport's recent history[citation needed]. Despite maintaining a stellar 7-12 career record across four tournaments, Liu has somehow managed to avoid winning a single title, leading experts to theorize that she may have discovered an entirely new form of competitive badminton focused on creative losing[citation needed].</p>

<p>Liu's most notable achievement came during her partnership with Andrew Mikhail Sinyushkin, with whom she played three matches, presumably because Sinyushkin was contractually obligated to continue[citation needed]. Her strategic diversity is evident in her willingness to disappoint alongside various partners, including Deeksha Suresh Babu and Lukas Gabriel Bayani, both of whom have since been spotted practicing with tennis rackets[citation needed]. Her brief collaboration with players bearing names like Kesan Murthysundar and Ruedi Cheng suggests either remarkable international appeal or a concerning pattern of foreign exchange students being assigned random badminton partners[citation needed].</p>

<p>The 2025 YONEX LIBC NORTHEAST ORC tournament marked a career milestone for Liu, as she achieved a perfect 0-4 record, demonstrating the kind of mathematical precision rarely seen outside of laboratory settings[citation needed]. Her subsequent performance at the 2025 South OLC, where she managed to win three whole matches, caused such shock among tournament organizers that they reportedly checked her ID twice[citation needed]. Currently ranked #188 in Girls' Singles U15 despite accumulating 961 points, Liu has revolutionized the concept of point efficiency by proving that quantity and quality can indeed be mutually exclusive[citation needed].</p>

<p>Liu's ranking progression tells the tragic tale of a meteoric fall from her March 2026 peak of #103 in GS U15 to her current position, a descent so rapid that NASA has reportedly requested her trajectory data for atmospheric re-entry research[citation needed]. At age 12, Liu continues to compete with the unwavering optimism of someone who either hasn't discovered calculators yet or possesses an admirable commitment to the sport's character-building potential[citation needed].</p>
