<p>⭐⭐⭐☆☆ Visited Steven Li's Badminton Bistro expecting a fine dining experience, but got more of a "hit-or-miss" buffet situation. With a 52% success rate, you're basically flipping a coin on whether your meal will be satisfying or leave you hungry for more. The establishment has been operating since 2022, and while they've managed to stay open across 27 different locations, the quality control remains... questionable.</p>

<p>The menu is surprisingly extensive for such a young chef (born 2012, if you can believe it). They offer singles specials where they're ranked #24 in the U15 category, but venture into their U17 or U19 offerings and suddenly you're dealing with #68 and #149 respectively - talk about a drop in quality as you move up the tiers! The doubles selection is where they shine brightest, particularly their U15 Boy's Doubles (#23) which has actually won them some local recognition. However, their Mixed Doubles department seems perpetually understaffed, hovering around #69-201 depending on which age bracket you're dining in.</p>

<p>The service model is interesting - they clearly believe in job rotation, having worked with Edwin Huang for 31 shifts, though they seem to cycle through partners faster than a busy restaurant cycles through busboys. Wayne Chien got 15 shifts, Marissa Zhang managed 12, and poor Charvi Srivastava only got 7 before presumably being transferred to another location. The good news? They actually managed to win the 2025 Dave Freeman Jr. SoCal tournament with their mixed service approach, so clearly some combinations work better than others.</p>

<p>Recent visits to the Seattle Northwest location showed promise (5W-1L), though they did choke in the finals there. Their 2024 Junior Nationals appearance was genuinely impressive (16W-8L), proving this little establishment can occasionally compete with the big chains. Sure, they lost as many matches as they won overall, but hey - at least they're consistent! For a restaurant run by someone who could barely hold a spatula in 2022, Steven Li's has shown steady improvement and managed to collect 4 championship trophies along the way.</p>

<p>Would I recommend? If you're looking for a reliable, predictable dining experience, maybe try elsewhere. But if you enjoy the thrill of not knowing whether you'll get a championship-worthy meal or walk away still hungry, Steven Li's Badminton Bistro offers exactly that coin-flip excitement. Just maybe stick to their U15 specialties - their older menu items are still a work in progress.</p>
