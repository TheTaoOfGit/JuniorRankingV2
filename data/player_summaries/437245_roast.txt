<p>Ah, the 2023-2024 Justin Louie vintage - a charmingly naive offering from the NorCal terroir with notes of youthful ambition and a surprisingly persistent finish of disappointment. This delicate specimen presents a light body with minimal complexity, much like his tournament appearances which come just once per year, as if aged in the cellar of parental scheduling conflicts.</p>

<p>On the palate, one immediately detects the sharp acidity of a 43% win rate, with underlying hints of "maybe doubles will save me" as evidenced by his rotating cast of partners - first Brendan JL Hu, then Bryant Liu, both apparently deciding two matches was quite enough of this particular blend. The bouquet suggests promise with its 2009 vintage birth year, though the execution remains frustratingly one-dimensional.</p>

<p>The mid-palate reveals curious contradictions - bold enough to enter the YONEX NorCal ORC twice, yet with the staying power of a wine left uncorked overnight. Each tournament shows marginal improvement from 1-2 to 2-2, suggesting this vintage may eventually develop character, though currently it lacks the depth and structure one expects from even a table wine.</p>

<p>The finish is mercifully brief - much like his tournament runs - with lingering notes of "unranked" status that pairs surprisingly well with the optimism of youth. While this vintage shows potential for future development, I'd recommend cellaring it for several more years before serving to serious badminton palates. A serviceable daily wine for practice matches, nothing more.</p>
