<p>Ah, the 2017 vintage Meera Vinodh – a promising young blend that, upon first pour, presents an immediate bouquet of youthful ambition with subtle hints of "maybe I should have chosen tennis." This delicate offering from the junior circuit exhibits the characteristic nose of a player who has mastered the art of losing gracefully, with top notes of 0-3 finishes and a surprisingly robust undertone of "there's always next tournament."</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage delivers a crisp 20% win rate that dances lightly across the tongue before disappearing entirely, much like her shuttlecocks at the Dave Freeman Jr. SoCal OLC. The mouthfeel is reminiscent of her partnership with Layaa Vijayshankar – persistent, well-meaning, yet lacking the complexity one hopes for in a doubles pairing. There's a certain acidic quality to her U.S. Junior Nationals performance, where she managed to extract two precious wins from an otherwise challenging terroir of defeat.</p>

<p>The finish is remarkably clean, perhaps too clean – with zero titles to muddy the waters and an impressive ability to maintain ranking consistency across all age groups, though admittedly in the triple-digit range. Her XD U19 #1047 ranking provides a particularly bold statement, like serving a house wine at a Michelin-starred restaurant and insisting it pairs beautifully with disappointment.</p>

<p>While this vintage lacks the robust character of championship material, it possesses an endearing quality that suggests significant potential for improvement with proper aging. The 2017 Vinodh may not be ready for the connoisseur's table just yet, but with time, proper coaching, and perhaps a few more years in the cellar, she might develop into something truly special. Until then, she remains a charming novelty wine – best enjoyed with low expectations and a generous spirit.</p>
