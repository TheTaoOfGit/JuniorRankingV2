<p>At just 16 years old, Vivian Song has established herself as one of the Northwest's most versatile junior badminton competitors, excelling across multiple disciplines with particular strength in doubles play. Her girls' doubles ranking of #66 in the U17 category and #90 in U19 demonstrates her ability to compete effectively against older opponents, while her partnership with Allen w Yang has produced 24 matches of valuable mixed doubles experience.</p>

<p>Song's competitive journey showcases remarkable consistency and determination, having competed in 21 tournaments over her young career. Her breakthrough moments include reaching the girls' singles final at the 2023 NW OLC and an earlier final appearance at the 2021 NW CRC as a U13 player. These finals appearances highlight her ability to perform under pressure and reach the biggest stages of junior competition, even before claiming her first title.</p>

<p>The 2022 NW Oregon OLC stands out as a perfect tournament for Song, where she went an impressive 7-0 across all matches. This dominant performance demonstrates the high level she can reach when everything clicks. Her willingness to compete nationally, including multiple appearances at the YONEX U.S. Junior National Championships, shows her ambition to test herself against the country's best junior talent.</p>

<p>What sets Song apart is her tactical approach to building partnerships across different disciplines. Beyond her primary mixed doubles collaboration with Yang, she has developed chemistry with multiple doubles partners including Camellia Wang, Anya Kuan, and Daby Huang. This adaptability and team-oriented mindset, combined with her steady ranking improvements in doubles, positions her perfectly for continued growth in the sport's most dynamic formats.</p>
