<p>Welcome, fellow hikers, to the Devin Shao Memorial Trail—a winding 22-tournament path that's proven surprisingly challenging for most visitors, with only a 44% success rate for those attempting the full journey. This moderately difficult trail begins promisingly at the 2021 NW CRC summit, where our young explorer claimed his first and only BD U13 peak at age 12. However, seasoned trail guides warn that this early vista may give hikers false confidence about the terrain ahead.</p>

<p>The trail quickly diverges into two distinct routes: the treacherous Singles Path, where hikers frequently find themselves lost in the wilderness at #669 elevation, and the more navigable Doubles Route, where our subject maintains a respectable #301 position. Park rangers note that Devin rarely attempts solo expeditions anymore, preferring the buddy system with his faithful trail companion Ethan Biren Fong, who has accompanied him on 28 separate excursions. When Ethan isn't available, he's been spotted with alternate hiking partners Jocelyn Zhang and Daniel Park, though these partnerships haven't yielded the same scenic overlooks.</p>

<p>Recent trail reports indicate increasingly uneven terrain, with notable washouts at the 2022 NorCal ORC (0-2 record) and 2024 SoCal ORC (another 0-2 showing), where hikers report finding our subject completely turned around and heading back to the parking lot. The 2024 U.S. Junior National Championships section proves particularly brutal, with a steep 2-4 descent that left many wondering if proper trail preparation was undertaken.</p>

<p>Trail maintenance crews report that while the path shows occasional signs of improvement—like the encouraging 4-2 stretch at Northwest CRC—the overall route remains inconsistent, with frequent detours and backtracking. Visitors should pack extra provisions and perhaps consider bringing a more experienced guide, as this trail seems to require significant external support to reach any meaningful destinations. The park service recommends this hike primarily for those seeking a humbling outdoor experience rather than summit photography opportunities.</p>
