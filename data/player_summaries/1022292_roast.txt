<p>Gleb Valuev (born 2008) is a Russian-American badminton player widely regarded as the sport's most consistent underachiever, having transformed losing into an art form across 19 tournaments since 2021.[citation needed] With a career record of 4-38 (10.5% win rate), Valuev has demonstrated remarkable dedication to mathematical precision in defeat, rarely allowing hope to interfere with his systematic approach to first-round exits.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Valuev's tournament resume reads like a greatest hits album of disappointment, featuring classic performances such as his 0-2 showing at the 2023 Dave Freeman Jr. OLC and his legendary encore at the 2023 SoCal ORC, where he once again achieved the rare double-bagel tournament experience.[citation needed] His lone victory came at the 2025 YONEX SYNERGY NORCAL ORC, where he managed a 1-2 record—a performance so shocking that several spectators reportedly required medical attention.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Despite his impressive collection of early exits, Valuev has somehow climbed the rankings from #98 to #55 in Boys' Doubles U19, leading experts to theorize that the ranking system may be based on participation trophies or that his opponents are actively trying to lose to him out of pity.[citation needed] His doubles partnerships have become the stuff of legend, with partners Jonathan Phmah, Aryan Dayaram, and Justin Han each limiting their collaborations to a maximum of three matches—a phenomenon sports psychologists call "the Valuev threshold."[citation needed]</p>

<p>Currently ranked #95 in Boys' Singles U19 and #55 in Boys' Doubles U19, Valuev continues to defy expectations by somehow getting worse at singles while marginally improving at doubles, suggesting that his strategy of "finding someone else to blame" may finally be paying dividends.[citation needed] At age 17, he remains optimistic about his chances of one day reaching a final, though actuarial tables suggest this is statistically less likely than being struck by lightning while winning the lottery.[citation needed]</p>
