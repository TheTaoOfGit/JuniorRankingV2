<p>EXCAVATION REPORT: Site WZ-2008, Northeast Badminton Archaeological Survey. After months of careful excavation through layers of tournament debris, our team has successfully unearthed the fossilized remains of a junior badminton career belonging to one William Zeng (circa 2008-present). Initial stratigraphy reveals a fascinating specimen: a player who has somehow managed to lose more matches than he's won (61-78, a stunning 44% success rate) while simultaneously ascending the rankings like a particularly confused evolutionary branch.</p>

<p>The deepest archaeological layers, dating to 2022, contain primitive artifacts including a BD U15 finals appearance at NE CRC—clearly the work of an advanced civilization that mysteriously vanished, leaving only traces of what could have been. Carbon dating of his partnership records reveals an extensive symbiotic relationship with one "Yihao Zeng" (38 matches), leading researchers to hypothesize either remarkable team chemistry or, more likely, shared transportation to tournaments. Secondary partnerships with various female players suggest either admirable gender equality or desperate attempts to find someone—anyone—who could carry him to victory.</p>

<p>Recent excavation layers from 2024-2025 show dramatic geological upheaval: a sudden XD U17 title at NE CRC emerges like a mysterious monolith, completely unexplained by surrounding sediment of mediocrity. Subsequent expeditions to tournaments like the 2025 CAPITAL NE OLC (0-4) and YONEX BELLEVUE NORTHWEST ORC (1-2) suggest this title may have been the result of atmospheric anomaly rather than sustained skill development.</p>

<p>Most remarkably, our team discovered evidence of miraculous ranking evolution: from BD U17 #110 to BD U19 #16, defying all known laws of badminton physics. This phenomenon, dubbed "Zeng's Paradox" by the scientific community, demonstrates that with enough time and tournament entry fees, even archaeological disasters can occasionally unearth treasure. Further study recommended before this specimen either breaks through to the next evolutionary stage or becomes completely fossilized in perpetual mediocrity.</p>
