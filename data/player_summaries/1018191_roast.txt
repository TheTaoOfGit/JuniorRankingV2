<p><b>Restaurant Review: William Zeng</b> — A bistro that serves an ambitious three-course menu (BD U19 #15, BS U19 #36, XD U19 #28) but leaves you checking the bill against what actually arrived. The 42% career win rate (53-73 across 22 tournaments) is like a restaurant with more negative Yelp reviews than positive ones.</p>

<p>The doubles tasting plate is the strongest offering, anchored by the house specialty: the Yihao Zeng collaboration (33 matches), a family recipe that clearly benefits from shared DNA. The XD U17 title at the 2024 NE CRC was the Michelin-star moment, and the BD U17 runner-up at the 2023 NE OLC hinted at a kitchen that can almost keep up with the ambition of the menu.</p>

<p>But the recent courses have been undercooked: a 0-2 appetizer at the 2025 NE ORC and a 2-3 main at the 2024 Midwest ORC. The progression from BD U17 #110 to BD U19 #15 is like a food truck that somehow earned a reservation-only reputation — impressive trajectory, questionable consistency. The singles entree at BS U19 #36 remains bland, and the XD partnership with Kamalini Packiyaraj (9 matches) is still developing its flavor profile.</p>
