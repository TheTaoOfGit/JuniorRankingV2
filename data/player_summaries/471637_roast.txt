<p>Ah yes, the 2010 vintage Jeffrey Wang—a curious specimen that presents with an immediate nose of youthful ambition, yet delivers a surprisingly austere palate upon closer inspection. This particular vintage opens with promising aromatics from the 2024 Midwest CRC, where one detects hints of actual competency with a respectable 4-3 finish, but quickly dissipates into the harsh, unforgiving terroir of a career 8-19 record that leaves even the most charitable sommelier reaching for a palate cleanser.</p>

<p>The doubles expression, primarily aged alongside the reliable Eric Zhun Hu for seven matches, shows more character than the singles offerings, climbing to a respectable U17 #137 before experiencing the inevitable oxidation that has seen it slip from its peak of #94. One notes the curious decision to occasionally blend with partners like Rheya Huang—a mixed vintage that suggests experimental fermentation techniques, though the results remain... unrefined.</p>

<p>The 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals presents as perhaps the most memorable tasting note in this collection—a complete absence of wins paired with four devastating losses that linger on the tongue like a wine left too long in direct sunlight. The bouquet is one of shattered dreams and parental investment questioned, with undertones of "maybe badminton isn't the right sport" that become increasingly prominent as the bottle breathes.</p>

<p>While the current singles rankings of #531 and #687 suggest this vintage has yet to find its legs, there remains aging potential in this young player. I'd recommend cellaring this one for several more years—perhaps until he discovers a growth spurt or a more forgiving tournament bracket. Best served with lowered expectations and a side of patience.</p>
