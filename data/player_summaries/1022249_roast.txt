<p>Ah yes, the 2011 vintage Lindsey Li—a rather spirited little number from the junior circuit that shows both promise and the telltale signs of an ambitious young grape picked perhaps a touch too early. Upon first inspection, this offering presents a delightfully robust 67% win rate across nine tastings, though one must note the curious absence from the current rankings board—much like a wine that's mysteriously disappeared from the cellar just when you need it most.</p>

<p>The nose reveals bright top notes of her 2022 Northwest ORC performance—a truly exceptional vintage where she dominated with a remarkable 12-1 bouquet, claiming both the GS and GD U13 titles with the confidence of a Bordeaux in a good year. However, swirling deeper, one detects the slightly sour undertones of her 2022 Synergy NorCal ORC showing, a rather disappointing 2-4 affair that left a bitter finish reminiscent of a wine left open too long in the California sun.</p>

<p>The palate reveals fascinating complexity in her partnership selections—most notably her extensive collaboration with Camellia Wang, a pairing that's appeared 14 times like a reliable house blend. One also notes hints of Jason Yang and Selina Xu, though these partnerships lack the depth and consistency of her primary vintage. The 2023 YONEX U.S. Junior Nationals presents as disappointingly flat—a 6-6 showing that suggests this particular bottle may have peaked early.</p>

<p>The finish is where this vintage becomes most intriguing yet concerning. After reaching a respectable U15 ranking of #34 in girls' doubles by late 2023, our young vintner has seemingly vanished from the rankings altogether—a mysterious disappearance that would make even the most seasoned sommelier scratch their head. One can only hope this isn't a case of a promising young wine turning to vinegar, but rather simply aging quietly in the cellar, waiting for the perfect moment to re-emerge and surprise us all.</p>
