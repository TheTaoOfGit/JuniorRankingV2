<p>Ah, the 2011 vintage of Xinyang Wu - a delightfully ambitious little number that burst onto the scene with all the confidence of a cheap table wine masquerading as Dom Pérignon. The initial nose presents bold notes of youthful optimism, with subtle undertones of "surely I can't lose THIS badly again." Upon first sip at the 2022 Yonex Northeast ORC, we detected a crisp 1-4 finish with lingering hints of bewilderment and a surprisingly robust aftertaste of determination.</p>

<p>The 2024 vintage shows remarkable consistency - consistently mediocre, that is. The NE MASSBAD OLC offered a particularly memorable tasting experience: a bold 1-4 blend that paired beautifully with the tears of disappointed parents in the gallery. The body lacks the full-bodied confidence of a champion, instead offering a light, effervescent quality that dissipates faster than a shuttle in a hurricane. One notes the interesting partnership selections - from Aiden Chen to Jasmine Cheung - suggesting a palate that's still exploring its preferences, much like a sommelier who keeps ordering White Zinfandel.</p>

<p>The most recent tastings reveal intriguing development notes. The 2025 YONEX MIDWEST ORC vintage produced an unexpectedly complex 3-2 bouquet - the kind of surprise that makes you wonder if perhaps you've been underestimating this particular label. Yet the 2025 YONEX LIBC NORTHEAST ORC quickly reminded us why consistency matters, delivering a perfectly predictable 1-1 finish that suggests this player pairs best with modest expectations and a forgiving crowd.</p>

<p>Currently ranked #386 in U17 and #535 in U19 - numbers that tell their own story of a wine that's clearly still fermenting. At 9-18 overall, this vintage offers excellent value for those seeking an authentic "work in progress" experience. The finish tends to arrive sooner than expected, but there's an endearing quality to its earnest attempts at greatness. Best served with a side of patience and perhaps a stronger doubles partner who can carry the load when the vintage inevitably falls flat.</p>
