<p>Rishabh Praveen (born 2011) is an American junior badminton player best known for his remarkable ability to reach finals and then immediately forget how to play the sport.[citation needed] Often referred to as "The Silver Collector" by tournament organizers who have run out of second-place trophies,[citation needed] Praveen has achieved the impressive feat of losing seven finals across various age groups, a consistency that would be admirable if it weren't so tragic. His career peaked at age 10 when he won the BS U11 title at the 2021 SoCal ORC, leading many to wonder if he should have retired while ahead.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Currently ranked #37 in BD U17 and #56 in BS U17, Praveen occupies what experts call "the sweet spot of mediocrity,"[citation needed] where he's good enough to make other players nervous but not quite good enough to actually beat them when it matters. His most recent doubles title came in 2023 at the BD U13 level, suggesting that his opponents have finally learned to read his apparently predictable playing style.[citation needed] Tournament directors have reportedly started scheduling his finals matches during lunch breaks, as attendance has dropped significantly when spectators realized the outcome.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Praveen's most enduring partnership has been with Cayden Wang, with whom he has played 31 matches, leading some observers to question whether Wang has been contractually obligated to continue the partnership or if he simply enjoys the guaranteed path to finals.[citation needed] Their doubles chemistry has been described as "functional at best" and "a case study in how two decent players can somehow make each other worse."[citation needed] Despite this, Praveen continues to maintain a 59% win rate, which is impressive until you realize that most of those wins come from matches where his opponents accidentally hit the shuttlecock into their own faces.[citation needed]</p>

<p>Recent performances at tournaments like the 2024 YONEX U.S. JUNIOR NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS (9W-6L) suggest that Praveen has found his calling as a perennial dark horse candidate—dark enough that no one sees him coming, but horse-like in his ability to stumble at the final hurdle.[citation needed] As he transitions into older age groups, badminton analysts predict he will either finally break through to claim his first title since 2023, or continue building what may become the most impressive collection of runner-up medals in junior badminton history.[citation needed]</p>
