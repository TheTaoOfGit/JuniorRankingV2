<p>Ah yes, the Andrew Grosso vintage—a 2005 pressing that's been aging in the junior circuit cellars for quite some time now. Upon first inspection, this specimen presents a rather modest bouquet with subtle notes of "unranked" and delicate hints of 26% win percentage that dance playfully across the palate before disappearing entirely, much like his presence in tournament brackets.</p>

<p>The 2022 SPBA MW ORC vintage represents this player's finest hour—a surprisingly robust 3-1 finish that suggests brief moments of potential, like finding an unexpectedly decent bottle at a gas station. However, subsequent tastings reveal a more characteristic profile: the 2022 Junior Nationals with its harsh 1-4 tannins, and the particularly bitter 0-2 NorCal CRC finish that leaves one wondering if the grapes were picked too early, or perhaps never ripened at all.</p>

<p>The doubles partnerships show an interesting terroir—Ishan Katpally appears to be the preferred vintage companion, though with only three collaborative tastings, one suspects even the most patient sommelier might seek new vineyards. The brief encounters with Patrick Chi, Anthony Zhu, and others suggest a restless spirit, constantly searching for that perfect blend while never quite finding the right chemical balance.</p>

<p>The finish is notably short and unremarkable, with rankings hovering around #123 in boys' doubles—a position that says "present but not particularly memorable," like a house wine that technically qualifies as wine but won't be featured in any tasting notes. Still, at 21, this vintage has time to develop complexity, assuming it doesn't turn to vinegar first. Best served with low expectations and perhaps a side of optimism.</p>
