<p><b>Three-Game Record: 16-17 (48%). Arundhathi Mohan Is a Top-10 Player Who Plays Like a Coin Flip in Tight Matches.</b></p>

<p>Arundhathi Mohan is GD U13 #9 and GS U13 #7 with five titles including a Junior Nationals GS U11 win — the kind of resume that should command respect. Then you see her 48% three-game record and the illusion shatters. She loses more three-gamers than she wins, which is baffling for someone with her pedigree. Her 5-2 finals record is strong, but her 7-6 semis record suggests she's barely surviving rounds she should dominate.</p>

<p>Her blowout losses to Adriel Ammy Chandran, Siddhi Thorat, and Bella Liu show that when she faces top-tier competition, the composure vanishes. Shravan Chockalingam has been her mixed partner for 45 matches — the most co-dependent partnership in junior badminton. Without Shravan, her XD record would probably collapse entirely.</p>

<p>At 65% overall win rate, Arundhathi is undeniably talented. But the three-game record is the elephant in the room: she's elite at beating weaker players and average at beating equals. The definition of front-running.</p>
