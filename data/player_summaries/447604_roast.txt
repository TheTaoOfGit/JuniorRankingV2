<p>★★★☆☆ "Arundhathi Mohan: The Badminton Chronicles" is a feel-good sports dramedy that peaks early and coasts on past glory. Director Time has crafted a narrative that starts with a bang—our protagonist's 2025 U.S. Junior Nationals performance was nothing short of a cinematic triple crown, sweeping GS, GD, and XD U13 titles like she was collecting participation trophies at a church picnic. The 12-0 record at that tournament had all the satisfying inevitability of a Marvel superhero film, though with significantly more shuttlecocks.</p>

<p>The supporting cast deserves recognition, particularly Shravan Chockalingam, who appears in 45 scenes as the reliable doubles partner—think Tom Hanks to Mohan's Meg Ryan, if Tom Hanks specialized in overhead smashes instead of romantic comedy timing. Their on-court chemistry carries the doubles plotlines, while Ayushi Raheja makes a memorable 20-match cameo that suggests untapped sequel potential.</p>

<p>Where the film falters is in its ambitious scope. While our 12-year-old star dominates the U13 category like a pint-sized Thanos, her rankings take a nosedive faster than a poorly executed drop shot as we venture into U15, U17, and U19 territory. Going from #7 to #126 across age groups isn't character development—it's mathematical reality meeting biological limitations. It's like watching "The Curious Case of Benjamin Button" in reverse, except instead of aging backwards, she's just playing increasingly tall people.</p>

<p>The tournament montages provide solid entertainment value, with satisfying wins at ABC OLC and those early YONEX championships, though some of the later SOCAL and South ORC sequences feel more like deleted scenes—present for completeness rather than narrative impact. The 66% win rate suggests a competent production, but the 1-3 record at YONEX South ORC was clearly this franchise's equivalent of "The Phantom Menace."</p>

<p>Ultimately, "Arundhathi Mohan: The Badminton Chronicles" succeeds as an origin story but struggles with sequel syndrome. At 100-51, it's a crowd-pleaser that knows its audience, even if that audience is getting taller and more competitive by the season. Worth watching for the nationals sweep alone, but don't expect the same magic when she inevitably faces the teenagers.</p>
