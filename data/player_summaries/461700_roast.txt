<p><b>One Title but 0-4 at NE MassBad: Aarush Mali's Resume Has One Peak and a Lot of Valleys</b></p>

<p>Aarush Mali won BS U15 at the 2025 Capital NE OLC, beating Anirudh Rudharraju for his sole title. That's the highlight. Everything else is a 33-43 career record (43%) with one 0-4 tournament at the 2024 NE MassBad OLC -- four matches, four losses, presumably four regrets about the entry fee. His 1-0 finals and 1-0 semis records look perfect until you realize the sample size is comically small.</p>

<p>His three-game record of 11-6 (64%) is genuinely good, making him one of those infuriating players who can grind out close wins but somehow still loses the easy ones. His blowout losses to Zaph Bayona and Zisheng Fan suggest he runs into walls when facing elite competition. Partner Shreya Kirikera has been with him for 8 matches -- yes, that Shreya Kirikera with the 21% win rate. Birds of a feather lose together.</p>
