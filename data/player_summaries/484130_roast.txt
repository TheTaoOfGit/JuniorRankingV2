<p>Ah yes, the Anthony Song 2024 vintage - a delightfully modest expression from the junior courts with all the complexity of a box wine left open overnight. Upon first inspection, this offering presents a perfectly balanced 6-6 record from the U.S. Junior Nationals, achieving that rare feat of mathematical mediocrity that sommeliers call "aggressively unremarkable." The nose reveals hints of fresh optimism mixed with the distinct aroma of shuttlecocks that have seen better days.</p>

<p>On the palate, we detect notes of youthful ambition tempered by the reality of mixed doubles at #271, where our young vintner partnered with the capable Angie Lu before exploring the terroir with Eason Chin. The mouthfeel suggests someone still learning the difference between a clear and a net shot, with a finish that lingers somewhere between "promising" and "please keep practicing." The boys' singles ranking of #95 shows hints of potential, though the boys' doubles at #200 suggests this vintage may need more time in the cellar.</p>

<p>This 2012 birth year vintage displays the kind of raw enthusiasm that reminds us why we fell in love with junior badminton in the first place - unpolished, earnest, and blissfully unaware that winning exactly half your matches is somehow both an achievement and a mathematical curiosity. Best enjoyed now while expectations remain refreshingly low, though we suspect this particular vintage may improve dramatically with age. Pairs well with participation trophies and parental encouragement.</p>
