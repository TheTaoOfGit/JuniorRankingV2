<p>⭐⭐ "Jay Chun's Badminton Bistro" - Used to be the hottest spot in town, now it's giving me serious food poisoning vibes. I remember when this place was ranked #15 in the U19 category back in '23 - the Boys Doubles special with David Zuo was absolutely divine, they served it up 44 times and it was consistently satisfying. But honey, something went terribly wrong in the kitchen because by 2025 this establishment plummeted to #172 faster than a shuttlecock with a broken feather.</p>

<p>The menu used to be impressive - eight signature dishes including that legendary U17 Boys Singles at the 2021 NW CRC (11-2 record, chef's kiss!) and the Boys Doubles triumph at the 2023 U.S. Junior Nationals. But lately? The service has been so inconsistent that they're not even ranked anymore. It's like watching Gordon Ramsay's worst nightmare unfold in real time. The 72% success rate sounds good on paper, but when you're diving from #15 to #172, that's not seasoning - that's just salt in the wound.</p>

<p>Don't even get me started on the revolving door of sous chefs - sure, David Zuo was the reliable head chef for 44 services, but then we got a parade of temporary help like Anvita, Coco, and the Kulkarni siblings doing 5-6 shifts each. It's giving me serious "we can't keep our staff" energy. The 2022 South Frisco ORC was particularly tragic - 5 wins, 5 losses - like they couldn't decide if they wanted to be a fine dining establishment or a food truck.</p>

<p>Look, I'll give credit where it's due - this place had some phenomenal moments. That 2022 NW Oregon OLC performance (11-1) was absolutely chef's kiss perfection. But going from ranked contender to completely off the map? That's not a temporary closure for renovations, that's a full-blown health department shutdown. Maybe it's time for Jay Chun's to either hire a new management team or close down for good and let David Zuo open his own place.</p>
