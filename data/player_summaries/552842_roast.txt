<p>⭐⭐ "Kason Wang's Badminton Bistro" - Went here based on the hype from some U11 food bloggers, but honestly? This place is still figuring out its menu. Tried the "Austin Leander Special" in February and while the presentation showed promise (3 wins!), the execution fell apart faster than their 5-loss finale. The chef clearly has potential but seems to be cooking in way too many kitchens - saw him listed on menus from U11 all the way up to U19, which is like a food truck trying to compete with Michelin-starred establishments.</p>

<p>The partnership with Meera Bhalekar as sous chef has been the highlight - they've collaborated on 7 dishes together, which shows some consistency in this otherwise experimental kitchen. However, their recent DFW collaboration was a disaster (1-3 record), tasting like they forgot to add seasoning. The brief experiments with other partners like Akul and Alex felt more like speed dating than serious culinary partnerships.</p>

<p>What really confused me was the ambitious menu spanning every age category imaginable. Like, buddy, you're ranked #614 in U17 Boys Singles - maybe focus on perfecting your U11 signature dishes first? It's like a hot dog stand trying to serve sushi, filet mignon, AND baby food simultaneously. The 33% success rate suggests this kitchen might be biting off more than it can chew.</p>

<p>That said, there's something endearing about this scrappy little establishment. The recent Vistara tournament showed real improvement (3-4 record), and you can tell the young chef is passionate about his craft. Sure, the trophy cabinet is emptier than a vegetarian steakhouse, but Rome wasn't built in a day, and neither are badminton champions. Might revisit in a year or two when the menu has matured - there's definitely raw talent simmering beneath the surface.</p>
