<p>Ah yes, the 2022 Makenna Wong vintage—a rather ambitious young label that burst onto the SoCal scene with all the confidence of a box wine masquerading as a Bordeaux. This particular specimen exhibits a distinctive 20% alcohol content, which coincidentally matches her career win percentage. The nose presents immediate notes of optimism, quickly overwhelmed by the pungent aroma of 1-5 records at the CRC, with subtle undertones of "maybe doubles is my calling" that linger just long enough to disappoint.</p>

<p>On the palate, this vintage displays a remarkably thin body—much like her tournament resume—with sharp notes of inconsistency dancing across the tongue. The SoCal Arena CRC offered a slightly more refined 1-3 finish, suggesting some potential for improvement, though the overall structure remains disappointingly fragile. One detects hints of partnership experimentation, having sampled Amanda Kelly Chang, Ryan Zhu, and Shihan John Xiu as co-vintners, though none of these collaborations managed to elevate the final product above "table wine" status.</p>

<p>The mid-palate reveals an interesting complexity in her 2023 U19 rankings—a Girls' Doubles #86 that shows more promise than her Girls' Singles #71, while the Mixed Doubles #135 suggests she's still learning which grapes pair well together. There's a certain earnestness to this vintage that's almost endearing, like a plucky little Pinot Grigio that knows it'll never be a Chablis but keeps trying anyway.</p>

<p>The finish is admirably brief—much like her tournament appearances—leaving one with questions rather than answers. Currently unranked, this vintage appears to be aging in obscurity, perhaps gathering wisdom for a future release. While seasoned collectors might pass on this particular bottle, there's something to be said for its honest simplicity. Best consumed young, with low expectations, and paired with supportive friends who won't judge you for your choices. A wine that reminds us that not every vintage can be extraordinary—and that's perfectly fine.</p>
