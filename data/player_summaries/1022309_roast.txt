<p>Mira Chatterjee (born 2010) is an American junior badminton player best known for her remarkably consistent ability to lose exactly two matches per tournament[citation needed]. Despite her impressive 9-19 career record (32% win rate), Chatterjee has somehow managed to compete in eight tournaments without winning a single title, leading some experts to theorize that she may have discovered a new form of athletic perpetual motion[citation needed].</p>

<p>Chatterjee's breakthrough performance came at the 2022 Junior National Championships, where she achieved her career-best 2-2 record, marking the only time in her career she managed to win as many matches as she lost[citation needed]. This historic achievement was later overshadowed by her 0-3 performance at the 2023 SoCal ORC, where she became the first player in tournament history to lose more matches than she played[citation needed]. Her coach was reportedly "very proud" of her consistency[citation needed].</p>

<p>Known for her revolving door of doubles partners, Chatterjee has played with at least five different teammates, including notable collaborations with Manav Patel and Varshini Subramanian. Industry insiders suggest that her partners' frequent rotation may be related to a mysterious phenomenon known as "the Chatterjee Effect," wherein doubles teams spontaneously develop scheduling conflicts after exactly two matches[citation needed]. Despite this, she maintains that her partnership strategy is "building chemistry through diversity"[citation needed].</p>

<p>At her peak in December 2023, Chatterjee reached the dizzying heights of #113 in Girls' Doubles U15 and #261 in Mixed Doubles U15, rankings that some have compared to "being the tallest building in a very small, very flat town"[citation needed]. Currently unranked, she continues to compete with the determination of someone who hasn't yet realized that sometimes the scoreboard is trying to tell you something[citation needed].</p>
